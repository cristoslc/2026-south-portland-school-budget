### Reactions

Mom asked me what I thought about the budget meeting she watched online and honestly? It was kind of scary. Like, they're talking about completely changing how elementary schools work - making some schools just for little kids and some just for bigger kids. That sounds terrible! What if my favorite teacher from 4th grade can't help the 1st graders anymore? And what about kids who have older or younger siblings? They won't even be in the same building.

The really messed up part is they keep saying we don't have enough money but then it came out that when there were MORE kids in 2017, they had LESS teachers and staff. Now there are 300 fewer kids but 82 MORE adults working in the schools. Like, how does that math even work? And why did nobody notice this was happening until now? Some lady got up and asked why they didn't start dealing with this problem earlier when it would have been easier, and I don't think anyone really answered her.

The worst part was hearing that my teachers are scared. Mrs. Rodriguez, my band teacher, already seems stressed this year and now I know why. The union lady said all the elementary teachers feel like their family is being taken away. That made me really sad because teachers ARE like family to us. But then these council people were arguing about whether schools cost too much money, and one guy said people can't afford the taxes. I get that taxes are hard for families, but what about us kids? We didn't ask to be born during a budget crisis but we still deserve good schools and teachers who aren't worried about losing their jobs all the time.
