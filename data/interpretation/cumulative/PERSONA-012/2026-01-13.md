# Cumulative Record: Jaylen (PERSONA-012)
## Meeting: City Council Workshop -- January 13, 2026 -- 2026-01-13

### Interpretation

The January 13 workshop is about a building on Broadway — the old Mahoney School, empty for years — and Jaylen shows up because he heard students were consulted and they're deciding about a theater. The committee picked the option that keeps the theater and gym. "Significant public input." And right there in the chair's fourteen-point outreach summary — between 526 survey respondents and 19 news flash posts — is a line about "dozens of high school and middle school students." Jaylen has spent three months becoming an expert at recognizing what this looks like. It's Alex and Angela at the budget forum, introduced and then ignored. It's Jules Vazquez's poem at city hall in December, applauded and moved on from. It's the community member at the February 10 joint session who named student exclusion out loud while the council nodded and changed nothing. The students are in the slide. Their presence is real and their influence is a bullet point.

The theater preservation turns out to be almost immediately hollow. The architect's renovation scope covers "life safety only" — fire codes, accessible seating, a handful of new lights. Not a working theater. A theater that meets code. And by the end of four hours, councilors are talking about stripping even that out entirely to get the $194 million price tag down — a number the architect himself said he was "not confident" in. The committee said the community wanted the theater preserved. The council's response is to ask what it costs to skip it. Jaylen knows this pattern. The district has been running it all year: acknowledge what people care about, then manage it away when the money doesn't work.

What's new here is the vote. The full project goes to a November referendum. The community decides. Jaylen will be 17. He cannot vote. He already learned from the March 2 workshop that 80% of voters have no children in the schools. Now he understands he is literally in the group with no ballot. Not just excluded from budget deliberations procedurally — not old enough to weigh in at all when it matters.

The clinical social worker at public comment named the police-proximity problem for immigrant families — the exact communities he's been watching navigate ICE fears at school since January. No councilor addressed it. He noticed. He always notices.

### Emotional Register

Jaylen feels the particular exhaustion of someone who keeps showing up and keeps being counted but never listened to — the Mahoney bullet point is the December forum, the February 10 joint session, the superintendent search workshop all over again, and discovering he can't even vote in November adds a hard edge of finality to a frustration that used to feel fixable.
