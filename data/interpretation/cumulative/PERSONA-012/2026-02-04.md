# Cumulative Record: Jaylen (PERSONA-012)
## Meeting: School Board Public Budget Forum -- February 4, 2026

### Interpretation

The budget forum was supposed to be the meeting where the community gets answers, and Jaylen got none. The district presented its three "big ideas" for closing the $7.2M gap -- elementary reconfiguration, schedule changes for grades 5-12, and operational efficiencies -- but even all three together only cover about $4 million, leaving $3 million more to cut from somewhere unspecified. Nobody mentioned theater, cross-country, AP course offerings, or anything that maps to Jaylen's actual school experience. The entire discussion was about elementary enrollment numbers and administrative categories.

The student representative situation crystallizes everything Jaylen has been feeling since December. Alex Davison and Angela were introduced at the forum, sat through the entire presentation, and were given no speaking role. They were decorative. The district put students in the room and then talked around them, designing the forum for adults to discuss impacts on children without asking children what matters to them. When Jaylen testified at a board meeting, he felt unheard; now he sees that even the students given formal roles are props in a process that claims to center them.

The class size data adds concrete detail to earlier fears. Elementary classes are moving from averages of 16 to 18-19 for younger grades and 22-23 for older elementary. If that is the acceptable range for younger kids, high school classes -- already at 28 in Jaylen's AP Lit and overcrowded in chemistry -- will get worse. The middle school schedule consolidation was dropped because it needs traffic studies and permits, revealing that some cost-saving measures require planning horizons that don't exist. The district is scrambling.

The health insurance increase jumped from 8% to 12%, another cost escalation that eats into whatever savings the "big ideas" generate. Jaylen is learning in real time that budget crises are not single events but compounding pressures: every new cost estimate moves against the district, and the remaining gap grows. The forum directed community members to submit questions through a Google form rather than providing direct answers -- another layer of mediation between students and information about their own education.
