# Cumulative Summary: Maria (PERSONA-001)

### Current Understanding

Maria understands the South Portland school budget crisis as a compound failure built across years and two governing bodies. The structural gap — initially presented as $7.2M, now cited as $8.4M — stems from a 23% elementary enrollment decline, state funding covering only 20% of costs against a 55% statutory obligation, staffing levels that grew while enrollment fell, and multi-year internal financial mismanagement (six finance directors in five years, repeat audit findings, illegal budget overages). The city council handed the school board guidance of 3–6% at the February 10 joint workshop, below the board's own requested floor, forcing deeper cuts than any scenario the board had modeled.

Kayler Elementary is now closed by board vote. Option A — PreK–grade 1 and grades 2–4 split across four remaining buildings — is the voted reconfiguration model. Seventy-two pink slips were issued March 18. Thirteen elementary teacher positions are confirmed cut. The behavioral strategist — the district's only general-education behavioral specialist, serving approximately 60 students across four schools — is slated for elimination, as are 16 ed techs, four elementary MTSS specialists, and seven lunch aides. Class sizes will increase in both options. Attendance boundaries remain undrawn. The FY27 budget has not yet passed.

Maria sees the participation infrastructure — Zoom blocked, expensive in-person attendance, forums canceled by ICE fear, immigrant children absent from the classrooms whose fate is being decided — as a structural feature, not a fixable design problem. The city is simultaneously advancing an $87M capital bond, raising sewer rates 22% annually for three years, and asking families to absorb a school tax increase, with no governing body integrating those combined household impacts. A $300,000 state appropriation secured by union lobbying remains unallocated. April 7 is the council deadline. Attendance boundaries are still unknown.
