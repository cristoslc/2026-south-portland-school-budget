# Cumulative Record: Linda (PERSONA-007)
## Meeting: School Board Workshop -- December 17, 2025 -- 2025-12-17

### Interpretation

This is the workshop I've been dreading since we started seeing the enrollment projections two years ago, and the numbers are even worse than what I'd modeled privately. The $7.2M structural gap is now the official figure, and it validates every warning I've been raising in finance committee. We hired 82 people while losing 300 students -- that staffing-enrollment mismatch is the single biggest driver of our crisis, and it happened incrementally over years while nobody connected the dots.

The 6% tax increase ceiling we set in December was the right call politically -- anything higher guarantees a failed validation vote -- but it forces us into cutting 78 positions, which is 12% of our total staff. I've been studying comparable districts, and our per-pupil cost of $26,651 is the highest among them. That's both our biggest vulnerability at the ballot box and our clearest signal that efficiency gains exist somewhere in the system if we're willing to find them.

What makes this even harder is the state funding situation. We're getting 20% of actual costs covered when the formula says it should be 55%. I've testified at the statehouse about EPS formula changes, but we can't wait for Augusta to solve this. The elementary enrollment decline of 23% in four years -- from 1,401 to 1,080 students -- is creating excess capacity and maintenance costs that we're paying for with positions we can no longer justify.

The path forward is going to require the hardest budget validation campaign I've ever been through. We need to cut deep while asking taxpayers to pay more anyway, and we need to do it in a way that's defensible to the community. The 78 position cuts will include 42 teachers, and every one of those will generate phone calls and emails. But the math is unavoidable -- you can't maintain the same staffing levels when a quarter of your elementary students have disappeared.
