# Cumulative Summary: Rachel (PERSONA-008)

### Current Understanding

As of March 23, Rachel understands the South Portland school budget crisis as a systemic, multi-front collapse with a compressed decision timeline she is running out of weeks to influence.

Option A would displace at least 580 students — 160 Kayler families plus every incoming second grader at Dyer, Small, and Kayler. Option B remains on the table; the district's own materials describe it as presenting "less change for families." Transportation has not been modeled. The board adjourned at 11:15 PM without voting. March 30th is the next decision point, with August — not September — as the implementation target.

Rachel understands this crisis as cutting across both governing bodies. Board Chair DeAngelos declared reconfiguration "fabulous" and stated there will be "no public referendum." In February the mayor fluently described the reconfiguration plan at a Mahoney facilities workshop as though it were settled policy. On March 19th, Councilor Matthews used "closing schools" as casual vocabulary at a non-school agenda item while 72 pink slips from the day before were called "just the first wave." The FY27 decision timeline is now locked: April 7 public hearing, April 14 council workshop, May 5 vote, June 9 referendum — eleven weeks. The $87 million public safety bond is confirmed for November. Hamlin is a formal workshop item with consultants already retained. Rachel's own children may live on a zone boundary — she still does not know which building they will walk into in August.
