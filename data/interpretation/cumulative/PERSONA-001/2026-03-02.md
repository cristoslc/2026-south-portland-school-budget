# Cumulative Record: Maria (PERSONA-001)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop I -- March 2, 2026

### Interpretation

The longest and most substantive meeting of the budget season so far -- three and a half hours. The administration presented nine specific reconfiguration options (labeled 1.1 through 1.8, plus alternatives) with building-by-building analysis. Every elementary principal presented data about their school's enrollment, demographics, and capacity. The educational case for grade-band campuses was made explicitly: better teacher collaboration, curriculum consistency, and more efficient deployment of specialist staff.

The pivotal moment came when Chair DeAngelis argued forcefully against closing Skillin school, citing its role serving the city's most vulnerable immigrant families. At least four board members publicly stated they oppose closing Skillin. This "Skillin pivot" fundamentally constrains which reconfiguration options remain politically viable: if Skillin is protected, the remaining options concentrate impact on other schools. Maria is now calculating which options specifically affect her child's school.

Public comment was extensive and emotional. Parents raised concerns that resonate deeply with Maria's own: disruption to young children being moved between buildings, transportation burden for families with children in different grade bands requiring multiple drop-offs, the rushed September 2026 implementation timeline, and the lack of any direct parent survey before proposals were developed. The board acknowledged it had not surveyed parents but did not commit to doing so.

A non-reconfiguration path was also modeled at ~$2.2M in savings through across-the-board class size increases and staff reductions. The superintendent explicitly characterized this approach as "kicking the problem down the road." Maria finds herself in a genuine dilemma: reconfiguration is disruptive but may be structurally sound; the alternative preserves neighborhood schools but at the cost of larger class sizes everywhere and unresolved structural problems.
