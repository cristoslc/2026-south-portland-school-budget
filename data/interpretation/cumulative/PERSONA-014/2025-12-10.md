# Cumulative Record: Lila (PERSONA-014)
## Meeting: School Board Workshop -- December 10, 2025

### Interpretation

Just two days after learning about the terrifying spending freeze and the $1.9 million budget shortfall, Lila is now watching the grown-ups spend $30,000 on a company to help them find a new superintendent -- money they openly admitted they don't have. This makes zero sense to her. If teachers can't buy books because there's no money, how can the board spend that much on something that doesn't even help kids directly? It's like when her mom says they can't afford ice cream but then buys something Lila doesn't understand.

The meeting was mostly confusing -- three different companies came to present and the adults argued about which one to pick. But what really scared Lila was when one of the presenters said that if you Google her school district, you'd see "bad articles." She doesn't know what happened, but hearing that the outside world thinks something is wrong with her district made her feel protective and anxious. Is that why the old superintendent left? Is that why everything is falling apart?

The one bright spot was Board member Richardson asking all three companies about finding someone who would understand South Portland's diverse community. Lila's school has friends from lots of different backgrounds and it mattered to her that at least one adult was thinking about kids like her friends. But that small comfort was overwhelmed by the empty room -- Board member DeAngelo pointed out that nobody from the community even showed up. If parents aren't coming to these meetings, who is fighting for kids like Lila?

The meeting ended with two board members saying they needed more time to decide, which only deepened Lila's sense that the adults don't really know what they're doing. They can't agree on who should help them pick a superintendent, they're spending money they don't have, and meanwhile nobody has told her what's happening to Dyer.
