# Cumulative Record: Jess (PERSONA-003)
## Meeting: School Board Workshop -- December 17, 2025

### Interpretation

The abstract financial fears from the last two meetings just became terrifyingly concrete. Forty-two teachers cut. Seventy-eight positions total -- 12% of all staff, gone. And elementary schools are getting hit the hardest because that's where enrollment collapsed, down 23% in four years. I keep doing the math in my head: if they're cutting this many teachers now, what does the classroom look like in 2028 or 2029 when my daughter is starting kindergarten? Are we talking 25, 30 kids per class with no specialists left?

What's making this worse is the financial picture underneath the cuts. Even after slashing $7.2 million, property taxes are still going up 6%. Without the cuts it would have been 18-19%. The fund balance -- their savings account -- is completely gone. There is literally no cushion left for anything unexpected. Two weeks ago I was worried about a spending freeze on supplies; now I'm watching them gut the workforce and it still isn't enough.

The per-student spending number -- $26,651, highest in the region -- is the one thing that messes with my narrative about a failing district. If they're spending more per kid than anywhere else and still in crisis, either the money is being poorly managed or there are structural costs I don't understand. Either way, it doesn't reassure me. I was already on the fence about whether South Portland is in temporary difficulty or permanent decline, and this meeting pushed me hard toward the latter. The pattern I identified at the December 10 workshop -- reactive crisis management instead of strategic planning -- is now playing out at scale. They're not reimagining the district for the future; they're just trying to survive this year. And that leaves parents like me, planning two or three years out, with absolutely nothing to hold onto. I'm seriously starting to research Scarborough and Cape Elizabeth, even though the thought of moving again makes me sick.
