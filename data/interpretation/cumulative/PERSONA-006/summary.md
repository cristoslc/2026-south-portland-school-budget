### Resolved Threads

- **How large is the nutrition fund deficit and what caused it?**: Revealed at $715K in FY25 and $1.1M projected in FY26, previously hidden from main budget tracking due to lack of continuity across seven business managers in six years; FY26 losses were large enough to make a current-year surplus impossible across all scenarios (resolved 2026-02-09)
- **Was the city council informed of the school district's emerging deficit before December 8, and what does the communication failure mean?**: Confirmed — the December 4 and December 9 council meetings showed no awareness, establishing a multi-day communication gap between school board knowledge and council awareness of the crisis (resolved 2025-12-09)
- **Does the city have other TIF agreements that erode the commercial tax base beyond the Ocean Street deal?**: Partially answered — the Texas Instruments TIF ($1.2M/year) was identified; unlike the Ocean Street deal, its extension nets the city $400K more per year than expiration, establishing that not all TIF agreements follow the developer-favoring Ocean Street pattern (partially resolved 2026-02-03)
- **What will the Mahoney City Center cost and how does it compete with the school budget crisis?**: Initially confirmed at $194M at the January 13 workshop with the architect stating he was "not confident" in the estimate; subsequently reduced to a $70-76M stripped-down scope (no library, no police station) with a 2027 referendum target after the council deferred in March (resolved 2026-01-13; scope subsequently revised through 2026-03-19)
- **What will the Pearl Street pump station specifically do to South Portland sewer rates and on what timeline?**: Fully quantified — 22% annual increases for three consecutive years; average monthly bill rising from approximately $56 to approximately $90; structured as revenue bonds requiring no voter approval, so rate increases proceed on a fixed schedule regardless of any referendum outcome (resolved 2026-03-19)
- **Will the 6% tax increase ceiling hold, or will the board break its own guidance?**: Confirmed held — the FY27 budget achieves the 6% ceiling; the finance director confirmed the per-household school tax impact of approximately $257/year at the March 23 workshop (resolved 2026-03-23)
- **Where does the remaining $3M+ gap get closed if the three "big ideas" only yield approximately $4M of the needed $7.2M?**: Resolved in aggregate — the full package of 78 position cuts and a school closure reached the 6% ceiling for FY27; specific mechanics of closing the residual gap were not detailed at the March 23 workshop (resolved 2026-03-23)
- **Did the January 22 community forum on school cost-saving measures produce meaningful public input?**: The forum was postponed to February 4 due to immigration enforcement activity displacing Red Bank families; when it occurred, it confirmed the 18.95% roll-forward and the three-option framework but produced no new cost-saving proposals from the public (resolved 2026-01-20 for the postponement; 2026-02-04 for the substance)
- **What is the magnitude of the deferred police and fire capital obligation?**: Confirmed at $87M total ($47M police station + $40M fire station), targeting a November 2026 referendum; Walgreens property at 279 Main Street purchased for $2.525M using asset forfeiture and TIF funds as the future police station site (resolved 2026-03-05)
- **What scope alternatives would the Mahoney design team produce in response to the council's "bare bones" directive?**: The range came back at $57M-$105M; the M3C committee chair confirmed there is "no such thing as a bare bones approach" because ADA, life safety, structural, and energy code upgrades trigger automatically on any change of use in a 100-year-old building (resolved 2026-02-17)
- **Can the board actually deliver $7.2M in cuts given their track record through the spending freeze?**: Partially resolved — the cuts were delivered to meet the 6% cap for FY27; however, the finance director's explicit structural warning means the underlying discipline question reopens immediately for the FY28 budget cycle (partially resolved 2026-03-23)
- **Will the city council hold the line on tax increases where the school board would not?**: Partially resolved — at the February 10 joint workshop the council provided 3-6% guidance, rejecting the school board's 6-10% proposals; the council demonstrated it would push back, though subsequent meetings showed this fiscal discipline to be inconsistently applied depending on question type and political salience (partially resolved 2026-02-10)
