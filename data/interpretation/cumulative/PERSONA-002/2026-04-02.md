# Cumulative Record: David (PERSONA-002)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- April 2, 2026 -- 2026-04-02

### Interpretation

Ketchem did what I have been asking for since December: she showed the multi-year actuals. Electricity overruns of $138K, $211K, and $165K in consecutive years, with tuition reimbursement running over budget in FY23, FY24, FY25, and projected over again in FY26. The thesis is clean and correct — when you have no fund balance, every dollar of wishful budgeting eventually becomes a position cut. That diagnosis belongs on the first slide of every budget presentation for the next five years. The fact that it took a crisis and seven finance directors to produce it is the March 23 story. What April 2 adds is the documentation: the under-budgeting was systematic, predictable, and now formally on the record.

But the forward-looking slide is what I keep returning to. Ketchem named four compounding FY28 pressures — labor auto-escalation above 6% by contract, 13-14% annual utility growth, $300K minimum in new debt service from the turf bond, and the Skillin boiler as an unpriced liability — and the budget currently sitting in front of the board solves none of them. The district has zero fund balance against a city-benchmarked floor of 9% of operating expenses, which works out to roughly $6.75 million they should be holding but don't. The 6% ceiling was a genuine achievement for FY27. It is not a structural fix. Ketchem said as much.

The administrative restructuring savings are the finding that closes a thread I opened in March. Director-to-strategist conversions yield $20-30K net per substitution, not the material budget movement some board members expected. Holman said she had hoped money would be "liberated." It wasn't. The DEI swap at March 30 produced zero confirmed savings. The pattern is confirmed across two data points: headline reclassifications in this budget are not reliable savings until verified at the salary-and-step level.

The mid-meeting state funding news is exactly the kind of thing that should slow a budget vote down — and did. Two board members received different figures by text, neither amount verified, the recurrence question unanswered. The finance director was right to hold the line on confirmation. If it is one-year money used to restore positions, the FY28 problem compounds. The board deferred correctly. The governance risk is that they deferred to an undefined Monday deadline while the April 7 council presentation is fixed on the calendar.

Fund balance policy going to committee is progress of the most minimal kind. It is motion in the right direction. It is not a target, a timeline, or a floor.
