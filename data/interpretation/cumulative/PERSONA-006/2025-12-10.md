# Cumulative Record: Tom (PERSONA-006)
## Meeting: School Board Workshop -- December 10, 2025 -- 2025-12-10

### Interpretation

Just two days after learning about the district's $1.9 million deficit and emergency spending freeze, this board proves they haven't learned a damn thing about fiscal responsibility. They spent four hours debating whether to hire a $30,000 consultant or an $8,000 one to find their next superintendent - money they don't have budgeted anywhere - and chose the expensive option. This is the same board that just implemented an emergency spending freeze and is asking parent groups to buy classroom supplies because they can't afford basic materials.

What really burns me is the hypocrisy. Board member Rich nailed it when he asked if this was a double standard - cutting non-personnel spending for everyone else while blowing premium dollars on themselves. At least two board members had the sense to vote against this waste, but the majority went with the fancy New York firm that promises to hold their hands through the process. Richardson's comment about "thinking 10-20 years down the road" while ignoring the immediate financial crisis shows how disconnected these people are from reality.

This confirms everything I suspected about the board's role in creating this mess. Richardson admitted the previous superintendent "neglected to pay attention to details and our finances," but they're the ones who were supposed to be providing oversight. Now they're compounding their failures by spending money we don't have on premium services when a cheaper option would do the job just fine. The empty meeting room tells you everything - they made this decision with zero public input, then complained nobody showed up. Maybe if they hadn't run the last guy out of town and managed the budget properly, people would trust them to make these decisions without supervision. This is exactly the kind of wasteful spending that ends up on my tax bill.
