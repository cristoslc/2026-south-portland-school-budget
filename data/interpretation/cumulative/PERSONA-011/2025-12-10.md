# Cumulative Record: Meg (PERSONA-011)
## Meeting: School Board Workshop -- December 10, 2025 -- 2025-12-10

### Interpretation

Two days after watching the board implement a spending freeze that stopped teachers from buying classroom supplies, I just watched them vote to spend $35,000 they don't have on a superintendent search. The cognitive dissonance is staggering and I'm dreading having to explain this to my parent networks.

The board had three options ranging from $8,000 to $35,000, and despite having zero budget allocated for this search, they chose the most expensive firm - Zeal Education Group. What makes this even more infuriating is that Zeal's approach actually reduces community input compared to traditional searches. Instead of having parent and community screening committees like NASDAQ proposed, Zeal will have the board make all candidate decisions themselves. So we're paying 4x more to give families less say in who leads their kids' schools.

The vote was 5-2, with only Holman and Rich pushing back on the cost. Holman literally called it "overspending" and compared it to falling for "bling" when facing taxpayer pressure, but they got steamrolled. Richardson kept emphasizing diversity in the candidate pool, which I absolutely support, but then we pick the firm that eliminates the community voices that could help evaluate whether candidates actually understand our diverse student population.

The timing couldn't be worse. My group chats are already buzzing about the supply freeze - parents asking if they should buy copy paper for their kids' teachers, wondering how long this will last. Now I have to tell them the same board that said there's no money for basic classroom materials just approved spending that could have bought supplies for multiple schools. The optics are terrible and the parent reaction is going to be explosive.
