# Cumulative Record: Marcus (PERSONA-004)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- March 10, 2026 -- 2026-03-10

### Interpretation

This is the meeting where I find out that the alarm is already ringing — and it still isn't enough. Matthews is up there arguing against a hundred thousand dollars for families facing eviction, and he drops it almost as a throwaway line: his phone was filled with texts from teachers and ed techs. We've been reaching out. The pattern I've watched since December — the council treating the school crisis as background noise while conducting its business — hasn't changed, but now it's clear that individual councilors are hearing from us directly, and they're using our distress as an argument for fiscal caution everywhere except where it would actually help.

The board chair's "cautious of every dime" statement from the night before gets carried into the council chamber by Matthews as the frame for the entire meeting's fiscal logic. Sixty-two percent of the entire budget. Cannot afford additional expenditures. He's not wrong about the math. But "cautious of every dime" as the governing principle means forty-two teaching positions get cut not because the board chose it but because the arithmetic demands it — and that framing insulates every decision-maker from the human consequences.

What actually lands is Councilor Scott. She makes the argument I've been making to colleagues for months — eighty families stabilized equals eighty students who stay enrolled, which means sections that justify teaching positions. She's connecting housing stability to staffing levels in a way the budget documents never do. That's the right argument. The question I can't answer yet is whether it travels from this rental assistance debate into the April 14 budget workshop, where it actually matters.

The ICE testimony sits inside me differently now. February 17 I understood the attendance crisis in the abstract. Here, a parent writes that her husband was detained on his way to work and she can't pay rent because the savings went to legal fees. Those are my students. Those empty seats in February are now academic gaps in March that somebody — probably an ed tech, if any are left by September — will have to close.

The Metro free-ride confirmation is a rare functional thing. Students get to school. Bell times might align. Small, real, good. I'll take it.

April 14 is the first budget workshop. That's the arena. Everything before it has been prologue.
