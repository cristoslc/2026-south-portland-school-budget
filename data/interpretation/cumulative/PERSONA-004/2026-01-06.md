# Cumulative Record: Marcus (PERSONA-004)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- January 6, 2026 -- 2026-01-06

### Interpretation

January 6 is another meeting where the council's agenda says nothing and the subtext says everything. On the surface: cemetery rules, council procedures, a donated flag. But the city manager slips in a sentence that I now know changed the shape of everything that followed — February 10th, budget guidance workshop, school board and council in the same room, tax increase parameters set. That's the meeting where the 6% ceiling gets locked in. That's where councilors like Matthews plant the seed that taxpayers "cannot afford any of your options." I didn't know it at the time, but I know it now: January 6 is the last quiet day before the machinery starts turning in earnest.

The January 22nd citizen forum catches my attention too, and not in a good way. The mayor frames it as "feedback on potential cost-saving measures." That phrase is doing a lot of work. Cost-saving measures means cuts, and going to the public before presenting numbers to the council means they're building political cover before the fight starts. This is the move you make when you already know what you want to do and need community permission slips. I need to get word to union leadership immediately — we cannot let that forum be dominated by voices saying "do whatever it takes, just don't raise my taxes." Our people need to be in that room with real numbers about what class sizes look like when you eliminate 42 teachers.

The state property tax task force is the kind of thing I'd normally file under "watch carefully." Statewide solutions sound promising until you realize the state is already covering 20% of what its formula requires. Another task force promising relief that arrives after the damage is done isn't a lifeline — it's a footnote. But I've been wrong before about these things, and if a January report changes the math even slightly, it matters.

Looking back from March, January 6 is the meeting that told me exactly how the city was going to engage with this crisis: procedural announcements, not substantive engagement. The real decision about the tax ceiling was already being negotiated behind closed doors, with the February 10 joint workshop serving as its public unveiling. The whole fight was already scripted before most teachers knew a fight was coming.
