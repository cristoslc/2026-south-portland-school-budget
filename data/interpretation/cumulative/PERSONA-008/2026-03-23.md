# Cumulative Record: Rachel (PERSONA-008)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop -- March 23, 2026 -- 2026-03-23

### Interpretation

Five hours. No vote. And somewhere in that room, between the moment Kayler's name landed and the moment the assistant superintendent confirmed "it would not just be Kayler families," everything shifted in ways I'm still working out at midnight.

I came in braced. March 2nd had given me nine scenarios and a list of specific school names on slides like a casualty report. I knew this workshop was supposed to narrow that list. What I didn't expect was the two seconds of relief — Kayler named, not our school — followed immediately by the board Q&A that collapsed it. The assistant superintendent confirmed that zone redistricting under any option would affect families near boundaries at Brown and other adjacent schools. Then a woman at the microphone did the math out loud: 160 Kayler families forced to move, plus 424 more second graders across the district under Option A. Over 580 children total. Every incoming second grader at Dyer, Small, and Kayler, reassigned. And I realized I still don't know if my family is sitting on one of those zone lines.

I have been tracking transportation as an open wound since February 4th — seven weeks ago — when the administration admitted they hadn't modeled the bus routes. Tonight I learned why: they're waiting for the board to pick an option before completing the modeling. A parent at the microphone had to tell the board that school starts in August, not September. Five months. No routes. No before-care plan. No answer to the most basic question: are we one of the boundary families?

The 12-to-16 additional positions needed if no school closes is the number nobody is saying loud enough. The administration linked the closure decision to job preservation directly — keep all five buildings, cut 12 more people. I understand the math. I also know the children most directly in the crossfire still don't know which building they'll be walking into in five months.

Option B is still on the table. The district's own presentation said it "presents less change for families." That phrase is going in every text I send tonight, because the board adjourned at 11:15 without voting, and March 30 is not locked.
