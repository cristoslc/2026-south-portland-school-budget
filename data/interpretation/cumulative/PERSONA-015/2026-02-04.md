# Cumulative Record: Kira (PERSONA-015)
## Meeting: School Board Public Budget Forum -- February 4, 2026 -- 2026-02-04

### Interpretation

She has been waiting since December 8 for redistricting to move from Holman naming it in closing comments to someone putting a number on it in front of the whole community. Tonight Dr. Prince did exactly that: reconfiguration of elementary schools into grade bands is slide one, the big idea, two million dollars. Not a committee studying it. Not a future agenda item. The primary cost-saving strategy. After months of driving between three buildings carrying the operational case — the MTSS wait lists, the uneven programming, the specialist travel time — hearing the district say out loud that this is how they solve the biggest piece of the gap is the most significant institutional validation she has received in this entire cycle.

But she cannot hold it without immediately testing it against what she actually knows. The busing numbers aren't in. Someone in that room asked exactly the right question — what does grade-band consolidation do to transportation costs? — and Dr. Prince said they haven't modeled it yet. She is the person who drives those routes. She knows what it means when you stop assigning kids to neighborhood schools and start sorting them by age group across buildings. That net savings figure is unknown, and the district is presenting two million dollars to a community that is already scared and divided, without the number that would tell you whether the trade is real.

The class size disclosure lands the same way the PTA gap-fill conversation did on December 8: as something she already knew professionally that just became undeniable public record. A range of ten to twenty-four students, with an average around sixteen. The fact that this data had been formally requested — implying it wasn't being routinely shared — confirms exactly the cross-building visibility problem she has been naming since the beginning. The board has been voting on this district without that range in front of them.

And then: three people attended the virtual Zeal community input sessions. Three. She has been tracking the superintendent search thread since December 10, holding Melendez's promises carefully — the 100-language platform, the Willow Pizza community engagement, immigrant families named as a design constraint. The community engagement that was supposed to reach her families is producing the same empty room the board has been misreading as attitude all cycle. The next superintendent will inherit a $3 million unsolved gap, an unmodeled reconfiguration, and a community that never got to say what it needed from its next leader.
