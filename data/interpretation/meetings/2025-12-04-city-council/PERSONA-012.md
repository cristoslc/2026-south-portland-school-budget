### Reactions

Ok so that meeting was like 2.5 hours and honestly most of it was just boring procedural stuff, but there were a few things that actually matter. The big one was this new budget process for committees - basically they're trying to make it so groups like the arts committee can actually ask for money instead of just hoping someone remembers they exist. Which sounds good in theory but then they capped it at $2,500 unless you do some special presentation thing during budget season.

The thing that really got me was when they were talking about Zoom public comment. Like, I get that there were problems before with people being awful online, but come on. Half my friends can't make it to a 6:30 meeting on a school night, and in winter it's even worse. One councilor was like "oh people can just email us" but that's not the same as actually speaking up when they're making decisions about stuff that affects us. It's so frustrating that a few jerks ruined it for everyone who just wants to participate.

The one bright spot was the new councilor - Scott I think? She actually talked about wanting to make government accessible and understanding that not everyone can just show up to speak at a podium. Finally, someone who gets that democracy shouldn't just be for people who have nothing else to do on Thursday nights. But honestly, I'm not holding my breath that anything will actually change. Adults love to talk about including young people until it means actually changing how they do things.
