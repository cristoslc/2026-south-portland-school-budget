### Reactions

So they spent most of the meeting on this housing workshop about the state mandates. Bottom line: the state is basically taking over local zoning for small housing projects. We can't require more than 5,000 square foot lots anymore, and we have to allow up to four units on those lots in most of the city. Planning board gets cut out of the approval process entirely for 2-4 unit projects.

The good news is Milan's team actually ran the numbers on what this means practically. They're saying if 10% of our single-family properties added one extra unit, we'd meet all the regional housing demand projections. And they think our infrastructure can handle it through normal capital planning. That seems reasonable, though I'd want to see the actual capacity analysis for sewer and schools before I'm fully convinced.

What bugged me was all this talk about "design controls" to make duplexes look like single-family homes. Either we want more housing or we don't. The state is forcing our hand anyway, so let's just implement it efficiently instead of adding bureaucratic layers to preserve some aesthetic ideal. Council seemed to agree - they basically told staff to streamline everything and not worry too much about making duplexes invisible.
