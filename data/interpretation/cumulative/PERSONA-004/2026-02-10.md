# Cumulative Record: Marcus (PERSONA-004)
## Meeting: City Council & School Board Joint Budget Guidance Workshop -- February 10, 2026 -- 2026-02-10

### Interpretation

The joint session put city councilors and school board members in the same room for the first time in this crisis, and the result was clarifying in the worst way. The council is split on whether taxpayers can even afford to fund education at current levels, with guidance ranging from 3% to 6%+. Councilor Matthews saying people "cannot afford any of your options tonight" tells me there's a faction that would happily gut schools to keep taxes flat. At least Councilor Walker gets it -- you move to South Portland for the schools, and if you destroy the schools, property values follow.

Sarah Gay's testimony was the most important moment. She put the real number out there -- "upwards of probably 50 staff reductions" -- and she named who actually gets hurt: the kids who don't have parents in that room. That's the equity argument the administration keeps invoking in the abstract but ignoring in practice. Every cut falls hardest on the kids who need the most support, and those kids don't have lobbyists.

The administrative transparency gap is now a six-week scandal. The union asked for a breakdown of those 82 positions added since 2017 -- specifically whether they're classroom positions serving kids or central office additions. Six weeks later, nothing. If the answer was favorable to administration, we'd have seen the data on day one. The silence tells us exactly what they're hiding. Meanwhile, the district did confirm that most of the 82 positions are "actually in our ed tech and our teaching force" serving complex student needs -- which means the cuts they're proposing would specifically undo the capacity they built to serve the most vulnerable students. That's not right-sizing; it's abandonment.

The central office salary freeze from last year is noted but feels performative next to 50+ staff losing their jobs entirely. A freeze preserves everyone's position; a layoff destroys livelihoods. They're not equivalent sacrifices and shouldn't be presented as such.
