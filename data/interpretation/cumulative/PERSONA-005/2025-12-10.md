# Cumulative Record: Priya (PERSONA-005)
## Meeting: School Board Workshop -- December 10, 2025 -- 2025-12-10

### Interpretation

This superintendent search process crystallizes everything I've been watching unfold in South Portland - genuine equity intentions colliding with systemic barriers and budget constraints. Richardson's direct questioning about diversity shows real leadership growth from the board's earlier acknowledgment of their demographic limitations. She's not just talking about representation anymore; she's actively centering it in the district's most critical hiring decision, calling South Portland "a microcosm of the world" that needs leadership capable of serving that reality.

But the firms' responses expose how shallow most equity commitments really are. NSEC admitting they don't even track diverse placements? That's not oversight - that's willful ignorance. You can't improve what you refuse to measure. Only Zeal demonstrated actual equity infrastructure: diverse team members, concrete outreach strategies to minority leadership organizations, multilingual recruitment platforms. They understood that finding diverse candidates requires intentional, resourced work, not just good intentions.

The final vote devastates me because it proves my worst fears about this district's priorities. After praising Zeal's diversity focus and comprehensive community engagement, two board members still voted against them primarily over cost. This is exactly the pattern I've seen repeatedly - equity gets celebrated in principle but abandoned when it requires real investment. Chair DeAngelo's point about their financial crisis stemming from poor leadership makes the penny-wise, pound-foolish logic even more maddening.

The empty meeting room tells its own story about community engagement. Despite outreach efforts, no community members showed up to hear about the search for their next superintendent. This disconnect between the board's equity rhetoric and actual community participation suggests deeper problems with how the district builds authentic relationships with the families it claims to serve.
