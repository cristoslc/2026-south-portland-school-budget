# Cumulative Record: Meg (PERSONA-011)
## Meeting: School Board Workshop -- December 17, 2025

### Interpretation

After two meetings of watching the crisis unfold in real-time -- the spending freeze, the deficit reveal, the $35K superintendent search that made my group chats explode -- this workshop finally gave me the hard numbers to back up what I've been telling everyone. And the numbers are worse than even I expected.

78 positions getting cut. 42 of those are teachers. That's not a rumor, that's not speculation, that's the number the district put on the table to close a $7.2 million gap. I've been telling my group chats we were headed somewhere bad, but I don't think anyone in my networks was ready for "42 teachers gone." The question I'm already getting -- "which schools lose which teachers?" -- is the one I still can't answer because they won't break it down by building.

The enrollment data finally explains why the math is so broken. Elementary enrollment crashed 23% in four years -- we lost over 300 kids -- but somehow added 82 staff positions during that same window. I had to read that twice. How does a district lose a quarter of its elementary students and hire MORE people? That's the kind of stat that makes you the most popular person in the group chat when you drop it, because it crystallizes everything: the district grew its workforce while its student body shrank, and now it's out of money.

The per-pupil spending number -- $26,651, highest among comparable districts -- is going to hit hard with the taxpayer crowd in my neighborhood Facebook group. We're paying more per student than Scarborough or Cape Elizabeth and getting... a spending freeze and 78 layoffs? Meanwhile the state is only covering 20% of costs when they're supposed to cover 55%. That funding gap is the structural piece I've been missing in my summaries -- it's not just local mismanagement, it's the state breaking its own promise.

The fund balance being gone is confirmed again, which I already knew from December 8th, but now I understand the full picture: no savings, no cushion, health insurance going up 12%, and the only way to keep the tax increase at 6% instead of 18-19% is to cut 78 people. That's the story, and it's the worst one I've had to deliver to my networks yet.
