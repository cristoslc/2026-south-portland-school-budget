# Cumulative Record: Jaylen (PERSONA-012)
## Meeting: School Board Workshop -- December 10, 2025

### Interpretation

This meeting deepens Jaylen's growing sense that decisions about his education are made by adults in rooms where students are invisible. Two days after learning about the spending freeze and the 78-position cut proposal, he watches the board spend three hours debating which consulting firm to hire for the superintendent search -- and then vote to spend $30,000+ on the most expensive option while the interim superintendent says there is "zero" budgeted for it. The same district that froze spending on laminating paper found money for a premium consultant.

What stings most is not the money itself but the total absence of students from the conversation. All three search firms described community engagement plans involving teachers, parents, and board members. Not one mentioned students. The board chose Zeal specifically because it keeps candidate selection with the board rather than opening it to community screening committees, which means even fewer people get input on the person who will lead Jaylen's schools for years. Board member Richardson noted the room was empty, but Jaylen knows students were never invited or considered.

Combined with the December 8 revelations about the spending freeze and the overloaded ELO coordinator, a pattern is forming: the district celebrates student achievement (chess team, cross-country, ELO internships) while systematically excluding students from every structural decision that shapes those achievements. Jaylen attended one board meeting and felt unheard; now he sees that even the process for choosing who runs the district was designed without a student seat.
