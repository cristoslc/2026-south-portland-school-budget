# Cumulative Record: Tom (PERSONA-006)
## Meeting: Budget Forum -- February 4, 2026

### Interpretation

I actually went to this one -- the public budget forum. Figured if they're going to stick me with a tax increase I ought to hear the pitch in person. And it was worse than I expected. They confirmed the 18.95% number -- that's what it would cost just to keep everything the same. Nearly $570 more a year on my house. The board's trying to hold the line at 6%, which means cutting $7.2 million, but even their three "big ideas" only get them to about $4 million in savings. There's still a $3 million hole they can't explain how to fill.

The enrollment-staffing mismatch is now official district talking points. The superintendent himself called it "the math problem" -- enrollment at a ten-year low while staffing kept climbing. At least they're not hiding it anymore, but it took a fiscal crisis to get them to admit what was obvious to anyone looking at the numbers. They also confirmed the fund balance is completely gone -- they spent it down in prior years to hold taxes artificially low, which is just kicking the can down the road.

Health insurance jumped from 8% to 12% projected increase -- they don't even know the real number until late March. Class sizes would go up under reconfiguration but stay within guidelines, which honestly sounds reasonable to me. When I was in school we had 25 kids in a class and we survived. The superintendent tried to soften things by saying this is happening statewide, not just here, but that's cold comfort when I'm the one writing the check. Statewide problem or not, my tax bill is local.
