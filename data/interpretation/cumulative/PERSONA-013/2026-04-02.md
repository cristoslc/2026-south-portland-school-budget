# Cumulative Record: Amira (PERSONA-013)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- April 2, 2026 -- 2026-04-02

### Interpretation

The thing that landed hardest was Laura Stevens. She's a teacher — inside the building, inside the system — and she stood at the microphone and said it felt disingenuous to send students to guidance when they asked why their teachers weren't coming back, instead of telling them the truth. She said she "kind of felt like maybe that was by design."

I found out about my ELA teacher leaving from a classmate. In the hallway. I've been carrying that since before any of this started, and I always assumed it was because the adults just didn't think to tell students things. Stevens is a teacher inside that building, and she named it as something that felt possibly intentional. I'm not saying she proved it. She said "maybe." But she named the gap in a room where the board was sitting, and I have never once heard anyone do that before.

The other thing I keep coming back to: Eva Morin. She's a student — no board seat, no special invitation, no teacher-assigned moment. She walked to the microphone and said computer science showed her a career she didn't know she could want, and that related arts are what make school fun. Lucy Hutzel came to speak for her father, Mr. Wetzel, whose computer science class Eva was defending. I didn't know you could just do that. When library hours got cut I wrote a letter to the principal and got nothing back. These students walked into the actual room and said what they actually thought. And Angela Kabisa — the student board member who couldn't attend — sent a letter someone read aloud: the music programs and the ed techs and the DEI position are what make students feel like they belong, and she knows because she experienced it herself as a new music student. That's the first near-peer voice I've encountered that named belonging from the inside.

There's also a seventh grader at Memorial who has been there three years and never once been to STEM or art class. I didn't know that was happening at my own school. The cuts proposed would take more of those things away from kids who already have less.

The $300,000 from Augusta is something different from anything I've seen before. Teachers went to the state and came back with a specific number tied to specific students — homeless students, economically disadvantaged students. That's not testimony at a board meeting where the administration prepares a counter-argument slide. That's going somewhere else and coming back with something real. Several board members said it should go to staff, not to director roles or the fund balance. But there was no vote. Again. I still don't know if band survives the next meeting.
