Okay, so I just sat through two and a half hours of this budget meeting and I'm honestly pretty rattled. Chair DeAngelis was practically gushing about it, saying how "fabulous" it is and how kids are more flexible than parents. Easy for her to say when she's not the one who has to explain to a second grader why they can't see their favorite teacher from kindergarten anymore.

But here's what really got to me - Abigail Chen dropped the bomb that we're already running a deficit THIS year, not just next year like we thought. All those projections we've been working with? Yeah, they're all underwater now. And then in the same meeting, they're talking about expanding the community school model to the high school with no guaranteed funding. I mean, I get that it's good programming, but we're literally bleeding money and they want to take on more responsibilities?

The one bright spot was when Feller started talking about early retirement buyouts. If we could get even 20 of the 41 eligible staff to take enhanced packages, we're looking at over a million in savings without anyone getting pink-slipped. That's the kind of thinking we need more of. But I'm worried they're just going to rush into this reconfiguration thing because it looks like the biggest number on a spreadsheet, without really thinking through what it means for our kids and our teaching teams. Sarah from the union tried to warn them about the bargaining requirements and the timeline, but I'm not sure they were really listening.
