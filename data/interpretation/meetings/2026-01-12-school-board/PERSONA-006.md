### Reactions

Well, there's the school budget circus again. Chair starts off by reminding everyone that schools already eat up 61% of our property taxes - as if we needed the reminder every time we get our tax bills. Then in the same breath she's warning about "another difficult budget year" and needing "creative ways to save money." Creative ways? How about just spending what you have instead of always coming back to us taxpayers for more?

What really gets me is they're crying poor while the finance committee reports they've already blown through 92% of their budget and we're only halfway through the year. That's not budget management, that's spending like there's no tomorrow and expecting us to bail them out. Meanwhile, they're approving $3,500 for some fancy national advertising for their superintendent search and another $1,600 per candidate for background checks. I get background checks, but when you're pleading poverty, maybe you do the basic version like everyone else instead of the Cadillac package that checks their childhood Facebook posts.

The one thing that gave me hope was hearing about all those retirements - seven positions including teachers and counselors - and the superintendent saying they're not automatically backfilling them. Finally, someone's talking about right-sizing the staff instead of just adding more bodies. If they can save a million bucks through early retirement buyouts like one board member suggested, that's real money that doesn't have to come out of my pocket. But I'm not holding my breath - next month they'll probably find some reason why they absolutely must hire replacements.
