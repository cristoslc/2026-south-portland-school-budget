# Cumulative Summary: Priya (PERSONA-005)

### Current Understanding

South Portland's FY27 budget crisis has produced a compounding equity disaster across both the school district and the city. The district faces a $7.2–8.4 million structural gap driven by state underfunding (20% actual coverage against a 55% statutory obligation) and years of internal financial mismanagement, including a repeat material weakness finding and serial mismanagement of equity-designated federal grants for ELL students and students with IEPs. The resulting 78-position elimination — 42 teachers, 16 ed techs, all 7 lunch aides, McKinney-Vento liaisons, and community partner staff — disproportionately dismantles the support infrastructure for the district's most vulnerable students.

The board voted 4-2 to adopt Option A reconfiguration, closing the second most diverse school (Kayler: ~45% BIPOC, 30–35% multilingual learners) without a Title VI civil rights compliance analysis and before any equity support infrastructure is secured. The DEI Director has been demoted to a teacher-contract instructional strategist role — saving only $20,000–$30,000 — eliminating the district's only BIPOC central office leader. ESOL positions are down two teachers. The behavioral specialist facing elimination has confirmed the referral pipeline logic that will drive IEP identification rates higher and cost more next year. Achievement gap data (22% BIPOC/white, 37% ML/non-ML, 32% IEP/non-IEP) appeared once as reconfiguration justification and has not been applied to evaluate any cut.

At the city level, the council has demonstrated four documented failure modes on equity — erasure, inversion, weaponization, and equity-as-termination — while approving an $87 million public safety bond and approving only $100,000 for immigrant rental assistance. Immigrant families face redundant structural exclusion: ICE enforcement fear, a state GA ruling that excludes fear-based income loss from eligibility, and in-person-only participation requirements. The community engagement model has collapsed: multilingual families are absent from every forum determining their children's schools, with proxy presence their primary form of participation.
