# Cumulative Summary: Amira (PERSONA-013)

### Current Understanding

As of March 23, 2026, Amira understands the South Portland school budget crisis as an $8.4 million structural gap requiring elimination of 78 positions — 42 teachers, 16 educational technicians, and facilities, food, and transportation staff — with 72 layoff notices already delivered on March 18. The crisis has three documented causes: chronic state underfunding (paying ~20% vs. the mandated 55%), staffing growth during a 300-student enrollment decline since 2017 (82 positions added while enrollment fell), and internal financial mismanagement (six finance directors in five years, 34 audit errors, $400K in misallocated grants) — all against a completely depleted fund balance with no fallback.

Elementary reconfiguration is actively advancing. The district's top-ranked option (Option 1.7) closes Skillin and reorganizes remaining buildings into grade-band schools, saving $19.5M over five years primarily by avoiding a $3M HVAC replacement — not for educational merit, which scores equally across all nine options. At least four board members oppose it, but the June 9 budget adoption deadline and September implementation target compress every decision.

Amira's own school day is now personally inside the threat: her combination of band and gifted pull-out enrollment may eliminate PE entirely under proposed schedule restructuring. The budget process is cyclical, not linear — the percussion ed tech position, restored by community advocacy 378 days ago, has been reintroduced as a cut.

Property taxes are a four-party competition: school needs, the $194M Mahoney capital project, the $87M police/fire bond, and corporate tax abatements (Texas Instruments TIF), all drawing on homeowners already at the limit. The ICE crisis and school enrollment loss have been formally named as competing for the same limited municipal fund — by Councilor Matthews in opposition and Councilor Scott in advocacy — with no authority treating them as an integrated problem requiring a single response.
