# Cumulative Record: Linda (PERSONA-007)
## Meeting: School Board Workshop -- December 10, 2025 -- 2025-12-10

### Interpretation

Just two days after discovering our $1.9 million deficit crisis, this board makes a decision that epitomizes everything wrong with our governance: spending $30,000 we don't have on the most expensive superintendent search option available. The irony is staggering—we're cutting 78 positions, asking taxpayers to absorb an 18-19% tax increase, and then we turn around and hire premium consultants because we can't figure out how to work together as a board.

The four-hour vendor selection process was thorough, I'll give us that. Three solid presentations with dramatically different approaches and price points. MSMA at $6,000-$8,000 made perfect sense—we're already members, Eric Waddell knows Maine districts inside and out, and the mentoring services are included. NSEC was competent and reasonably priced. But the majority got seduced by Zeal's promise that paying triple the cost would somehow manufacture the board unity we should be developing through better governance practices, not expensive consultants.

I understand the appeal of their consensus-building approach and the importance of having diversity on the consulting team, but this decision reveals how disconnected we've become from fiscal reality. The 4-3 vote split shows we're still the divided board we were trying to fix, just now we're a divided board that's spending money we don't have. At least Richardson and DeAngelo insisted on reference checks as a contingency—some due diligence is better than none.

What troubles me most is the complete absence of community engagement. We publicized this meeting, and the room was empty. How do we justify premium spending decisions when the taxpayers who'll fund them aren't even showing up to watch us make them? This choice will be impossible to defend during budget validation, especially when people start asking why we couldn't make do with the more affordable options that other districts use successfully.
