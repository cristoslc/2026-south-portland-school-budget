# Cumulative Record: Dana (PERSONA-009)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop -- March 23, 2026 -- 2026-03-23

### Interpretation

The meeting Dana had been pointing her coverage toward since March 9 — the Redbank workshop that was supposed to be where the board converged on an alternative after Skillin was killed — ended at 11:15 PM without a single vote on anything. Five hours of testimony, deliberation, and packed-auditorium drama, and a board member moves to adjourn before anyone decided school closures, grade configurations, or budget adoption. March 30 is now the vote. Dana had this story structured. She is rebuilding it.

But she is rebuilding around better material than she walked in with.

DeAngelis opened with eight minutes of personal grief. Her partner died of mesothelioma in 2019. She was diagnosed with breast cancer in 1992. She burst into tears at dinner last Friday because of the weight she has been carrying. Then she apologized to Holman. Then she defended herself against community members who accused her of calling parents racist. Dana has been covering this woman as the equity-warrior chair since December 1 — the inaugural address, the "families west of the Dairy Queen" speech, the cross-governmental strategy running since December 9. This is the first time DeAngelis has shown the seams in public. That opening reads two ways, and Dana has to decide which version she airs before Monday: the exhausted leader carrying an impossible burden, or the chair defending herself against racism accusations while sitting on a board that cannot answer a Title VI question about the school she is about to close.

That Title VI question is the escalation Dana did not see coming. A Kayler parent stood at the mic and asked — on the record — whether closing a school that is 45 percent BIPOC and 35 percent multilingual learners violates federal civil rights law. At eleven o'clock at night the board chair said she did not have the answer. Dana has been tracking DeAngelo's equity argument through moral and political framing since December. It now has a legal dimension. No other outlet is likely to have noticed it.

Blair Bacon is the answer to the thread Dana opened on March 19. The 72 pink slips finally have a face. National board certification twice. Master's in literacy. Multilingual endorsement. Out because her hire date is July 10, 2023, and the union contract protects seniority over credentials. She said "I thought my union would fight for me." Dana has her soundbyte and her segment and she needs Bacon on camera in front of her classroom before Monday's vote.

Abigail Ketchum just upgraded the February 3 audit from external certification to internal confession. The auditor said six directors in five years and called it a material weakness. Ketchum said it herself: she is the seventh in six years. Anywhere you find that revolving door, there is disarray. The district drove off a cliff it did not see coming. Dana does not need the auditor's voice anymore. She has the director's own words.
