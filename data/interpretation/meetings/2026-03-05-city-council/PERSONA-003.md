### Reactions

Honestly? I'm kind of freaking out after watching this. I went in thinking "oh good, they're finally doing something about these old buildings" but it's becoming really clear that nobody is thinking about families like mine who are going to be using these services in a few years.

The library thing really got to me. Kevin, the library director, was talking about how they have 15,000 people in their programs every year and 27 visitors every single hour they're open, including tons of young families for story time. But then the council just... removes it from their renovation plan? With no backup plan? He said they've been waiting since 2008 for improvements and it sounds like they could be waiting another decade. By the time my daughter is old enough for library programs, will there even be decent spaces for kids?

And then there's this whole mess with the fire station where they're debating spending $87 million on new buildings versus just patching things up. I get that public safety is important, but when someone mentioned the school budget needing to be cut by $8 million at the same time, it's like... how is any of this sustainable? Are we going to have great new fire and police buildings but terrible schools and a crumbling library? What kind of trade-offs are we making here? Nobody seemed to be asking "what will South Portland look like for families with young kids in 10 years" - it was all about managing the current crisis.
