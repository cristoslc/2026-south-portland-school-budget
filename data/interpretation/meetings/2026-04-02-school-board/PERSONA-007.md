### Reactions

We did the right thing not voting tonight. When Connie walked up to the mic and said the unions had secured an additional $300K from Augusta -- and then Claire got that text from Kessler about another $750K from EPS changes while we were literally sitting in the action items -- there was no way to lock in a budget responsibly without knowing if those numbers are real and whether they recur. That's exactly the wishful thinking Abigail spent twenty minutes telling us we can't afford. We had to hold. Passing the council motion unanimously was the easy part. Now we have until Monday to figure out what the numbers actually are.

Abigail's presentation was the clearest accounting I've heard in this entire process. The electricity charts, the tuition reimbursement pattern -- year after year we budgeted what we hoped to spend, not what we actually spent, and the gap ate into our fund balance until there's nothing left. That thesis -- realistic costing protects jobs in future years -- that's the principle we should have been running on all along. But the FY28 slide is what I can't stop thinking about. Labor costs growing faster than 6% by contract every year, the Skillin boiler, the chimney stacks at the high school that need $700K we don't have, debt service going up another $300K for the turf loan. We reset FY27 and we're already staring at the next cliff with zero reserves. Zero. Abigail confirmed it. The city's own policy says 9-12% minimum. That's roughly $7 million we don't have, and nothing in this budget starts building toward it.

The thing I need to sit with is the SPTA vice president's warning about process. Abby Anderson was exactly right. If we put positions back based on who sent the most emails or who showed up at the mic, we own the precedent and the consequences. Feller and Holman both flagged that the director-to-strategist restructuring saved maybe $20-30K -- nowhere near the administrative right-sizing people expected -- and I felt that disappointment too. The cuts did land almost entirely on classroom-facing roles. But before we touch that state money, I need criteria on the table: what is the unmet need, who does it serve, and is the revenue it's funded on recurring or one-year. If we put $750K in position salaries on a one-year appropriation, we are back here in FY29 making the same cuts with the same community in the same room. That conversation has to happen before Monday, not after.
