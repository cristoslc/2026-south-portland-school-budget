### Reactions

Okay, so tonight was interesting but honestly kind of frustrating. The big thing was they approved this new process for committees to request money - which sounds good in theory, right? Like finally there's a clear way to ask for funding instead of just hoping your committee chair has connections. But then it came out that the Human Rights Commission usually asks for around $10,000 and now there's this $2,500 cap on regular requests. I'm thinking, wait a minute - if they're capping equity programs while we're facing this massive school budget crisis, what does that say about priorities?

The Zoom comment thing really got to me. I mean, I get why they had to stop it after people were disrupting meetings with awful stuff, but Councilor Pride basically said their hands are tied because of First Amendment rules. Meanwhile, working parents like me are trying to figure out how to get to 6:30 meetings when we're dealing with dinner, homework, and bedtime routines. At least three councilors seem to really understand that people don't show up because they're scared of their neighbors or employers finding out what they said. That's so true - I see it in the parent Facebook groups all the time. People will complain privately about class sizes or program cuts but won't speak up publicly.

What really struck me was when that guy from Willard Beach talked about how people don't participate even though this is supposed to be participatory government. He's right - I hear parents griping at pickup all the time about things the city or school board is doing, but then crickets at actual meetings. I wish there was a way to bridge that gap because honestly, we need more parent voices in these budget discussions, especially with everything that's coming down the pike.
