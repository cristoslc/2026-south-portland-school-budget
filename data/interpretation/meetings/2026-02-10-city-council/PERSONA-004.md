### Reactions

Did you catch Sarah Gay's testimony? Finally someone had the guts to put real numbers on what we're talking about here - 50+ staff reductions. Not "efficiencies" or "right-sizing" or whatever corporate speak they're using. Fifty of our colleagues losing their jobs. And she nailed it when she said the kids who'll suffer most don't have parents in that room fighting for them.

The whole elementary school thing is just class size increases with fancy packaging. They're talking about consolidating grades to make bigger sections so they can cut teaching positions. Call it "intermediate schools" if you want, but it's still 25+ kids per class instead of 20. And meanwhile we still haven't seen the breakdown of those 82 positions they added since 2017. Six weeks the union has been asking for that data. If it was all teachers and ed techs serving kids, why not just show us? The silence tells you everything.

What really got to me was watching the council debate whether taxpayers can "afford" education. Councilor Walker at least gets it - you move to South Portland for the schools, then gut the schools and wonder why property values tank. But hearing Councilor Matthews say people can't afford any of the options? These are the same people who'll rubber-stamp another police cruiser without blinking.
