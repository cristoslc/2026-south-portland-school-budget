# Cumulative Record: Amira (PERSONA-013)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- January 12, 2026 -- 2026-01-12

### Interpretation

The cuts I was afraid of are already starting to happen, and I'm finding out the same way I always do - not from my school, but from watching adults talk about it somewhere else. Ms. Hassen and Mr. Heffernan, both math teachers at my school, are retiring. And the superintendent said they're NOT automatically replacing teachers who leave. So that means those math positions might just... disappear. That's two fewer math teachers at Memorial, and I was already worried about class sizes after hearing about the 42 teacher cuts.

But the scariest thing wasn't even about the budget. Kids didn't come to school because their families were afraid of immigration enforcement. Nobody told US students what was happening or what to do. Teachers and staff felt unsafe too. This is the kind of thing that makes me realize the adults are just as lost as we are sometimes. How are we supposed to feel safe at school if even the adults don't feel safe?

The finance committee said 92% of the budget is already spent or committed at the halfway point of the year, which means the district is running toward a deficit THIS year, not just next year. And the middle school field is STILL broken - they're talking about maybe aerating it in spring to hopefully have it ready by fall 2026. Four-plus years of a broken field and the best they can do is "hopefully."

There is some good news though. They're having a community budget forum on January 22nd at our school, which means people might actually get to say what they think. And the student board reps, Alex and Angela, will get to help interview superintendent candidates. That's pretty cool - at least some students get a voice. The teachers' union president also specifically asked for meaningful engagement with staff on budget decisions, not just surveys. I wish someone was asking for meaningful engagement with students too.
