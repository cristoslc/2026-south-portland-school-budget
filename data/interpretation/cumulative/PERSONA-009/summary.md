# Cumulative Summary: Dana (PERSONA-009)

### Current Understanding

South Portland's school district enters April 2026 with its FY27 budget still unsigned after two consecutive failed votes. The board voted 6-1 to close Kaler Elementary — a school serving approximately 45% BIPOC and 35% multilingual learners — and 4-2 to adopt a Primary/Intermediate grade-band reconfiguration model (Option A). Seventy-two staff received reduction-in-force notices on March 18. The April 7 city council deadline is now the operative forcing function for a budget the board has not yet passed.

The district's fiscal condition has been externally certified as a material weakness for a second consecutive year. An auditor found 34 journal entries flagged as unusually high and noted that approximately $400,000 in grants was absorbed into general fund expenditures due to ineligibility or overspending. Finance Director Ketchum has stated on the record that she is the seventh director in six years. Elementary enrollment declined 23% over four years while staffing grew by 82 positions; per-pupil spending of $26,651 was highest among comparable districts in data presented to the board. The status-quo FY27 scenario requires an 18.95% school tax increase. The city itself received a clean audit opinion and holds approximately $28.8 million in fund balance — a fiscal asymmetry the two bodies share.

State funding covers approximately 20% of actual costs against a 55% statutory obligation. Union lobbying in March produced an unconfirmed mid-meeting announcement of $300,000 in additional state funding, with a separate signal of $750,000 more through formula changes next year; both figures are pending official confirmation. The public safety bond ($87M, November 2026 referendum) and projected 22% annual sewer rate increases for FY27–FY29 add to the tax pressure on South Portland homeowners. Community engagement — largely absent through February — has shifted to active mobilization, with parents naming board member term expiration years and announcing referendum no-votes as leverage ahead of the June 9 ballot.
