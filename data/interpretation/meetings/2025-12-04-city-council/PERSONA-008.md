### Reactions

Honestly, I'm not sure what to make of tonight. I went expecting to hear SOMETHING about the school budget situation - I mean, it's December and we know there's a $7.2 million gap that could mean cuts or closures. But there was literally nothing. Not one word about schools, teachers, programs, nothing. Either they're handling this behind closed doors, which makes me nervous, or they're putting off the hard conversations until after the holidays.

The whole meeting was pretty routine city stuff - committee appointments, some grants for the police department, rules about how committees ask for money. Nothing earth-shattering there. Though I did notice they're talking about maybe getting rid of the Transit Advisory Committee since we're part of Metro now. I hope that doesn't mean bus routes that kids use are going to get changed without proper input.

What really bugs me is they shot down the idea of letting people comment over Zoom. I get that they've had problems with troublemakers in the past, but there are so many parents who can't make it to these meetings because of work schedules or because they can't find babysitters. It feels like they're making it harder for regular families to participate right when we might need to speak up most about school issues. At least Councilor Matthews reminded everyone that they're all reachable by email and phone, but it's not the same as being able to address the whole council publicly.
