### Reactions

Honestly, I'm pissed. They told us all year that the contract increases were built into the budget, that everything was accounted for. Now suddenly we have a spending freeze because the contract they said was budgeted for is creating a million-plus deficit? That doesn't add up, and somebody needs to explain how we got here.

The non-personnel freeze is going to hit us immediately. No more copy paper when we run out, no replacement markers, no lab supplies. I heard Richardson asking if PTAs could help fill the gaps — that's not how public education should work. And what about field trips? Transportation costs? We're basically putting everything on hold for the rest of the year. 

At least Sarah spoke up about getting teacher input on this. She's right that they need to hear from us about what this actually looks like day-to-day. But I'm worried about the substitute situation too — if we can't get coverage when people are sick, that means bigger classes for the rest of us or losing our planning periods to cover. This whole thing feels like a house of cards, and I'm not sure the new board members really understand what they're walking into.
