FY21 - FY25: Some Factors Leading to Today
Staffing, Enrollment, and Funding misalignment
● Enrollment has broadly declined, interrupted by a brief increase that resulted in additional hires
● Federal COVID relief funds further enabled the expansion of staffing levels
● Nuance with special education needs
Policies
● One example: Absence of a formal Fund Balance Threshold Policy perpetuated short-term
preservation of staffing levels, followed by the gradual depletion of of those savings
Leadership instability
● Leadership instability contributed to financial inconsistency, including Superintendent turnover from
FY25/FY26; and six different Finance Directors serving over the past five years
● Financial leadership instability leads to many difficulties that show up in operations, audit findings,
planning, risk management, analyses, budgeting, and recommendations available for discussion
4

FY26 and FY27
● Enrollment continues to decline; staffing and funding at pivotal juncture without a
financial buffer
● Projected FY26 deficit; we are reducing spending as much as possible
● Interim Superintendent
● FY26 New union contracts finalized
● FY26 New Finance Director -the 7th in 6 years
FY27 requires a significant financial adjustment, both to meet tax increase limits and
also to sustainably move forward financially
5

Preparing for FY28 and Beyond
FY27 resets our financial path but does not solve our core problems.
Moving forward effectively requires a longer view with decision making, new policies,
stabilized leadership, and a commitment to continuous adjustment as data/evidence evolve.
● Financial challenges
○ Labor costs, by contract when all lane/step/adjustments are included and if all
employees remain in place, increase by more than 6% per year
○ Utilities are increasing at high rates
○ FY28 debt service to increase by $300k+
○ Skillin boiler may require new debt
○ Declining enrollment reduces non-local tax revenue
● Opportunities
○ Multi-year financial planning
○ Careful review of roles and position requirements with natural attrition
○ Yearly review of all non-personnel costs -tech, subscriptions, service contracts

Option A Analysis
● Positives
○ More opportunities for grade level collaboration
○ Furthermore, the current grade level leadership structure is supported by two Instructional
Leadership Team members per grade level. If these two ILT members are housed in each of
the buildings that houses a grade level, every teacher at a grade level will have greater
access to instructional support.
○ Resources and support staff can be allocated more efficiently.
○ Developmentally specialized structures: Early childhood (foundational skills) and upper
elementary (increased academic rigor)
○ Allows for all schools to more closely mirror the demographics of the district.
● Concerns/Challenges
○ Time needed to build new school identities and cultures
○ Time and logistics required to make this scale of change for Fall 26
○ Lack of older role models for younger students
○ More family adjustment to new schedules, staff and schools

Option B Analysis
