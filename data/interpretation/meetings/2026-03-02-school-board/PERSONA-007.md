### Reactions

God, what a night. Three and a half hours and I think we learned more about what won't work than what will. The administration did their homework - I have to give Joe and Abigail credit for the sheer volume of analysis they put together. Nine different scenarios, all the financial modeling, the matrix scoring system. But honestly, the minute they said their top recommendation was closing Skillin, I knew we were in trouble.

Look, I get the math. Skillin saves us the most money long-term because of that HVAC system. But Tyler was absolutely right when he called it what it is - we'd be busing our most vulnerable kids across town while keeping all the schools on the east side open. That's not equity, that's just moving poor kids around. And when Rosemarie laid out that whole geographic argument about everything west of Cash Corner, she wasn't wrong. We'd basically be saying that side of town doesn't deserve a neighborhood school.

The public comment was brutal. Three hours of parents who clearly had no idea this was coming, talking about walking their kids to school and community connections. That survey Rachel presented - 245 responses and only 12% felt adequately informed? That's a massive failure on our part. We're going to get killed at the June referendum if we don't figure out how to bring people along better. And don't even get me started on the fact that we still don't have Spanish interpreters at these meetings when a quarter of our families speak languages other than English at home.

I think we're looking at Options 1.3 or 1.6 now - closing either Kaler/Dyer or Small instead. Still saves substantial money, still allows some reconfiguration benefits, but doesn't gut the west side of town. The question is whether we have time to do this right. September is six months away and we don't even have a redistricting plan yet.
