# Maine DOE Enrollment Data — Synthesis

## Key Findings

South Portland's enrollment declined 9.0% overall from 2013-14 to 2024-25 (3,096 to 2,816), but the decline is not uniform across grade levels. The elementary grades (K-4) bore the steepest losses (-17%), while the high school actually grew (+9.2%). This divergence matters for the closure decision: the district is proposing to close an elementary school based on elementary-level decline, but the total district enrollment picture is more nuanced than the headline numbers suggest.

## Points of Agreement with District Claims

- The district's cited total enrollment figures (approximately 3,085 to 2,744) align directionally with NCES data, though specific year references may use different baselines.
- Elementary enrollment decline is real and sustained — K-4 enrollment dropped from 1,244 to 1,030 over the period.

## Points of Divergence or Missing Context

- The district frames this as a simple "declining enrollment" story, but NCES data shows the high school is actually growing. The decline is concentrated in elementary and middle school.
- Pre-K enrollment nearly doubled (28 to 51), reflecting CDS expansion that is not factored into elementary enrollment trend narratives.
- The NCES data does not include multilingual learner breakdowns by grade — a critical gap given the district's demographic shift from 1% to 17% ML students since 2015.

## Gaps Requiring Additional Data

- **Multilingual learner enrollment by grade:** NCES total enrollment doesn't capture the ML pipeline, which is a major demographic driver.
- **School choice transfers in/out:** NCES district totals reflect net enrollment but don't show inter-district movement.
- **Birth data for kindergarten pipeline projection:** Enrollment data alone can't project future kindergarten cohorts without municipal birth records.
- **Building-level enrollment:** NCES provides school-level data separately (available via ELSI at school level) — not yet collected.
