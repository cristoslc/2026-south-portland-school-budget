# Cumulative Record: Marcus (PERSONA-004)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- December 8, 2025 -- 2025-12-08

### Interpretation

This meeting hits me like a gut punch. All year, we've been told that our contract increases were built into the budget — that the district had planned for these costs. Now suddenly there's an immediate spending freeze and deficit projections of up to $1.9 million because of the very contract they said was accounted for. Either they were lying to us then, or they're not telling us the whole truth now. Either way, my trust in their budget planning is shot.

The non-personnel freeze means my classroom reality changes tomorrow. No more supplies when we run out, no field trip transportation, no equipment repairs. I'm already calculating how long my current stock of copy paper will last and wondering if I'll be buying markers out of my own pocket again. When Richardson suggests PTAs might fill the gaps, it confirms my worst fears about how they view public education funding — as something families should subsidize rather than something the district should properly fund.

At least Sarah Gay is pushing for teacher input on the budget strategy. She gets it — she knows we're the ones who see how these cuts actually play out in classrooms. But I'm worried about the substitute situation too. If they can't get coverage when teachers are out sick, that means larger classes for the rest of us or losing our planning periods to cover. The whole system feels unstable, and I'm not confident this board understands the cascading effects of these decisions on actual instruction.
