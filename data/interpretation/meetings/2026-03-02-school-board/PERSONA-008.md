I knew there were budget problems but I had NO IDEA they were seriously considering closing entire schools or making kids switch buildings every two years. The worst part? They ranked closing schools as their TOP recommendations. Thank God some of the board members actually spoke up - four of them said they won't support closing Skillin, which knocked out the administration's favorite options. But even the "better" alternatives still mean massive disruption. And Dr. Prince had the nerve to say that unless the board officially objects, they're just going to stick their closure plan right into the budget anyway. Like, excuse me, did you not just hear four board members say no? The whole thing feels like they've already made up their minds and this was just for show.
