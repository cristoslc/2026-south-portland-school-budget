# Cumulative Record: Linda (PERSONA-007)
## Meeting: City Council Workshop (Goal Setting) -- January 15, 2026 -- 2026-01-15

### Interpretation

Two days after watching the council fracture on the Mahoney bond package, they're back in the room for their annual goal-setting session — and I have almost nothing to show for it. One agenda item, one attachment I can't access, and a meeting that may or may not have touched the fact that the school district is staring at a $7.2 million hole while cutting 78 positions.

In any ordinary year I'd treat a city council goal-setting workshop as background noise. But this isn't an ordinary year. The school budget is 61 cents of every property tax dollar in this city. There is no responsible conversation about fiscal priorities or resident affordability that doesn't eventually land on us. What the council decides its goals are right now — in January, before the public forums, before the April guidance workshop, before the validation campaign — will shape whether we have political cover or a second front to fight. And I can't read that document.

What I keep returning to is the state piece. We're receiving coverage for roughly 20% of actual per-pupil costs when the EPS formula implies 55%. That's not board mismanagement — it's a structural legislative failure that compounds every local decision we make. If the council's goal document included any kind of coordinated advocacy posture toward Augusta, that would be genuinely useful and I want to know about it. If they ignored it entirely and set goals around "affordability" without any context for why the school line is what it is, that's a problem I need to anticipate before someone uses their language against us in a budget forum.

I sat through January 13th learning that Mahoney could potentially share the November ballot with our validation vote, and that the cumulative homeowner impact of city GO debt, school increases, and sewer rates has never been aggregated anywhere. Now I have a goal document that might tell me how the council frames its own fiscal priorities — and it's sitting in an attachment I can't get to. That's my homework before the next meeting. I need to find that document and read it before anyone else starts building a narrative from it.
