### Reactions

The most striking thing about tonight was watching the district lay out a problem they clearly don't have a full solution for yet. That enrollment-staffing chart tells the whole story -- enrollment dropped to its lowest point in a decade while they kept adding staff. Dr. Prince called it a "math problem" but it's really a management problem that's now become a community crisis.

The numbers are just brutal. An 18.95% tax increase if they change nothing, versus a 6% ceiling the board set. That's a $7.2 million gap they have to close. But here's what really got me: even their three "big ideas" -- elementary reconfiguration, middle/high school efficiency reviews, operational changes -- only get them to maybe $4 million in savings. There's still over $3 million hanging out there with no plan. They're asking the community to weigh in on partial solutions to a problem they haven't fully solved.

And the timeline pressure is real. They pulled middle school consolidation off the table because it needs DOT traffic studies -- bureaucratic reality hitting budget fantasy. Meanwhile, health insurance projections jumped from 8% to 12% just since December because other districts are seeing double-digit increases. It feels like they're trying to hit a moving target while blindfolded. The community engagement is genuine, but I'm not sure how much input really matters when the fundamental math is this unforgiving.
