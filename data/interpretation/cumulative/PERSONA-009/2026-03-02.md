# Cumulative Record: Dana (PERSONA-009)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop I -- March 2, 2026

### Interpretation

This is the meeting Dana has been pointing her coverage toward since January -- the one where the school closure question gets a concrete answer. And the answer was pure television: the superintendent walked in endorsing the closure of Skillin Elementary as his top recommendation, and four board members torpedoed it in rapid succession. Dana has covered enough municipal politics to know that a superintendent getting publicly overruled by his own board is rare and dramatic. The speed of the rejection -- Dowling, Feller, Richardson, and DeAngelo all stating opposition within minutes -- suggests this was coordinated, which makes it even more newsworthy.

Chair DeAngelo's eight-minute equity speech is the centerpiece of Dana's segment. The "all the families west of the Dairy Queen" framing gives viewers a geographic anchor they can visualize -- this is not abstract policy but a specific part of town with specific families. DeAngelo calling the Skillin closure "abhorrent" is a pull-quote that can anchor a 30-second tease. The underlying argument -- that closing the school serving the most vulnerable immigrant and low-income families while preserving east-side neighborhood schools is inequitable -- resonates with the immigration storyline Dana has been tracking since the January ICE fears.

The parent turnout is the other major story element. Over 20 speakers during public comment with 85% opposition to reconfiguration in survey data gives Dana the populist-uprising angle. But she also notices the repetitiveness -- after the first few speakers, the concerns become interchangeable (logistics, community bonds, communication failures). She needs the two or three most emotionally compelling testimonials, not all twenty.

The financial matrix showing Skillin closure saving the most ($19.6M over five years versus $15-16M for alternatives) means the board just rejected its most financially advantageous option on equity grounds. That tension -- doing the right thing versus doing the cheap thing -- is the analytical frame Dana will carry forward. The administration admitting it lacks redistricting details when pressed by board members underscores that nobody has a complete plan, even as the March workshops accelerate toward decisions.
