# Cumulative Record: Jaylen (PERSONA-012)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- February 3, 2026 -- 2026-02-03

### Interpretation

February 3 is the night the city's priorities show up in the agenda order. Texas Instruments gets three hours of detailed charts about tax increment financing. The people whose neighbors are being picked up by ICE wait until 9:25 PM for their three minutes. A speaker calls it out directly — "you make us wait for three and a half hours to talk about it" — and the meeting continues as scheduled. Jaylen has watched this since December 1, when Jules Vazquez opened city hall with a poem and the council applauded and moved on. But the TI deal makes the implicit ranking visible in a way that can't be explained away as oversight. Corporate tax structures get the meeting's prime deliberation time. Immigrant community members get what's left over.

The school audit information is the thing he didn't expect. Thirty-four journal entries. Illegal budget overspending. $400,000 moved from grants to cover costs they weren't supposed to touch. He's been hearing "structural budget crisis" since December — state underfunding, declining enrollment, depleted reserves — and now there's evidence that the district was also mismanaging money it had. Six finance directors in five years, three of them temporary. The "amazing" new hire who's fixing it is probably real. The audit record is definitely real. The crisis has a management dimension he didn't have before, and it complicates the story everyone's been telling.

The most significant exchange of the night is two paragraphs from Chair DeAngelis. She calls it a "very difficult budget year," reminds the council they are responsible for overseeing the 61% of property taxes that funds schools, and explicitly asks for "clear guidance" at the February 10 joint meeting. Jaylen already knows what that guidance was: 4-6%, which forced millions more in cuts. He'd been thinking of it as a ceiling imposed from outside — the council making a unilateral call that constrained the board. DeAngelis was asking for it. She invited the council into the decision. The constraint was solicited, not imposed. That doesn't make it less damaging for students, but it changes who owns it.

The community forum is tomorrow night. He walks in knowing, from everything that came after, exactly what it will deliver.
