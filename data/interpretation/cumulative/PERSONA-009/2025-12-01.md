### Interpretation

This is the origin meeting — the one Dana missed, or filed away as ceremonial, before the budget story swallowed her winter. Reviewing it now, three months and ten meetings into the most consequential school budget season she has ever tracked in this market, the gap between what was said that night and what happened next is the most compelling editorial frame she has encountered all season.

Tipton stepped into the mayoralty with a twelve-minute inaugural address promising bold transformation — the Mahoney city center as a "game changer," oil tank land converted to development, public safety facilities modernized, city hall freed up for housing. Dana now knows that within a week of this speech, the school board would surface a $1.9 million mid-year deficit, and within two months, Tipton's council would be publicly feuding with the school board over a 3% tax guidance that the board called "not sufficient, not adequate, not appropriate, and not acceptable." The inauguration reads like a dispatch from a parallel universe where the money works out.

DeAngelos is the bigger discovery. The man Dana has been covering as the combative equity-warrior chair — who torpedoed the superintendent's Skillin recommendation on moral grounds, who gave the "families west of the Dairy Queen" speech, who fought the city council in open session — opened his tenure here with this sweeping, personal twelve-minute address about immigrant neighbors, former students, and the district's obligation to include. The six multilingual speakers who came to welcome him that night directly prefigure the ICE fears story from January and the Skillin equity argument from March. He has been consistent from day one. Dana has been covering the drama without the origin story.

The deliberate silence on the budget crisis is the sharpest detail. Both inaugurating bodies were staring at a $7.2 million gap that would define their entire terms, and neither said a word about it. That is either disciplined political messaging — let the new leaders have their moment before the hard conversations start — or it is the first evidence of the avoidance pattern that produced the December 8 deficit shock. Either reading is a story.

Dana is now sitting on a perfect cold open for a recap segment she has not yet produced: the optimism of December 1 against the wreckage of everything that followed.
