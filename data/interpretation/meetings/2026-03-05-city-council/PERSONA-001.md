### Reactions

I can't believe what I just watched. The library director gets up there and tells them they have 27 people PER HOUR using the library - that's families, kids doing homework, parents attending story time - and the council just... cuts it completely? Kevin said they've been ignored for 25 years and this is their second study telling them the same thing. Meanwhile they're totally fine spending $87 million on a police station and keeping the fire station in a spot that doesn't even cover the eastern part of town properly.

And here's the thing that really gets me - they KNOW we're in crisis. Councilor Matthews straight up said the school budget needs to cut $7-8 million while asking for a 6% tax increase. That's teachers, that's classroom aides, that's art and music programs. But sure, let's ask taxpayers to fork over $87 million for municipal buildings while our kids lose educational support staff. The math doesn't work for families like mine.

What really bothered me was how they kept talking about the "taxpayer burden" but couldn't connect it to actual families. That woman who testified about the rental assistance? She had her daughter with her. These are real people trying to stay in their homes while the city spends money on everything except what actually serves families with kids. I'm sharing this in the parent Facebook groups tomorrow because people need to know what's really going on before they vote on this bond.
