### Reactions

So they finally put some structure around committee budgets - thank god. The $2,500 cap with line-item detail requirements makes total sense. Walker asked exactly the right questions about the Human Rights Commission and potential budget impacts. That's the kind of due diligence you want to see.

The bigger issue that jumped out at me was the accountability gap on regional boards. We've got former councilors representing us on Metro and EcoMaine with basically no reporting back. West was right to push for quarterly updates. I mean, EcoMaine is dealing with major infrastructure changes and Metro just merged with us last year - we need to know what's happening there.

The Zoom comment thing was interesting from a legal standpoint. Pride laid out the First Amendment constraints pretty clearly - you can't really control open public comment once you allow it. Makes sense why they killed it after the disruptions. At least they're being honest about the legal limitations instead of just saying "technology is hard."
