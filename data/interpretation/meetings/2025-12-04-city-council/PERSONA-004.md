### Reactions

Jesus Christ, they spent two and a half hours on appointments and liquor licenses while 42 of my colleagues are about to lose their jobs. Not one word about the school budget. Not one. We're facing the biggest education crisis in South Portland's history - $7.2 million in cuts, 78 positions gone including 42 teachers - and they're debating whether committees can spend $2,500 on office supplies.

I kept waiting for someone to bring up the elephant in the room. The per-pupil cost, the health insurance increases, the state funding disaster. Nothing. They know the budget workshops are coming. They know we're looking at 18% tax increases or massive cuts. But tonight it was all about who sits on what committee and whether people can comment over Zoom. 

The union needs to see this. The community needs to understand that while we're preparing for the fight of our professional lives, city council is worried about tree lighting ceremonies and cable franchise agreements. When they finally get around to discussing our budget, they better be ready for some very pointed questions about priorities.
