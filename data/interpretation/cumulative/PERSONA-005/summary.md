# Cumulative Summary: Priya (PERSONA-005)

### Current Understanding

Priya understands South Portland's FY27 budget crisis as a convergence of three compounding failures: chronic state underfunding (20% actual coverage against a 55% statutory obligation), years of internal grant mismanagement that degraded equity-designated funding for multilingual learners and students with IEPs, and a pattern of governance decisions that consistently subordinate structural equity protection to fiscal and political convenience.

The district's proposed cuts — 78 positions including 42 teachers, 16 ed techs, all 7 lunch aides, the behavioral strategist, and the DEI Director's administrative authority — fall disproportionately on the positions and schools serving the highest-need students. Confirmed demographic data shows Skillin at 60% BIPOC and 33% multilingual learners, Kayler at approximately 45% BIPOC and 30–35% multilingual learners. The reconfiguration voted through on March 30 closes schools serving those populations without a Title VI civil rights compliance analysis and without resolved equity support infrastructure. The DEI Director role has been reduced to an instructional strategist in the teacher unit at zero additional savings, eliminating the district's only BIPOC central office leader. Achievement gap data — 22.1% BIPOC/white, 37.4% multilingual/non-multilingual, 32.5% IEP/non-IEP — appeared once in budget proceedings and was not used to evaluate which cuts would widen those gaps.

At the city level, Priya has documented a housing and civic life crisis in which market-rate TIF subsidies, the failed eviction moratorium, the $87 million public safety bond, and a $2,500 HRC operating budget together constitute an ordering of priorities. ICE enforcement in Red Bank has structurally suppressed immigrant family participation in democratic processes. A $100,000 rental assistance appropriation via Project Home passed 6-1, representing the most direct structural protection the council has produced, though a redundant exclusion system — enforcement visibility risk plus state GA ineligibility for fear-based income loss — limits its reach. Approximately $300,000 in new state funds secured by union advocacy exists without an allocation commitment, and Priya intends to track where it lands.
