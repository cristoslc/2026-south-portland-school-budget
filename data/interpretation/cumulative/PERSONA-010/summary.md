# Cumulative Summary: Ben (PERSONA-010)

### Current Understanding

Ben has spent four months tracking a school fiscal crisis that began as a $1.9M mid-year deficit and has grown into an approximately $8M structural gap requiring the elimination of roughly 78 positions. The root cause is a decade-long mismatch: elementary enrollment fell 23% (from 1,401 to 1,080 students) while the district added 82 positions, producing per-pupil costs of $26,651 — highest among comparable regional districts. The finance function was never equipped to catch this drift: six directors in five years, three interim, with a repeat "material weakness" audit finding, a $400K grant management failure, and a $1.1M nutrition fund deficit that went untracked until February. The administration's top reconfiguration solution — closing Skillin Elementary — is politically dead on arrival, and the remaining options save less while the board fractures between those willing to exceed the 6% tax ceiling and those facing council pressure to hold at 3–4%.

This is not solely a school story. The city's own departments are requesting a 15% levy increase. Development deals return roughly 50 cents per dollar to the municipal budget before a 50% thirty-year TIF rebate, deferring meaningful school fiscal gains for decades. A scaled-down Mahoney municipal center ($70–76M) is now targeting a November 2027 referendum; a separate public safety bond is targeting November 2026. Sewer infrastructure debt is driving 22% annual rate increases through FY29. Federal immigration enforcement has disrupted school attendance and the budget calendar, surfaced a community support gap (Project Home, bridged to June 1 with a $100K city appropriation after the eviction moratorium failed 4-2), and — in Councilor Matthews' lone no vote — produced the first official moment where the immigration crisis and the school crisis were named as competing claims on the same fiscal pool. Ben is heading into a June 9 referendum with a complete picture of the individual cost vectors and no single official calculation of what all of them mean together for a median South Portland homeowner.
