### Reactions

I can't believe what I just watched. They spent three hours talking about how to pick the next superintendent and basically decided that parents like me don't get a real say in it. The company they chose - for THIRTY THOUSAND DOLLARS we don't even have - literally said their whole approach is to keep the community out of the actual selection process. We get to fill out surveys and attend focus groups, but when it comes to actually meeting the candidates and helping decide who's best for our kids? Nope, board knows best.

What really gets me is how they talked about it. Board member Richardson kept going on about how this approach would help them "build consensus" and avoid "pain points" with community involvement. Since when is wanting input on who leads my child's school a pain point? And DeAngelo made some comment about how the meeting room was empty so they can just make whatever decision they want. Maybe if they actually valued parent input, more people would show up!

The worst part is they're spending all this money on fancy consultants when they could have just used the Maine association for $8,000 and let us actually be part of the process. Now I have to figure out how to get organized with other parents because clearly this board thinks they know better than the people whose kids will actually be affected by whoever they pick. This is exactly the kind of decision-making that got us into trouble in the first place.
