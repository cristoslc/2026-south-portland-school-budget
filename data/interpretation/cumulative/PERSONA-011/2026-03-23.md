# Cumulative Record: Meg (PERSONA-011)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop -- March 23, 2026 -- 2026-03-23

### Interpretation

Five hours. No vote. I watched until 11:15pm when Chair DeAngelis called for adjournment and said March 30 is what's on the books. Of course I stayed to the end. That's what I do.

The one thing I can hand my group chats clean and without caveats is the number: $257 more per year on the average household. That's what the 6% ceiling looks like when Finance Director Ketchem translates it into a dollar figure. After months of trading percentages and scenarios and structural gap language, I finally have something people can picture on their actual bill. I led with it in every summary tonight.

The Kayler closure recommendation is going to land hard in some of my networks. Not Dyer — Kayler. Dr. Prince's reasoning is documentable: dead-end street, single point of egress, no nearby building to use as an emergency evacuation site, and fewer kids in transition than a Dyer closure would require. I can relay all of that. But Kayler's student body is approximately 45% BIPOC and 30-35% multilingual learners, and a parent at public comment asked directly whether closing that specific school violates Title VI of the Civil Rights Act. DeAngelis said she wanted a legal answer, not quick research. The question went home unanswered. If I post the Kayler recommendation without flagging that open question, someone in my thread is going to raise it anyway — and they'd be right to. I'm flagging it.

The 32 teachers on the recall list is the nuance I need to get right. They are not fired in the traditional sense. They have first-eligibility for any vacancy that opens in their certification area before the district can go outside to hire. With seven retirements announced since January and more retirements expected, some of those 32 may land somewhere. But they have no position in the FY27 budget. I've been saying "42 teachers cut" since December 17 and that was accurate as a budget-position count. It wasn't the whole picture of what actually happens to those people.

Option A versus Option B — two elementary transitions versus one — is still unresolved. I have been reporting on this as a community logistics question for six weeks. Sitting at 11pm watching the board adjourn without a vote, I was doing the math on my own kids' school years, not just my networks'. That part stays out of the group chat summary. But it's there.

The finance director also put on record that FY26 — this year — is projected to end in deficit. That barely surfaced in the room by the time they hit hour four, but I wrote it down. No fund balance left. Any shortfall pulls from city reserves and gets repaid through a future tax increase. The FY27 budget vote in May and the June 9 referendum may not be the last time this hits the property tax bill.

March 30 at 6pm. That's the next date. Write it down.
