# Qualitative vs. Quantitative Market Research: Methods and Best Practices

*Source: Drive Research — https://www.driveresearch.com/market-research-company-blog/difference-between-qualitative-and-quantitative-market-research/*

## What Is Market Research?

Market research is the practice of collecting, analyzing, and interpreting information about consumers, competitors, and market trends to help business owners make evidence-based decisions. Methods divide into two major categories: **primary** (firsthand data) and **secondary** (existing data). Primary research includes both qualitative and quantitative approaches.

## Quantitative Market Research

Focuses on gathering **numerical data** to analyze patterns, trends, and behaviors. Answers questions like "how many," "how often," and "what percentage."

**Key characteristics:**
- Large sample sizes for statistical significance
- Designed to measure and quantify
- Enables pattern identification and hypothesis testing

**Key tools:**
- Online surveys (most common)
- Online panels
- Analytics dashboards

## Qualitative Market Research

Focuses on exploring the **deeper motivations, attitudes, and perceptions** behind customer behaviors. Seeks to understand the "why" behind decisions.

**Common qualitative methods:**

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Focus Groups** | Structured discussion with 6–12 participants moderated to generate open-ended responses |
| **In-Depth Interviews** | One-on-one conversations allowing detailed exploration of individual experiences |
| **Observation** | Watching how customers behave in naturalistic settings |
| **Open-ended Questionnaires** | Written responses that allow nuanced expression |
| **Document Analysis** | Reviewing existing records, communications, and artifacts |

Qualitative research uses **small samples** (often a dozen or fewer participants) and allows the researcher to deviate from the script and pursue emergent topics.

## Key Differences at a Glance

| Feature | Quantitative | Qualitative |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Data type | Numerical | Non-numerical |
| Purpose | Identify patterns, test hypotheses | Explore motivations and opinions |
| Sample size | Large | Small |
| Methods | Surveys, analytics | Interviews, focus groups, observation |
| Answers | "How many?" / "How often?" | "Why?" / "How?" |

## The Hybrid Approach

Combining both methods allows businesses to gain both breadth and depth:

1. **Start qualitative** — exploratory research to understand the landscape and develop hypotheses
2. **Follow with quantitative** — measure how widespread those findings are at scale

"Marketing research projects often start with qualitative research activities to get a more complete picture of an issue... With a better understanding of the issue, they follow up with quantitative research that provides more specificity about what proportion of the population shares common preferences, beliefs, or behaviors."

## Practical Application for Segmentation

Qualitative research informs which variables to include in segmentation surveys. Quantitative surveys then collect data at scale for cluster analysis. The combination validates both the segment definitions and their relative sizes in the market.
