# Cumulative Record: Tom (PERSONA-006)
## Meeting: Workshop -- December 17, 2025

### Interpretation

I'll give the board this much -- they finally put a number on the disaster and it's even uglier than I feared. An 18-19% tax increase just to keep things the way they are? That's almost $570 more a year on a house like mine. Thank God they set a 6% ceiling instead, but that means they have to cut $7.2 million, and I'm not convinced they have the stomach for it based on what I saw two weeks ago with that $30,000 consultant hire.

The enrollment data they presented is the smoking gun I've been waiting for. Elementary enrollment crashed 23% in four years -- from 1,401 kids down to 1,080 -- but they actually added 82 staff positions during the same period enrollment was dropping by 300 students. That's not a rounding error, that's systemic mismanagement. Any business that lost a quarter of its customers while growing its payroll would be bankrupt. And we wonder why South Portland's per-pupil cost of $26,651 is the highest among comparable districts?

At least they're finally talking about cutting 78 positions, but that should have happened years ago when the enrollment decline started. The fund balance is gone -- burned through nearly $6 million in savings to mask the problem -- and the state is only covering 20% of costs when it's supposed to cover 55%. Health insurance is up 12% on top of everything else. The writing's on the wall: either they make these cuts stick, or this whole thing lands on my tax bill. I'm not optimistic.
