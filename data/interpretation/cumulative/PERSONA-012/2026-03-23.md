# Cumulative Record: Jaylen (PERSONA-012)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop -- March 23, 2026 -- 2026-03-23

### Interpretation

The March 23 workshop is the first meeting where Jaylen gets actual names. Seven positions at SPHS — Science twice, English, Art half a position, two special ed case managers, social work, CTE — listed on a slide that Principal Glenn was given approximately five minutes to present before the agenda moved on. He has been carrying 42 teachers as the fixed number since December 17, and now there is a partial answer, and it still does not tell him which specific courses disappear when you lose two science teachers or whether the AP sections he is counting on will be offered in the fall. Nobody on the board asked those follow-up questions. Nobody asked what happens to the junior who registered for AP Chem because of two science cuts. The SPHS section of a five-hour meeting lasted roughly five minutes. The rest was about elementary schools he has never attended.

The Art cut is the one that shifts something. It is 0.5 FTE, which sounds small until Jaylen remembers that "small" arts positions have been the first things cut in every budget season he has watched. Theater is not on the slide. He notices that. He also notices that the boundary between safe and gone is now drawn inside the same department, and nobody explained where exactly it falls. March 9 said theater was thriving. March 23 says Art is being cut. Those are both true and he still cannot answer the school newspaper's question.

The evening shuttle cut is the one that makes cross-country concrete. Three or four after-school buses reduced to one loop of the city — the operations director called it "a potential decrease in the level of service" and kept moving. For students who cannot drive and whose families cannot pick them up from late practice, that is not a service level. That is whether they run. His coach will know more than anyone at this meeting.

The student board member moment is the one that gets filed permanently. DeAngelis announced to the full room that Davidson and Kabesa had to leave early because "they both have studying to do." Each got two minutes. Then they went home, and the meeting about their school's seven teacher cuts continued without them. He has been cataloging this pattern since December 10. This is the same pattern with a microphone in front of it.

The two eighth graders who testified about the percussion ed tech cut did what the district has refused to do for five months: gave the room a specific loss with a specific mechanism and a specific population of affected students. He has been at that podium. He knows that feeling. He knows also what happened after.
