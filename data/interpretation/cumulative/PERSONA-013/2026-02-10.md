# Cumulative Record: Amira (PERSONA-013)
## Meeting: City Council & School Board Joint Budget Guidance Workshop -- February 10, 2026 -- 2026-02-10

### Interpretation

Now I'm seeing the school board and the city council arguing about our schools at the same time, and it's even worse than I thought. The interim superintendent put real numbers on the elementary reconfiguration plan: reorganize five schools into two primary (PreK-1) and three intermediate (grades 2-4) buildings to save $2 million. But two million isn't even close to enough when the total gap is so huge.

The enrollment-vs-staffing numbers are even more stark than I realized: since 2017, the district lost 300+ students (from over 3,000 to 2,748) but ADDED 82 positions. That's insane. No wonder we're in this mess. And still nobody can explain why they didn't notice this was happening sooner. Some parent asked exactly that and didn't really get an answer.

The teachers' union president spoke again, and hearing her say all elementary teachers feel like "their family is being taken away" made me really sad. Teachers ARE like family. Mrs. Rodriguez seems stressed this year and now I know why. But then there are city council members saying schools cost too much and "the people of South Portland cannot afford any of your options." One guy bragged that when he was on the school board 12 years ago, they never went above 3% increases. Cool story, but we didn't ask to be born during a budget crisis and we still deserve good schools.

The middle school schedule fix could save $400-500K but needs a $25,000 traffic study that takes 6-12 months. So that's off the table for now. Parents spoke against reconfiguration too - one talked about kids losing their bike-to-school mornings, another about neighborhood school connections. These are the kinds of things that matter to actual families but don't show up on a spreadsheet.

At least several councilors said the most valuable people are the ones facing students. But then they also said there might be "upwards of 50 staff reductions." So they value teachers but are going to fire them anyway? Adults say one thing and do another, and kids are the ones who pay for it.
