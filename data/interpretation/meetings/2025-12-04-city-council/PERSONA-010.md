### Reactions

So here's the thing about tonight - the council basically created a formal allowance system for their volunteer committees, but buried in that reasonable-sounding policy is a potential problem for the Human Rights Commission. They've been getting around $10,000 a year, and now there's a $2,500 cap unless they come hat-in-hand to a budget workshop. Walker tried to flag this, but the council steamrolled ahead anyway.

The more interesting story might be what Natalie West surfaced about regional boards. We've got former councilors like Linda Cohen sitting on Metro making transit decisions that affect thousands of residents, and the current council basically gets radio silence about what's happening. That's not transparency, that's institutional amnesia. Same thing with Eco Maine - they're bringing a major recycling facility online, and the council is like "oh right, maybe we should get updates on that." 

The Zoom comment thing was predictable - Pride basically said "we tried this, people said horrible things, First Amendment says we can't stop them." But the budget committee process? That's going to create winners and losers, and I'm not sure the council fully grasped which committees are going to get squeezed when budget season hits.
