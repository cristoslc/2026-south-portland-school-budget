# Cumulative Record: Kira (PERSONA-015)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- April 2, 2026 -- 2026-04-02

### Interpretation

Forty behavior plans. Sixty students. Four of five buildings. When Nicholas Boggs read Jenna's statement tonight Kira had to sit with it, because it is her territory — literally. She has been in those buildings. She knows what it looks like when a kid is escalating in general education and there is no one trained in tier two behavioral intervention close enough to catch it before it becomes a special education referral. The district's answer — BCBAs will bridge, instructional strategists will assist, PBIS teams remain — is not wrong as a description of who will be physically present. It is wrong as a description of what will be possible. BCBAs carry their own caseloads. Instructional strategists carry their own caseloads. The role Jenna described — someone whose entire job is navigating the general education behavioral tier across multiple buildings, building the formal plans, holding the relationships — does not exist in the distributed-BCBA model. She knows because she lives inside that model every day.

What she noticed in the board's Q&A is something she has been watching all cycle: the distance between what a dollar saves and what it costs. Feller and Holman pressed the administration on whether restructuring director roles freed money for student-facing positions. The answer was twenty to thirty thousand dollars per change. Not enough to restore a behavioral strategist. Not enough to put a trained ed tech back in Stacy Lauren's room at Skillin, where Lauren ended the day in a physical restraint with a substitute covering a position that should be filled by someone who knows what to do when a kid bolts. She believes Feller when he says there's no money. She also believes the community was told right-sizing was the answer, and now right-sizing has produced the answer, and the answer is not enough.

Chelsea Smirky is the detail she cannot put down. Five FLS classrooms. Three schools. Traveling as a substitute to cover gaps across buildings. That is the itinerant pattern she lives. That is what a specialist role looks like when it is being informally collapsed into substitute coverage as full-time positions are cut. She does not know yet whether her own position is in that dynamic. She is watching.

The three hundred thousand dollars from the state is the first new revenue that has materialized inside the window that matters, since January when the TIF math closed the door on development relief. The union went to Augusta and got it. Connie was clear: the ask is student-facing positions, not administration. Richardson flagged a potentially larger figure in subsequent years. Whether the board directs this specifically to the behavioral strategist, the MTSS roles, or absorbs it into general balancing is not settled because the board did not adopt the budget tonight.

The reconfiguration planning infrastructure is better than she expected. Thirteen listening sessions. Staff preference surveys starting tomorrow morning. Two hundred digital responses already in. Specific outreach to multilingual families through the director of multilingual programs. Specific outreach to IEP parents. This is the accountability structure DeAngelos warned about on March 2 — diversity and equity are different things, you need structures and training — beginning to be built. It does not answer where Kaler's families go. That question resolved badly at March 30. But it is the kind of process that could, if executed with fidelity, make the redistricting something more than buildings renamed.

April 14 is twelve days away. She is going with everything she has.
