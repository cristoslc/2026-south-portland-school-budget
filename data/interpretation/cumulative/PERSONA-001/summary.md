# Cumulative Summary: Maria (PERSONA-001)

### Current Understanding

Maria understands South Portland's school budget crisis as a structural, compound failure now in active execution. The district faces a gap cited at $8.4M (grown from the $7.2M quantified in February), produced by 23% elementary enrollment decline over four years (1,401 to 1,080 students), state funding covering only 20% of actual costs against a 55% statutory obligation, and years of internal financial mismanagement — six finance directors in five years, repeat material-weakness audit findings, and $400,000 in improperly spent grant funds. The fund balance is fully depleted. On March 18, 72 employees received pink slips, moving the crisis from proposal to execution even as nine reconfiguration options remain unfinalized.

Maria understands the governance failure as clearly as the fiscal one. Chair DeAngelis's equity rhetoric — immigrant families, belonging, connection — is irreconcilable with her governance choices: a superintendent search process explicitly designed to minimize community input, a premature public endorsement of reconfiguration before any formal proposal existed, and dismissal of neighborhood schools as things the city "doesn't have anymore." The city council's budget guidance (3–6%, mostly 4–5%) fell below the school board's own requested floor, compounding cuts beyond any scenario the board had modeled. The participation infrastructure — Zoom blocked, ICE raids canceling forums, expensive in-person requirements — has ensured the families most at risk from reconfiguration have had the least voice in it. Children of immigrant families are already missing from the classrooms whose fate is being decided.

Three simultaneous household tax shocks — school budget increase, potential Mahoney debt service (~$1,152/year), and sewer rate increases of 22% annually for three years — compound in ways no governing body is integrating the way Maria has been calculating at her kitchen counter all winter.

The April 14 school budget workshop is the remaining critical entry point before the May 5 council vote and June 9 referendum.
