# Cumulative Summary: David (PERSONA-002)

### Current Understanding

David understands South Portland's budget crisis as a multi-layered institutional failure with compounding capital obligations converging on the same property tax base. The school district requires $8–9M in cuts — tightened from the original $7.2M structural gap when the city council set 3–6% tax increase guidance in February — against a fund balance of only $739K and a second consecutive material weakness audit finding documenting six finance directors in five years, 34 correcting entries, and a $400K grant misallocation. Elementary enrollment has fallen 23% (1,401 to 1,080 students) while staffing grew by 82 positions; per-pupil cost runs $26,651, reportedly the highest among comparable districts; and state funding covers only ~20% versus the 55% formula target, pushing 61% of property taxes onto local taxpayers.

Elementary reconfiguration options have been quantified — Option 1.7 closing Skillin saves $19.6M over five years — but four board members publicly oppose it, transportation costs remain unmodeled, and Pre-K mandate impacts are unfunded. Health insurance actuals are not expected until March 25. FY26 projections have narrowed to a $170K surplus to $1M deficit range as spending controls take effect, but the nutrition program carries a persistent $1.1M deficit. Budget workshops are scheduled for March 23, March 30, and April 14 with the council; the June 9 referendum is the terminal deadline.

On the capital side, the city carries $89.1M in existing bonds, a $51.68M Pearl Street sewer obligation producing 22% annual rate increases for three consecutive years, a Mahoney renovation ranging $70–76M with the referendum deferred to November 2027, and a forthcoming public safety bond for November 2026. No governing body has produced an integrated analysis of what these obligations look like simultaneously on a single household's tax bill — a gap David has been building a private spreadsheet to fill since February, and which Councilor Matthews named in ninety seconds before being outvoted 6–1.
