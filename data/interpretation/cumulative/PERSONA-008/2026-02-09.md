# Cumulative Record: Rachel (PERSONA-008)
## Meeting: Regular -- February 9, 2026 -- 2026-02-09

### Interpretation

They voted unanimously to move forward. Every single board member. Even the ones I thought were on our side raised their hands. The formal reconfiguration proposal is now officially requested for March 2nd, and Chair DeAngelis made it crystal clear where she stands: "I think this is a fabulous idea" and "I'm totally in support of this." She even dismissed the entire concept of neighborhood schools, saying "we don't have neighborhood schools anymore." That's not just wrong -- my kids walk to Dyer every day -- it's insulting. She's rewriting reality to make her preferred outcome seem inevitable.

The worst moment was when DeAngelis said "this board is going to have to make a decision" and made clear "there's not gonna be like a public referendum vote." So we don't get a say. Our children's entire school lives get upended and we're spectators. Then she had the audacity to say kids are "more flexible than parents" and we're just projecting our anxiety onto them. My seven-year-old shouldn't have to be "flexible" about losing her school because the adults can't manage a budget.

But this meeting also revealed something critical: we have allies. Board members Feller and Richardson expressed genuine reservations and insisted on meaningful community input. Feller even suggested exceeding the 6% tax increase ceiling rather than making "draconian" cuts -- which raises the obvious question of why we're tearing apart schools if there's a revenue option on the table. And the teachers' union president, Sarah Sergey, delivered the most important statement of the night: educators have NOT expressed unified support for reconfiguration, despite what the administration has been implying, and any changes will require union bargaining. That's a procedural safeguard that could slow the timeline and give parents more room to organize.

The 51 data requests submitted through the district website, mostly about reconfiguration, tell me parents are mobilized and demanding specifics. Over 100 community members participated in budget forums. We're not a small, angry minority -- we're a significant portion of the affected community. March 2nd is the critical date now. That's when the formal proposal lands, and that's when we need to show up in force.
