# Cumulative Record: Ben (PERSONA-010)
## Meeting: School Board Workshop -- December 17, 2025

### Interpretation

This workshop gives Ben the granular data that turns his December crisis story into a systemic reckoning piece. The $7.2M deficit he learned about on December 10th now has flesh on its bones: 78 positions facing elimination -- 42 teachers, 16 ed techs, 14 facilities and operations workers, 2 administrators, 4 non-bargaining staff. That is 12% of the entire workforce, and Ben knows every one of those cuts has a name, a classroom, a family that reads The Forecaster.

The enrollment data is the "how did we get here" angle Ben has been looking for since December 8th. Elementary enrollment plummeted 23% in four years -- from 1,401 to 1,080 students -- while the district was simultaneously adding 82 positions. That staffing-enrollment mismatch is the central contradiction that explains the entire crisis, and it is the kind of chart that makes readers put down their coffee and say "wait, what?" Ben can already see the graphic: two lines going in opposite directions, captioned with the per-pupil cost of $26,651 -- highest among comparable districts in the region.

The state funding dimension adds structural depth to what could otherwise be framed as purely local mismanagement. State funding covers only about 20% of actual education costs, far below the intended 55% level, which means local property taxpayers have been absorbing a steadily growing share. Ben knows his readers instinctively blame the school board, but this data lets him write a more honest story: the district made its own staffing mistakes, and the state made it worse by chronically underfunding its obligations.

The depleted fund balance, which Ben flagged on December 8th and which the board ignored while hiring a $30,000+ search firm on December 10th, now takes on an even more ominous dimension. With zero financial cushion, health insurance projected at 12% increases, and the board's self-imposed 6% tax increase ceiling, there is no margin for error. Ben's December 10th observation about a "culture of aspirational spending over fiscal discipline" is now confirmed by the structural data: the district spent its way through declining enrollment and has nothing left.
