# Cumulative Record: David (PERSONA-002)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- March 9, 2026

### Interpretation

This was largely a procedural meeting, but the signals it sent about budget process maturity were reassuring. Three budget workshops are now scheduled -- March 23, March 30, and a City Council workshop on April 14 -- showing a structured timeline that should prevent last-minute decision-making. That methodical approach is exactly what I have been pushing for since December.

The Finance Committee report shows continued detailed tracking: 92% of funds expended or encumbered through February 28, with scenario modeling projecting outcomes from a $170K surplus to a $1M deficit depending on spending controls. The range has narrowed somewhat from earlier projections, which suggests the spending freeze and attrition-based vacancies are having some effect, but the deficit risk remains real.

The $1.1 million nutrition program deficit surfaced again in the Finance Committee notes -- confirming this was not a one-time disclosure but a persistent problem the board is tracking. The note that if the school fund balance is depleted, the city would need to step in using their fund balance (with repayment required) makes the cross-governmental fiscal interdependence explicit. Given the fund balance sits at only $739K as of the February 10 joint workshop, this bailout scenario is not hypothetical.

Enrollment has stabilized at 2,754 students as of March 5, up 6 from February. The stabilization at the secondary level provides at least one variable that is not getting worse, though elementary trends remain the central challenge. Routine personnel actions -- two resignations, one retirement -- continue the natural attrition pattern that the superintendent committed to managing through the budget process rather than automatic backfill.

The real action is clearly in the upcoming workshops. The elementary reconfiguration proposal that was formally requested at the February 9 meeting and presented at the March 2 workshop now needs to be resolved alongside the remaining $3M+ gap. With health insurance actuals not arriving until March 25 and three workshops before the April 14 council session, the next six weeks will determine whether the process maturity I have been tracking actually translates into sound budget decisions.
