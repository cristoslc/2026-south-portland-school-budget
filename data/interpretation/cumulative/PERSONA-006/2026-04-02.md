# Cumulative Record: Tom (PERSONA-006)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- April 2, 2026 -- 2026-04-02

### Interpretation

Three days after the board couldn't pass its own budget, they're back in the room, and this time the 6% ceiling holds. Tom's been watching that number since February, watched it nearly collapse on March 30th, and now the finance director is confirming it on the record. That's the win. It's real. He writes it down.

But the meeting hands him something harder than another threat: it hands him the full accounting of how the district got here. Three consecutive deficits — FY24, FY25, FY26. Not one bad year. Three. And the electricity budget, which the finance director laid out in a slide that Tom would have framed and hung on the wall if he could: the district overspent its power budget by $138K in FY23, $211K in FY24, $165K in FY25, and is now projecting a $368K overage this year — after setting the FY26 budget *lower* than what FY25 actually cost. That's not incompetence at the margin. That's a pattern of looking at the evidence and budgeting in the wrong direction anyway. He has been saying since December that this crisis was self-made. Now he has the utility bills to prove it.

The fund balance confirmation lands the same way. Gone. Zero cushion. The finance director says she refuses to use optimistic numbers precisely because there's nothing to catch the fall if she's wrong. Tom respects that. He also recognizes that the reason there's nothing left is five years of drawing it down instead of building it up — the same story the March 23 record told him, now stated again with no ambiguity.

The state money is the one thing he didn't see coming. Three hundred thousand from union legislative outreach, potentially another $750K from a formula change. That's real money. He is genuinely glad. What he wants to know now is whether it gets used to close the structural gap or to soften the political pain of the reconfiguration. The distinction matters.

And then the board didn't vote. Again. Tuesday is the council meeting. They're going in with the superintendent's recommendation rather than a board-adopted budget. Tom has been watching this board fail to make decisions for five months. One more night without a vote is not surprising. It is exhausting.
