# Cumulative Record: Rachel (PERSONA-008)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- February 3, 2026 -- 2026-02-03

### Interpretation

Rachel walks into this meeting already wound tight. She has spent January tracking two governing bodies across seven meetings, building a picture of compounding fiscal pressure, deliberate process opacity, and a board leadership that views parent input as a nuisance to be managed. She is fourteen days out from the February 4th forum she has been rallying parents to attend — the one that was postponed from January 22nd because ICE enforcement made gathering unsafe. She is not here for routine civic engagement. She is here to gather intelligence.

What she gets is a school audit that reads like a dossier of institutional dysfunction. A material weakness in reconciliations. Thirty-four audit journal entries. Four hundred thousand dollars moved from grants to the general fund because the school department overspent its budget — and the auditor noting pointedly that this is a *repeat finding* from last year. Six finance directors in five years, three of them interim. The assistant superintendent offers reassurance that the new finance director is "amazing," and Rachel has to physically stop herself from laughing. She has heard versions of that sentence before. The person saying it is the same administration that will be presenting nine reconfiguration scenarios in less than a month.

The audit details matter to Rachel not primarily because she cares about accounting controls — but because she has been watching the administration position itself as reluctant stewards of a crisis they inherited. The financial mismanagement picture cuts against that narrative. This deficit didn't arrive from nowhere. It was built, year by year, through staffing decisions made while enrollment was already declining, and through a finance operation so unstable that no one was watching the ledger carefully enough to catch overruns before they became audit findings.

When DeAngelos stands at the podium and tells the city council "you are not off the hook" and demands "clear guidance during a very difficult budget year," Rachel's read is immediate: the board chair is coming to the council with her hand out, and the phrase "very difficult budget year" is doing the same work it has been doing since December — softening the audience for decisions that have already been shaped. The community forums announced for this week and next feel, in this context, less like genuine input-gathering and more like the final procedural checkbox before a proposal that is already written gets delivered on March 2nd.
