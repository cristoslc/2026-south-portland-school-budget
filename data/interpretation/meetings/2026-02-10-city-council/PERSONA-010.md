### Reactions

Look, I've covered a lot of budget meetings, but this one felt different. Usually there's some kabuki theater where everyone pretends there are good options if we just work hard enough. Monday night, Interim Superintendent Entwistle just laid it out: Option A is financial suicide at 19% tax increase, Option B leaves a $3.5 million hole even with massive cuts, Option C still needs $1.1 million in cuts for a 9.8% increase. Pick your poison.

The visual that's going to stick with readers is that chart showing enrollment falling off a cliff while staffing kept climbing. Down 300 kids since 2017, up 82 employees. That's not about serving needier students - that's about a district that couldn't or wouldn't adjust to reality. When School Board Chair DeAngelo lectured the council about doing their job and giving concrete guidance instead of saying "do your best," you could feel the frustration. But what's she supposed to tell taxpayers facing 6-10% increases when their Social Security went up 2.8%?

The elementary school "reconfiguration" is going to be the flashpoint. They're calling it efficiency, but parents heard neighborhood schools getting carved up to save $2 million. The middle school fix could save another $500K but it's stuck in DOT permitting hell for a year. Meanwhile, 50 people are about to lose jobs and class sizes are going up. The city budget adds more pain on top. This isn't a budget crisis - it's a reckoning with years of decisions that didn't match the demographics. The question now is whether voters will accept the medicine or blow up the whole thing at referendum.
