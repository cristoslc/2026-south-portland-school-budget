### Reactions

Well that was something. I sat through four hours of this dog and pony show last night, and let me tell you what really happened. The school board - the same people crying poverty and asking us to swallow an 18% tax increase - just voted to spend thirty grand on a fancy consulting firm to find them a new superintendent. Thirty thousand dollars they don't have budgeted anywhere.

They had three options. The local Maine outfit would've done it for eight thousand max. The guy from Massachusetts was honest about the work and seemed competent for maybe twelve to fifteen. But no, they went with the slick operation from New York that promises to hold their hands through the whole process and make them feel important. Member Richardson actually said with a straight face that they need to "think 10-20 years down the road" while voting to blow money we don't have RIGHT NOW.

At least George Rich had the guts to call them on it. He asked point blank - "we just told our staff to cut non-personnel spending, and now we're doing this?" Exactly right, George. They're cutting programs for kids but spending premium dollars so they don't have to do any actual work themselves. And the kicker? The room was empty except for a few of us. They made this decision with zero public input, then complained that nobody showed up. Maybe if you hadn't run the last guy out of town, people would trust you to make these decisions without supervision.
