# Cumulative Record: Tom (PERSONA-006)
## Meeting: Regular -- March 9, 2026

### Interpretation

Went to another meeting, told my neighbor Bob about it afterward. The numbers haven't changed but they're getting more concrete -- and that makes them harder to ignore. The $7.2 million gap is now translating into 78 specific positions: 42 teachers, 16 ed techs, 14 facilities/food/transport workers, 2 administrators, and 4 non-bargaining staff. That's 12% of the entire workforce. When they say "cuts" in the abstract it's easy to dismiss. When you see the breakdown by job category, you realize this is going to be felt in every school building.

The per-pupil spending comparison keeps nagging at me. $26,651 per student -- more than Portland, more than Yarmouth, more than Gorham. We're paying premium prices and I don't see premium results. And the state's failure to meet its 55% funding obligation means 80 cents of every dollar comes from local property taxpayers. That's the structural problem nobody wants to talk about because it requires going to Augusta, not just yelling at the school board.

I keep coming back to the same fundamental question: how did we hire 82 people while losing 300 students? The administration still hasn't provided the breakdown of what those positions are -- they promised it to the city council a month ago and as far as I know it hasn't materialized. The fund balance is zero. Health insurance is up 12%. School taxes are 61% of my bill. The board took the biggest savings option off the table last week. I support good schools -- they protect my property value -- but there has to be a limit to what we ask homeowners on fixed incomes to absorb. Somebody needs to show me outcomes that justify this spending level, because right now all I see is costs going up and enrollment going down.
