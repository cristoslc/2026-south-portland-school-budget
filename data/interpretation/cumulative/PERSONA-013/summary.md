# Cumulative Summary: Amira (PERSONA-013)

### Current Understanding

As of April 2, Amira understands South Portland's budget crisis as the product of three overlapping failures: state underfunding (covering ~20% of costs against a mandated 55%), staffing growth during a 300-student enrollment decline since 2017, and internal financial mismanagement — six finance directors in five years, 34 audit errors, $400K in mishandled grants. The district faces an $8.4 million gap and has proposed eliminating 78 positions, including 42 teachers. No final budget has passed; the board has deferred votes across four consecutive meetings.

The reconfiguration fight has shifted from Skillin to Kayler — the district's most diverse elementary, roughly 45% BIPOC and 30–35% multilingual learners — which was authorized for closure 6–1 on March 30. Elementary reconfiguration into Primary (Pre-K–1) and Intermediate (grades 2–4) buildings was also adopted 4–2. Families of affected students were largely not informed in time to respond, repeating a communication failure Amira has tracked since December.

Programs she cares about are concretely threatened: four middle school related arts teachers and the percussion ed tech (serving 500 students grades 5–12) remain in the proposed cuts; computer science at the middle school is proposed for complete elimination; the ESOL teacher at Memorial is being cut and the co-teaching model for multilingual learners is ending. Her gifted program is confirmed safe at 3.0 FTEs.

The immigrant community Amira belongs to has been welcomed, defended, endangered by ICE activity at neighborhood businesses, denied eviction protection in a 4–2 vote, handed a symbolic resolution, and finally given $100,000 in rental assistance on a 6–1 vote — with the lone no explicitly framing immigrant family relief as competing against the school budget. The budget failure at March 30 means a fourth meeting on April 2 produced more testimony and no final vote.
