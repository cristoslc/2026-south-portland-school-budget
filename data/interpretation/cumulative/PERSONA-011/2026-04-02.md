### Interpretation

I've been watching this board since December 8th when the spending freeze hit and nobody had a number yet. Four months later, I watched them punt again — and this time they had a reason I actually respect, even if my group chats are going to lose it.

Connie DeSanto from the SSPA walked up to the podium and dropped $300,000 in new state money — right there, mid-meeting, while the information was still coming in. Homeless student funding, economically disadvantaged students, union advocacy in Augusta that apparently worked. I was already composing the text before she finished. That's why I can't post a clean dollar figure tonight: I don't know if it's $300K or $750K or whether those are the same money or different money. I'll hold the post until I can source it. That's the job.

The board voted unanimously on one thing: meet with city council on April 7th. That's Tuesday. But item 4.3 — the actual budget adoption — didn't happen. At least four members said they weren't ready. The superintendent delivered the warning I'd have led with if I were running that meeting: if the board doesn't act, her budget goes to council, not theirs. A Monday meeting is supposedly being discussed. I'm watching for confirmation.

Three consecutive deficit years. No fund balance. $360,000 over budget on electricity alone this year, and they won't know the final bill until July. Finance director Ketchen confirmed all of it on the record tonight — I've been reporting "no savings buffer" since December 8th, but now I have her saying it directly in April, about FY24 and FY25 behind us and FY26 still bleeding.

And still — still — no attendance boundaries. No bus ride length limits. No answer on whether siblings stay together. Dr. Prince said "by end of next week" for a timeline. That's the one sentence every parent in my group chats is going to ask about, and "end of next week" is the only answer I have.
