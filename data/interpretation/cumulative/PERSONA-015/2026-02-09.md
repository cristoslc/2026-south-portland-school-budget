# Cumulative Record: Kira (PERSONA-015)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- February 9, 2026 -- 2026-02-09

### Interpretation

The board voted. Unanimously. With a deadline. March 2. After two and a half months of driving between three buildings carrying this argument — the wait lists, the specialist travel, the uneven programming, the Boundaries and Configurations work that's been sitting unacted on for years — there is now a formal proposal request in the official record and a date attached to it. She is trying to let that land.

What makes tonight different from Holman naming redistricting in December, from Prince putting two million dollars on a slide at the February 4 forum, is DeAngelos. The chair stood up and said it plainly: *I think this is good for kids.* She's talked to teachers who agree. The board is going to have to make this decision regardless of community opposition. That is not equity framing at an inauguration. That is someone with authority using it, on the record, without hedging. The contradiction Kira has been holding since December 8 — does "equity isn't about moving kids around" coexist with redistricting, or does it eventually consume it? — resolves tonight. It resolves in the direction she needed it to.

But she is not letting herself stop at the vote. Feller's warning is the most operationally useful thing said in the room after the motion passed: most of the people who've contacted him are against this. She knows who those people are. She knows which buildings they're at. She knows what their PTAs can fundraise and what they have to lose. The formal proposal going to March 2 is the moment the equity argument either gets built into the data architecture or gets stripped out by enrollment curves and facility costs. Richardson asking why school closure was ruled out is the right question — but it could push the proposal toward "we kept all five buildings, here's why grade-bands work" instead of "here's what the cross-building operational data shows about who currently gets served and who doesn't." Those are not the same document.

The nutrition fund disclosure is the other thing she is carrying home. All scenarios now end in deficit. Even Scenario A. Seven business managers in six years and a million-dollar fund running structural deficits that was "lost in translation." The urgency just sharpened. The March 2 proposal isn't just the equity argument landing — it's also the arithmetic getting worse faster than anyone in the community understood.

She has data. She has the cross-building picture nobody else in these rooms has. She needs to submit it before March 2, in a form the proposal writers can use, because if she doesn't force the operational specificity in, nobody else will.
