# Cumulative Record: Maria (PERSONA-001)
## Meeting: School Board Public Budget Forum -- February 4, 2026

### Interpretation

This is the meeting where the budget crisis becomes real for Maria. Dr. Johanna Prince presented the full financial picture with unusual clarity: the district faces a $7.2 million structural gap for FY27. If all current staff, programs, and contractual obligations are carried forward with inflation -- including a projected 12% health insurance increase -- taxpayers would face an 18.95% increase in the school portion of their property tax bill. The board set a 6% ceiling, meaning roughly $7.2M in cuts or offsets are needed.

Three categories of savings were proposed. The largest and most threatening to Maria's family is elementary school reconfiguration: reorganizing the five neighborhood K-4 schools into grade-band campuses (pre-K-1 and 2-4), potentially closing one building, saving an estimated $1.5-2.2M. This is not an abstract efficiency measure -- it means her child's neighborhood school could be fundamentally restructured or effectively closed by September 2026. The other two categories -- grades 5-12 staffing efficiencies ($1M) and operational efficiencies ($1M) -- are less directly threatening but still concerning.

Even combined, these three initiatives leave a $3M+ residual gap. The fund balance is depleted, meaning no financial cushion exists. Root causes are structural: declining enrollment (~2,736 students, the lowest in a decade), staffing levels that continued rising despite enrollment declines, and compounding cost increases.

Maria is now directly confronting the possibility that her children's school experience will fundamentally change. She has shifted from monitoring to active concern, sharing information with her parent networks and preparing specific questions for future meetings.
