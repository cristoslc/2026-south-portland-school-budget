# Cumulative Record: Meg (PERSONA-011)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- March 10, 2026 -- 2026-03-10

### Interpretation

I've been tracking the ICE crisis alongside the school budget since January 12, when kids first started staying home. I watched the forum get canceled because families were too scared to leave their houses. I watched the eviction moratorium die 4-2 in February when the symbolic council solidarity cracked under real financial pressure. Tonight the crisis finally has names attached to it — not data points, names. A person in a freezing detention cell who fell because the shoes they were given didn't fit. A parent who kept their kids home after a neighbor reported ICE agents near the school driveway, who then lost income staying home to be with them. A woman who spent the rent money on a lawyer when her husband was detained on his way to work. Margot Kralik stood at that podium and read all three stories aloud, and I am absolutely posting that in my group chats tonight.

The numbers that go with those stories: 80 South Portland families on Project HOME's waiting list right now, all tied to ICE-related income loss, $168,000 to help all of them. The council is trending toward roughly $100,000 — the city manager averaged out the positions around the room and said he'd bring an order at that level to the next meeting. Not tonight; this was a workshop. The vote is still coming. That gap between $168K and $100K is somewhere between 30 and 40 families who probably don't get help, and I need to say that clearly.

But the thing that will not leave me is DeAngelis at that podium. I have spent three and a half months watching her be the person in the room who fights for the kids "west of the Dairy Queen," who made closing Skillin a non-starter on equity grounds, who told the council to their faces that they have an obligation to the school district. Tonight she stood at the same podium and recommended the city start with $50,000. Fifty thousand. Not because she doesn't care — she said explicitly she's dealing with the school budget and is "very sensitive to what the financial responsibilities are on everyone." And then Matthews did the same thing from the other direction: he watched the school board the night before, heard the board chair say the district needs to be "very cautious of every dime," and used that as justification to vote against immigrant relief entirely. Two different people, same arithmetic. The school budget crisis is now being invoked inside city hall to constrain what this city will do for the families most hurt by ICE enforcement — families who overlap significantly with the very kids DeAngelis fought to protect at Skillin. I watched those two things and couldn't reconcile them in real time.

The good news, and I did lead with some of it: Metro confirmed high school students are still riding free, the confusion during the merger got sorted. New Route 1 bus route launching this summer — Cash Corner, Thornton Heights, Sunset Park finally getting direct service to Portland. I can hand my networks that without dread attached.

The bike share rejection and the e-bike ordinance are minor and I'll mention them, but tonight belongs to the 80 families and the complicated thing I watched happen in that room.
