# South Portland School Budget — Public Briefing

## Since Last Meeting

The most significant development since the previous school board session is the full budget proposal that was laid out at the **March 23, 2026 School Board Budget Workshop**.

The superintendent presented a proposed FY27 budget totaling **$75.6 million** — up from $73.1 million in FY26, and up from $69.3 million the year before that. The increase exists even after a substantial round of proposed cuts, because costs — particularly in special education ($16.4M, up from $15M in FY26) and facilities ($7.3M, up from $6.2M) — continue to rise.

**Key reductions already built into the proposal:**
- A **12% reduction in district staffing** overall
- **$703,000 in non-personnel cuts**, including reduced building supplies and services, less software and hardware spending, elimination of a second lunch period at some schools, and reductions in transportation parts and legal contingencies
- **$808,000 in other reductions**, including a smaller nutrition program transfer, removal of lunch aide positions, reduced transportation overtime, and deferring a payment to the city for a prior-year deficit until FY28

**Elementary school closure:**
The superintendent recommended closing **one elementary school** for the 2026–27 school year. Two specific options were presented to the board. The district explicitly said it does not recommend permanently decommissioning a closed building — they want the option to lease it for up to four years to generate some revenue while keeping future flexibility. If the board chooses **not** to proceed with any closure, the administration estimated that **12 to 16 additional positions** would need to be eliminated from across the district to balance the budget.

**Pre-K:**
The proposal keeps four locally funded pre-K classrooms (64 seats) unchanged, while expanding total pre-K capacity to 120 seats using state and partnership funding — not local tax dollars.

**Tax impact — still pending:**
As of when the March 23 slides were prepared, the administration noted it was still meeting with City Finance to finalize tax impact numbers, which were expected to be posted publicly before the workshop. Those figures had not yet appeared in the available sources at the time this briefing was written.

**Important context from the City Council:**
At its March 19 meeting, the City Council voted to set the **FY27 budget public hearing date** — a procedural step that locks in the broader city budget calendar. The school budget feeds into that process.
