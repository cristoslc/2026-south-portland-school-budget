# Cumulative Record: Linda (PERSONA-007)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- April 2, 2026 -- 2026-04-02

### Interpretation

Five days after the budget failed 5-2 and I walked out of March 30 knowing exactly which amendments could get to five votes, I came into April 2 needing two things: honest accounting and forward motion. I got one of them fully and the other only partially, and I'm trying to be precise about which is which.

Abigail's presentation on the under-budgeting pattern is the most important thirty minutes this board has had in a budget meeting in years. Electricity overspent by $138K to $368K annually. Tuition reimbursement overspent by $30K to $153K annually. She named it directly: we were never actually budgeting for what things cost, and that systematic gap is what emptied the fund balance. I've been on the finance committee through all of it. I've been carrying the honest account since December 8 — when the $1.9 million hole appeared out of a "rosy scenario" — waiting for administration to own it publicly. On March 23, Abigail started that work when she named the root causes in public testimony. Tonight she finished it. "I'm refusing to be optimistic" is the right posture for a district with zero in reserves, and it's now on the record where the community can hold us to it.

What's harder to absorb is the administrative restructuring math. The premise going into this budget cycle was that converting director roles to instructional strategists would liberate dollars to protect student-facing positions. When it got interrogated, we're talking $20,000 to $30,000 per conversion — not the meaningful savings the restructuring model implied. Richardson and Holman were right to push on it. That gap between what was promised and what materialized is something we owe the community a clear accounting of before April 7.

The state funding news landing mid-meeting — $300K confirmed, potentially $750K more from the EPS adjustment — is the first genuine positive surprise in this entire cycle. But I heard the caution that the EPS piece might be a one-year change, and I'm not willing to build permanent positions on a temporary appropriation. That's precisely the wishful budgeting Abigail just spent an hour telling us we have to stop doing. I want a responsible recommendation Monday on how those dollars get deployed — ideally with a portion seeding the fund balance toward the 9% floor.

We passed the council meeting vote unanimously. But the board didn't vote on the budget, which means the superintendent's budget — not the board's budget — goes to council on April 7. That procedural distinction matters. I need us to own this proposal before we walk into that chamber.
