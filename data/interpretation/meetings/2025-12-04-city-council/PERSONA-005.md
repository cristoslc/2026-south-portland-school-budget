### Reactions

This meeting was a perfect example of how equity gets lost in seemingly neutral policy decisions. The committee budget process sounds reasonable on paper - standardize funding, create transparency, cap spending. But when you actually look at WHO this impacts, it's a disaster waiting to happen. The Human Rights Commission has been operating on around $10K annually, and now they're supposed to make do with $2,500? Meanwhile, committees like Conservation can just tap their buddy departments for extra funding whenever they need it. It's structural inequity dressed up as fiscal responsibility.

What really got to me was Councilor Walker pushing back on this and actually naming the problem - that some committees have departmental backing while others don't. That's exactly the kind of analysis we need more of. But then the majority just steamrolled ahead anyway because "it's an experimental year" and "we can adjust later." When has South Portland ever gone back to increase funding for equity work after cutting it?

The Zoom comment discussion was equally frustrating. Yes, there have been incidents, but the solution can't be to just give up on accessibility. Working parents, people with disabilities, folks without reliable transportation - they're the ones who get shut out when everything has to be in-person. And of course Jeff Steinbrink's comments about our Somali neighbors were important, but it shouldn't take federal-level hate speech for us to actively affirm that our immigrant community members belong here. We should be proactively creating welcoming spaces, not just responding to crises.
