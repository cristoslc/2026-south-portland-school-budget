### Interpretation

Okay so on top of everything from the last meeting - the spending freeze, the broken field, all that stuff - now I find out the school board spent three and a half hours talking about hiring a company to help them hire a new superintendent. And they picked the MOST expensive one! Like, $30,000 for Zeal Education Group when the cheapest option was only $8,000. And they literally said they don't have money budgeted for it. How does that make any sense when they can't even buy us paper and books?

The part that really got to me was how the company they picked basically said the school board gets to make all the final decisions about who becomes superintendent - not the community. Sure, they'll do surveys and meet with people, but community members won't actually be part of choosing the candidates. It's the same thing as always - adults pretending to listen but making the decisions without us. And the board chair even pointed out that almost nobody from the community even showed up to this meeting! So they're making this huge decision in an empty room.

One board member said they need to spend more because the last superintendent messed up the finances, which... okay, I kind of get that? But it still feels wrong to spend $30,000 on consultants when my school can't afford basic supplies. Mom says it's like when you buy an expensive lock after you've already been robbed - maybe it helps, but it doesn't get your stuff back.
