FY27 adjusts from prior under-budgeting
FY24 deficit; FY25 deficit; FY26 projected deficit
Without a savings buffer, under-budgeting leads to personnel losses in following year(s).
Avoiding ‘optimistic’ budgeting protects personnel long-term.
Example 1: Tuition Reimbursement
● FY23 $41k spent more than budgeted
● FY24 $153k spent more than budgeted
● FY25 $140k spent more than budgeted
● FY26 ~$30-62k estimated spending more than budgeted
Example 2: Electricity
● FY23 $138k spent more than budgeted
● FY24 $211k spent more than budgeted
● FY25 $165k spent more than budgeted
● FY26 ~$368k+estimated spending more than budgeted

Preparing for FY28 and Beyond
FY27 resets our financial path but does not solve our core problems.
Moving forward effectively requires a longer view with decision making, new policies,
stabilized leadership, and a commitment to continuous adjustment as data/evidence evolve.
● Financial challenges
○ Labor costs, by contract when all lane/step/adjustments are included and if all
employees remain in place, increase by more than 6% per year
○ Utilities are increasing at high rates
○ FY28 debt service to increase by $300k+
○ Skillin boiler may require new debt
○ Declining enrollment reduces non-local tax revenue
● Opportunities
○ Multi-year financial planning
○ Careful review of roles and position requirements with natural attrition
○ Yearly review of all non-personnel costs -tech, subscriptions, service contracts

Proposal: Budget Guidance from City Council
1. Pass the budget on 2 April
2. Present budget to the Council, as planned, 7 April
3. Convene meeting allowing the Board, if inclined, to
vote to make adjustment to the budget

Council can provide financial guidance and
discuss potential adjustments
5. This sequence will keep Board and Council on
track for Council’s May 4 School Budget Hearing
and vote to send to public referendum
