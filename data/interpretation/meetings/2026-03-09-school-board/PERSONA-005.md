### Reactions

Okay, so this meeting had me going through every emotion. On one hand, they actually stepped up for immigrant families with that City Council resolution - that's the kind of concrete solidarity we rarely see from school boards. And the community school model? That could be game-changing if they do it right, bringing mental health services and family support right into the schools.

But then we get to this elementary school mess and I'm seeing red flags everywhere. They're talking about "diversity" and "reflecting our community" as reasons to close neighborhood schools, but where's the actual equity analysis? Which students are currently underserved and how exactly does forcing families to travel further solve that? When one parent said it would be harder for families to coordinate drop-offs, that hit me - we're talking about working families, single parents, folks without reliable transportation.

The worst part is they're expanding special education services for 3 and 4-year-olds - which is amazing and necessary - but they can't figure out how to keep five elementary schools open? Something doesn't add up. And that non-biased hiring training keeps getting pushed back while they're making massive decisions about which communities get neighborhood schools. This feels like equity language being used to justify budget cuts, and that's exactly the kind of thing that erodes trust in the system when families most need support.
