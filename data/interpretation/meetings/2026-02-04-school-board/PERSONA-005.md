### Reactions

This meeting was exactly what I expected and feared - big budget cuts presented as "efficiency" without any equity analysis whatsoever. They want to consolidate elementary schools and increase class sizes by 20-40% in some grades, but couldn't tell us which schools currently have the smallest classes or serve which populations. When someone asked about transportation impacts, they basically admitted they haven't thought it through. Classic.

The most telling moment was when that board member said outright that the people at these forums are "teachers, administrators, ed techs, parents" but not "the rest of our taxpayers." At least someone's being honest about how these processes exclude renters, child-free adults, and working families who can't make evening meetings. But then why are we making decisions that will reshape which kids get what resources?

I need to dig into the enrollment data by school and cross-reference it with free/reduced lunch rates, ELL populations, and special education numbers. If they're talking about moving from 10-student classes to 18-student classes, I guarantee you those small classes aren't at the schools serving our newest Americans or families in poverty. This reconfiguration could easily become a way to reduce resources for the students who need them most while calling it "efficiency." The fact that they're cutting educational technicians - the people who directly support our highest-need students - tells me everything I need to know about their priorities.
