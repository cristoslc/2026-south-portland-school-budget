## Open Questions

Kira is carrying all of the following into tonight's meeting. These are unresolved, and tonight's decisions may lock some of them permanently:

- **What are the two closure options?** Is Kaler vs. Brown the operative framing, or has a third building entered the discussion? Cullen put Brown in the city real estate planning record weeks ago without any equity architecture — has that already shaped which buildings the board is even considering?

- **Is the 78-position list being cut on a blunt cost-per-position basis, or has anyone modeled what each cut means for actual students across buildings?** I've watched this district talk about equity and then eliminate the positions serving the kids who most need support. I need to see the analysis, not just the number.

- **Are itinerant and specialist positions specifically enumerated on the personnel reduction list?** "Not all from one unit" does not protect cross-building roles if no one is tracking them as a distinct category. If my position — or positions like mine — are collapsed into "ed techs," they disappear from the analysis.

- **What does each closure option mean for MTSS access, multilingual learner services, and intervention wait lists across the remaining buildings?** The space crisis the SPTA named — students receiving MTSS in hallways — gets worse, not better, if you close a building without redistricting equity data driving the design.

- **Has the $2M+ reconfiguration savings projection been revised to include transportation costs?** If the net savings number drops significantly, does the board's political will to absorb closure opposition drop with it?

- **Will redistricting mechanics be modeled with demographic equity as an explicit goal before any closure vote?** Dr. Prince couldn't commit to a timeline when Richardson pressed on this directly at the March 2 workshop. That was three weeks ago. If there's still no timeline tonight, the vote is being set up to happen before the equity architecture exists.

- **Is there any operational modeling of what reconfiguration means specifically for itinerant staff?** Not cost modeling — operational modeling. How many minutes of instruction do I lose or gain per week under each closure scenario? Does grade-band consolidation reduce the distance I travel, or does it reorganize buildings in a way that makes the specialist coverage problem worse?

- **Has the Title I sweep been assessed for multi-year pattern?** If federal equity funding wasn't reaching the students it was designated for, I want to know whether that was an isolated transition error or whether the students on my caseload have been underserved for years without anyone tracking it.

- **Are there McKinney-Vento and MTSS placement surge projections built into the staffing model?** Project HOME is exhausted. Families are weeks behind on rent. I am going to see more housing-unstable kids on my caseload. That means more referrals, more placements, more case time. None of that shows up in a position-count reduction analysis.

- **Will the Metro/school bus integration idea surface tonight as a cost-reduction mechanism?** If secondary transportation gets restructured as a budget line before reconfiguration is finalized, it changes the operational reality for cross-building roles in ways no one has modeled yet.

- **What happens to the Kaler school counselor position in tonight's reduction list?** That vacancy has been open, and the families I know in that building — including families who have been afraid to come to school at all since January — depend on that counselor.

*What to listen for*: Whether any board member moves to condition a closure preference on the delivery of redistricting equity modeling. *Questions to prepare*: "Before the board expresses a closure preference, can we confirm that redistricting equity modeling is complete or scheduled with a specific date — not 'we'll get to it'?"
