# Synthesis: South Portland Redistricting Tool

## Key Findings

The Data4Good redistricting tool models four elementary school closure scenarios for South Portland, each removing one school (Brown, Dyer, Kaler, or Small) and redistributing students across the remaining buildings. Skillin (capacity 380) cannot be closed because the other four schools lack combined capacity to absorb its students.

**Closing Kaler produces the least disruption** — only 27% of students change schools. Closing Brown causes the most disruption at 42%, with Close Small close behind at 41%.

**Walkability varies significantly by scenario.** Small consistently shows the highest walkability (75%) when it remains open, while Skillin hovers at 31% across all scenarios. Closing Kaler keeps walkability patterns closest to the current distribution.

**Capacity utilization is tight in every scenario.** Skillin runs at 91-99% across all models. Several scenarios push schools to within 2-10 students of capacity, leaving almost no buffer for enrollment fluctuations or new residents.

**Drive distances reveal geographic tradeoffs.** The Close Dyer and Close Kaler scenarios produce max drives of 4.55-4.96 mi (Brown and Small absorbing distant zones), while Close Small keeps max drives under 3.15 mi but pushes Brown to near-capacity.

## Points of Agreement (across scenarios)

- Skillin cannot be a closure candidate — the math doesn't work
- Census 2020 data is the enrollment basis (not actual enrollment rolls)
- 0.75 miles is the walkability threshold
- Zone boundaries are algorithmically generated, not hand-drawn
- PreK pilot sites (Dyer and Kaler at 29 students each) affect capacity math

## Gaps

- No modeling of the Grade Centers viewing modes (PreK-1 / 2-4 splits) was captured in this snapshot — only the Community Schools model with Current PreK
- No data on current actual enrollment vs. Census-estimated enrollment
- No cost analysis (operating costs, transportation costs, facility maintenance)
- No demographic or equity analysis (income, race, special needs distribution)
- No timeline or phasing information for any closure scenario
- The tool's Census 2020 basis is now 6 years old — population shifts since then are unaccounted for
