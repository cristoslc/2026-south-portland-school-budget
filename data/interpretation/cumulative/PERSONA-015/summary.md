### Resolved Threads

- **Whether the board would ever name redistricting as live institutional business**: partially resolved — Holman named redistricting explicitly and DeAngelos assigned incoming committee member Jimenez Gildo Paolo to carry it forward; whether it would produce structural action rather than further study remained open (resolved 2025-12-08)
- **Whether the complete absence of budget discussion at the inauguration represented ceremonial restraint or a real information vacuum**: substantially resolved — DeAngelos felt compelled to announce budget season at a city council meeting rather than through prominent school board communication, confirming the vacuum is real; the pattern was doubly confirmed January 6 when the school budget was again relegated to a three-minute closing item (resolved 2025-12-09)
- **Whether structural participation barriers would prevent multilingual and working-class families from engaging in reconfiguration hearings**: confirmed and concretized — the $40+ babysitting testimony provided a specific dollar threshold for participation access; the mayor's non-committal response left the structural barrier standing (resolved/confirmed 2025-12-09; subsequently superseded in character by the ICE enforcement crisis)
- **Where the four new board members stand on equity, the budget, and programming for underserved students**: fully resolved — Richardson: confirmed equity-oriented questioner who names what others won't; Smith: consistent operational impact advocate; Rich: financial conservative whose priority calculus in a budget crisis weighs cost heavily; Feller: politically cautious pragmatist who supports reconfiguration but is watching organized opposition closely (resolved 2026-02-09, building on partial resolutions at 2025-12-10 and 2026-01-12)
- **Whether the Mahoney City Center workshop would consume council fiscal and political bandwidth in the week before the January 22 citizen forum**: confirmed — the January 13 meeting consumed four hours, produced no decision, and revealed a structural connection between enrollment decline and capital investment that was not named by any official; the resolution was worse than the concern (resolved 2026-01-13)
