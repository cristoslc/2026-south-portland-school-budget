### Reactions

Honestly, I walked out of there feeling both energized and completely deflated at the same time. On one hand, it was incredible to hear board member Richardson center diversity and equity in her questions to every single firm. She wasn't tiptoeing around it or using coded language - she straight up said South Portland is "a microcosm of the world" and they need a leader who can navigate that reality. That took courage.

But then watching the other two firms stumble through their responses? NSEC literally admitted they don't even track whether they successfully place candidates of color. How do you improve what you don't measure? And MSMA's answer was basically "we'll put it in the job posting and hope for the best." That's not a strategy, that's wishful thinking.

The thing that really got to me was how the cost conversation played out. Yes, Zeal was expensive, but they were the only firm that showed up with actual diversity on their team and concrete strategies for reaching underserved communities. They talked about going to reservations, meeting people where they are, using multilingual platforms. But when it came down to the vote, two board members basically said "we can't afford equity." I mean, they didn't say it that bluntly, but that's what it was. After everything this district has been through with leadership failures, they're still trying to go cheap on the most important hire they'll make. Some things never change.
