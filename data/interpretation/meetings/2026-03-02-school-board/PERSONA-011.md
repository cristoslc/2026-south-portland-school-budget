### Reactions

OK everyone, just got out of the 3.5 hour budget meeting - here's what you need to know:

The district wanted to close Skillin and bus all those kids across town (their top recommendation was Option 1.7), BUT four board members including the chair basically said hell no. Dowling, Feller, Richardson and DeAngelis all came out against closing Skillin. DeAngelis gave this whole speech about how closing Skillin would mean every family "west of the Dairy Queen" has to bus their kids and how that's basically making the marginalized families bear the burden while the east side keeps their neighborhood schools. She was fired up.

The numbers are brutal though - they're cutting 78 positions district-wide (42 teachers!) and elementary enrollment has dropped 300 kids in 4 years while we ADDED 82 staff positions. That math is not sustainable. They presented 9 different options but honestly most involve either closing a school, reconfiguring into grade bands (pre-K-1 at some schools, 2-4 at others), or both.

Here's the kicker - they did ZERO analysis of academic outcomes by school. Richardson called that out as an "obvious void" and she's right. How are they recommending closing schools without showing us which ones are actually struggling academically? Also that parent survey someone did showed 85% opposed to reconfiguration and only 12% of parents felt they were adequately informed. The communication on this has been garbage.

Bottom line: Skillin is probably safe for now but something big is still coming. They have to present a budget Monday and they're defaulting to some version that closes a different school unless the board gives other direction. This is far from over.
