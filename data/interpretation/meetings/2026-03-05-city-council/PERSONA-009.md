### Reactions

Okay, this is actually a pretty solid meeting for TV. The Walgreens announcement is perfect - concrete, affects everyone, and I can get great b-roll of the site. That's my lede right there, especially since they're calling it the "geographic center" which is a nice visual hook.

But the real story tonight is this fire station fight. Councilor West came loaded for bear with response time data and flood maps, basically accusing the fire chief of putting the station in the wrong spot for the next 75 years. When she said they're only meeting response standards 60% of the time in the eastern part of the city - that's a stat that'll make people pay attention. The chief pushed back hard, and you could feel the tension. That's my second story angle - "Is South Portland's fire station in the wrong place?"

The rental assistance piece is interesting but tricky. The mom bringing her daughter to testify about helping an immigrant family facing eviction because of ICE raids - that's compelling human interest, but it's also politically loaded. The numbers they want ($150K) are specific enough to be actionable, which makes it more than just advocacy. I need to follow up on whether council will actually workshop this on March 10th like they said.

The $87 million bond number is staggering - that's going to be the property tax impact story that writes itself come November. When Councilor Matthews said he's worried voters won't support it given all the other financial pressures, that's the money quote that frames the challenge ahead.
