# Cumulative Summary: Rachel (PERSONA-008)

### Current Understanding

As of April 2, 2026, the South Portland school board has voted 5-1 to close Kaler Elementary and 4-2 to implement Option A full-grade-band reconfiguration, effective September 2026. All PreK–1 students will be reassigned to Dyer and Small; all grades 2–4 students to Brown and Skillin. Board Chair DeAngelos stated at the April 2 meeting that the structural vote is a final action not subject to reversal through the budget process or city council override.

The deficit driving these decisions stands at $8.4 million — larger than the $7.2 million figure cited in public school board presentations through most of the process. Seventy-eight positions have been cut, including 42 teachers, and 72 layoff notices were issued on March 18. The deficit formed during a period of 23% elementary enrollment decline (1,401 to 1,080 students over four years) and persistent financial leadership turnover: six finance directors in five years, repeat audit non-compliance findings, and $400,000 moved from grants to the general fund. State aid covers approximately 20% of actual district costs against an expected 55%, a structural shortfall outside local control.

As of April 2, no attendance boundaries, sibling policies, or transportation routes have been modeled. Thirteen listening sessions are scheduled, but families cannot yet be told where their children will attend school. The union has identified several dozen items requiring meet-and-consult or bargaining before fall; summer work cannot be compelled without pay, creating timeline risk for a September implementation. The administration's stated plan for IEP continuity — to consult educators and parents — was called insufficient from the dais by Member Richardson. The FY27 budget failed 2-5 on March 30; a public hearing is set for April 7, a council vote for May 5, and a referendum for June 9.

Concurrent city fiscal pressures include an $87 million public safety bond confirmed for November 2026, projected sewer fee increases of approximately 22% annually for three years beginning FY27, and an active Hamlin future-use planning process with design consultants already retained.
