# Cumulative Summary: Marcus (PERSONA-004)

### Current Understanding

Marcus understands the South Portland school budget crisis as a multi-layered structural failure that has moved from deliberation into execution. The district faces a structural deficit now stated at $8–$8.4 million, driven by three simultaneous failures: state funding that delivers 20% of the 55% it is statutorily required to provide; labor costs that structurally increase faster than the 6% political ceiling the board set in December; and five years of financial leadership instability — six finance directors, repeat material-weakness audit findings, and legally non-compliant overspending — that has made the district's own budget numbers unreliable inputs. The fund balance is fully depleted. A $1.1 million nutrition program debt threatens the city's credit rating. Eighty to ninety new four-year-old special education students arrive in September with no corresponding revenue.

Seventy-two RIF notices went out on March 18, with Matthews explicitly calling them "the first wave." The operative SPTA cut count is now 40 net positions: 9 are vacancies or known retirements, 11 employees are offered different positions, and 32 certified teachers sit on a recall list with no guaranteed employment. Elementary reconfiguration (Option A, Primary/Intermediate grade-band model) passed 4-2 on March 30. The FY27 superintendent's budget failed 5-2 the same night, with an April 2 follow-up meeting scheduled before the April 7 public hearing and April 14 council workshop. Teacher meet-and-consult began the morning after the board voted on reconfiguration, confirming the administration preempted union impact-bargaining leverage by design.

The city council has never treated the school crisis as a sustained governance priority. A November public safety bond, annual sewer rate increases of ~22%, and an unresolved Mahoney capital project compete for the same voter goodwill needed for the June 9 school budget referendum. Federal immigration enforcement in Red Bank has suppressed school attendance among the most vulnerable students and hollowed out the community organizing base Marcus counted on.
