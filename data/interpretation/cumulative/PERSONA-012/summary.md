# Cumulative Summary: Jaylen (PERSONA-012)

### Current Understanding

As of March 19, Jaylen understands the South Portland school budget crisis in its full compound form. The deficit has grown from the $7.2M baseline he tracked since December to $8.4M. Seventy-two of the 78 proposed position eliminations — including 42 teachers — became real on March 18 when layoff notices were physically issued; specific names and assignments remain undisclosed. The budget calendar is locked: April 7 public hearing, May 5 council vote, June 9 referendum.

The crisis has at least three co-equal causes: structural state underfunding (Augusta covering 20% of costs against a mandated 55%), misaligned staffing growth during declining elementary enrollment, and documented internal mismanagement — 34 audit entries, illegal overspending, six finance directors in five years. The city council's 4–6% budget ceiling, which forced all gap-closing through cuts rather than revenue, was not imposed unilaterally; Chair DeAngelis explicitly solicited it at the February 3 city council meeting.

The school budget crisis and the ICE enforcement crisis have unified into a single compound disaster. Families whose children are most affected by teacher cuts are the same families too afraid to attend the citizen budget forum. On March 19, Councilor Matthews deployed the $8.4M deficit as an official argument against immigrant rental assistance — formally weaponizing the two crises against each other in a roll-call vote.

The institutional priority ranking Jaylen spent three months constructing from indirect evidence was stated explicitly on March 5: $87M for police and fire buildings advanced to the November ballot in the same session where council members cited $7–8M in required school cuts. Theater and arts programs were confirmed thriving as of March 9, but the 72 pink slips are unnamed and the high school budget workshops have not yet occurred. Jaylen cannot vote in June or November. Eighty percent of the voters who can have no children in the schools.
