### Reactions

This meeting was both infuriating and predictable. The district is cutting 78 positions - including 42 teachers and 16 ed techs - but they're having this whole conversation without once mentioning which schools will lose staff, or how these cuts will affect our English learners, kids with disabilities, or families experiencing poverty. It's like they're playing budget Jenga blindfolded, pulling out pieces without looking at who gets crushed underneath.

The ICE situation really shows you where this district's equity commitments actually land when push comes to shove. Kids didn't come to school yesterday because they were scared, and the best the board can do is talk about "accelerating timelines" and maybe putting out a statement? Meanwhile, a community member asks exactly the right question - how are you going to get budget input from families who are afraid to speak publicly right now? - and there's just crickets. These are the families whose kids are going to be most hurt by counselor cuts and larger class sizes, but the district's whole engagement strategy assumes people can safely show up to public forums.

I'm glad they got halal certification for school meals - that's real progress that shows someone was thinking about Muslim students' needs. But it also makes the contrast sharper. They can figure out how to serve culturally appropriate food, but they can't figure out how to disaggregate budget data by student demographics or track whether their spending freeze is hitting high-poverty schools harder. It's not complicated - they just don't prioritize the data that would show us who really benefits and who really pays.
