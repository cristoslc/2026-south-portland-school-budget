# Cumulative Record: Kira (PERSONA-015)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- April 2, 2026 -- 2026-04-02

### Interpretation

She drove four buildings before the meeting. She sat down already emptied, and then Nicholas Boggs started reading Jenna Walsh's statement.

Nearly 60 students. Forty formal behavior plans. Fifty individualized social-emotional support cases. Across Brown, Small, Dyer, and Kaler — her buildings. Jenna has been the person working in the gap Kira sees from her car every day: the one who catches a kid before a teacher breaks, before a parent gets a call, before a referral spirals into a special education qualification. When Richardson pressed on who covers that caseload after the role is gone, the district said BCBAs with a regular-ed funding allocation and instructional strategists. Kira has worked alongside BCBAs for years. She knows what they do and what they don't do. "Bridge space" is not a model. It is a description of a hole.

Then Stacy Lauren stood up and described ending her day in a physical restraint — OT occupied with a behavior student, ed tech one-on-one with someone else, no one left. The OT as emergency backup is a structural layer that does not appear on any staffing chart. When it goes, the math changes in ways that produce the conditions for the next vacancy, not savings. Kira knows this because she is the analogous position in her own cluster: cross-building infrastructure no single building's budget fully accounts for.

What she is taking home alongside Jenna's numbers and Stacy's restraint: Connie's announcement, and what Richardson said next. Staff drove to Augusta. Staff got $300,000, tied to homeless students and economically disadvantaged students — exactly the populations on Kira's caseload. Richardson said it in the chamber with her colleagues present: staff, not directors. That is an institutional claim on new money that no board member has made publicly in this entire cycle.

And then Dr. Prince said the words "multilingual families" and "IEP parents in self-contained settings" in the context of reconfiguration outreach — with dedicated focus group language, not a generic community engagement line. That is the specific gap the Boundaries and Configurations work never closed. Whether those sessions happen and whether the input actually shapes where kids go, or whether this becomes a slide that makes it into a plan no one follows — that is the question she is carrying into Monday's meeting and April 7.

The staff survey goes out tomorrow. She will fill it out very carefully.
