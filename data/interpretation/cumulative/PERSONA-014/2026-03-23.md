# Cumulative Record: Lila (PERSONA-014)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop -- March 23, 2026 -- 2026-03-23

### Interpretation

The answer Lila has been asking since December arrived on a Friday afternoon, two days before this meeting, and it wasn't the answer she expected. New slides appeared on the district website: the school in the administration's formal recommendation wasn't Dyer. It was Kaler.

The relief is real. But relief that arrived forty-eight hours ago, after three months of carrying "CLOSED" next to her school's name, is still fragile at the edges. A Dyer parent stood at the microphone and named it exactly: *I'd be lying if I didn't say I felt relief once it was off the chopping block, but now I also deeply empathize with the Kaler community.* That is precisely what Lila holds. The Kaler families are feeling right now what she felt since December — the recess rumors, the same questions repeated to parents who won't say the real answer, finding out through the news instead of from the school. She knows exactly what that feels like. She was there until Friday.

But the threat didn't disappear. It transformed. Under Option A — the administration's preferred path — Dyer survives as a building, but only for the youngest children: Pre-K through first grade. Lila is in fourth grade. She would leave Dyer regardless. And her sibling, currently in first grade, would be entering second grade next fall — which means her sibling leaves too. The building stays open. They both go somewhere else. The adults told her Dyer was safe. They didn't say that she would still have to go somewhere new.

And then the board chair cried at dinner. DeAngelis — who said in February that reconfiguration is "good for kids" and that children are "flexible" — opened this meeting by sharing that she burst into tears when a friend simply asked how she was doing. She is tired, overwhelmed, sleepless. Lila has resented that dismissal for six weeks. Now the person who made it is breaking too. The adults who seemed most certain are no longer certain about anything.

Five hours. No vote. Reconvene March 30. The waiting continues.
