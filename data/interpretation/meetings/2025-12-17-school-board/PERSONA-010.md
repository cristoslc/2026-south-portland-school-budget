### Reactions

This is the budget crisis story I've been waiting for - not the incremental "schools need more money" piece, but the full reckoning. The numbers are stark: $7.2 million gap, 78 jobs gone, fund balance wiped out. But the real story is how we got here - enrollment down 23% while staffing grew by 82 positions. That disconnect is going to be impossible for taxpayers to ignore.

The human cost is going to hit every school. Forty-two teachers losing jobs means bigger class sizes, eliminated programs, parents scrambling to understand what their kids are losing. I need to get into individual schools and talk to principals about what this actually looks like on the ground. The district will try to frame this as "right-sizing," but families are going to experience it as cuts to what they've come to expect.

What keeps me up is the fund balance being gone. This isn't just about surviving this year's budget - there's literally no cushion left for the unexpected. Health insurance up 12%, state funding stuck at 20% instead of 55%, and we're already at the highest per-pupil cost in the region. I need to help readers understand we're not just looking at one bad year, but a fundamental shift in what South Portland schools can afford to be.
