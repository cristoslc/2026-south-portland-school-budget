# General Budget Briefing
## Current Budget Picture

## Budget Snapshot

South Portland Schools has a proposed FY27 (2026–27) school budget of approximately **$75.6 million**, up from $73.1 million in FY26. As of late March 2026, the budget is in the board workshop phase — the school board is actively reviewing and refining the proposal before it moves to the City Council in April and then to a public vote in June.

The proposal rests on three major structural elements:

1. **Closing one elementary school** to consolidate operations and reduce costs
2. **A 12% reduction in district-wide staffing**
3. **Approximately $1.5 million in non-personnel and other operational cuts**

Despite those reductions, the overall proposed budget is higher than FY26. The main drivers are rising special education costs (proposed at $16.4 million, up from $15 million in FY26 — an increase of roughly $1.36 million, or 9%) and increased facilities expenses ($7.26 million, up from $6.23 million — an increase of roughly $1 million, or 16%). Regular instruction spending is actually proposed to decrease slightly, from $28.5 million to $27.9 million.

The school board had not yet voted on the final proposal as of March 30, 2026.

## What Changed

Based on the March 23, 2026 school board workshop presentation:

- **Two specific options for an elementary school closure** were formally presented to the board for the first time in this format. The administration recommends closing one school but **not decommissioning** the building — instead, it would be leased for up to four years while the district evaluates longer-term options, potentially generating revenue in the interim.
- **If no school is closed**, the administration estimates that an additional 12 to 16 positions would need to be eliminated from across the district to produce a balanced budget.
- **Pre-K programming was adjusted**: the proposal maintains four locally funded Pre-K classrooms and expands access through state-funded (CDS) and Headstart partnership seats, without committing new local dollars to expansion.
- **Non-personnel cuts were itemized**: approximately $703,000 in reductions to building costs, software and hardware, transportation supplies, and instructional supplies; and approximately $808,000 in reductions to previously anticipated transfers and other line items — including a $200,000 payment to the city for an FY26 anticipated deficit that has been deferred to FY28.
- **The tax impact on property owners remained unfinalized** as of March 23. The administration was still meeting with city finance staff, with final figures expected to be posted shortly after that meeting.

## Major Decisions Ahead

1. **Which elementary school to close — or whether to close one at all.** The board must choose between two presented options. This is the most consequential single decision in the current proposal. Choosing no closure would require cutting an additional 12–16 positions.
2. **Middle and high school reductions.** Specific secondary-level cuts were on the March 23 agenda. The scope and detail of those reductions need to be confirmed and voted on.
3. **Adoption of the full FY27 budget by the school board.** A follow-up workshop is scheduled for March 30; the budget is then presented to City Council on April 7.
4. **City Council budget workshop** — scheduled for April 14 — and **City Council vote** — scheduled for May 5, 2026.
5. **Public referendum** — the school budget goes to voters on June 9, 2026.
