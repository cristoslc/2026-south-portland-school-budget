# Cumulative Record: Jess (PERSONA-003)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- April 2, 2026 -- 2026-04-02

### Interpretation

On March 30, the board voted Option A and closed Kaler. I thought that was the moment things became real. April 2 is the moment I understood that "real" and "planned" are not the same thing.

They voted to restructure every elementary school in this district -- splitting pre-K and kindergarten and first grade into one set of buildings, second through fourth into another -- and showed up to the very next meeting with no attendance boundaries, no transportation plan, and no published timeline. A parent stood at the microphone and said the assumption in his neighborhood is that Small kids go to Brown, but it's possible they go to Skillin across town instead. Nobody corrected him. Because nobody knows. Dr. Prince said they're still gathering community input. That is not a plan. That is the absence of a plan, dressed in process language.

I have been tracking the reconfiguration thread since January, when I didn't even know what the word meant. I've watched it move from a concept to nine scenarios to a named school closure to a 4-2 vote. And the whole time I held onto the idea that once the decision was made, the legibility would follow -- I'd be able to say, okay, Mia will go here for pre-K and here for kindergarten and here for second grade. April 2 tells me I was wrong to assume that. The decision came first. The facts for families come later, on a timeline nobody announced.

The behavior specialist revelation hit differently. I've spent months thinking about class sizes and teacher ratios and test scores and reconfiguration. I hadn't thought about the layer underneath all of that -- the person who catches the kids falling through the cracks before they need a formal special education referral. Forty formal behavior plans this year. Fifty more informal interventions. Cut. In the same meeting where the board is acknowledging class sizes may exceed their own policy cap by fall, the one person bridging behavioral needs to classroom teachers just disappears. That is a specific, concrete thing that will affect the environment my daughter walks into.

The $300,000 to possibly $750,000 in new state money felt like air for about two minutes. Then someone said it might be one year only, and then the meeting ended without a budget vote. They restructured an entire school system and still haven't passed the budget to run it.
