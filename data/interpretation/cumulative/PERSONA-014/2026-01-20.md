# Cumulative Record: Lila (PERSONA-014)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- January 20, 2026 -- 2026-01-20

### Interpretation

Lila has spent weeks watching the city council ignore her school. December 4: silence. December 9: silence. January 6: finally, words — a forum on January 22nd, a promise that the adults were starting to pay attention. She held onto that. January 22nd was going to be the first time her school got a real conversation in a city room.

Then they canceled it.

The school board chair tells the city council they had to push the January 22nd forum to February 4th because there's too much "fear, tension, and anxiety in the community." Lila has been anxious about her school for two months. But this is a different kind of fear — kids are staying home from school in Red Bank because ICE agents are at the car wash and the laundromat, and families are too frightened to leave their houses, let alone walk into a public meeting. The police chief gets on and tells adults not to try to help people if something happens, because it's dangerous. Even the people whose job it is to be brave are telling everyone to stay back.

Lila has been tracking the city council's pattern: silence, then vague engagement, then displacement. The Mahoney building swallowed four hours two weeks ago. Now immigration fear swallows the January forum. She's not saying the fear isn't real — it is, and she can hear it — but the meeting about her school keeps getting pushed further away. And every time it moves, the June 9 budget deadline stays fixed.

The mayor is the one moment that lands differently. Mayor Tipton, who in December told a story about families given nine days to leave their homes, is now the one who says it "breaks my heart and makes me so angry" that families are too scared to come to meetings about their kids' schools. She calls it un-American. She sounds like she means it. Lila has watched adults deliver bad news calmly and adults talk in circles for hours. The mayor being visibly upset is something different. Adults that upset are adults who feel something slipping away.

But the meeting still ended with the forum postponed. The mayor's anger didn't change the date. Caring out loud and fixing the problem are not the same thing. Lila knows this now in a way she didn't in December.
