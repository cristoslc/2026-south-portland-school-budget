### Reactions

So I went to this city council goal-setting session last night hoping for something — some signal that the council understood what's at stake before everything gets locked in. And the whole agenda was one line: "Annual Goal-Setting Session, see attachment." That's it. Whatever they actually discussed, whatever goals they set, that attachment isn't in anything I can read. I sat through the whole thing and I genuinely have no idea whether a single person in that room said the words "school budget" or "teaching positions." Forty-two of them. That's the number I can't stop thinking about. Forty-two teachers gone means sections collapsing, electives disappearing, AP courses that just don't run anymore. Every one of those positions is a real class that real kids won't have.

The briefing they distributed has this line I keep turning over: staffing grew by 82 positions while enrollment dropped by 300 students. I know exactly what that's going to do in a public hearing. It's going to sound like proof that the district overhired and teachers need to be the correction. What it doesn't say is *which* 82 positions, or *why* they were added — whether those were state-mandated special education staffing increases, whether those were COVID-era hires that federal relief paid for, whether those were counselors we added because the mental health crisis demanded it. The framing strips all of that out. It just becomes a number that justifies cuts, and that number is going to follow us into every budget hearing this spring.

And the state funding situation is genuinely enraging — we're supposed to be getting 55% of costs covered from Augusta and we're getting 20%. That's not a South Portland problem, that's a legislative problem. But the city council is setting a 6% ceiling, and the school board is doing the math, and nobody in last night's session apparently said anything on the record about going to the State House. So here we are. My colleagues who are in their third or fourth year are nervous. The ones who are newer than that are updating their LinkedIn profiles. I don't blame any of them, and I hate that I can't tell them something different.
