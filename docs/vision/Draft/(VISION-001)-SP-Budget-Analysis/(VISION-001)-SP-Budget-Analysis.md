# South Portland 2026 School Budget Analysis

## Target Audience

South Portland residents who want to understand the proposed 2026 school budget and its implications -- parents (current and incoming), teachers, community advocates, taxpayers, and school board participants. See PERSONA-001 through PERSONA-008 for detailed stakeholder profiles.

## Value Proposition

Make the South Portland school budget legible to the people it affects. The raw budget document is a 100+ page financial instrument designed for compliance, not comprehension. This analysis translates it into a form where a parent can find out what's changing at their child's school, a teacher can see how staffing decisions affect their department, a taxpayer can understand the mil rate impact, and an equity advocate can trace how resources reach underserved students.

## Problem Statement

School budget documents are structurally opaque. They organize information by fund, function code, and object code -- categories that make sense for accounting but not for the questions stakeholders actually ask: "Will my kid's school lose a teacher?" "Why is the budget going up if programs are being cut?" "How does South Portland compare to similar districts?" "Where does the state money go?"

The gap between the published budget and public understanding creates dysfunction: decisions are made without informed input, misinformation fills the void, and trust erodes between the district and the community it serves.

## Existing Landscape

- **District budget documents** -- comprehensive but inaccessible. Published as PDFs with limited narrative context.
- **School board presentations** -- summarize key points but are designed to support the administration's narrative, not to enable independent analysis.
- **Local journalism** (Sentry, Forecaster) -- covers the headline numbers and controversies but lacks the depth or interactivity for sustained engagement.
- **State DOE data** (Maine Education Data Warehouse) -- per-pupil expenditures, staffing ratios, and outcome data exist but require significant effort to connect to local budget decisions.
- **No existing independent analysis** of the South Portland school budget bridges the gap between the raw document and stakeholder questions.

## Build vs. Buy

Tier 3 -- build from scratch. No existing tool or service provides South Portland-specific budget analysis. The value is in the analysis itself (interpretation, contextualization, comparison), not in a reusable platform. The "product" is a set of research artifacts -- documents, visualizations, and data extracts -- not a software application.

## Maintenance Budget

Low ongoing effort. This is a point-in-time analysis of the 2026 proposed budget. May be updated if the budget is amended during the approval process (typically March-June). No commitment to cover future budget years, though the methodology and data structures could be reused.

## Success Metrics

- Stakeholders represented by the persona set can find answers to their primary questions in the analysis outputs
- Analysis is cited or shared in at least one public forum (school board meeting, local news, community group)
- The author (cristos) develops a working understanding of the budget sufficient to participate meaningfully in the public process

## Non-Goals

- Building a software application or interactive dashboard (analysis artifacts are static documents and data files)
- Covering the full municipal budget (city side) -- this is school department only
- Advocating for a specific budget outcome -- the analysis is descriptive and comparative, not prescriptive
- Replacing or competing with official district communications
