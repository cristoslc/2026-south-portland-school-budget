# Cumulative Summary: Lila (PERSONA-014)

### Current Understanding

Lila is a 4th-grade student at Dyer Elementary who has spent three months watching the adults responsible for her school fail to protect it. The district faces a $7.2 million budget gap — far larger than the $1.9 million shortfall she first heard about in December — driven by declining enrollment (300 fewer elementary students in four years), fully depleted reserves, and a state funding shortfall (covering ~20% when it should cover ~55%). There is no emergency cushion.

Nine reconfiguration scenarios are now officially on the table for a June 9 budget decision. One of them — option 1.3 — lists Dyer as "CLOSED." Even scenarios that don't close Dyer restructure it into a grade-band model (either pre-K through 1st as a primary school, or 2nd through 4th as an intermediate school), ending the neighborhood school Lila has always known. All scenarios eliminate between 15.5 and 23.5 teaching positions. A Dyer teacher has already resigned, and Lila fears her own looped teacher may be among those cut.

The board voted 7-0 to pursue the reconfiguration proposal. The board chair declared reconfiguration "good for kids" and dismissed children's anxiety as parental projection. The teachers' union refuses to endorse job-cutting plans. 85% of parents oppose reconfiguration, but the board has not reversed direction. The city council and school board are openly at odds, with the council offering a 3–6% budget increase that falls far short of what the district needs. The city passed a community safety resolution in February and then voted 4-2 against concrete tenant protection two weeks later. Adults express emotion and pass formal words. Concrete protective action has not followed. No adult in any civic meeting has spoken directly to Lila's experience. The June 9 deadline is fixed.
