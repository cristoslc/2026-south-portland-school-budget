### Reactions

So my mom came home and she was all quiet and I could tell. I asked her right away — is our school closing? And she said NO. Dyer is NOT closing. It's Kaler. And I felt this huge relief, like finally I could breathe. But also kind of bad? Because the Kaler kids are probably feeling exactly how I felt last week when Emma told me at recess that our school was closing. That was the worst. And now those kids feel like that. One Kaler mom said she found out from the NEWS, not even from the school. I didn't know that and it made me feel sad for them.

But then I asked if everything was okay now and she said not totally. She tried to explain this reconfiguration thing. Under one option, Dyer would only be for the really little kids — like kindergarten and first grade. So even though Dyer STAYS, my [sibling] would have to leave next year because they'd be in second grade and second graders would go somewhere else. I kept asking WHY and she said something about equity and using resources better and I don't totally know what that means. It doesn't make sense to me that you keep the school but then some of the kids still have to go somewhere new anyway.

The other thing is they talked for FIVE WHOLE HOURS and still didn't vote on anything. They're going back Monday. And I asked if my teacher still has her job — she's the same one I had last year, we do looping — and my mom said she didn't know and kind of looked away. That's the part I keep thinking about. Not even which school closes anymore. Just: is SHE going to be there in September?
