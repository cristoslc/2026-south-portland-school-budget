# Cumulative Record: Kira (PERSONA-015)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- January 20, 2026 -- 2026-01-20

### Interpretation

She has been documenting the participation barrier since December 4. She named it as structural. She watched it confirmed by two women paying forty dollars each in babysitting on December 9. She watched Montgomery name it with clinical authority at city council on January 13 and watched the council proceed for two hours as if she hadn't spoken. She has been carrying the open thread — will her families be able to get to the January 22 forum? — for two weeks. And on January 20, the forum disappears.

D'Angelo's announcement lands differently than she expected. It is not a failure of the participation apparatus — it is the apparatus acknowledging its own collapse in real time. The board chair walking into a city council meeting to say *we cannot in good conscience ask families to come out right now* is, on one reading, the most honest thing an official has said about the participation barrier since December 4. On the other reading, it is the confirmation of what she has been dreading: the families who most need to be in that room are the families currently hiding. And postponing to February 4 does not solve that. ICE does not have a February 4 deadline.

Pedro Vasquez had cell phone video of agents at the Red Bank laundromat and car wash. She spent the day at three buildings knowing some of her kids were missing and not yet knowing why. Now she knows why, and it is worse than a logistical barrier. The participation problem she has been tracking is no longer about babysitting costs or bus schedules or evening scheduling. It is about whether federal enforcement activity makes the physical act of engaging with local democracy too dangerous for the families with the most at stake in the budget decisions ahead.

Chief Ahern's statement — SPPD will not interfere with lawful ICE activity — is honest in a way she finds both clarifying and insufficient. The 2017 resolution is real and she will share it. Steinberg's warrant information is actionable and she will share that too. Mayor Tipton naming the forum postponement as "deeply disturbing" and putting it into the record is something. But the $7.2 million gap still exists. The forty-two teachers and sixteen ed techs are still on the cut list. The Kaler counselor vacancy is still unresolved. The MTSS wait lists at two of her buildings are still full. And the community engagement process on which all of those decisions are supposed to rest has just been suspended because the families most affected cannot safely attend.

She is going to be up for a while.
