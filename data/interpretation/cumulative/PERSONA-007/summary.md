### Resolved Threads

- **Will Mayor Tipton and the reconstituted council engage substantively with school budget pressures when they become unavoidable?**: Yes — city council provided concrete tax increase guidance of 3–6% (most at 4–6%) at the February 10 joint budget guidance workshop (resolved 2026-02-10)
- **Does DeAngelos's public silence on the budget crisis at inauguration reflect a leadership communication posture that will persist?**: No — at the February 3 city council meeting, DeAngelos publicly named the 61%/80% structural reality and explicitly requested "clear guidance during a very difficult budget year," marking a shift from inaugural caution to active public advocacy (resolved 2026-02-03)
- **Will the January 22nd public budget forum proceed as announced on January 6th?**: No — postponed to February 4 due to ICE enforcement activity creating community fear in the Red Bank neighborhood (resolved 2026-01-20)
- **Will city council provide a specific tax increase target rather than continuing to defer with "do your best" guidance?**: Yes — 3–6% range with most members gravitating toward 4–6%; Assistant Superintendent Prince clarified on the record that 3% would require 50+ additional staff cuts beyond those already planned (resolved 2026-02-10)
- **Will the Mahoney referendum actually slip past the July-August 2026 council deadline for November ballot placement?**: Yes, ultimately — after an interim resolution in February in the opposite direction (council re-targeted November 2026), the council definitively settled on 2027 at the March 19 meeting with city manager confirmation and no dissent (first resolved as "No" 2026-02-17; final resolution 2026-03-19)
- **Will Councilor West's fire station objections force the November 2026 public safety bond back into full scope uncertainty?**: No — the council voted unanimously at March 19 to purchase the Walgreens site for the new police station and the November 2026 public safety bond referendum remains on schedule (resolved 2026-03-19)
- **How did budget projections fail so dramatically between November and the December 8 crisis disclosure?**: Finance Director Ketchem publicly named three structural root causes at the March 23 workshop: enrollment-staffing misalignment accelerated by COVID relief funding propping up positions without sustainable backing, the absence of a minimum fund balance threshold policy, and chronic leadership instability with seven finance directors in six years (resolved 2026-03-23)
- **Can any reconfiguration be properly implemented by September 2026 given that no redistricting plan existed at the March 2 workshop?**: Yes — administration confirmed at the March 23 workshop that both Option A (primary/intermediate grade band reconfiguration) and Option B (full K–4 grade bands functioning as a bridge year to full reconfiguration in 2027–28) are operationally achievable for fall 2026 (resolved 2026-03-23)
- **What happens if the school side runs a deficit and requires city fund balance intervention?**: Confirmed as the operational default — the district will draw on the city's undesignated fund balance for any unanticipated FY27 costs and repay through a future tax increase; no alternative backstop exists in the FY27 budget (resolved 2026-03-23)
