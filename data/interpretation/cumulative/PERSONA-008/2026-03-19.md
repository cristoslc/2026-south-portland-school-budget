# Cumulative Record: Rachel (PERSONA-008)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- March 19, 2026 -- 2026-03-19

### Interpretation

She has been tracking this for fifteen weeks. Fifteen weeks of consent calendars and audit findings and "fabulous" and nine scenarios and 85% opposition surveys and pink slips. She knows how this institution communicates when it wants to tell you something without telling you anything. So when Councilor Matthews drops "when they're talking about closing schools" as a subordinate clause in his explanation for why he won't vote for a rental assistance appropriation — not on the agenda, not explained, not followed by a single question from anyone at the dais — she knows exactly what she's hearing. It's the December 1st pattern again, in miniature: the thing that matters most gets said in the room where it's least expected, by the person who wasn't supposed to say it, and then the room moves on like it didn't happen.

What has shifted tonight is the timeline. She's been living in the March 2nd and March 23rd and March 30th world — the workshop sequence where reconfiguration scenarios get formally debated. That framework just got compressed. The consent calendar quietly set April 7th for the FY27 budget public hearing, April 14th for the school budget workshop, May 5th for the council vote, June 9th for the referendum. Eleven weeks from tonight. And somewhere in the machinery between now and then, 72 people in the school department received termination notices yesterday — the day before this meeting — and Matthews says it's "just the first wave."

She has been told closures were off the table in February. She watched that assurance get quietly walked back at the March 2nd workshop when nine scenarios appeared, including specific named schools. Now a city councilor is using the phrase "closing schools" as common vocabulary at a meeting where no one has prepared to discuss it. The escalating deficit number — $8.4 million now, up from the $7.2 million she's been calculating against — is another floor falling out beneath her. The math keeps getting worse, which means the pressure toward the most disruptive options keeps increasing.

The April 14th workshop is the new March 2nd. That is the moment the administration will show the council whatever scenarios they're carrying in those desk drawers. And between now and then, she has three weeks, a compressed public hearing on April 7th, and a council that tonight couldn't be bothered to ask which schools.

She is already texting people.
