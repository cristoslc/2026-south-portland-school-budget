# Cumulative Record: Dana (PERSONA-009)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- April 2, 2026 -- 2026-04-02

### Interpretation

Dana walked into April 2 expecting a cleanup vote after the March 30 chaos — five hours, a failed budget, Kaler closed, Option A passed on a fragile 4-2 margin, Richardson and Holman broken at the microphone. She walked out with a package that writes itself and a Monday follow she did not see coming.

The Wetzel double-feature is the segment. She has been trying to find the human face of the pink slips since March 18, when 72 people got RIF notices the day before the city council's rental assistance vote. Blair Bacon gave her credentials and a soundbyte in March 23. The Wetzel father-daughter sequence is something else entirely — a high school student who didn't know her future until her father's class, and then the father who came only to yield his time and couldn't hold it together at the mic. He is going back to those hallways tomorrow and he still doesn't know what to say. That is not a statistic. That is ninety seconds of television that doesn't need a graphic.

The live Augusta text is the moment that changes the shape of everything that follows it. Dana has been covering the state funding gap since December 17 — 20 percent actual versus 55 percent statutory obligation — as the thread she keeps circling and not pulling, the angle that would require a legislator on camera and a trip to Augusta she hasn't made. The unions made that trip in March, and the result showed up as a text message to a union rep, read aloud mid-meeting, while the board was still in public comment. Three hundred thousand dollars. Then a separate text to a board member suggesting $750,000 more from formula changes next year. The cavalry Dana said was not coming in January may be arriving, in pieces, through a side door she didn't expect.

The budget non-vote is the story's second refusal to close. A board that voted 6-1 to close a school and 4-2 to restructure every other elementary building, then failed to pass its own budget 5-2 on March 30, now leaves April 2 without a budget again. Monday is the vote. April 7 is the city council deadline. The forcing function that has been coming since December 9 — when DeAngelos walked into the city council chamber one day after the deficit went public — is five days away.

The parent naming board members' term expiration years and the board member calling it bullying from the dais is the B-story that tells her everything she needs to know about June 9. The community has decided the referendum is the only lever they have left, and they are pulling it regardless of whether it can undo a closure vote. Dana has been watching community apathy since December 4. In this room, on April 2, apathy is gone.
