### Reactions

So we've got a board that just voted to spend $30,000 they don't have on a consultant search while they're literally asking teachers and staff to sacrifice for budget cuts. That's the lede right there - the hypocrisy angle writes itself. Board Chair DeAngelo made this incredible comment about the empty room, basically calling out the community for not showing up, then used that as justification for spending whatever they want. "We're the only people sitting in this room," she said. That's pure gold.

The diversity questions were interesting but honestly kind of repetitive - every board member asked basically the same thing about finding non-white candidates. Board Member Richardson was really driving that bus. But the real story is this philosophical split about whether the community gets a voice in picking their next superintendent. The expensive firm basically said "trust us, we'll handle everything, the board should decide alone." The cheaper local option said "we'll help you organize community input." Guess which one they picked?

What kills me is they're facing $7.2 million in cuts - that's like 78 jobs - but they can't find $8,000 in the budget for a search consultant so they go with the $30,000 option instead. Member Holman kept trying to be the voice of reason about costs, but she got steamrolled. The 5-2 vote tells you everything about this board's priorities. This is definitely worth a crew for the follow-up story.
