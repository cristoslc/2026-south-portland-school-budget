# Cumulative Record: Priya (PERSONA-005)
## Meeting: School Board Special Budget Meeting -- March 30, 2026 -- 2026-03-30

### Interpretation

The meeting opened with the data I have been demanding since December. A 22% BIPOC/white math gap. A 37% gap for multilingual learners. A 32% gap for students with IEPs. The assistant superintendent said the words I have been waiting months to hear someone say at a podium: "our white students performed substantially above our BIPOC students." And then the meeting moved on. No budget line was tied back to those numbers. Nobody asked which of the positions being eliminated would widen each gap. The data appeared to justify the reconfiguration vote, not to interrogate the cuts that would fund it. Equity as graphic design.

Then came the DEI Director — again. I documented the coordinator demotion at March 23 and called it a stripping of institutional authority. March 30 introduced a further revision I had not anticipated: downgrade the role past coordinator to "instructional strategist" in the teacher unit, for zero additional savings. Board member Holman said exactly what needed to be said: the incumbent has already taken a $20,000+ pay cut, she is the district's only BIPOC person in central office leadership, and the further demotion saves nothing. The board chair acknowledged in the same meeting that the seven-person body governing these schools has no BIPOC representation. The district is eliminating its only BIPOC central office leader to respond to pressure about the director count — and doing it for free.

The middle school ESOL teacher named the methodological lie at the heart of the reconfiguration data: NWEA math scores administered to students on their first day in the country measure language proficiency, not mathematical knowledge. She asked for ACCESS data, seal of biliteracy rates, measures that might actually show what multilingual students can do. Nobody responded. Meredith Diamond presented equity initiative data showing Kaler up 12.83% and Skillin up 9.79% after one year, narrowing the gap with Small from 25.28% to 7.76% — gains arriving precisely at the schools being closed or disrupted. Nobody responded to that either.

The board voted 4-2 to pass Option A reconfiguration and failed to pass the budget. The only general education behavioral strategist in the district — whose elimination Richardson says "essentially forces kids to special ed" — remains on the cut list. The reconfiguration has been voted through while the supports that would make it equitable are still unresolved. March 2 offered the first evidence that naming racial impact directly could change a structural outcome. March 30 shows what the subsequent course correction looks like: the DEI role further dismantled for no savings, the data that could hold anyone accountable deployed as a slideshow and dropped, and a vote taken before the equity infrastructure needed to execute it is anywhere in sight.
