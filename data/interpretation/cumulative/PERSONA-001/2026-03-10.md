# Cumulative Record: Maria (PERSONA-001)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- March 10, 2026 -- 2026-03-10

### Interpretation

Maria has been carrying the ICE story since January 20, when DeAngelis stood at a city council podium and calmly announced the January 22 forum was canceled because immigrant families were too frightened to attend. She tracked it through February 17, when Julia Edwards' white son was noticing empty desks and Zenya Pantos described a specific South Portland child kept home for over a week. What March 10 adds — and it lands hard — is the elementary school driveway. A neighbor reported seeing ICE agents near an elementary school during the January surge. A parent lost work because their children were too scared to attend class. This is not a civic forum that got postponed. This is a seven-year-old afraid to walk into the same building Maria walks her own daughter into every morning.

DeAngelis's public clarification — no ICE activity occurred inside any South Portland school — is the kind of reassurance Maria wants to believe and cannot fully accept. The fear is real regardless of what the legal boundary of "inside" means. She has known since December 4 that participation barriers shape which voices reach decision-makers. She has known since January 20 that federal enforcement can silence those voices entirely. She knows now that the fear reaches all the way to the school driveway, and that specific children did not go to school because of it.

What breaks through the exhaustion is Councilor Scott. Maria has been running the enrollment math in her head since December 17 — 1,080 students, down 23% in four years, the same decline that is driving the 42 teacher cuts and the nine reconfiguration options she has spent weeks dissecting. Scott says what Maria has been thinking: those 80 families are students. If they leave South Portland, the enrollment crisis gets worse. Keeping them housed is not charity — it is fiscally responsible. It is the first time a city councilor has linked the school budget math to the immigrant community displacement math in a way that Maria recognizes as true, and it is the first time she has felt politically understood rather than politically managed at one of these meetings.

The $100,000 Project HOME vote is not final yet, but the direction is clear. Maria will take it. She has been watching this council absorb information without changing course all winter; tonight, something moved.

And then the school budget recedes back into the background — Councilor Matthews citing the school board chair, DeAngelis saying she is "very sensitive" to finances — and nobody names a teacher or a program or a school. April 14. April 28. May 12. She has the dates. That is what she leaves with.
