### Reactions

I've got to say, after months of asking for real numbers, Abigail and the team finally delivered. That matrix with nine scenarios, FTE breakdowns, and five-year projections is exactly what we needed to have an informed discussion. You can see why option 1.7 scores highest financially - closing Skillin saves almost $20 million over five years versus $16 million for the Kaler/Dyer closure. The capital cost difference is huge because of that HVAC system.

But here's what bothers me - they did all this detailed modeling for reconfiguration options but left the no-change scenario as "more analysis needed." That's not objective analysis, that's steering the conversation. Joe said it would save "at least $2 million" so why not model it properly? And when the superintendent gets up there and basically says anything other than his preferred options is "kicking the problem down the road," it stops feeling like we're getting neutral staff recommendations.

The good news is the board pushed back hard. Four members saying they won't close Skillin sends a clear message, and I appreciated the questions about transportation costs and implementation timeline. We're talking about completely reshuffling a thousand kids with less than six months to plan, and they have no idea what the actual bus routes would cost. That's the kind of detail that matters when you're making an $8 million decision.
