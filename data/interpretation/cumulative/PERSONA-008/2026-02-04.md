# Cumulative Record: Rachel (PERSONA-008)
## Meeting: Budget Forum -- February 4, 2026 -- 2026-02-04

### Interpretation

I went into this forum braced for bad news and what I got was worse: specifics. The reconfiguration isn't some abstract cost-saving exercise -- they're talking about completely reorganizing the elementary schools into grade bands. My daughter wouldn't go to her neighborhood school anymore; she'd be assigned to a K-1 building and then shipped to a different building for grades 2-4. Every relationship she's built, every morning routine we've established, gone. And the administration presented this like it was a reasonable, measured approach.

There is one genuinely important piece of good news buried in here: the administration explicitly said school closures are not on the current proposal list because the district needs buildings for pre-K expansion mandates. That's a real constraint that protects the buildings themselves, even if the grade-band reconfiguration would still disrupt everything inside them. I'm cautiously updating my assessment -- the buildings may stay open, but the schools as my kids know them could still be fundamentally transformed.

What destroyed my confidence in this process was learning that they haven't even modeled the transportation impacts. They want to move hundreds of kids to different buildings but they don't know what that means for bus routes, ride times, or costs? My daughter's 10-minute bus ride could become 30 minutes and they literally can't tell me. That's not planning; that's guessing. And even if they execute this entire disruption perfectly, it only closes $4 million of a $7.2 million gap. So after they've upended our kids' lives, they still need another $3 million in cuts. What comes next?

The compressed timeline is the final insult. They want to make these decisions by March for September implementation. That gives parents essentially no time to understand, organize, and respond. Combined with the simultaneous superintendent search -- meaning we don't even know who'll be leading these changes -- it feels like they're deliberately rushing past the people who'll be most affected.
