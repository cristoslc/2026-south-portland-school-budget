# Cumulative Summary: Priya (PERSONA-005)

### Current Understanding

Priya understands South Portland's FY27 budget crisis as the convergence of three compounding failures: state structural underfunding (20% actual coverage against a 55% statutory obligation), years of internal grant mismanagement that drained equity-designated funds for ELL students and students with disabilities, and a political culture at both governing bodies that consistently subordinates structural equity to administrative convenience and market-rate priorities. The $7.2–$8.4 million deficit has produced 72 real pink slips, with 42 teachers and 16 educational technicians eliminated — the positions most directly supporting students with IEPs and multilingual learners.

The elementary reconfiguration has twice recommended closing the district's most diverse schools (Skillin, then Kayler) under cover of equity language. The March 23 board meeting adjourned with a direct Title VI civil rights question unanswered — the first time Priya's documented pattern of equity failure has acquired a specific legal exposure. The DEI Director role is being downgraded to coordinator. ESOL co-teaching has been discontinued as "not required." Ten of 127 community questions had been answered on the district's own platform, and multilingual families were largely absent from the five-hour proceeding that will determine their children's schools.

At the city level, Priya has catalogued four council failure modes on equity: erasure (testimony not registered in deliberation), inversion (equity language deployed to defeat equity claims), weaponization (one equity crisis used to foreclose action on another), and equity-as-termination (equity research invoked to close a program rather than improve it). The $100,000 rental assistance passed March 19 through Project Home but is constrained by redundant structural exclusions: institutional contact creating enforcement visibility, and a state ruling pre-defining fear-based income loss as ineligible for General Assistance. The TIF portfolio shelters assessed value from state aid formulas while consuming public resources. The HRC operates at $2,500. The priority ordering is now a documented ratio: $87 million for public safety infrastructure passed unanimously; $100,000 for immigrant households passed 6-1 after months of resistance.
