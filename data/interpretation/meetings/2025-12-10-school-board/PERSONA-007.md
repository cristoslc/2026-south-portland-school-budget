### Reactions

Well, that was quite a meeting. Four hours to select a search firm, and we ended up with the most expensive option when we literally have zero in the budget for this. I appreciate that the board wanted to do their homework - three presentations plus the Alma proposal they looked at separately - but the final decision has me worried.

The Zeal presentation was compelling, I'll give them that. Their focus on getting the board to consensus, the diverse consulting team, the deep community engagement - it all sounds great on paper. But we're talking about $30,000 when we're cutting 78 positions and asking taxpayers to absorb an 18-19% tax increase just to maintain services. The optics are terrible. How do we tell the community we're cutting music programs and laying off ed techs, then turn around and spend premium dollars on consultants because we can't manage to work together as a board?

What really gets me is that we dismissed MSMA so quickly. Eric was honest that their capacity is stretched thin, but we're already members, the mentoring is included, and their Maine-specific expertise is exactly what we need. Dave from NSEC struck me as very competent too - practical, experienced, significantly less expensive. But the majority got caught up in the idea that paying more would somehow manufacture the board unity we should be developing ourselves through better governance practices. I hope those reference checks Richardson and DeAngelo are doing turn up gold, because we're going to need to justify this choice when the community finds out what we spent.
