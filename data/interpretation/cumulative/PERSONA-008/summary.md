# Cumulative Summary: Rachel (PERSONA-008)

### Current Understanding

South Portland's school district faces a confirmed $8.4 million structural deficit — larger than the $7.2 million figure Rachel was given in December — requiring at minimum 78 position cuts including 42 teachers. Elementary enrollment has collapsed 23% in four years (1,401 to 1,080 students), a decline the administration attributes to demographics but which Rachel understands is partly produced by ICE enforcement keeping immigrant children home. Nine formal reconfiguration scenarios, including closures of specific schools (Kaler, Dyer, Brown, Small, Skillin) and grade-band splits forcing every elementary student to change buildings at second grade, are on the administrative record as of March 2nd. Four board members publicly opposed closing Skillin, but Skillin's $3 million HVAC need provides a fiscal rationale to reverse that position. Seventy-two pink slips were issued March 18th — explicitly described by Councilor Matthews as "just the first wave." The decision is no longer being debated inside the school board; it has been absorbed as institutional consensus at the mayoral and city council levels, with "closing schools" now casual council vocabulary.

The fiscal picture extends beyond the school budget. An $87 million public safety bond is heading to the November ballot, drawing on the same property tax base as school increases. Sewer fees are rising approximately 22% per year. State aid covers only 20% of actual costs against an expected 55%, a $25 million structural gap outside local control. The administration's chronic mismanagement — six finance directors in five years, repeat audit non-compliance, $400,000 overspent — helped build this crisis, though officials continue to frame it as inherited.

Rachel has repositioned her organizing target. The March 23rd and March 30th school board workshops remain, but the April 7th public hearing and April 14th school budget workshop at city council are now the critical intervention points. The council votes May 5th; the referendum is June 9th.
