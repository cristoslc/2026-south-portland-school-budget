### Reactions

Look, this was one of those meetings where you could feel the strain. They're trying to juggle four major building projects while the school district is cutting $8 million from its budget, and it shows. The Walgreens announcement was the cleanest decision they made all night - Mayor clearly wanted some good news to lead with, and that location makes sense for a police station.

But the fire station debate got messy fast. Natalie West came armed with response time data showing the eastern part of the city doesn't meet standards, and she's not wrong about the flooding risks. Problem is, Chief Silverstein made it pretty clear there aren't great alternative sites, and the political reality is voters might reject an $87 million bond if they think the city's being indecisive. The compromise they reached feels shaky - yes to the current site, but nobody seemed thrilled about it.

The library piece is what really bothers me, though. Kevin Davis laid out numbers that should terrify any councilor - 27 visitors per hour, infrastructure so old that replacing a door lock costs $300. When he said "I've been here 25 years, this is the second time" consultants have told them what they need and nothing happened, you could hear the frustration. They keep saying "we'll deal with the library later," but later never comes. That's the story voters need to understand before they're asked to spend $87 million on other buildings.
