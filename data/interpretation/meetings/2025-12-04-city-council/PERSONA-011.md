### Reactions

Okay here's what happened tonight - not a huge meeting but a few things parents should know about.

The big vote was creating a formal budget process for city committees. They passed it 6-1 (Walker voted no). Basically committees can now request up to $2500/year for operating stuff like training, and they have to submit detailed requests during budget season for bigger projects. Walker was worried about the Human Rights Commission losing funding and the council budget looking bloated. Honestly makes sense given how tight the school budget is - city's probably facing similar pressure.

The Zoom public comment thing came up again. Walker pushed for it for accessibility but Pride shut it down hard - apparently they had people "zoom bombing" meetings with racist stuff during COVID and legally they can't stop it because of First Amendment. They talked about registration and email comments but nothing's changing. Super frustrating for people who can't get to 6:30pm meetings.

One thing that affects us - they moved the deadline for getting items on the agenda from Wednesday noon to Tuesday 4:30pm. With all the Monday holidays we get, this could mean stuff takes longer to get addressed. Worth knowing if you're trying to get something in front of council.

Tree lighting tomorrow night 4:30-6:30 at Mill Creek if your kids want to see Santa. Dress warm - it's gonna be brutal out there.
