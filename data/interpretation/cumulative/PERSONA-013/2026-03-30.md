# Cumulative Record: Amira (PERSONA-013)
## Meeting: School Board Special Budget Meeting -- March 30, 2026 -- 2026-03-30

### Interpretation

The percussion ed tech was saved last year because people showed up. I've been holding onto that since March 23 — holding it like proof that showing up matters. Thirty-two emails. Multiple speakers at the microphone. And the district's answer was a dedicated slide in the presentation explaining why they're cutting the position anyway. They didn't just not listen. They prepared their counter-argument in advance. That's different from not hearing. That's hearing and deciding the answer is no.

Then Matthew Emory got up. A student at the middle school — maybe in some of the same hallways I walk through — and he said what I would have said if I knew how. Most kids aren't interested in math and science, he said. They're interested in band and art and singing. He asked directly: who is going to teach the drums? That's the fourth time since December I've encountered a voice my age in these proceedings — Jules reading a poem, the 10-year-old's question read by someone else, Davidson named the inequity and got sent home with his backpack, and now Matthew standing at the microphone alone, saying exactly the right thing. Board member Holman said she was scared about losing middle school related arts because it's a "formative time." That's the most any adult in one of these rooms has sounded like they understand what actually happens inside school.

Then Richardson said they already cut PE. Past tense. Gone. A teacher who ran extracurriculars for kids who couldn't afford outside sports — already gone. I walked through middle school all year not knowing something had disappeared.

Kayler is closing. Six to one. The most diverse elementary in the district. And then the budget didn't pass — five to two against — so another meeting Thursday, and I still don't know if more things are getting cut on top of what was already proposed, or whether the percussion ed tech somehow survives through the failure or disappears in a version two round of cuts I haven't seen yet.

My mom always notices when people who look like us disappear from positions of authority. One board member said the DEI director is the only BIPOC person in leadership, and they're eliminating her for essentially no savings. Nobody answered that.
