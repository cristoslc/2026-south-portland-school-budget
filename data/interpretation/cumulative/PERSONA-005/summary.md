### Resolved Threads

- **Whether Chair DeAngelos's equity rhetoric would translate to equity-centered structural decisions**: Answered — celebration of diversity does not reliably produce structural equity protection; the Skillin closure recommendation confirmed the gap between inauguration rhetoric and operational reality (resolved 2025-12-01, confirmed by 2026-03-02 and 2026-03-09)
- **Whether Mayor Tipton's housing equity commitments would produce concrete anti-displacement protections and income targeting**: Answered — the One 70 Ocean TIF vote established that when the structural mechanism for affordability was available and the leverage moment arrived, the council chose the market-rate path without dissent (resolved 2025-12-09)
- **Whether the 82 additional staff positions since enrollment decline served equity-supporting roles or administrative growth**: Answered — special education mandates and ELL services drove much of the staffing complexity, though full composition data was never provided (resolved 2026-01-15)
- **Whether the budget season would proceed without equity analysis of which student populations bear the cost of cuts**: Answered — confirmed; both governing bodies advanced through the goal-setting phase without any public equity framing applied to the elimination list (resolved 2026-01-15)
- **Whether the federal anti-immigrant rhetoric visible in December would translate into measurable attendance and safety impacts for South Portland's immigrant students**: Answered — Red Bank ICE enforcement and the budget forum postponement confirmed that federal climate became a direct, measurable barrier to both school attendance and civic participation (resolved 2026-01-20)
- **Whether the eviction moratorium and rent relief requests would receive any formal council response**: Answered — Ordinance 17 voted down 4-2; the dissenting majority proposed the GA fund as an alternative while the city manager had already named the structural barrier making formal mechanisms unavailable to the most at-risk families (resolved 2026-02-17)
- **Whether the city manager's March 5 redirection of immigrant rental assistance toward nonprofit intermediaries would produce a mechanism accounting for the institutional-contact catch-22**: Partially answered — the Project Home mechanism with a no-disclosure commitment addresses the institutional contact dimension; the state GA ruling excluding fear-based income loss from eligibility establishes a second, independent structural barrier the mechanism cannot resolve (resolved 2026-03-10)
- **Whether Walker's $168,000 position or some lower figure would carry to the March 19 vote**: Answered — the city manager synthesized at approximately $100,000; the March 19 vote confirmed 6-1, leaving approximately 32 of 80 identified households unserved (resolved 2026-03-10)
- **Whether Project Home's emergency housing fund would be replenished or replaced by any public action before running out**: Answered — $100,000 appropriated 6-1 with Project Home as the intermediary; approximately 50 households reached through direct landlord payments (resolved 2026-03-19)
- **Whether alternative reconfiguration options following the Skillin closure rejection would address confirmed de facto segregation**: Answered — Option A described by district administrators as capable of producing more heterogeneous schools; Option B explicitly acknowledged as perpetuating demographic stratification; Option A passed 4-2 on March 30, though without equity implementation guardrails and ahead of a failed budget vote (partially resolved 2026-03-23, completed 2026-03-30)
- **Whether Option B would be eliminated from board consideration given the district's own acknowledgment that it perpetuates demographic stratification**: Answered — Option A passed 4-2; Option B did not advance (resolved 2026-03-30)
- **Whether the DEI Director position downgrade would be contested by the board**: Answered — Holman and Richardson named the BIPOC leader argument on the record; the demotion was nonetheless retained in the failed budget proposal and further reduced to instructional strategist in the teacher unit for zero additional savings; no vote to restore the position was taken (resolved 2026-03-30)
