### Reactions

This meeting was a real eye-opener about how the state is basically taking control away from our local government. The biggest thing that got my attention was this housing law - LD 1829 - that's going to force the city to allow up to four units on pretty much any lot in town that's got sewer access. And that's everywhere in South Portland. They're saying it might only affect 10% of properties, but that could still mean hundreds of single-family homes turning into apartment buildings over the next few years.

What really ticks me off is they're also taking away our right to have these developments go through the planning board. Used to be, if someone wanted to build something that would affect the neighborhood, we'd get a public hearing where residents could voice concerns. Now it's just going to be some city staff person making the decision behind closed doors. They kept saying the "review standards are the same," but without public input, who's looking out for the neighbors who have to live with these changes?

The one bright spot was hearing about that Suncor tank farm situation. If we could actually get them to clean up that site and develop it properly, we're talking about $4 million a year in new tax revenue - that's more than double what the Maine Mall brings in. That's the kind of development we should be focusing on, not forcing density into established neighborhoods. But knowing how these things go, we'll probably end up with both - more apartments everywhere AND still dealing with that eyesore tank farm.
