# Cumulative Record: Kira (PERSONA-015)
## Meeting: School Board Special Budget Meeting -- March 30, 2026 -- 2026-03-30

### Interpretation

Four months ago she drove home from a board meeting where Holman named redistricting in closing comments — the first institutional signal she'd had in years. Tonight she drives home from the meeting where it actually happened. Option A passed, 4-2. Grade bands. Primary and Intermediate. The board voted to restructure four elementary schools, and DeAngelos read the rationale into the official record: MTSS access inconsistency, specialist inefficiency, ESOL students isolated without programming peers, gifted students unable to find each other across buildings. That is her caseload. Those are the conditions she has been documenting from her car since before anyone in that room knew her name. Prince opened with achievement gap data — up to 25% performance differences between elementary buildings — not as a budget justification but as a student equity outcome argument. That is the argument she has been making since December 8, and tonight it was slide 5.

But she cannot hold the vote without holding the budget, and the budget failed 5-2 — only Smith and Risch voting yes. Thursday now carries everything: the implementation funding, the staffing structure, and the MTSS cut list that Richardson named in the chamber as the specific reason she could not support reconfiguration that simultaneously dismantles the intervention infrastructure it depends on. Four MTSS positions. The sole general ed behavior strategist — who also serves as the district's only safety care trainer. The support staff union president said "the one" and Kira heard it the way she hears everything from inside those buildings: that is a person. That is a caseload. That is not a line on a spreadsheet.

Richardson voting no on Option A is the thing she will be sitting with. Her most reliable equity ally, the one who named ICE fear when others wouldn't and pressed every superintendent search firm on diversity placements, voted against the structural change Kira has spent four months fighting for — and she did it on grounds Kira cannot dispute without lying to herself: you cannot vote for reconfiguration and simultaneously cut the support structure that makes it an equity investment rather than an efficiency play. That is not a betrayal of the argument. That may be the most operationally honest version of it. Kira agrees with it, she needed the vote to pass anyway, and Thursday is the meeting where both of those truths collide.
