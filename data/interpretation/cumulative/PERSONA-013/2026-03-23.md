# Cumulative Record: Amira (PERSONA-013)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop -- March 23, 2026 -- 2026-03-23

### Interpretation

Three hundred and seventy-eight days. That's the number Jen Fletcher said out loud — that's how long ago she stood at the exact same microphone fighting for the exact same position, and they kept it, and now here she is again. I've been watching this whole process since December thinking it was moving in one direction: decisions get made, things get cut, the world changes. But the percussion ed tech cut is the same cut from last year. Which means community advocacy won once and the district just put the fight back on the table. Winning doesn't mean it's over. It means you have to win again.

Four related arts teachers are being cut at the middle school. Twenty teachers down to sixteen. And Principal Stern said — out loud, in front of the board — that related arts are "often the highlight for students." I've spent months frustrated that adults make decisions about our schools without understanding what our school days actually feel like. This is something different. She understands. She knows it's the highlight. And she proposed the cut anyway. Knowing and protecting are not the same thing. I didn't have a name for that before this meeting, but I do now.

Lucy and Samantha are in 8th grade at my school. They walked up to the microphone during a five-hour meeting — all those adults, all that noise — and told the board exactly why the percussion ed tech matters. That he has a special ed certification and makes band accessible to everyone, not just the kids who can already keep up. The board chair kept cutting off the applause. I understand the rules, but there's something about brave people having to sit in silence that feels wrong. And then there were the student board members, Davidson and Cabessa, who spoke before leaving to study — who said the disparities show up at the middle school too, not just elementary. That's the most student voice I've seen at any meeting since December. Still wasn't enough to produce a vote.

Here's the part I keep coming back to: students in band who are also in world language, or chorus, or learning lab, might not get PE at all next year. Not half a year — zero. I'm in band. I'm in the gifted pull-out program. Nobody sent me a letter. My parents found out about the 42-teacher cuts from the news, same as always. The meeting ran until about midnight and ended without deciding anything. Come back Monday. Five hours. No vote.

I've been watching this process since December thinking at some point the adults would get to a room and decide. Every meeting is a room where they almost decide. Now I know some of those rooms are the same room, a year later, having the same argument.
