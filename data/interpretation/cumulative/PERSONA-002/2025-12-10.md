# Cumulative Record: David (PERSONA-002)
## Meeting: School Board Workshop -- December 10, 2025 -- 2025-12-10

### Interpretation

Just two days after learning about our $1.9 million deficit and depleted reserves, the board decides to spend $30,000+ on a superintendent search when they literally have zero dollars budgeted for it. This is exactly the kind of financial decision-making that got us into this mess in the first place. I sat through three vendor presentations with costs ranging from $8K to over $30K, and despite facing cuts to 78 staff positions, the board chose the most expensive option because they liked the "consensus-building" approach.

The data analyst in me was particularly frustrated by the quality issues with Zeal's methodology - only 32 participants in their community survey sample, which George Rich correctly identified as statistically meaningless. MSMA offered a straightforward service at $8K with existing membership benefits, and NSEC presented a solid middle-ground approach, but the board got seduced by Zeal's polished presentation and premium features we frankly can't afford.

What's most concerning is the pattern this reveals. We're in crisis mode with spending freezes and staff cuts, yet the board prioritizes feel-good processes over fiscal discipline. At least they added reference checks as a contingency after the vote, though that should have happened before committing to the expense. This decision reinforces my fears from Sunday's meeting - we have new board members talking about analytical rigor, but when it comes to actual spending decisions, emotion still trumps data. The disconnect between our financial reality and our spending behavior is getting worse, not better.
