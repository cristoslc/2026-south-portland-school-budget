# Cumulative Record: Marcus (PERSONA-004)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- February 9, 2026 -- 2026-02-09

### Interpretation

Two and a half hours of this meeting and the ground keeps shifting under our feet. The biggest bombshell was Abigail Chen confirming that all FY26 budget scenarios now end in deficit -- we're not just facing a crisis next year, we're already drowning this year. Every projection is underwater. That changes the calculation entirely: the spending freeze isn't a precaution, it's triage for a patient that's already coding.

The elementary reconfiguration got its unanimous vote to move forward, and Chair DeAngelis tipped her hand completely -- calling it "fabulous" and dismissing parent concerns by saying "kids are far more flexible than parents." The fix is in. They've made up their minds and the March 2 workshop is theater, not deliberation. But Sarah Sergey from the union landed an important punch: she reminded the board that every one of these changes requires impact bargaining with all staff unions before implementation. They can vote on all the studies they want, but they can't just reorganize schools without negotiating the working conditions changes. That's our leverage, and we need to use it.

The early retirement buyout idea from Feller is the first proposal I've heard that doesn't involve firing people. If 20 of the 41 eligible staff took enhanced packages at roughly $50,000 in savings each, that's over a million dollars without a single pink slip. That's the kind of creative thinking that's been completely absent from the administration's approach. And Feller's admission that the 6% ceiling might need to move is significant -- at least one board member is being honest about the math. You cannot close a $7 million gap without being "draconian," as he put it, unless you're willing to talk about revenue.

The community school expansion to the high school is a head-scratcher in this context. Good programming, sure, but we're bleeding money and taking on more responsibilities with "no guaranteed funding" -- the vote explicitly stated they weren't dedicating any money to it. Another unfunded mandate piled onto a system that's already cracking.
