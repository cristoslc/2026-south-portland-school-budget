# Cumulative Record: Dana (PERSONA-009)
## Meeting: School Board Workshop -- December 17, 2025

### Interpretation

Two days after the $30,000 consultant debacle, the district hands Dana the biggest story of the season so far: 78 positions on the chopping block, including 42 teachers, 16 ed techs, and 14 support staff. This is the lede she has been building toward since the December 8 deficit reveal. The $7.2 million budget gap she first heard about on December 10 now has faces attached to it -- and faces are what make television.

The data dump in this workshop gives Dana the analytical backbone for the multi-part series she has been envisioning. Elementary enrollment collapsing 23% in four years while the district added 82 staff positions is the single most damning statistic she has encountered in this story -- it is visually intuitive, factually devastating, and immediately understandable to a general audience. Combined with the per-pupil cost of $26,651 (highest among comparable districts), it transforms the narrative from "schools face budget cuts" to "district spent wildly while losing students and now taxpayers and workers pay the price." That is a fundamentally different and more compelling story.

The state funding angle -- covering only 20% of costs against a 55% statutory obligation -- adds a layer of complexity Dana will use selectively. It provides context that prevents the story from being purely a local incompetence narrative, but it also means there is no cavalry coming. The fund balance is gone, the state is not stepping up, and the board set a 6% tax cap when the math requires 18-19%. The impossible arithmetic is now Dana's through-line: every subsequent meeting will be measured against whether the board is narrowing or widening the gap between what they need and what they are willing to ask for.

Dana is already planning her next moves: find one of the 42 teachers slated for cuts and get them on camera, get reaction from a board member willing to say whether this is sustainable, and shoot B-roll at the middle school's still-broken athletic field as a visual metaphor for institutional dysfunction.
