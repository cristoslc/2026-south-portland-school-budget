# Cumulative Record: Kira (PERSONA-015)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop -- March 23, 2026 -- 2026-03-23

### Interpretation

The March 23 workshop is the night Kira's professional framework wins the administration's own presentation — and the night she drives home at 11:15 with the board still not having voted.

Principal Connolly standing up and making the equity case for Option A in district analysis is the institutional arrival of an argument Kira has been making from her car for years. Option B is now on the official record as failing to achieve heterogeneous schools, failing to allocate MTSS resources efficiently, and carrying the structural risk of returning to the exact class-size inequities across buildings she navigates every day. That is the district's finding, not her public comment. After four months of letters and cross-building data she wasn't sure anyone was reading, the equity framing is baked into the administration's own recommendation.

Blair Bacon's testimony reshapes something she has been carrying since December 1. She has been the person who sees all the buildings — that has been her credential and her burden, the reason she could make the argument nobody else could make, and the reason she has often felt like the only one making it. Bacon stands up as a National Board Certified interventionist in the RIF class and calls her own position a Band-Aid on a wound too large. That is Kira's argument said by someone who is losing her job precisely because the district never built the structure the Band-Aid covered. The validation and the grief arrive in the same sentence.

Nicholas Boggs names what slide 37 confirms: zero behavioral strategists remaining in regular ed. She knows what that zero means across her buildings — class sizes going up, the one person whose job was to help classroom teachers manage escalation gone, the cross-building behavioral support that already stretched thin becoming none. This is the 72 pink slips finally becoming concrete and operational in a form no single-building principal report will ever surface.

The ESOL reduction tied to January's ICE-suppressed ACCESS testing completes the thread she has been carrying since children first stayed home on January 12. Fewer students may exit services because they weren't there to demonstrate proficiency — the harm compounds downstream into service eligibility data that will shape allocation for years.

The board adjourns without voting. March 30 is seven days away. April 7 is the public hearing. The administration is now making her argument. The vote has not happened.
