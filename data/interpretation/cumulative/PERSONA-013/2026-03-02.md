# Cumulative Record: Amira (PERSONA-013)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop I -- March 2, 2026 -- 2026-03-02

### Interpretation

This is the meeting where it all got really specific and really scary. They didn't just talk about reconfiguration in general anymore - they presented NINE different options for closing and reorganizing elementary schools, and the leadership team's top pick was closing Skillin and turning the remaining four schools into grade-band buildings. Skillin is where a lot of the Somali families and other families without cars send their kids. Closing it and busing those kindergarteners across the whole city is just wrong.

The reason Skillin is the top choice? Money. It saves the most over 5 years - $19.5 million - mainly because they'd avoid a $3 million HVAC replacement. The educational benefits scored the SAME as other options. So they're picking the option that hurts the most vulnerable families because it saves money on a heating system. Not because it's better for kids.

But here's the thing that gave me hope: at least four board members said they won't support closing Skillin. The board chair gave this whole passionate speech about how it would hurt the most marginalized students. Finally, adults standing up for the kids who'd be hurt the most. I don't know if that'll be enough to stop it, but at least someone sees what I see.

The public comment section was overwhelming. Dozens of parents came to say this was wrong, and so many of them said the same thing: nobody told them this was happening. They found out from neighbors. Sound familiar? That's literally how I find out about everything - not from the school, not from the district, but from overhearing people or reading things after the fact. The whole district has a communication problem and it starts with not telling the people affected by decisions that decisions are being made.

The budget has to be adopted by June 9th for a referendum, which means they're trying to plan these massive changes in just a few months. Kids would have to switch schools TWICE during elementary - once for PreK-1 and again for grades 2-4. The $8 million gap and 78 position cuts are still the backdrop for everything. My band program, my gifted classes - nothing feels safe.
