Okay so that was... frustrating. I sat through two hours of ceremonies tonight expecting SOMEONE to mention that our schools are about to lose 78 positions, including 42 teachers. Instead we got beautiful speeches about welcoming immigrant families and creating vibrant downtown spaces - which I support! - but it felt like watching people rearrange deck chairs while the ship is sinking.

The new school board chair's speech was honestly moving. She talked about this student whose parents let him live with them his senior year so he wouldn't have to switch schools, and all these connections she's made with former students. But I kept thinking - how are we going to maintain those relationships when class sizes balloon because we're cutting staff? How do we welcome new families when we're eliminating the very programs that help kids succeed?

And four new school board members just got sworn in who will be voting on these massive cuts in the next few months, and not ONE person mentioned the budget crisis. I need to know where these people stand. Are they going to fight for our elementary programs or just rubber-stamp whatever the superintendent recommends? I'm already planning to be at every single budget meeting because clearly if parents don't show up, they'll just pretend the problem doesn't exist.
