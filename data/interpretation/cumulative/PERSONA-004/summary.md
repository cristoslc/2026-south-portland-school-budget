# Cumulative Summary: Marcus (PERSONA-004)

### Current Understanding

Marcus is a South Portland teacher who has tracked the FY27 budget crisis from inauguration night through April 2. His current picture: the district is functionally insolvent, with a fully depleted fund balance, a confirmed $8–8.4 million structural gap, 40 net SPTA positions eliminated (down from 42 after two director reclassifications that saved only $20,000–$30,000 each), 32 certified teachers on a recall list with no guaranteed return, and elementary reconfiguration adopted 4-2 before teacher consultation began.

The budget itself failed 5-2 on March 30 and remains unadopted. A deferred board vote, $300,000 in new state funding secured by union lobbying in Augusta, an unconfirmed $750,000 in potential FY28 EPS formula changes, and Richardson's on-the-record statement that new money should go to teacher positions — not directors — have reopened a narrow window before the April 7 public hearing and April 14 council workshop.

Marcus understands the structural trap as permanent: labor costs outpace the 6% tax ceiling annually, meaning FY28 faces the same collision regardless of what is cut this cycle. The city is running two additional fiscal asks — a Mahoney renovation and an $87M public safety bond — plus sewer rate increases of ~22% annually for three years, all drawing from the same voter households being asked to support the June 9 school referendum.

City council has been an attentional and fiscal competitor throughout, never an ally. ICE enforcement has suppressed multilingual family participation and degraded classroom attendance. The April 7 public hearing and April 14 budget workshop are the last meaningful intervention points.
