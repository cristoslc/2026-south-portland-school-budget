### Reactions

Honestly, this meeting was a masterclass in how not to manage public finances. We're four months into the fiscal year and suddenly discover we're looking at nearly a $2 million deficit because nobody properly calculated what the teacher contract would actually cost us? That's not a rounding error - that's a fundamental failure in budgeting. I've been watching these meetings for two years, and the pattern is always the same: rosy projections followed by crisis management.

The scenarios they presented tell the real story. Scenario A requires cutting 90% of all non-personnel spending - that's basically impossible unless you want schools without paper, books, or basic supplies. Scenario C shows what happens if we keep operating normally, which is financial disaster. The fact that we have zero fund balance left means we're one unexpected expense away from having to borrow money or make emergency cuts mid-year.

What gives me some hope is the new board members, especially Tyler Smith talking about developing actual decision-making processes. Maybe we'll finally get some fiscal discipline instead of just reacting to crises. But man, the $46K in donations is great and all, but it's a drop in the bucket compared to our structural problems. We need to figure out how we got into this mess before we can fix it.
