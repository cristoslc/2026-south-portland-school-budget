# Cumulative Record: Amira (PERSONA-013)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Forum -- February 4, 2026 -- 2026-02-04

### Interpretation

The budget forum I was cautiously hopeful about? It turned out to be terrifying. They're not just cutting teachers anymore - they're talking about completely reorganizing how elementary schools work. Instead of going to your neighborhood school, little kids would be sorted by grade into different buildings. A 2nd grader and their 4th grade sister might not even be at the same school. And class sizes would jump from 16 to 22 or 23 kids. I already feel like I can't get help when there are too many kids in my class, and it's only going to get worse.

The really disturbing part is that even if they do ALL of these big scary changes - reorganize elementary schools, change middle and high school schedules, shift operations around - they'd STILL need to cut over $3 million more. So all this disruption to kids' lives might not even be enough. They'll have to find more things to cut on top of everything else. My gifted program? Band going to part-time like they've been threatening? Who knows.

I'm starting to understand how we got here though. Enrollment is at a 10-year low, but the district actually HIRED more staff while fewer kids were enrolling. They called it a "math problem" which... yeah, no kidding. It IS a math problem, and now kids like me have to pay for it with bigger classes and schools getting rearranged. Oh, and health insurance costs might go up 12% instead of 8%, which means even less money for actual school stuff.

The middle school schedule change got postponed at least, because they need traffic studies and permits. But they can reorganize all the elementary schools without those? It doesn't make sense. Nobody asked us kids what we think even though we're the ones who have to live with whatever they decide. That hasn't changed one bit.
