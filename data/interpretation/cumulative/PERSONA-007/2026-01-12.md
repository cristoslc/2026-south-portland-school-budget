# Cumulative Record: Linda (PERSONA-007)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- January 12, 2026 -- 2026-01-12

### Interpretation

The finance committee report tonight confirmed what I've been seeing in the numbers: we're not just heading for a deficit, we're practically guaranteed one. Having 92% of funds committed or expended when we're only at the halfway mark of the fiscal year, even with a spending freeze in place for two months, tells the whole story. Claire's presentation was stark but accurate, and I keep thinking about how this is going to land at the community forum on the 22nd.

The most constructive moment came from an unexpected source -- Sarah Gay, the teachers' union president, essentially told us to stop asking for input in a vacuum and give people something concrete to respond to. She's absolutely right. We can't expect meaningful feedback if we're not sharing our actual thinking and proposals. Her point about getting a specific tax increase target from the city council is strategically important too, though I doubt Tim and the council want to give us that kind of political cover right now. We need council to own a number, not just tell us to "do our best."

The immigration enforcement situation is adding an operational and political complication I hadn't fully anticipated. Students are missing school, attendance is dropping, and the board is being asked to make public statements and accelerate staff training. Eleni Richardson is right that we need to move faster on protocols, but I'm also thinking about how this plays in a validation vote. We need to be seen as responsive to families without getting dragged into federal politics when we're already asking taxpayers for more money.

Meanwhile, we formalized the Zeal superintendent search contract with the additional advertising and background check costs. The cumulative spending keeps climbing on a decision I was skeptical about from the start. And the staff resignations -- two high school teachers plus seven retirements -- are creating vacancies that we can't automatically backfill given the budget situation. Member Feller was right to confirm that point publicly.
