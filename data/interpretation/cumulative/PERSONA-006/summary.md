# Cumulative Summary: Tom (PERSONA-006)

### Current Understanding

Tom is a South Portland homeowner on a fixed income who has tracked the school budget crisis from its first public disclosure through March 2026. His picture of the crisis is now fully stacked and sequenced across four simultaneous cost pressures.

The school deficit has grown from a $1.9M emergency in December to a $7.2M structural gap and now to $8.4M, with 72 pink slips issued March 18. Seventy-eight positions are proposed for cuts across 42 teachers, 16 ed techs, 14 facilities/food/transport workers, 2 administrators, and 4 non-bargaining staff — 12% of the workforce. School taxes are 61% of his property tax bill. Per-pupil cost of $26,651 is the highest among comparable districts. Enrollment fell 23% while the district added 82 positions. The state covers 20% of costs against a 55% statutory obligation. Two consecutive years of budget non-compliance and six finance directors in five years have reframed the crisis for Tom from structural bad luck to operational dysfunction. The nutrition fund ran hidden deficits of $715K in FY25 and a projected $1.1M in FY26.

Three additional simultaneous pressures now have dollar figures and timelines: the Pearl Street sewer upgrade ($51.7M, revenue bonds requiring no referendum, 22% annual increases for three years raising the average monthly bill from ~$56 to ~$90); the police and fire bond ($87M combined, November 2026 referendum); and the Mahoney City Center (stripped to $70-76M, deferred to 2027, still without a binding council ceiling). The city itself confirmed no capital planning or preventive maintenance existed until five years ago, extending the dysfunction pattern from the school board to the full municipal apparatus.

Tom's central unanswered demand: someone produce the combined per-household annual cost of all four pressures. No public body has done it.
