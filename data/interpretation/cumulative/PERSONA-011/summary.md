# Cumulative Summary: Meg (PERSONA-011)

### Current Understanding

Meg has tracked the South Portland school budget crisis from its first public signal — the December 8 spending freeze — through four months of escalating revelations. As of April 2, the picture is this: the district has run deficits in FY24, FY25, and is projecting one in FY26, with no fund balance remaining and approximately $360K in electricity overruns still unresolved. The structural gap that started as a vague $1.9M risk is now a confirmed $7.2M–$8.4M hole depending on the source, requiring 78 position eliminations — 42 teachers, 32 on a recall list, plus all seven lunch aides and a behavioral strategist who serves nearly 60 students. Pink slips went out March 18. The state covers roughly 20% of costs against a 55% statutory obligation, though a mid-meeting public comment on April 2 introduced a possible $300K–$750K in new state funding that has not been reconciled.

The board has voted to close Kaler School and adopt Option A reconfiguration (PreK–1 at Dyer and Small; grades 2–4 at Brown and Skillin), but the FY27 budget itself failed 5-2 on March 30 and has not been adopted. The April 7 council presentation is the hard deadline. If the board does not act before then, the superintendent's budget — not the board's — goes to council. No attendance boundaries have been published. The 6% tax ceiling ($257/year on a $514,000 home) remains the proposed framework but is not yet adopted. The school funding crisis is now actively shaping city council votes on unrelated agenda items, and FY28 is already projected to be structurally harder than FY27.
