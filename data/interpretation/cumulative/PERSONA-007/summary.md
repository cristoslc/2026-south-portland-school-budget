# Cumulative Summary: Linda (PERSONA-007)

### Current Understanding

The district faces a $7.2M structural deficit requiring 78 position cuts — including 42 teachers — to hold a 6% tax increase ceiling, but the board is not unified on that ceiling: Member Feller has publicly broken from it, and city council guidance only narrows the range to 3–6%. The school fund balance is functionally zero ($739K with $850K budgeted for use), FY26 is already running at a deficit, and the $1.1M nutrition fund shortfall is now integrated into projections. Health insurance is jumping from 8% to 12% without confirmed figures until late March.

The three "big ideas" deliver roughly $4M in savings, leaving more than $3M in cuts still unidentified. The board has rejected the administration's recommended Skillin closure on equity grounds and is converging on closing Kaler/Dyer or Small instead, but no redistricting plan exists and implementation by September 2026 is in doubt. A community survey showed only 12% of parents felt adequately informed — a massive engagement failure that compounds the validation risk.

The fiscal environment around the June 9 referendum is severe and now largely calculable: sewer rates are doubling over three years (22% annual increases, average bill from $56 to $112/month), a $87M public safety bond is targeting the November 2026 ballot, and the 50-cent state subsidy capture rate means rising local valuations automatically reduce what Augusta sends. ICE enforcement is displacing South Portland families, which reduces enrollment and per-pupil state subsidy — a budget pipeline risk no one in either governance room has named publicly. The full cumulative homeowner impact has not been assembled in a single public document. The April 7 hearing is two weeks away, and Matthews' "72 pink slips" framing is already in City Council minutes.
