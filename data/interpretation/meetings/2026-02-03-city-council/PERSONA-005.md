### Reactions

Look, I've been to enough of these meetings to know the pattern, but tonight was particularly stark. We spent nearly four hours on audit details and corporate tax incentives - finding creative ways to give Texas Instruments millions more in tax breaks over the next 20 years - and then at 9:25 PM we finally got to the resolution about families who are literally sitting in dark houses because they're afraid to turn on the lights.

The audit findings were infuriating but predictable. Six finance directors in five years at the school department, $400,000 in grant money mismanaged - these are the targeted funds that are supposed to serve our most vulnerable students, our English learners, our kids with disabilities. And where's the accountability? Where's the plan to ensure this doesn't keep happening? Instead we get corporate consultants explaining how to "shelter" property value from state education funding formulas so we can get more subsidies while giving tax breaks to profitable companies.

But what really broke my heart was seeing community members - people who've been delivering groceries and walking kids to bus stops and providing translation services - having to wait until almost 10 PM to speak about families facing eviction. And then watching the city manager explain they only have $2 million in "discretionary" funds after all the corporate incentives and capital projects are accounted for. We can structure complex 50-year financing deals for business parks but suddenly discover fiscal limitations when it comes to preventing homelessness. The priorities couldn't be clearer.
