# Cumulative Summary: Amira (PERSONA-013)

### Current Understanding

Amira understands the South Portland school budget crisis as a $8.4 million gap requiring 78 position eliminations — including 42 teachers, 16 ed techs, special education staff, the Memorial ESOL teacher, the DEI director, four middle school related arts teachers, and the percussion ed tech who supports band students in grades 5–12. She knows this gap has multiple causes: state funding covering only ~20% of what the state is obligated to pay (55%); enrollment declining 23% over four years while the district added 82 positions; a revolving finance department (six directors in five years) and 34 audit errors that misallocated $400,000 in grant funds; and a depleted fund balance with no emergency cushion remaining.

She understands the budget as a multi-party competition drawing on the same taxpayers: school needs, the $194 million Mahoney municipal building project, the $87 million police and fire bond, and corporate tax abatement (Texas Instruments TIF extension). Schools represent 61% of South Portland's property tax bill.

Kayler Elementary — the district's most diverse school, approximately 45% BIPOC and 30–35% multilingual learners — has been authorized for closure on a 6–1 vote. Elementary reconfiguration into Primary (PreK–1) and Intermediate (grades 2–4) buildings has been adopted 4–2. The budget itself has failed to pass twice and a third meeting is scheduled.

The ICE enforcement surge beginning in January produced school absences, a cancelled community forum, children too scared to attend class, families facing eviction, and a $100,000 rental assistance appropriation passed 6–1 in March. Councilor Matthews explicitly named immigrant family relief and school budget needs as competing for the same fund, without student input on that choice.

Amira's gifted program is confirmed safe at 3.0 FTEs. Band is not. The April 2 meeting produced no votes.
