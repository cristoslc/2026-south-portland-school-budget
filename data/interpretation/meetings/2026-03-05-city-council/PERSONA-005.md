### Reactions

God, this meeting was such a perfect microcosm of how this city operates. Two residents come with receipts - 140 signatures asking for emergency rental assistance for families devastated by ICE raids - and the city manager's like "well, maybe we could give money to nonprofits instead." Meanwhile they're ready to drop $87 million on police and fire without blinking.

The library piece really got to me. Kevin had the numbers that should shame everyone on that council. Books stored seven feet off the ground because they've crammed twice as many shelves into the same footprint. Kids can't reach materials, there's no study space, they can't even add exterior lighting because the electrical system is from 1966. But sure, let's spend $31.5 million rebuilding a fire station in a flood zone that doesn't even serve the eastern part of the city properly.

And speaking of that - did you catch how Councilor West had the data showing the eastern neighborhoods don't meet response time standards? The fire chief basically brushed it off, but those are the areas where more Black and immigrant families live. It's the same pattern over and over - underinvest in services for the most marginalized communities while spending big on enforcement infrastructure. At least West tried to push back, but she was completely outnumbered.
