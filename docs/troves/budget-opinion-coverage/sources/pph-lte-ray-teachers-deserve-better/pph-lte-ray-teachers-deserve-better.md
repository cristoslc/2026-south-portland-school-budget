# South Portland's teachers and school staff deserve better | Letter

**Author:** Margaret Ray, South Portland
**Published:** March 28, 2026
**Source:** Portland Press Herald, Letters section

## Summary

Ray, identifying herself as a parent of three children in South Portland public schools, opens with an apology to educators and staff on behalf of the parent community.

Her argument proceeds in three beats:

1. **The scale of the problem.** The district is facing "another large budget shortfall" requiring elimination of more than 70 positions. She describes this as "beyond maddening."

2. **Accountability failure.** As a taxpayer, she states there has been "little public accountability as to how we got here" and "little clarity as to who is responsible for the mismanagement" that produced these staffing cuts. The word "mismanagement" is explicit and directed at district leadership/governance, not at teachers.

3. **Solidarity with staff.** She affirms that parents "value" teachers and staff, and that parents share the anger and hurt about the process. The framing separates educators (victims) from district leadership (accountable parties).

She closes by directing a call to action at "the government of South Portland" to better manage school budgets in the future.

## Key direct quotations (under 15 words each)

- "beyond maddening that our district is facing another large budget shortfall"
- "little public accountability as to how we got here"
- "little clarity as to who is responsible for the mismanagement"
- "we are angry and hurt about this process, too"
- "do better in the future with managing school budgets"

## Factual claims

- More than 70 positions are being cut from South Portland schools.
- Ray self-identifies as a parent of three children in the district.
- Ray self-identifies as a South Portland taxpayer.
- This is characterized as a recurring ("another") large shortfall, implying prior budget crises.
