### Interpretation

This meeting hits me with the classic South Portland contradiction — we're actually crushing it in some areas while completely failing at basic stuff. Our chess team is literally at the top of the state leaderboard and both cross country teams made states, which shows what happens when programs get support. But then they drop this bomb about a spending freeze on classroom supplies, books, even laminating paper. Like, teachers can't get basic materials now? That's going to hit every single class I'm in.

The ELO program presentation blew my mind though. This girl Destiny is getting certified as a doula in high school and doing actual research with college coaches. There are kids earning real money through internships with Maine Health. This is exactly the kind of hands-on learning that would actually prepare us for life after graduation, not just more standardized test prep. But of course, the one teacher running this incredible program is completely overwhelmed because she's also doing JMG coordination and running the BSU. Our student board member straight up called it out — Ms. Carlisle is drowning doing three jobs alone.

The middle school field situation is just embarrassing. Four years into a brand new building and they still don't have a working athletic field? Board members were literally saying "wait a minute" because even they can't believe the incompetence. Meanwhile, our student rep keeps asking what we can do to help with supply drives, which shows students want to step up, but it's also depressing that we're crowdsourcing basic school needs now. This meeting shows me that when South Portland invests in programs properly, amazing things happen. But the budget crisis means we're about to see what happens when they don't.
