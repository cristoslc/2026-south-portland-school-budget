# Cumulative Summary: Jess (PERSONA-003)

### Current Understanding

Jess entered this process in December 2025 as a parent with a toddler, evaluating South Portland as a place to raise a family. Four months later, she has watched the district move from an abstract financial warning to executed layoffs, a voted school closure, and a reconfiguration model that will require her daughter to change buildings between first and second grade — while the FY27 budget that funds those structural decisions has not yet passed.

The core fiscal picture: a deficit that has grown from $1.9 million to at least $8.4 million, 72 layoff notices delivered March 18, 13 elementary teacher positions eliminated, a depleted fund balance, and a state funding gap (actual coverage ~20% against a 55% mandate) with no near-term legislative remedy. Seven finance directors in six years have compounded a structural enrollment decline driven partly by demographic shift, partly by ICE enforcement activity that has kept families home and threatens continued departure.

Option A reconfiguration (PreK–1 in two buildings, grades 2–4 in three) passed 4–2 on March 30. Kaler Elementary is voted closed. Boundary and building assignment details for incoming families are not available and no timeline has been given.

Pre-K expansion — once Jess's anchor — has softened materially: district-operated seats are flat at 64, growth depends on external partnerships with eligibility restrictions, a Pre-K teacher FTE is being cut in FY27, and placement legibility for incoming families does not yet exist.

The city council has operated largely on a parallel track throughout. The household financial picture includes a school tax increase, confirmed sewer rate increases (~22%/year for three years), and an unresolved Mahoney capital bond — none of which has been presented as a combined figure to residents. Family-facing library and early childhood infrastructure was removed from the Mahoney project scope with no replacement plan.

Jess has decided to stay in South Portland for now, anchored by early childhood programming and equity-driven board resistance to the worst reconfiguration outcomes — but she is watching a system whose FY28 pressures are already enumerated and whose practical details for incoming families remain unanswered.
