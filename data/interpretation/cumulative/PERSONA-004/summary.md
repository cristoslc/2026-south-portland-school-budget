# Cumulative Summary: Marcus (PERSONA-004)

### Current Understanding

Marcus understands the South Portland school budget crisis as a structural trap that predates any single decision and will outlast this year's cuts. The district faces a confirmed $8–8.4 million gap driven by three simultaneous failures: state funding delivering roughly 20% of the 55% it owes under its own formula, a tax increase ceiling set at 6% by city council guidance, and a finance function that operated through six directors in five years and produced repeat audit findings of material weakness and legal non-compliance. The fund balance is fully depleted. Seventy-two RIF notices went out on March 18, with more described as a likely second wave. The operative SPTA elimination count stands at 40 net certified positions, of which 32 teachers are on a recall list with no guaranteed employment. Elementary reconfiguration under Option A has been formally adopted by a 4-2 board vote. The FY27 budget itself has not been adopted: a 5-2 rejection on March 30 was followed by a unanimous deferral on April 2 pending city council guidance, with unconfirmed additional state funding of approximately $300,000 in play. The Finance Director has confirmed that labor costs structurally outpace the 6% ceiling annually, meaning the same deficit gap is mathematically projected to recur in FY28 regardless of what is cut this cycle. Meet-and-consult obligations generated by reconfiguration number in the dozens and have not been costed into the budget. Director-level administrative restructuring has been confirmed to produce approximately $20,000–$30,000 in net savings — insufficient to restore a single teaching position. The April 7 public hearing and April 14 council workshop are the remaining intervention points before a May 5 council vote and June 9 referendum.
