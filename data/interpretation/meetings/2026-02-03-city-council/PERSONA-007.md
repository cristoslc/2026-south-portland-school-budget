### Reactions

God, where do I even start with this audit. Six finance directors in five years? That explains a lot, but it doesn't excuse it. We're talking about a repeat material weakness, 34 audit entries - Tim said he likes to see that under 10 - and $400,000 in grant money that had to be moved because it was either ineligible or overspent. This is exactly why the council has fiscal oversight responsibility, but it's maddening to see the same problems cycle through year after year. Joe seems confident about the new finance director, and I hope he's right, but we've heard that before.

The TIF extension conversation was fascinating but honestly gave me a headache. Josh did his best with that presentation, but by the time we got to captured assessed value and tax shift benefits and whether we're combining three districts or just extending one, I think half the room was lost. The bottom line seems sound - Texas Instruments generates serious revenue, the new state law lets us extend for transit development, and we reduce their incentive significantly. But Natalie's right that we need to be strategic about the mall area if we're going to make this work.

What really stuck with me was Rosemary coming to the podium as school board chair and basically saying "you can't hide from this budget." Sixty-one percent of the property tax bill, 80% of our budget in salaries and benefits, and she's asking for "clear guidance" in February. Translation: we're going to have to make some really hard calls, and soon. The audit shows we have about $8.5 million in flexibility, but that's not going to solve a structural problem. We're the ones who ultimately have to face the voters with whatever tax increase we approve.
