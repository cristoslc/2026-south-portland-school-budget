# South Portland School Department proposes closing Kaler Elementary

**Portland Press Herald** — Staff Writer Dana Richie — March 24, 2026

## Summary

The South Portland School Department proposed closing James O. Kaler Elementary School for the 2026-27 school year at a packed school board workshop on Monday, March 23. The closure proposal is one of two reconfiguration options presented to the board; no vote has been taken.

## Key claims and data points

- The district presented **two options** after closing Kaler:
  1. **Grade-level reconfiguration**: Dyer and Small serve pre-K through grade 1; Brown and Skillin serve grades 2-4.
  2. **Redistricting only**: Keep K-4 at all four remaining schools but redraw attendance boundaries.

- Current enrollment (this school year):
  - Dyer: 197 students
  - Small: 190
  - Brown: 201
  - Skillin: 310
  - Kaler: 182

- Projected capacity after reconfiguration: Dyer 232, Small 240, Brown 248, Skillin 384.

- Under redistricting-only option, Skillin would reach 384 — at or above building capacity.

- **Savings figures** presented:
  - Grade-level reconfiguration: $3.1 million this year; $16.4 million over five years.
  - School closure with redistricting: $2.1 million this year; $12.8 million over five years.

- Approximately **50 department employees** were notified the prior week that their positions would be eliminated.

- If Kaler closes, **no further staff reductions** would be needed beyond those already announced, per Assistant Principal Johanna Prince.

- The district does **not recommend decommissioning** Kaler; the building could be leased for up to four years, generating revenue.

- The board had earlier considered closing Skillin (most expensive facility needs) but reversed course because it is "the city's most diverse elementary school." The board then considered closing Dyer before shifting to Kaler.

- Kaler demographics noted by community members: more than 50% qualify for free/reduced lunch; more than 50% are Black, Indigenous, or other people of color; 30-35% are multilingual learners.

## Notable quotes (under 15 words each)

- "If we maintain current boundaries... we will always have a disparity." — Johanna Prince, Assistant Principal
- "I'm here to stand for the Kaler community." — Kate Hurd, Kaler parent (who noted the community had only three days' notice before the meeting)

## Process notes

- The board workshop was described as "packed."
- Next budget workshop scheduled for Monday, March 30.
- Bethany Connolly, principal of Skillin, spoke in favor of grade-level reconfiguration for demographic equity and teacher collaboration.
- Some parents criticized the process as rushed and as pitting school communities against each other.
