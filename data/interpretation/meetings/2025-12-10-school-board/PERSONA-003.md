Okay so I watched this whole meeting online and honestly I'm not sure if I should be impressed or terrified. On one hand, it's actually really encouraging to hear the school board asking every single firm about diversity and reaching candidates of color - like they actually GET that South Portland isn't your typical Maine town. Board member Richardson kept pushing on this and I was like yes, finally someone who understands our community.

But then they dropped this bombshell that they have literally ZERO dollars budgeted for finding a new superintendent. Like, what?! They force out the last guy and then realize "oops, we need to actually hire someone and that costs money." The interim superintendent just sat there and said "nothing" when they asked about the budget. And then - THEN - they pick the most expensive option at like $30,000! I mean I get that you want quality but this is the same district that's probably going to ask us for another tax increase.

The whole thing just makes me wonder what else they haven't thought through. Like are they going to have a solid plan in place by the time my daughter starts kindergarten in two years? I want them to find someone amazing but I also need them to actually manage money responsibly. At least the search should be done by spring so we'll know who's running things before I have to make any big decisions about staying in the district.
