Okay so I went to both meetings tonight because I heard there might be news about the budget cuts, and honestly? It was the most frustrating thing ever. Like they had Jules up there doing this beautiful poem about South Portland's future, and she's literally a student at the high school, but then nobody - NOBODY - talked about the fact that we're about to lose 42 teachers. The city people are all "we invest in our children" and "incredible middle school" but then the school people are like talking about students with drug problems and mental health issues but NOT talking about how we're literally cutting the programs that help with that stuff. And they brought up all these immigrant families to speak, which was actually really moving, but like... current students exist too? We're also part of the community? We're the ones who are actually going to be sitting in those overcrowded classes if they cut teachers.

I'm just so tired of adults making decisions "for us" without actually including us in the conversation. They love talking about how everything is "for the children" but then when it comes time to actually figure out what's getting cut, we don't get a say. I want to know if AP Bio is happening next year, if we're still going to have a spring musical, if my cross-country coach is keeping her job. But instead I got two hours of adults talking past each other about everything except the thing that's actually going to affect my senior year.
