### Reactions

Oh my god, I stayed up until almost 11 PM watching this meeting online and I'm honestly kind of freaking out. I knew the budget was tight, but I had no idea it was THIS bad. They're talking about cutting 78 positions - including 42 teachers! - and they might completely change how elementary schools work by the time my daughter gets to kindergarten.

The scariest part was when they voted to study this "reconfiguration" thing where instead of neighborhood schools, they'd have some schools with just pre-K through first grade and others with second through fourth grade. The board chair was SO enthusiastic about it, saying it's "fabulous" and "good for kids," but honestly that just made me more nervous. Like, if it's such a great idea, why does it feel like it's being driven entirely by budget cuts? And what does this mean for my daughter - will she have to switch schools in the middle of elementary? Will there even BE a school in our neighborhood?

The pre-K stuff was somewhat reassuring - at least they're expanding services and there might be more options with partnerships. But even that's a lottery system because they can't serve everyone who wants it. It just feels like everything is so uncertain. One board member flat out said he'd be comfortable with tax increases over 6% because covering a $7 million shortfall any other way would be "draconian." But then another member said they already agreed to 6% so I don't even think they know what they're doing. The whole thing felt like they're making huge decisions about our kids' futures while figuring it out as they go along.
