# Cumulative Record: Kira (PERSONA-015)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop I -- March 2, 2026 -- 2026-03-02

### Interpretation

Smith said *desegregation*. She wasn't expecting that word, not in that room, not attached to a budget workshop agenda. He named the civil rights history, pointed at who was present, and said whose interests get political pressure — and Dr. Prince followed it with the operational logic she drives between three buildings every day: address-based attendance will always reproduce housing inequity. The 6%-to-33% multilingual learner spread that April Perkins put on the slide is not a statistic to her. It is the reason she can form a tier-two intervention cohort at some buildings and can only reach individual students at others because the critical mass isn't there. That data has been in her caseload notes for years. Tonight it is in the official district record with a structural explanation attached, and a board member named it as a civil rights problem.

Four members said they won't close Skilling, and DeAngelos made the geographic equity argument Kira would have made herself — the Dairy Queen, the kids who walk because they have no other way, the specific neighborhood names. That is right. But the room was drifting toward option 1.3 or 1.4, which close Kaler. She has been carrying the Kaler thread since December 1 — Jean Tam's daughter, the counselor vacancy flagged in January, the building where her families are the second-most-concentrated population for free-and-reduced lunch and multilingual learners in the district. And when Richardson asked when redistricting mechanics would be available before the vote, the answer was: not yet, we haven't modeled it. No timeline. No accountability structure.

Richardson named the bait and switch, and Prince confirmed it honestly: the board approved the Boundaries and Configurations proposals and never took the next step. Kira has been making that argument in letters that she wasn't sure anyone read. Tonight it is in the official record. What she cannot resolve is whether this time is different. DeAngelos gave the warning at the very end — diversity and equity are two different things; mixing kids across buildings without structures, training, and policy does not build belonging — and she believes it, and it is exactly the thing she needs that warning to become. It hasn't become it yet. The equity case has won the language in that room. The accountability structure for making redistricting actually move demographic equity, rather than just move kids, has not been built.
