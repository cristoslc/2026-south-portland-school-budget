### Reactions

Honestly, this was the most data-rich budget discussion I've seen in years. The enrollment numbers don't lie -- we dropped 300+ elementary kids while adding 82 staff positions. That's just unsustainable math, and I'm glad the board is finally acknowledging it instead of pretending we can magic our way out of this.

The fund balance situation is what really concerns me though. Being essentially tapped out means we have zero room for error -- no buffer if enrollment drops more, if the state cuts funding further, or if we hit unexpected costs. That's extremely poor financial management for any organization, let alone one with a $100M+ budget.

The 6% tax ceiling is the right call. An 18% increase would have been political and economic suicide. The $26,651 per-pupil number needs serious scrutiny -- if that's accurate and we're really the highest among peers, then we have fundamental efficiency problems beyond just the enrollment decline. I want to see the methodology behind that comparison and understand where our cost structure differs.
