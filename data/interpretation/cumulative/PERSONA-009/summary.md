# Cumulative Summary: Dana (PERSONA-009)

### Current Understanding

Dana understands South Portland's FY27 school budget as a certified institutional failure compounded by structural governance dysfunction. The district carries an $8.4 million deficit and cannot close its budget without a 6% property tax increase — a compression from the 18.95% status-quo scenario achieved through 72 pink slips, a contested school closure, and a fragile reconfiguration mandate. FY26 is also heading to deficit across all scenarios; the fund balance is gone; Finance Director Ketchum — the seventh director in six years — described the institution on the record as having "driven off a cliff it didn't see coming."

The board voted 6-1 to close Kaler Elementary, whose 45%-BIPOC, 35%-multilingual student body raises an unanswered Title VI civil rights compliance question. Option A reconfiguration passed 4-2, but the budget has failed twice. A surprise $300,000 state funding figure — texted to a union president mid-meeting on April 2 — may allow restoration of some positions before the April 7 city council deadline.

Layered on the same property tax bill: an $87 million public safety bond (police at Walgreens/Cash Corner, fire on Broadway) heading to a November 2026 referendum, and projected 22% annual sewer rate increases through FY29. The city itself is fiscally healthy — $28.8 million fund balance, clean audit — creating an asymmetry that reshapes the intergovernmental conflict running since December. Chief Ahern publicly apologized for a federal agent's racist texts during January's ICE enforcement surge, which canceled the school budget forum and kept children home from school. The council's $100,000 appropriation to Project Home is the only binding financial protection immigrants have received from local government all season.
