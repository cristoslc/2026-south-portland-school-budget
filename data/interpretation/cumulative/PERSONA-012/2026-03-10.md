# Cumulative Record: Jaylen (PERSONA-012)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- March 10, 2026 -- 2026-03-10

### Interpretation

March 10 is where the city finally gave Jaylen something practical. Metro confirmed free bus rides are still active and, more than that, had already sat down with Glenn specifically about aligning bus schedules with bell times. After months of budget categories and object codes and nothing that maps to actual life at SPHS, this is a fact about how he gets to school. It matters. He writes it down.

Then Councilor Scott says the sentence he has been assembling in his head since the January forum got cancelled. Eighty families at risk of eviction means eighty students who may not be in the school system next year — and that demographic loss costs more than the $100,000 being debated. That is the math Jaylen has been running silently since January 20, when the forum cancellation made clear that the people most hurt by the budget are also the people least able to show up and fight it. She is the first person at a council microphone to say it out loud as a formal argument, and she is right.

But he already knows what happens nine days later. Matthews takes the same fiscal reasoning — the school crisis is real, the money is tight, every dollar counts — and uses it to vote against the same families Scott is defending. The argument that should protect the most vulnerable students in South Portland gets inverted and attached to a roll call. Scott's logic is correct. It does not win.

The school budget crisis appears five times in this meeting as a reason not to spend money. It appears zero times as a subject with program-level content. The same gap he has been cataloging since December 9, when DeAngelis got three minutes and no follow-up questions. The budget is everywhere in these chambers as a fiscal ghost and nowhere as a specific question about whether AP Lit will have a teacher in September.

The parent's letter read by someone else because the family could not be there — kids too scared to go to class, a neighbor saw agents near the school driveway — is his school, his hallways, his classmates. He does not know which elementary school. He does not need to.

The new bus route names the high school. It arrives in 2027. Senior year starts in September 2026.
