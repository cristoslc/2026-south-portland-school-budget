Jesus Christ, what a shitshow. So we're cutting 42 teaching positions - that's 42 classrooms worth of kids that are getting shoved into bigger sections - and these people are sitting there talking about an $87 million bond for buildings? I mean, I get that the police station is falling apart and the fire station needs work, but the timing is absolutely tone-deaf.

You should have seen Councilor West trying to argue for spending just $2 million to patch up the fire station instead of building new. Finally, someone who gets it - we can't afford to do everything at once. But then the facilities director comes up and says even that would cost $5-10 million because the buildings are so far gone. Twenty-five years of deferred maintenance, just like our schools. This is what happens when you don't invest in public infrastructure.

The library director's testimony hit hard too - 27 visitors every hour, over 15,000 people in programs last year, and they're telling him "we'll deal with you later." Sound familiar? That's exactly what they tell us in education every single time. Public safety first, everything else can wait. Meanwhile our class sizes are going to be insane next year and they wonder why teachers are leaving the profession.
