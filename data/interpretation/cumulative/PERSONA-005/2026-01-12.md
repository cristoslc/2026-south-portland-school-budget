# Cumulative Record: Priya (PERSONA-005)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- January 12, 2026 -- 2026-01-12

### Interpretation

This meeting introduced an entirely new dimension of equity crisis that was not even on my radar a month ago. Children did not come to school because their families feared ICE enforcement. Staff did not feel safe coming to work. This is not a hypothetical threat -- it is an active, measurable disruption to educational access for some of our most vulnerable students, and the district's response was painfully inadequate. Richardson was right to push for faster action, but "accelerating timelines" and "considering a statement" is not the urgent intervention this demands.

What struck me most was the intersection of the immigration crisis with the budget crisis. The district is about to cut 78 positions while simultaneously failing to protect the students who are already losing school days to fear. When Melinda Hull asked how the district would ensure budget input from families who are afraid to speak publicly, the silence was deafening. This is the core of the engagement problem I have been tracking since the superintendent search workshop where nobody showed up -- the district's entire engagement model assumes people can safely and freely participate in public forums, and that assumption is now violently false for a significant portion of our community.

The halal meal certification was a genuine bright spot -- concrete, culturally responsive action that materially improves the school experience for Muslim students. It demonstrates that the district can identify and meet the specific needs of marginalized student populations when someone decides to prioritize it. The contrast with the budget process, where no one can produce disaggregated impact data, makes the omission feel more deliberate. They figured out halal certification but cannot figure out which schools lose counselors first? The absence tracking data exists -- the superintendent confirmed they track it -- but nobody has analyzed the ICE-related patterns. The data is there; the will to use it for equity purposes is not.
