# Cumulative Record: Linda (PERSONA-007)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- April 2, 2026 -- 2026-04-02

### Interpretation

We did the right thing not voting tonight. I've been saying since December that the problem with this district's finances is that we kept budgeting what we hoped to spend rather than what we actually spent — and then Abigail stands up and proves it with electricity charts and tuition reimbursement lines going back three fiscal years. The $211K overage in FY24, the $165K in FY25, and the projected $368K gap this year aren't anomalies; they're the pattern. Her thesis — that realistic costing in FY27 is a job-protection strategy for FY28 and beyond — is the clearest articulation of what I've been trying to explain in finance committee for months. I want it in the April 7 presentation.

Then the state funding arrived in real time. Connie DeSanto at the mic saying the unions had secured $300K from Augusta — $150K for homeless students, $150K for economically disadvantaged — and within the same action item block, Claire gets a text from Rep. Kessler about $750K in EPS formula changes. I have been following the EPS subsidy story since December 9, when I first understood that rising local valuations automatically reduce what the state sends us. The gap between the 20% we receive and the 55% the formula implies has been my standing frustration for two budget cycles. If $750K in EPS relief is real and recurring, it changes the math in ways I cannot absorb tonight without verification. If it's one-year money and we embed it in position salaries, we are guaranteeing the same crisis in FY29 with zero reserves and no warning.

That is the discipline Abby Anderson demanded from the dais, and she was right to demand it. The SPTA vice president's warning about ad-hoc position restoration was the most important procedural intervention of the evening. If we decide which jobs come back based on email volume, we own that precedent forever. I need criteria — unmet educational need, population served, recurrence of the funding source — on the table before Monday, not after we've committed the dollars.

What I cannot let get lost is the FY28 slide. Labor costs growing above 6% annually by contract. The Skillin boiler. The high school chimney stacks. Another $300K in athletic turf debt service. We reset FY27 and we are already staring at the next cliff with a fund balance confirmed at zero — against a city benchmark of 9-12%, which is $6.8 to $9.1 million we do not have and nothing in this budget starts building. April 7 is tomorrow, and we are going into that council chamber with authorizations but no adopted budget, state money we haven't verified, and structural problems that survive this year's reset. I am going to be precise about every one of those facts.
