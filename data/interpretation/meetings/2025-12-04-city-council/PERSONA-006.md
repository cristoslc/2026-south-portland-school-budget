### Reactions

What a complete waste of time. I sat through two hours of this meeting hoping to hear something - anything - about how they're going to handle the school budget crisis that's going to hit us with an 18% property tax increase. Instead, they spent the whole time appointing people to committees and arguing about whether people should be allowed to make comments over Zoom. 

The worst part was when they approved this new system that lets every committee ask for up to $2,500 plus unlimited "special projects" money. Councilor Walker was the only one with any sense who voted against it. Meanwhile, we're facing a $7.2 million deficit and they're creating new ways to spend money. And when Councilor West mentioned we need money for building maintenance - which we absolutely do after seeing those deteriorated fire stations - I'm thinking where exactly is that money supposed to come from?

I've been to plenty of these meetings over the years, and I've never seen them so disconnected from reality. They spent more time debating meeting rules than they did talking about the financial emergency facing every homeowner in this city. Dick Matthews was right when he reminded people they can email or call councilors directly - because clearly showing up to these meetings isn't going to get you answers about the budget.
