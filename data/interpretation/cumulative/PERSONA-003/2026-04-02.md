# Cumulative Record: Jess (PERSONA-003)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- April 2, 2026 -- 2026-04-02

### Interpretation

Four months ago I didn't know what a reconfiguration was. Now I know exactly what it is — Option A, PreK-1 and grades 2-4, Kaler closed — and I still can't tell you which building my daughter will walk into in two years. That is the single sentence that summarizes April 2 for me. The structural decisions are locked. The fiscal framework they run on is not. And the details that would actually let a family plan — boundaries, building assignments, infrastructure — don't exist yet.

The behavioral support specialist news is the thing I keep coming back to. Since December I've been building a mental model of what Mia's classroom will look like: reconfigured, yes; larger, yes; but presumably staffed to handle the range of kids who show up. The statement read into the record at this meeting dismantled the last piece of that assumption. One person in the whole district whose entire job was to catch struggling kids before they fall into special education — sixty kids, forty formal behavior plans — is being eliminated. And in the same meeting, a board member confirmed there is no written policy limiting how many IEP students can be concentrated in a single classroom. Thirteen elementary teacher positions are also gone. I've been telling myself that what's being cut is "overhead," "administration," "management layers." It isn't. It's the person in the room with the kid who is starting to fall.

The $300,000 in potential new state funding is real and I'm not dismissing it. Teachers and union staff actually went to Augusta and lobbied and came back with something. That matters. But $300,000 against a structural deficit that was $7.2 million in December and $8.4 million last month — it softens the landing somewhere, it doesn't change the trajectory.

The question a parent asked about whether reconfigured buildings even have child-sized sinks and bathrooms for kindergartners and pre-K students went unanswered. That is a facilities question, not a policy question. It has a yes or no answer. The board didn't give it one. And the budget still hasn't passed. And the new superintendent is arriving into a system where every structural decision has already been made without them. I've watched this process for four months and I understand, now, that "the process is still open" and "decisions have already been made" can both be true at the same time. March 30 taught me that. April 2 confirmed it.
