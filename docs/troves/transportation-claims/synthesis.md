## The Chronological Pattern

The transportation evidence record tells a clear story of escalating questions and persistent non-answers:

### February 4 — The Question Is Raised
A parent asks about busing costs at the first budget forum (TC-001). No answer is provided. The administration does, however, note that middle school bell time changes require a $25K DOT traffic study and 6-12 month permitting — establishing that they understand transport changes require analysis (TC-002).

### March 2 — The Question Returns, With Testimony
The busing question comes back with force. Parents describe 45-minute bus rides under the current system (TC-008), working families that "will be crushed" by split-building logistics (TC-006, TC-007), and the lack of transportation cost estimates (TC-009). The administration's survey data shows 90% of parents worried about busing complications and 57% concerned about before/after care disruption (TC-011).

The administration responds with: "we would need to kind of look at the whole picture" (TC-003) and "have budgeted for transportation consultancy" (TC-004). They also state, without supporting analysis: "we don't anticipate [route cost changes] being a significant cost" (TC-005).

### March 9 — The Gap Is Formally Documented
The evidence pool synthesis identifies transportation cost modeling as a critical gap: "A transportation consultant is referenced as a cost but no routing analysis has been shared" (TC-012).

### March 23-30 — Confirmation of No Analysis
The Director of Operations confirms on the record that "no modeling exists and none will be done before the board votes" (TC-014). The board is asked to choose between configurations with materially different transportation implications, without transportation analysis.

## Key Contradictions

1. **Double standard on traffic studies**: The administration deferred middle school bell time changes because they require a $25K DOT traffic study (TC-002, TC-013). Elementary reconfiguration affects more students and more buildings, but no equivalent analysis was required or produced.

2. **"Not significant" without modeling**: The claim that route cost changes "are not anticipated to be significant" (TC-005) was made without having modeled the routes. This is an assumption presented as a conclusion.

3. **Cutting transport staff while expanding routes**: 14% SEA staffing reduction (TC-016) decided before any assessment of whether the reconfigured system needs more or fewer routes.

4. **Survey results ignored**: Administration's own survey shows 90% of parents worried about busing (TC-011), yet no analysis was commissioned to address this concern.

## Unfunded Obligations Not Addressed

The following cost categories are absent from public discussion:

- **McKinney-Vento transport obligations**: 10% of students are eligible. School closure triggers school-of-origin transport rights.
- **SPED door-to-door mandates**: 23% of students have IEPs, many with transport requirements.
- **Before/after care gap costs**: Split-building families face mismatched schedules with no district plan.
- **Portland bus agreement renegotiation**: Cross-billing arrangement (TC-017) may be affected by route restructuring.
