# Voter Guide Best Practices — League of Women Voters Minnesota

*Source: League of Women Voters Minnesota — https://www.lwvmn.org/voter-guide-best-practices*

## Purpose

The League aims to combat voter participation barriers by offering dependable, nonpartisan election resources. "The public respects the League's commitment to factual, unbiased information."

Voter guides with candidate questionnaires require especially careful attention and sound judgment in order to protect your nonpartisan reputation and the public's trust.

## Candidate Questionnaire Development

### Coordination
LWVMN coordinates Vote411.org for federal, state, and local elections. Local leagues select topics relevant to their communities. Partnerships with other organizations are permitted if they don't compromise nonpartisan perception.

### Prohibited Practices

Questions must **not**:
- Incorporate organizational positions
- Ask candidates to pledge support for causes (e.g., "Will you promise to support action on climate change?")
- Provide evaluative judgments of responses
- Include personal or hostile content
- Violate judicial conduct codes
- Originate from candidate or party suggestions

Voter guides should **not** compare candidates to an organizational position or ask for pledges of support or opposition.

## Writing Question Standards

Effective questions should:
- Apply uniformly across **all candidates** in a race
- Remain **open-ended** rather than yes/no format (yes/no questions have a more partisan appearance)
- Address diverse issues rather than single topics
- Reflect broad electorate interests, not just organizational member concerns
- Relate directly to **the office being sought**
- Avoid leading or biased language

"Questions should not be biased or leading...written in such a way that one answer appears to be favored."

## Candidate Solicitation Requirements

Invitations must:
- Distribute equally to **all candidates** using identical methods and timing
- Clearly state word limits and submission deadlines
- Provide a notice that responses exceeding word limits will be truncated
- State that responses will not be edited for grammar or spelling
- State the deadline and what will be printed if a candidate does not respond
- Identify whom to contact with questions
- **Not share one candidate's answers** with another candidate before publication

Put the same amount of effort into outreach/reminders for each candidate.

## Publication Standards

Guides must:
- Present questions **verbatim**
- Publish **unedited** candidate responses (except truncation for length)
- Maintain **consistent formatting** across all responses
- Include the disclaimer that the organization "does not support or oppose any political party or candidate"
- **Not publish** if only one candidate responds

## Partnerships

Organizations may work with others to publish voter guides. They must:
- Consider carefully how a partnership might affect public perception of nonpartisanship
- Communicate to partners that they cannot deviate from nonpartisan policy
- Ensure fair treatment of all candidates is maintained in any partnership
