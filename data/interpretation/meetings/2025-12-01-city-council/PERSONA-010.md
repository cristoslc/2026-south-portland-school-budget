### Reactions

This was classic South Portland - beautiful ceremony, soaring rhetoric, and a complete disconnect from the financial reality bearing down on taxpayers. Cohen's parting shot about property taxes "going through the roof even more than it already is" was the only acknowledgment of what families are actually dealing with. That's going to be my lead.

Tipton's inaugural was ambitious - Mahoney redevelopment, comprehensive planning, housing initiatives, even potential oil tank redevelopment. But there's a $7.2M gap in the school budget that requires either massive cuts or an 18% tax increase, and neither she nor the new school board chair even mentioned it. DeAngelos gave this moving speech about immigrant inclusion while 42 teachers are on the chopping block. The cognitive dissonance is stunning.

I need to dig into the Mahoney numbers and timeline - that could be a game-changer for downtown, but residents need to understand the true costs and trade-offs. Same with the housing push and oil tank redevelopment. Big visions are great, but someone has to pay for them, and Cohen just told us who that's going to be.
