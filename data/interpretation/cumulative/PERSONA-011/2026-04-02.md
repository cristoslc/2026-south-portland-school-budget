# Cumulative Record: Meg (PERSONA-011)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- April 2, 2026 -- 2026-04-02

### Interpretation

I came into tonight expecting a budget vote — the thing that's been missing since March 30 when the five no votes sent everyone home with nothing resolved. Instead I got a five-hour meeting that moved some pieces forward and left the big one hanging again. But here's the thing: the reason it didn't move is actually the most significant development in weeks.

Connie DeSanto walked to the microphone during public comment and announced, mid-meeting, that the state house is likely sending South Portland roughly $300,000 — $150,000 for homeless students, $150,000 for economically disadvantaged kids — because union leaders went to Augusta and lobbied for it. Then, while the board was deliberating hours later, Richardson got a text from Rep. Kessler suggesting another $750,000 might be coming from changes to the EPS formula. Neither figure is confirmed. But I understand why several board members looked at a budget packed with deep cuts and said: wait. Not tonight.

Since December I've been reporting on a structural funding problem — the state covering 20% when it's supposed to be 55%, the $7.2 million gap (now $8.4 million per Matthews), the 78 positions. State funding has been the missing piece every single time. Tonight is the first meeting where someone stood at that podium and said out loud that the state might actually be moving. I'm not posting those numbers as resolved. But I'm noting them as real for the first time.

The other thing I needed to report tonight: DeAngelis opened the meeting by saying clearly that a no vote on the June 9 budget referendum does not reverse the reconfiguration. Those are legally separate actions. Kaler closes. Option A proceeds. A referendum failure just means the board works inside last year's smaller budget — which is actually harder, not easier. I've been waiting for someone to say that on the record because it's been muddied in my group chats for a week.

What doesn't have an answer yet: where elementary kids are going next year. Attendance boundaries still unset. Behavioral strategist serving 60 kids across four schools still on the chopping block with no named successor for those 40-plus behavior plans. Every middle school program hit — computer science, PE, STEM, percussion — still gone under the current proposal, with no process established for how new state money, if it arrives, gets allocated back into positions. The early release days are gone, unanimously, which is a clean win. Everything else waits for Tuesday.
