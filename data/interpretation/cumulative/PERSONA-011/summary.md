# Cumulative Summary: Meg (PERSONA-011)

### Current Understanding

South Portland Schools is deep into the worst budget crisis Meg has tracked. The district faces a structural FY27 gap of $7.2 million — possibly $8.4 million, per an unverified on-the-record statement from Councilor Matthews on March 18 — requiring elimination of approximately 78 positions, 72 of which received layoff notices on March 18. Meg is holding the $8.4M figure until she can reconcile it with the $7.2M she has cited since December. The current fiscal year (FY26) is also definitively in deficit, driven in part by a $1.1 million nutrition fund shortfall; school fund balance is down to $739K, and the city may need to cover school deficits from city reserves.

The primary structural fix is elementary school reconfiguration. Nine specific options were presented March 2; the superintendent's top pick (Option 1.7, close Skillin) was rejected by at least four board members, including Chair DeAngelis, who framed Skillin's protection as an equity imperative for immigrant families west of the Dairy Queen. The March 23 workshop is the next decision point. The 78 position cuts are happening regardless of the final tax rate; council guidance is 4–6%, and even the 9.8% moderate scenario retains all cuts.

Compounding the structural crisis is a layer of internal management failure: six finance directors in five years, $400,000 in mismanaged grant funds, and two consecutive years of budget overspending in violation of legal limits. The city's development deal pattern — multiple TIF agreements diverting property tax revenue for decades — is now understood as systematic, not isolated. The Mahoney City Center November 2026 ballot has been abandoned, removing one feared taxpayer burden. The budget calendar is set: April 14 workshop, May 5 council vote, June 9 referendum.
