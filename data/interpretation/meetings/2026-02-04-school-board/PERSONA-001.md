When I saw the agenda I figured they'd be talking about cutting some supplies or maybe a few positions, but they're talking about completely restructuring how elementary school works? My daughter's in second grade at Memorial and she loves her teacher, she has friends in her neighborhood school, and now they want to ship all the second graders to some other building with 22 kids in a class instead of her current 16?

And the fact that they're running FIVE different bus schedules every day already - no wonder our taxes are through the roof. But then they say even if they do all three of these big changes, there's still another $3 million they need to cut. What does that mean? More teachers? Art and music? The librarian?

I get that enrollment is down and costs are up, but it feels like they're destroying what makes elementary school special to save money. I'm definitely going to those board meetings in March.
