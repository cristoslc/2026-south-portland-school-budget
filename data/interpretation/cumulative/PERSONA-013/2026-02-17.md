# Cumulative Record: Amira (PERSONA-013)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- February 17, 2026 -- 2026-02-17

### Interpretation

The image that won't leave me is the neighbor on the car floor. Not "ICE was in the neighborhood" — I already knew that from January when it was at the laundromat. Not "families were scared" — I knew that too. This was a grown woman throwing herself flat in the back seat of a car so no one could see her through the windows, calling her kids to lock the door and not open it for anyone, even people they know, and then going to work. That happened on a street in my city. She was just trying to get to work.

And then there's the 10-year-old. The same woman read what this kid said to her — a kid basically my age — "Why are they doing this to us? My parents work and pay taxes and do everything right." I've been trying to answer questions like that this whole time. Why are they cutting teachers if enrollment going down isn't the kids' fault? Why does the school board make decisions about our schools without asking us? Every time, I hit the same wall this kid hit: it's not fair, and fairness doesn't seem to be the point.

Hours and hours of people stood up. Families behind on rent because they were too scared to leave the house to go to work. A woman who said the group helping families pay rent has ten days of money left. Eviction notices already being sent. And the vote at the end of all of it was four against, two for. They had a chance to stop the evictions while all this was happening. They said no.

Councilor Scott voted no. This is the same woman who toured schools in December and said out loud that staff does more than their job with challenging facilities and resources. She saw our schools. She said it was hard. And then she voted not to protect the families of kids in those schools.

At midnight, Councilor Walker said the city might be becoming one that doesn't invest in schools or libraries anymore, and asked what that says about who they are. I've been watching the answer form since December 1st, when immigrant families were welcomed in their native languages and the budget gap was already sitting there quiet, waiting.

### Emotional Register

Amira carries the car floor and the 10-year-old's question as a single unbearable weight — she has spent months watching adults fail the immigrant community in incrementally larger ways, but this is the first time she has heard a voice exactly her age ask the question she has been unable to answer, and then watched the adults in the room vote, four to two, to let the consequences land anyway.
