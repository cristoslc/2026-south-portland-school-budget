# Cumulative Record: Linda (PERSONA-007)
## Meeting: School Board Special Budget Meeting -- March 30, 2026 -- 2026-03-30

### Interpretation

Two out of three. That's what I'm carrying home tonight, and I need to be honest with myself about the weight of each piece.

Kahler closure passed 6-1. That's as clean as you get on a decision this painful. I've watched this board fracture repeatedly since December — 4-3 on Zeal, Feller breaking on the 6% ceiling in public, a workshop that produced three hours of community opposition and a 12% informed rate. Getting six votes on a school closure, after everything, is not a given. Option A passed 4-2. Thin, but it's a majority. The superintendent can write the commissioner letter. The reconfiguration has authorization. Those decisions are real and recorded, and they represent the hardest governance work this board has done all year.

Then the budget failed 5-2 at 11 PM.

I've been watching for the governance failure pattern since December 10, when this board spent $30,000 it didn't have on the most expensive superintendent search option available — a last-minute decision, fractured vote, no committee preparation. Tonight it arrived wearing different clothes: the DEI reclassification, dropped the night of the vote, produces no guaranteed savings because Article 18 recall rights mean a more senior teacher fills the slot at equivalent or higher cost, removes the district's only BIPOC senior leader, and was never run through committee. Rosemarie called it exactly right from the dais. That intervention produced a no vote. And five no votes means I walk into city council on April 7 without the one deliverable that gives this board standing in that chamber.

I can see the path to five votes if I'm being precise about it. Feller said his condition plainly: the percussion EdTech position. That's two votes plus his. DeAngelis has the DEI question and some special education positions — those are addressable if someone brings a specific amendment Thursday. Richardson's objections sounded more structural, about the shape of the cuts rather than a single line. That's the harder one. There is a deal here if the board wants to make it Thursday. What I cannot afford is another adjournment without votes.

What's keeping me up isn't the budget vote. It's the meet-and-consult exposure. Three simultaneous labor disputes with no mandatory completion deadline, prohibited practice complaint escalation as the backstop, and a reconfiguration that depends on working conditions now in active dispute. We have authorization but not implementation. And the fund balance policy the superintendent recommended — the exact structural fix that would have prevented December 8 — is still a slide, not a board vote. Thursday is the moment someone needs to move it. If we're going to April 7 without a passed budget, we should at minimum arrive with that policy on the books.
