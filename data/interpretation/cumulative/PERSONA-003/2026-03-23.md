# Cumulative Record: Jess (PERSONA-003)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop -- March 23, 2026 -- 2026-03-23

### Interpretation

The pre-K anchor is shaking, and I don't know what to hold onto instead.

For two months -- since January 12, and then every meeting after that -- pre-K has been the one thread I kept coming back to. State mandates. Physical space requirements. Head Start partnerships building in. Multiple structural supports. Every time I started researching Scarborough real estate, I'd come back to that: the pre-K expansion was real, it was durable, it was actually designed for Mia's cohort. And now I find out the district reversed the locally-funded expansion before this meeting even started. The 40 "replacement" seats come from Child Development Services -- eight seats, IEP-eligible only, not for my daughter -- a United Way classroom that's off-site, and a Head Start partnership that is, I'm not softening this, "in conversations." That's not seats. That's a slide deck full of hope.

The finance director said it plainly: this budget "does not solve our core problems." She's the seventh person in that role in six years -- six years during which, she said herself, the books couldn't have been in order -- and she named everything that gets worse in FY28 and FY29: labor costs rising faster than the 6% tax cap, electricity up 13-14% a year, new debt from the athletic field bond, enrollment still declining which means less state money year over year. Those are the years Mia enters kindergarten. Nobody pushed back on that. Nobody said "we have a plan for that." Because there isn't one yet.

Reconfiguration came down to two options: Option A splits elementary into primary (pre-K through first grade) and intermediate (second through fourth) buildings, meaning Mia would switch schools mid-elementary. Option B keeps K-4 but still closes Kayler. Neither is the neighborhood school model I was imagining. Public comment speakers raised something that landed harder than any statistic: kids from Mia's COVID cohort came in with higher needs than typical, and the board didn't respond to it at all.

They went five hours. They voted on nothing. The board chair cried during her opening remarks.

I'm still staying. But my anchor is a partnership that hasn't been signed yet.
