# Cumulative Record: Ben (PERSONA-010)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop -- March 23, 2026 -- 2026-03-23

### Interpretation

Ben has been hunting the district's origin story since December 8 — the clear, honest, public accounting of how a district with an $87 million budget could miss a multi-million dollar contract liability, drain its fund balance to zero, and build toward a crisis nobody stopped. Abigail Ketchem delivers it on Monday night, and she delivers it as the seventh finance director in six years. That is worse than the figure Dr. Prince disclosed in February. This time it comes from the person who inherited the mess.

The account is as plain as anything Ben has heard at a school board meeting all season. COVID money inflated staffing levels that were never brought back down when the federal relief expired. There was no minimum fund balance policy — the warning light was never wired in. Revolving finance leadership meant institutional memory could not form across fiscal years. The credit card analogy writes itself into his lede: FY27 wipes the balance, but if the underlying habits do not change, the district is back at the cliff. That sentence is in print.

Then Ketchem buries a bomb in plain sight: labor costs under existing contracts increase faster than 6% per year by staying in place. Ben has been tracking the 6% tax ceiling as a political constraint since December. He now understands it as a structural ceiling that existing salary schedules already push past before a single new dollar is spent. With $300,000 in additional debt service arriving in FY28, the district may be presenting FY27 as a reset while quietly building toward a repeat. That is the call he needs to make before the April 14 council presentation.

The $257 per year figure is the nut graf for his referendum piece — the first concrete, plain-English impact number offered to a general audience across nine board meetings. He files it immediately. And immediately notes what it does not include: sewer rates, the public safety bond, Mahoney. The combined figure he has been assembling since January 13 still has not been stated in any public forum, by anyone.

The board adjourns at 11:15 PM without a single vote on the three items it was supposed to decide. Option A or B — still unmade. The school closure report — unsent. The FY27 budget — unadopted. The City Council presentation is April 7. The calendar is already broken and the September timeline is not doubtful anymore; it is done.

What Ben cannot put down is Jess Elsner's question. Kayler is 45% BIPOC and 30-35% multilingual learners. The Title VI question sits on the record at 11:15 PM with the chair saying she does not have a legal answer. Nobody in the local press seems to have picked it up. That is a call in the morning.
