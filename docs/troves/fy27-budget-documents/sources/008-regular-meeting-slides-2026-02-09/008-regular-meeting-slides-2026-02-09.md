### SPSD: Community Schools for All?

Becoming a Community School District - Dr. Rebecca Stern, Principal, SPMS; Liana Popkin, Community School Director

#### Why We're Here

Problems: Schools today face overlapping challenges: Chronic absenteeism, Youth mental health concerns, Academic disparities, Housing insecurity, Funding gaps. Schools cannot overcome this alone!

Solutions:
1. Scale up the SPMS Community School Model to the whole district
2. Develop an Education Foundation to support the Community School Model in all schools

#### History

- 22-23: $664k City of SP ARPA grant awarded to The Opportunity Alliance & Memorial Middle School
- Fall 2022: Community School work begins; Advisory Board identified chronic absenteeism and sense of belonging as priorities
- 23-24: SPMS opens its doors! Needs and Assets Report completed; $90k Maine DOE Grant awarded
- 24-25: Full Implementation @SPMS; $40k grant from Maine DOE; seeing many positive outcomes for students
- Fall 2025: Community Support for Education Foundation; Community members meeting bi-weekly to organize a 501c3 that would support CS work
- February 2026: School Board Vote on all schools becoming Community Schools; Maine statute requires a school board vote for schools to be eligible for state CS funding

#### What is a Community School?

A Community School is a school that:
- Serves as a hub to respond to the unique needs of its community
- Reduces barriers to school and achievement
- Integrates academics with health, social, and community services
- Organizes the resources of the school, families, and community around student success

Community Schools are a STRATEGY, not a PROGRAM. Every Community School is different, serving the needs of their students, families, staff, and community.

#### Why Community Schools Work

- Responsive - offers hyper-local solutions
- Holistic - addresses root causes and supports the whole child
- Cost-effective - $1 invested = $7.11 in net benefits

#### Community Schools have Nationally Proven Results

Learning Policy Institute (2025): Results from a recent California study:
- Improved Test Scores
- Reduced Chronic Absenteeism
- Reduced Suspension Rates
- Largest gains among historically underserved students

#### A Scalable Solution

- SPMS as a Community School = replicable success story & scalable over time through all SP schools
  - Start with SPHS and using CS strategies to inform the CDS transition
  - Expand over time to all Elementary Schools
- All SPSD Community Schools can become the "community hubs," aligning local needs and assets
- Our Community Schools will further the mission of community organizations, businesses, government, AND schools, bringing everyone together to collaborate
- We serve the same families: let's support them through this model at all levels!

#### Financial Implications

- Community schools need:
  - School Board and Administrator commitment
  - Coordinated vision and approach
  - Dedicated positions to coordinate the strategy
  - Given our potential for grant dollars, we anticipate the local commitment to be $100,000 or less in FY27
- Cost-effective: $1 invested = $7.11 in net benefits
