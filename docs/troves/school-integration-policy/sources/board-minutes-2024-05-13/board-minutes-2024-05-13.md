# South Portland Board of Education Regular Meeting Minutes — May 13, 2024

## Public Comment

Five citizens commented on the Boundaries and Configurations Steering Committee recommendations:

- Eleni Richardson
- Chelsea Byun
- Jenny Lupica
- Erin Masterson
- Julia Edwards

## Item 20: Attendance Zone Analysis

Ms. Schen moved to authorize the B&C committee recommendation for attendance zone analysis with outside experts. The motion did not move forward for lack of a second.

Item 20 was subsequently divided into Items 20A and 20B.

### Item 20B: Triennial Attendance Zone Review Policy

Motion for the School Board to approve a new policy requiring the Board to review and approve attendance zones every three years, with annual demographic reporting by administration by November 30 of every school year.

**Approved 7-0.**

### Item 20A: Attendance Zone Analysis

Amendment: Attendance zone analysis to be conducted with a consultant selected by a committee including the Superintendent, two Board members, and two residents.

**Amendment approved 5-2.**

**Amended recommendation approved 4-3.**

## Item 21: Brickhill Student Assignment Review

Motion for district administration to immediately initiate the process of reviewing the assignment of Brickhill students to Brown School, beginning with securing language-accessible input from Brickhill families. If Brickhill families are rezoned, current Brown School students should be able to complete their elementary education at Brown if their families so choose.

**Approved 7-0.**

## Item 22: Supporting Economically-Disadvantaged Students

Motion that schools with disproportionate numbers of economically disadvantaged students should receive more resources including staffing.

Amendment: Include a full report to the Board from the Superintendent by August 2024.

**Approved 7-0.**

## Item 23: Magnet Programs and Controlled Choice

Initial motion to explore magnet programs and controlled choice was postponed. **Postponement approved 7-0.**

Amendment by Ms. DeAngelis: "District administration, with participation (minimally) of two Board members, two residents and a vote of the Board will select a consultant to assist in garnering information about magnet programs that foster innovation and equity, and about controlled choice programs. The findings and the proposal should be presented to the board by January 30, 2025."

**Amendment approved 5-2.**

**Amended recommendation approved 4-3.**

## Item 24: Additional Topics

Motion for the Board to be informed about Pre-K expansion, districtwide PTA, and strategic planning with diverse community involvement.

**Approved 7-0.**

## Post-Vote Public Comment

- Chelsea Byun commented on the benefits of hiring consultants.
- Jenny Lupica encouraged involving more elementary school parents.
- Eleni Richardson thanked the Board for revisiting Item 20.

## Adjournment

Meeting adjourned at 9:06pm. **Approved 7-0.**
