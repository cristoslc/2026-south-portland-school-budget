### Reactions

Well, that was a meaty rules discussion - exactly the kind of governance housekeeping that needs doing but never feels urgent until something breaks. The committee budget process finally gives us a real framework instead of ad hoc requests scattered through department budgets. Scott did good work on that policy structure, though I'm watching how it plays out with Human Rights Commission - they've had consistent funding needs that might not fit neatly into that $2,500 operating cap.

The Zoom comment debate was predictably thorny. Misha laid out the First Amendment constraints pretty clearly - we learned this the hard way during COVID when the trolls found us. Much as I'd love the accessibility, you can't selectively mute people in an open comment period, and registration doesn't really solve anything when people can just make up names and addresses. Dick's right that we're all accessible by email and phone, but I get why people want that real-time voice in the room, especially on winter nights like this.

Really impressed with Carter Scott's questions tonight - asking for a table of contents, pushing on the committee review process, getting her bearings on all the facilities. She's got that systematic mindset we need more of. And her point about staff pride and competence rings true - our department heads really do punch above their weight given some of the infrastructure challenges they're working with.
