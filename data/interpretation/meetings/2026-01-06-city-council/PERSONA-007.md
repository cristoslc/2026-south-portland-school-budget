### Reactions

Well, that was a relatively light agenda before the storm hits. Scott's timeline for budget season is making it real - we're just over a month away from having to give guidance on tax increases without knowing half of what we'll need to know. The February 10th workshop with the school board is going to be brutal as usual. At least we'll have had the school board's public forum on January 22nd by then, though I'm not holding my breath that we'll get much beyond the usual "don't cut anything but don't raise taxes" feedback.

The property tax relief task force update was actually encouraging. Finally, some recognition at the state level that this isn't just a local problem we can solve by being more efficient. We've cut everything we can reasonably cut, and state funding hasn't kept pace with mandates for years. If they can come up with real solutions that don't just shift the burden around, that would be the first good budget news we've had in years.

Union negotiations in executive session right now tells me we're going to be dealing with personnel cost increases that we can't control, on top of everything else. That's always the tightest squeeze - you can't negotiate away health insurance premium increases or required wage steps, but those are exactly the costs that drive our budget problems. At least the cemetery ordinance passed smoothly - sometimes the small administrative fixes actually work the way they're supposed to.
