# Cumulative Record: Jaylen (PERSONA-012)
## Meeting: School Board Special Budget Meeting -- March 30, 2026 -- 2026-03-30

### Interpretation

March 30 is the night the art cut stopped being a number on a slide. Hannah — the art teacher at SPHS — gets to that microphone at 9:30 at night and tells the board she has already been laid off by this district once before, and now it is happening again. She talks about students who don't speak English yet, for whom art class is the one place they can actually participate. She says "to strip students of their access to art is to strip them of their humanity." Nobody on the board tells her she is keeping her job. The meeting keeps moving.

Jaylen wrote down "0.5 FTE reduction in art" from the March 23 slide and filed it with two science teachers and an English teacher and special ed case managers and the rest of Glenn's five-minute presentation with zero follow-up questions. He knew it was a person. Now it is Hannah, and it is her second time, and she is not going to be the last SPHS cut that has a face on it — he just doesn't know the others yet. That gap between budget category and what it costs has been the through-line since December. It has arrived at his school specifically.

The budget failing 5-2 is the first structural surprise in months. He has been watching this train move since December 17 — pink slips already out, countdown locked. Then five board members vote no. Richardson named what she couldn't support: PE gone from the middle school, percussion gone, behavior support gone. Holman said arts losses worry him most and then acknowledged he didn't know if anything could change. Naming the problem correctly without having the votes to fix it is Councilor Walker's move. It keeps happening in both buildings.

Matthew Emory from the middle school just walked up to that microphone and said what Jaylen has been trying to say for four months: most kids care about band and art, not science. He got two minutes. That is the model. The public comment slot at 9:30 PM is the only real seat in this process — not the formal student representative role that gets you sent home to study mid-meeting, not the forum where Angela and Alex sat without speaking, not the superintendent search precedent that didn't transfer. The voluntary insertion. Jaylen already knows where he is going on April 7.

Five hours. Still no AP teachers named. Theater status still unconfirmed. "Prepared for change" is not an answer for senior year planning.
