### Reactions

Okay, so I knew going into this meeting that the costs were going to be higher than the original estimates, but $194 million? Are you kidding me? That's like asking every family in South Portland to take out another mortgage. The tax impact alone would be over $1,100 a year for us - that's groceries for three months or dance lessons for both kids. And that's on top of everything else going up.

I actually got excited hearing about all the library improvements - the kids would love having that much more space and programming. But then reality hit when I started doing the math. We're already stretching to afford dance and soccer fees, and now they want us to basically pay for a palace? I mean, I get that the buildings need work, but this feels like they went from "let's fix what's broken" to "let's build the Taj Mahal." 

What really bothered me was hearing that parent talk about how the police station location would scare away immigrant families. I hadn't even thought about that, but she's right - if you're already nervous about accessing city services, having police cars right there isn't exactly welcoming. At least some of the counselors seem to get that this price tag is just not realistic. But now I'm worried about what "bare bones" actually means. Are we going to end up with something that doesn't really solve the problems, or are they going to find a way to keep the good parts for our kids while making it actually affordable?
