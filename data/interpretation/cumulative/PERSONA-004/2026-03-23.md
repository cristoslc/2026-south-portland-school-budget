# Cumulative Record: Marcus (PERSONA-004)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop -- March 23, 2026 -- 2026-03-23

### Interpretation

Three months of tracking a number — 42 — and March 23 is the meeting that finally splits it open. Thirty-two colleagues have no position in the new staffing plan. They're on a recall list, first in line if someone quits or retires. Eleven more are getting involuntary transfers. The aggregate I've been citing since December was always a compression of individual fates, and now those fates have categories.

But the finance director is what I'll be sitting with for a long time. Ketchem said it plainly: labor costs, even with nothing beyond contractual step increases, grow faster than 6% every year. Personnel is the overwhelming majority of the budget. The board set a 6% ceiling. The state funds 20% of what it owes. She called it "mathematically impossible not to have a problem" and kept going. I've been fighting this as an emergency with a solution — better state funding, a higher ceiling, a different administration. She's describing a machine that generates this outcome on schedule. That changes the strategic question entirely. The April 7 hearing still matters. The June 9 referendum still matters. But if the underlying math is permanent, what are we actually winning when we win?

Prince publicly acknowledging union leaders — "deep knowledge, care, and partnership" — is the most direct institutional validation the association has received since this started. The joint communication before individual notifications was the right process, and it matters that it happened. But it happened alongside 32 people on a recall list. You can be consulted and still lose.

The seniority challenge from Blair Bacon is the thing I can't put down. Two national board certifications, a master's in literacy, multilingual endorsement, 25 years. Lost her position to a July 2023 hire date. She's right that "Contracts not closures" lands differently when your contract is the reason you're being closed out. I understand why seniority protection exists — without it, administration picks whoever they want for whatever reason they want. But Bacon just became the most sympathetic counter-example anyone could ask for, and she'll be cited in every argument against us between now and the referendum. The principle is still right. The terrain just got harder.

And Jen Fletcher: 378 days ago at the same podium, the same fight, the same percussion ed tech position. The community showed up last year and the board restored it. It's back on the list. Administration doesn't revise its analysis — it waits for the next cycle.
