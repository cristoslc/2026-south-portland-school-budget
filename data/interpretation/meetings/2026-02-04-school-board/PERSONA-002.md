### Reactions

Honestly, this was the most useful budget meeting I've attended in years. They finally put the real numbers on the table - $7.2 million gap, fund balance gone, 18.95% tax increase if we do nothing. No sugar-coating, no "we'll find efficiencies somewhere." The enrollment versus staffing chart was particularly telling - you can see exactly where we hired up to meet the asylum seeker enrollment spike but then kept hiring even as those numbers came down.

What concerns me is that health insurance estimate jumping from 8% to 12% in just a few weeks. If they're revising major assumptions this late in the process, it makes me wonder what else might shift. And even with all three of their "big ideas" - the elementary reconfiguration, the middle/high school efficiency reviews, the operational changes - they're still looking at over $3 million that has to come from somewhere.

I do appreciate that they're being transparent about the constraints. Like explaining why the middle school bell time consolidation got pulled - permitting and traffic studies that can't happen by September. That's the kind of realistic timeline assessment I want to see. The new Q&A system where they'll post data-driven responses to community questions is exactly what this process has been missing. No more emotional appeals - just show us the numbers and let us make informed decisions.
