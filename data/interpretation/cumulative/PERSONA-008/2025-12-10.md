# Cumulative Record: Rachel (PERSONA-008)
## Meeting: School Board Workshop -- December 10, 2025 -- 2025-12-10

### Interpretation

Just two days after learning about the $7.2 million budget crisis and potential redistricting, I'm watching this board make decisions that confirm my worst suspicions about how they view parents like me. They're spending over $30,000 we don't have on a superintendent search firm that explicitly excludes community members from the actual candidate selection process. This isn't just about money - it's about power and who gets to make decisions that affect our children.

The timing couldn't be worse. We're facing massive budget cuts, potential school closures lurking in the background, and talk of restarting redistricting discussions, yet the board chooses the most expensive search option that gives parents the least voice in selecting who will lead us through this crisis. When Board Chair DeAngelo commented that the empty meeting room meant they could make whatever decision they wanted, it crystallized everything wrong with their approach. Maybe more parents would engage if they felt their input actually mattered.

What's particularly galling is how they framed community involvement as a "pain point" to be avoided rather than a resource to be valued. Richardson's emphasis on building board "consensus" while shutting out parent voices tells me they see us as obstacles rather than partners. This is exactly the kind of top-down decision-making that creates the problems they're now trying to solve.

The silver lining is that school closures still haven't been mentioned, even as they discuss the challenges facing the next superintendent. But I can't shake the feeling that they're planning to spring major changes on us after they get their handpicked leader in place. I need to start organizing other parents now, because this board has made it clear they don't value our input in the formal process.
