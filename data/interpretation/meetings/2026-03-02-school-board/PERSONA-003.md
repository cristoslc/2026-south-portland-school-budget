### Reactions

OMG this meeting was intense. I watched it online after putting the kids to bed and I'm honestly kind of freaking out. Like, I knew there were budget problems but I had no idea they were talking about completely changing how elementary schools work by the time my 2-year-old gets to kindergarten.

The whole thing felt so overwhelming - nine different options with all these spreadsheets and numbers, but what really got to me was when they started talking about closing schools and making little kids switch buildings after first grade. The top plan they recommended would literally have my daughter go to one school for pre-K and kindergarten, then switch to a totally different school for second through fourth grade. And then off to middle school! That's like four different schools before she's even 10 years old.

But honestly, some of the board members gave me hope. When that one guy talked about how closing Skillin would basically force all the immigrant families to bus their kids across town while the wealthy families get to keep their neighborhood schools - like, thank god someone gets it. And seeing all those parents at public comment talking about how this would destroy their morning routines and community connections... I mean, we chose to live here specifically for the schools, and now they might not even exist by the time we need them. I'm definitely going to that Red Bank meeting if I can find childcare.
