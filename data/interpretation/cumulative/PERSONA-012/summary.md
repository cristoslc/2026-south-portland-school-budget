# Cumulative Summary: Jaylen (PERSONA-012)

### Current Understanding

As of March 9, 2026, Jaylen has mapped the South Portland school budget crisis in comprehensive, compounding detail. The core facts have been constant since December: a $7.2M gap, 78-position elimination including 42 teachers, and a depleted fund balance. He now understands the crisis has two dimensions — structural (state funds only 20% of costs against a required 55%, declining enrollment, 12% health insurance increase, $99K athletic complex debt payment) and internal management (34 audit journal entries, illegal overspending, $400K in misused grants, six finance directors in five years).

The city council, a layer of government he barely registered before December, was constructing its austerity posture in closed sessions as early as January. The 4-6% budget ceiling issued at the February 10 joint session was not a unilateral constraint — Chair DeAngelis explicitly solicited it on February 3. Three "big ideas" save roughly $4M; a $3M+ residual gap remains for line-by-line review across all cost centers including the high school. SPHS-specific impacts have not been disclosed; high school workshops were deferred to late March.

Student voice has been systematically ornamental across both school and city governance — with one meaningful exception: student representatives Angela and Alex were approved for superintendent search executive sessions on February 9. Theater, music, and robotics are currently active and thriving, but are not explicitly protected from the residual cuts.

The immigration enforcement crisis is not a parallel thread — it is the same crisis: the families most threatened by budget cuts are the families missing school and at risk of eviction, and the council voted 4-2 on February 17 to leave them exposed. Jaylen cannot vote in the November referendum, and 80% of voters have no children in schools. His procedural exclusion and his democratic exclusion are the same condition at different scales.
