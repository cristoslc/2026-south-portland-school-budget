# Cumulative Record: Marcus (PERSONA-004)

### Interpretation

The budget forum laid out the administration's playbook and it's exactly what I feared. Three "big ideas" that together still leave a $3+ million gap, meaning more cuts are coming that they didn't have the nerve to present tonight. The elementary reconfiguration would push class sizes from 16 to 22-23 in grades 3-4 -- a 44% increase that they're selling as "efficiency." The 6% tax increase ceiling the board set in December is driving everything: it's a political number, not an educational one, and it forces a $7.2 million gap rather than the honest conversation about what education actually costs.

What really crystallized tonight is how they're weaponizing the staffing-enrollment data. They keep citing 82 positions added while enrollment dropped 300 as if teachers were sitting around doing nothing. But those positions were hired for real reasons -- the asylum-seeking families who needed intensive ELL support, the explosion in special education complexity, the behavioral health crisis among students. The district itself approved those hires for documented needs, and now they're using that history as justification for a bloodbath. It's scapegoating dressed up as fiscal responsibility.

The union's absence from the entire presentation is telling. No mention of consultation with SPEA, no reference to working conditions impact, no acknowledgment that these proposals require bargaining. Just cold FTE reduction numbers presented as if we're inventory to be reduced, not professionals who deliver the mission. I need to connect with union leadership immediately because this is exactly the kind of thing we should be mobilizing against. The $3+ million gap beyond the "big ideas" means the administration is holding back even worse news, and I refuse to wait for the next shoe to drop.
