# Cumulative Record: Linda (PERSONA-007)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop -- March 23, 2026 -- 2026-03-23

### Interpretation

The thing that stayed with me longest from tonight wasn't the Title VI question, though that's the one keeping me up. It was Abigail saying, in a public meeting with a full transcript, that the district had no fund balance threshold policy. Seven finance directors in six years. Those are the words I've been carrying since December 8, when the $1.9 million hole appeared out of what we'd been calling a rosy scenario. I've been waiting for the honest accounting to land in a room where the community could hear it. Tonight it did.

But Abigail also said what I already know and what the public doesn't fully absorb: FY27 resets the path and does not solve the core problems. Labor costs grow more than 6% per year by contract even with no new hires. Utilities at 13–14% actual inflation. Three hundred thousand more in FY28 debt service from the athletic field bond alone. LD 2666 offering no reliable estimate with only two of nine provisions modeled. We are asking the community to absorb the 6% ceiling this year. They need to understand we will be back.

Then Jess Elsner stood up and asked about Title VI. Kayler is 45% BIPOC and 30–35% multilingual learners. She asked how closing that school doesn't violate the Civil Rights Act of 1964. In March, when I voted against Skillin closure on equity grounds — 33% multilingual learners, 72% walkability, the west side deserved its neighborhood school — I was reasoning politically and geographically. What I did not fully reckon with is that Kayler has equal or greater demographic exposure on a different legal axis. Rosemarie's answer was the right one for a board member in that moment: I don't have the answer, we need legal counsel. But we need that opinion in writing before the March 30 vote, not after we've acted.

We adjourned at 11:15 without the three Special Meeting votes — school closing report, Option A/B, budget adoption — and I think it was the right call. We are still missing the legal memo. We have no framework for the A-versus-B decision beyond "it's essentially a board decision." And Daniel's fund balance question just hung there: no rebuild plan, because this year is too dire. I accept that answer for one year. I cannot accept it as a permanent posture. April 7 does not move.
