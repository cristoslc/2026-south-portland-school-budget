### Reactions

Well that was a marathon. Three and a half hours to basically say we'll put police at Walgreens and argue about everything else. The police station announcement was the only clean win of the night - finally we can tell voters exactly what they're voting for on that piece.

But Jesus, the fire station turned into a mess. Natalie did her homework - I'll give her that. The response time data and inundation maps are real concerns for a 75-year building. But bringing this up now, after we've spent two years and God knows how much on design work? The timing is awful. We either stick with the plan and ask voters for $87 million while the school board is cutting $8 million, or we punt and look indecisive. Neither option is great politics.

The library situation is what's going to keep me up tonight. Kevin laid out the case perfectly - 27 visitors per hour, can't upgrade anything without hitting asbestos, the building is falling apart. But we just postponed the orders that would formally remove it from Mahoney without any alternative plan. We're basically telling the community "your library is falling down but we'll figure it out later." That's not governing, that's procrastinating. And those usage numbers - 13,000 people in two months - this isn't some sleepy backwater service. These are our constituents who deserve better than "we'll deal with it eventually."
