However, four board members have publicly rejected that option, and David is suspicious that the analysis was steered toward reconfiguration by leaving the no-change alternative unmodeled. Member Feller's willingness to challenge the 6% tax ceiling with honest math and propose creative solutions like early retirement buyouts represented the kind of pragmatic trade-off analysis David values most. As of March 9, with three budget workshops and a council session scheduled through April 14, David sees a process that has matured significantly but faces a decisive six-week window where analytical rigor must translate into actual decisions.
