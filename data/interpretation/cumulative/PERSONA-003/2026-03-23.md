# Cumulative Record: Jess (PERSONA-003)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop -- March 23, 2026 -- 2026-03-23

### Interpretation

This meeting did something to my pre-K anchor. I've been holding onto pre-K expansion since January as the one thing in this entire budget saga that was clearly getting better -- state mandates, physical space needs, partnership funding, now even a new Early Childhood Strategist position. All of that is still real. But I also found out tonight that the district had originally planned to grow its own locally-funded pre-K seats in FY27 and then quietly pulled back. The 64 seats the district runs directly? Flat. The "expansion" is entirely external: CDS slots for kids who qualify for special education services, a United Way classroom that isn't even in a district building, a Head Start partnership that's income-eligible and still pending. I've spent months building this thread into my reason for staying, and for the first time it genuinely feels like it might not have a spot in it for us.

Then the finance director said the thing I most dreaded hearing. She warned that the FY27 budget "resets our financial path but does not solve our core problems," and she went down a list -- labor contracts that automatically exceed the 6% ceiling, utilities rising 13-14%, new debt service, possibly a boiler replacement. My daughter starts kindergarten in 2028. She wasn't speaking abstractly. She was speaking about the year my family is planning around. She also mentioned it herself: she is the seventh finance director in six years. I knew from February it was six in five. It got worse.

Kayler Elementary is now the specific school named for closure in the administration's recommendation. That is a real school with a real name, and four board members who had been pushing back on closures, specifically on equity grounds, apparently shaped the options enough that it's Kayler and not Skillin. The equity argument held, at least in framing the choice. But the board didn't vote. After five hours, at 11:15 PM, they adjourned on three action items -- closure authorization, configuration choice, budget adoption -- without taking any of them. The process is technically still open. That is the one thing tonight that isn't a loss. It is a very small one.
