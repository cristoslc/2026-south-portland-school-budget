### Reactions

Well, that was a hell of a thing. Three and a half hours and I'm not sure we got much closer to a real solution, but at least most of the councilors seem to understand that regular folks can't afford these crazy increases they're talking about.

The school people came in asking for anywhere from 6% to almost 10% just for their piece, and thank God most of the council told them that's not happening. When that one guy got up and said his property assessment went up $27,000 - mine went up about the same - you could see people nodding. We're getting hammered from both ends: higher assessments AND higher rates. Meanwhile the school enrollment is way down but somehow they added 82 people since 2017? And they can't even tell us what those people do?

What really got me was when they couldn't answer basic questions about where all this money is going. The union president got up and basically said it's not the teachers' fault, and I believe her - it sounds like most of these new positions aren't even classroom teachers. But if the administration can't tell us what 82 people have been doing for the past seven years, how can they come in here with a straight face asking for more? The councilors who've been around longer seemed just as frustrated as I was. At least we got them down to something closer to reality, but even 4-5% is still going to hurt when everything else is going up too.
