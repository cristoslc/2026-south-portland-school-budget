### Reactions

Look, I get why they bought the Walgreens site - it's the right call geographically and gets the police station issue off the table. But the fire station discussion was frustrating to watch. Councilor West actually did the analysis, looked at response times, studied the flooding maps, and came back with real alternatives. The facilities director basically admitted we've been running these buildings into the ground for decades with zero preventive maintenance.

What bothers me is that they're rushing toward an $80-something million bond without answering basic questions. Will it be one question or split? How do we address the fact that the eastern part of the city doesn't get adequate fire coverage? And nobody wants to talk about what this does to property taxes on top of the school budget mess. The library director's presentation was actually impressive - he had real data on usage and could explain exactly why simple fixes cost so much in that building. But then council just punts all the hard decisions to "later."

I'm probably voting yes on public safety because we clearly need it, but I wish they'd been more honest about trade-offs instead of just barreling ahead. The facilities didn't get this bad overnight, and the solutions aren't going to be simple or cheap.
