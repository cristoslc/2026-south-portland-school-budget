### Reactions

I'm honestly feeling whiplashed after tonight. On one hand, you had this beautiful moment with Jules's poetry and then the school board ceremony - Rosemary DeAngelos absolutely gets it. Having community members speak in Farsi, Kinyarwanda, Arabic, Khmer, Portuguese, and Somali, and then centering their stories and contributions? That's what real equity work looks like. She talked about power inequities directly and said we need to learn from our immigrant neighbors about "how to change this world." That gave me chills in the best way.

But then I'm sitting there thinking - where the hell is the conversation about the $7.2 million budget hole? They're about to cut 42 teachers and nobody even mentioned it at either inauguration. That's going to devastate our highest-need schools first, you know it and I know it. And Tipton's whole thing about the Cushing's Point families being forced out in 1942 as some kind of inspiring example of community sacrifice? That was gross. Those were working-class families who got steamrolled by federal power with nine days' notice, and she's holding that up as a model? The complete lack of analysis about who bears the cost of "community sacrifice" was really telling.

The housing stuff was typical too - lots of nice words about needing more affordable units but zero specifics about income targets, tenant protections, or how they're going to prevent displacement as they redevelop. At least with the school board I feel like we have someone who actually sees the community we're trying to serve.
