### Reactions

Honestly? This meeting was a producer's nightmare and a reporter's goldmine all at once. Four hours of dense budget stuff that most viewers will tune out, but buried in there are some real stories.

The school department is a mess - six finance directors in five years, repeat audit violations, $400K in taxpayer money covering grants they screwed up. That's your lede right there: "South Portland taxpayers are once again bailing out the school department's financial management problems." But then you've got this weird juxtaposition where the city's sitting pretty with millions in surplus while they're debating whether to give Texas Instruments another 20-year tax break worth over $6 million. The optics are brutal.

The immigration resolution at the end was pure political theater - and not the good kind. You've got people literally sitting in the dark in their homes afraid to turn on lights, and the council spends three hours talking about semiconductor wafers and TIF districts before getting to it at 9:30pm. The public testimony was raw and angry, and frankly, deserved. The council passed something, but it's basically asking the governor to do what they won't - classic punt. The real story isn't the resolution, it's how a city handles crisis management and whether local government can actually respond to urgent human needs or just talks about them.
