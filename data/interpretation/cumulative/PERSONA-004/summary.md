# Cumulative Summary: Marcus (PERSONA-004)

### Current Understanding

Marcus understands the South Portland school budget crisis as a three-month arc that has moved from deliberation to execution. As of March 19, 2026, 72 RIF notices have been issued — described as only the first wave — against a structural gap now stated at $8.4 million, grown from the $7.2 million floor first identified in December 2025. The fund balance is fully depleted, the district is functionally insolvent, and FY26 itself ended in confirmed deficit across all scenarios. The 78 proposed position eliminations — 42 teachers, 16 ed techs, 2 administrators — bear disproportionately on frontline educators for a crisis rooted in structural financial infrastructure failure: six finance directors in five years, repeat audit material weakness, and legally non-compliant budget overspending. The 82 positions added while enrollment dropped 300, routinely cited as evidence of mismanagement, were in fact mostly classroom-facing ed techs and teachers hired for documented complex student needs — ELL, special education, behavioral health — now proposed for elimination precisely because of that work.

The city council has been institutionally absent throughout, its engagement limited to procedural announcements, a single joint session, and routine business conducted alongside a $194 million building project, an $87 million public safety bond, and annual sewer rate increases of approximately 22% — all drawing from the same finite pool of voter goodwill the June 9 school referendum requires. ICE enforcement has suppressed community participation and degraded classroom attendance. Union impact bargaining rights remain the primary structural leverage, and an early retirement buyout proposal (41 eligible employees, potential $1 million-plus in savings) is the only creative alternative to forced layoffs yet put forward. The budget calendar is locked: April 7 public hearing, April 14 school budget workshop, May 5 council vote, June 9 referendum. The April 7 hearing is the last real intervention point.
