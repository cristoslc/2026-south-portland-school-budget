### Reactions

Mom asked what I thought about the budget news after she saw something online, so I watched that whole long meeting. Honestly? I'm kind of mad. 

They spent like two hours arguing about fire stations and police stations and then just... voted the library out of getting fixed. Just like that. The library director guy seemed really upset - he said this is the second time they've studied what the library needs and then done nothing about it. And I get why he's mad! He showed them that like 27 people go there every single hour, and they have all these problems like asbestos under the carpet and locks that break and cost hundreds of dollars to fix. But apparently fixing the library isn't as important as building new buildings for the police.

The weirdest part is they kept talking about how they can't afford all this stuff, but then they're still planning to ask people to vote for $87 million just for police and fire buildings. And everyone was worried about how much that would raise taxes on top of what's happening with the school budget. One of the adults said some people might get "voted out of their houses" because they can't afford higher taxes. Like, that's terrifying.

I don't understand why they can't just ask students what we actually need before making these huge decisions. The library director at least brought actual numbers - how many people use it, what's broken, what it would cost to fix. But for schools they just talk about cuts and tax increases without asking us what we'd miss most or what actually matters for learning.
