### Reactions

The big story here is that they're finally telling the truth about the budget reality - DeAngelis opened by saying this will be "another difficult budget year" requiring "creative ways to save money," and Holman was blunt about being "likely to be in the red" despite the spending freeze. That's a different tone than we've been getting, and it sets up the January 22nd forum as potentially more consequential than the usual dog-and-pony show.

But the more immediate story is what's happening right now with ICE enforcement - kids missing school, staff afraid to come to work, and the district scrambling to figure out protocols in real time. Multiple speakers said children didn't go to school because their families were scared, and the union president said staff are also feeling unsafe. That's not abstract policy debate - that's kids missing learning today because of fear. Richardson pushed for faster training and clearer guidance, which seems like the bare minimum response.

The middle school field thing is just maddening - we're still arguing about who pays to fix a field that should have been delivered correctly in the first place, while the superintendent search keeps nickel-and-diming us with fees that should have been disclosed upfront. Richardson was right to call out the extra charges. These are the kinds of process failures that make people cynical about how the district handles money, which matters when you're about to ask for another tax increase.
