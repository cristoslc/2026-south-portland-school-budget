# Cumulative Record: Tom (PERSONA-006)
## Meeting: Regular -- January 12, 2026

### Interpretation

Another month, another confirmation that nobody's minding the store. The chair opened by reminding everyone that schools eat 61% of our property taxes -- yeah, we know -- and warning about "another difficult budget year." Then she talks about needing "creative ways to save money." Creative? How about spending what you have? That would be plenty creative for this board.

The finance committee reported they've already burned through 92% of their budget and we're only halfway through the fiscal year. Ninety-two percent at the fifty percent mark. That's not budget management, that's a spending problem dressed up in a spreadsheet. And while they're crying about deficits, they're approving $3,500 for national superintendent search advertising and $1,600 per candidate for premium background checks. This on top of the $30,000 consultant they hired last month. The superintendent search tab keeps growing while they tell the rest of us to tighten our belts.

The one bright spot was hearing about seven retirements -- teachers and counselors -- and the superintendent saying positions won't automatically be backfilled. That's the first time I've heard anyone in this district talk about right-sizing staff to match actual enrollment. Board member Feller floated early retirement buyouts that could save over a million dollars, which is real money. But then he turned around and suggested the tax increase "may need to exceed the 6% guidance." So which is it? Are we saving money or spending more? I'll be watching to see whether those retirement positions actually stay vacant or whether they quietly hire replacements when nobody's looking.
