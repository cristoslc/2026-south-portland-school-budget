# Cumulative Record: Tom (PERSONA-006)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- December 4, 2025 -- 2025-12-04

### Interpretation

Looking back at December 4th with everything I now know, this meeting reads like a preview of every disappointment that followed. Four days before the school board would drop the $1.9 million deficit bombshell -- which would itself get revised to $7.2 million two weeks after that -- the city council spent two hours talking about Zoom comment procedures and appointing people to regional boards. Not a word about the school budget. Not a word about property taxes. Two hours.

The committee budget process they approved that night bothers me more now than it probably should have then. Every board and committee gets up to $2,500 a year plus unlimited "special project" money, folded into the council budget. That's a new pipeline for spending, approved with barely any discussion, while the district was already silently burning through its fund balance. Councilor Walker voted against it -- the only one with sense -- and nobody asked the obvious question: where does this money come from when the overall budget is already under pressure?

What this meeting tells me, now that I've watched the whole crisis unfold, is that the council was in the dark or chose to stay there. The school board knew by early December that they had a serious problem -- they'd present it formally on the 8th, four days later. The council had no idea, or at least showed no sign of it. That gap in communication between the two bodies is part of why the whole thing spiraled. When the council finally weighed in at the joint workshop in February, they were months behind the curve and playing catch-up.

Councilor West at least mentioned building maintenance reserves -- deteriorating police and fire facilities -- which now sounds like one more cost pressure in a long queue behind school deficits, city health insurance increases, and rising solid waste costs. Every infrastructure need he mentioned that night is still sitting there, competing for the same overtaxed homeowners. The council was describing future problems while a present crisis was already building one meeting room over. Nobody connected the dots.
