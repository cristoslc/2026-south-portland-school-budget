# Market Segmentation Research Guide for 2025

*Source: Brandspeak — https://brandspeak.co.uk/blog/how-to-conduct-market-segmentation-research/*

## Two Main Approaches to Segmentation Research

### A Priori Segmentation
Groups people according to **predetermined factors** based on deductive logic. Uses direct variables like age, gender, or frequency of use. Straightforward to implement using existing data or simple surveys.

### Post-Hoc Segmentation
Analyzes research data to **derive segments without deciding on them in advance**. Suits situations where you're exploring attitudes, opinions, or motivations — resulting in richer, more nuanced customer groupings.

## Conducting Post-Hoc Research (The Detailed Process)

### Step 1: Qualitative Foundation
Start with exploratory research through focus groups or depth interviews. This guidance shapes questionnaire design and ensures the survey captures relevant variables using customer-resonant language.

### Step 2: Survey Design
Create questionnaires covering all differentiating factors aligned with your objectives.

### Step 3: Analysis
Apply multivariate techniques:
- **Factor analysis** — to reduce variables to underlying dimensions
- **Cluster analysis** — grouping respondents into homogeneous segments

The goal is creating "segments that contain people who are as similar as possible within each segment, and as different as possible to people in other segments."

## Critical Best Practices

### Segment Count
Aim for **4–8 segments**, ideally 5–6. Too few create unwieldy groups; too many become impractical and overly similar.

### Sample Size
Maintain minimum segment sizes of **n=200**. For six segments, expect at least 1,200 respondents total.

### Naming Segments
Assign descriptive names (e.g., "low-end budgeters") rather than generic labels like "Segment A." This creates mental clarity for implementation teams.

### Actionability
Segments must be **targetable** using demographic identifiers or real-world identifiers. A segment that cannot be reached is not useful.

### Internal vs. External Validity
Validate segments internally (do they hold together statistically?) and externally (do they respond differently to actual marketing stimuli?).
