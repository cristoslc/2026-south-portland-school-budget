# Cumulative Record: Tom (PERSONA-006)
## Meeting: City Council Goal Setting Workshop -- January 15, 2026 -- 2026-01-15

### Interpretation

Two days after sitting in that room and hearing an architect say he wasn't "confident" in his own $194 million estimate for the Mahoney project, I'm back watching the city council do goal-setting. Annual goal-setting. In January. While a $7.2 million school deficit is being worked through and a $194 million capital project is sitting in a design phase eating fees.

Every fiscal number that surfaced in this workshop I already know by heart: the 18–19% roll-forward that the board capped at 6%, the 23% enrollment drop that coincided with 82 new staff positions, the $26,651 per-pupil cost that's the highest among comparable districts, the state covering 20% when the formula says 55%, the fund balance that's essentially zero. I've been carrying these numbers since December. What I was watching for at this meeting was whether the council would translate any of that into something binding -- a spending ceiling, a referendum threshold, an explicit fiscal posture going into budget season.

What I got was an agenda item. An attached document. No recorded motions, no public deliberation, no commitments in the available record.

I keep returning to the same pattern. December 4: two hours on Zoom procedures while the school board was four days from announcing a $1.9 million deficit. December 9: verbal alarm from a councilor about the TIF deal, unanimous yes vote. January 13: most of them said $194 million was a non-starter, no binding ceiling set. And now January 15: goal-setting with no public output. The council has been present at every alarm bell this winter and produced enforceable action exactly once -- the 3-6% guidance at the February 10 joint workshop, which I was grateful for. One meeting in four months where the words translated into something you could actually hold them to.

I'm not writing the council off. The February guidance mattered. But I'd be fooling myself if I called this workshop anything other than another entry in the same ledger: concern without commitment, words without ceilings. When the budget referendum lands in front of me, I need to remember how many of these meetings I attended and what they produced.
