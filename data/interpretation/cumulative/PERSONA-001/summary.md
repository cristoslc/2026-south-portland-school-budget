# Cumulative Summary: Maria (PERSONA-001)

### Current Understanding

As of March 9, 2026, Maria has a comprehensive and grim understanding of the South Portland school budget crisis. The district faces a $7.2 million structural gap for FY27, driven by a 23% drop in elementary enrollment (from 1,401 to 1,080 students in four years), a state funding system covering only 20% of actual costs against a statutory 55% obligation, and years of internal financial mismanagement — including six finance directors in five years, repeated material weaknesses in financial reconciliations, and illegal budget overruns. The fund balance is fully depleted. The current year is also running deficits: 92% of the budget spent with only 67% of the fiscal year elapsed.

The board has advanced reconfiguration of the five K-4 elementary schools into grade-band campuses (pre-K–1 and grades 2–4) as its primary strategy, estimating $2 million in savings from nine specific options presented at the March 2 workshop. Skillin School is protected by near-consensus among board members. Seventy-eight positions — including 42 teachers and 16 educational technicians — are proposed for elimination. City council budget guidance came in at 3–6% (mostly 4–5%), below the school board's own requested floor, guaranteeing cuts deeper than any scenario the board had previously modeled.

Maria no longer believes the process is being conducted in good faith: the chair endorsed reconfiguration as "fabulous" before any formal proposal existed; parents were never surveyed; the superintendent search was explicitly designed to exclude community input; near-unanimous parent opposition and teacher union pushback have not altered the board's direction. Immigrant children are literally absent from classrooms whose fate is being decided — ICE enforcement disrupted the one forum meant to give their families voice, the council voted down an eviction moratorium on a 4–2 vote from the same bloc that constrained the school budget, and Project Home's emergency fund was projected to be exhausted within days of February 17. Maria is now strategically patient — maintaining her advocacy network and waiting for the March 23 workshop to narrow nine options to a recommendation — but holds no expectation that the outcome will reflect what parents have said.
