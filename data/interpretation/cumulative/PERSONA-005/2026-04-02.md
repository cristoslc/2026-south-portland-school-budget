# Cumulative Record: Priya (PERSONA-005)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- April 2, 2026 -- 2026-04-02

### Interpretation

The March 30 meeting gave me the achievement gap numbers I had been demanding since December — 22%, 37%, 32% — and then deployed them as a slideshow and moved on. I called it "equity as graphic design." April 2 is where that phrase gets its full meaning, because now I can see the complete picture of what the equity infrastructure looks like after the cuts: the DEI Director demoted to instructional strategist for twenty to thirty thousand dollars in savings, the McKinney-Vento liaisons and community partner staff zeroed out of local funding entirely — 3.5 positions, nearly $400,000, now dependent on a state grant that exists at the state's pleasure — and the ESOL team down two teachers, which no one mentioned once in five hours of budget discussion. The equity commitments are in the mission statement. The people charged with implementing them are gone.

The behavioral specialist's statement cut through everything else. Sixty students, forty formal behavior plans, a 23% IEP identification rate already higher than comparable districts. She named the preventative logic exactly: remove the middle layer of early intervention and kids either get nothing or they get fast-tracked into special education, which costs more and triggers more cuts next year. This is the reactive model I have been watching the district slide toward since the ed tech eliminations first appeared on the list in December. Richardson said on the record that eliminating that position "essentially forces kids to special ed." That was March 30. It is still on the cut list. And now we have the behavioral specialist herself confirming the math.

I have to hold the $300,000 alongside all of this. Union members drove to Augusta and came back with real money specifically for economically disadvantaged students and kids experiencing homelessness. That is not nothing. Richardson said publicly she wants it to go to frontline teachers and staff, not director positions. I want to trust that. But the district just eliminated the administrators who would have been accountable for tracking whether it reaches the students it was secured for. The McKinney-Vento liaisons who would have known which homeless kids needed it — gone. Meredith Diamond stood up and listed eight specific things the district should be tracking, disaggregated by race, income, disability, ELL status. The board said nothing. The $300,000 is real. The accountability infrastructure to verify where it lands is not.
