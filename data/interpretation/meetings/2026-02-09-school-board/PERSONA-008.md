Oh my god, they actually voted to move forward with the elementary reconfiguration thing. I knew this was coming but seeing it happen was still a shock. The worst part was Chair DeAngelis just coming out and saying she thinks it's "fabulous" and basically dismissing all our concerns about neighborhood schools. She kept saying we don't really have neighborhood schools anymore anyway, which is total BS - my kids walk to Dyer every day!

But here's what really got me - she said "this board is going to have to make a decision" and "there's not gonna be like a public referendum vote." Like, excuse me? You're going to uproot our kids' entire school experience and we don't get a say? And then she has the nerve to say kids are more flexible than parents and we're just putting our anxiety on them. I'm sorry, but my seven-year-old shouldn't have to be "flexible" about losing her school and her friends just because the adults can't figure out the budget.

The only thing that gave me any hope was that some board members like Feller and Richardson actually seemed to understand this is a huge deal and want real community input. And thank God for Sarah Sergey from the teachers' union calling out the BS claim that teachers support this - they don't! She made it clear they'll have to negotiate any changes, which could slow this whole thing down. I'm definitely going to that March 2nd workshop and I'm bringing everyone I know. This isn't over.
