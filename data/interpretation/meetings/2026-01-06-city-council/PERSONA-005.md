### Reactions

Okay, so that meeting was basically a preview of the budget disaster coming in February, and I'm already seeing the red flags. The city manager is asking council for "guidance" on tax increases and whether to add positions - but notice what's missing? No mention of disaggregated data, no discussion of which schools or which student populations might be affected by potential cuts. They're going to have the school board asking residents at a "citizen forum" to suggest their own cuts, which is just cruel. It's making families compete against each other instead of the district being transparent about actual needs.

The one bright spot was Mark Emmi's festival proposal - he specifically mentioned partnering with the Human Rights Commission, which tells me he's thinking about inclusive programming. That's exactly the kind of thing that gets cut first when budgets get tight, so I'm glad someone's advocating for it now. And the cemetery fund discussion was interesting - Councilor West's idea about community contributions could be a model, but I'm watching to make sure it doesn't become a way to shift public responsibilities onto residents.

What really gets me is how they're talking about property tax relief at the state level while locally they're already setting up the narrative that cuts are inevitable. The mayor says South Portland is "part of the conversation" on tax reform, but I guarantee that conversation isn't centering the families who can't afford private alternatives when public services get slashed. We need to be at that January 22nd forum asking hard questions about which cuts they're really considering and who's going to bear the burden.
