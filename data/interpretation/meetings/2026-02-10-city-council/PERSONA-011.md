### Reactions

OK guys, just got out of the joint budget meeting and this was ROUGH. Three hours of basically watching everyone realize there are no good options left. The superintendent laid out three scenarios and they're all terrible - we can either bankrupt taxpayers with an 18% increase or cut 78 jobs. Yes, 78 people losing their jobs, including 42 teachers.

The elementary school thing is happening - they're definitely moving forward with the reconfiguration. Three schools become grades 2-4 only, two schools become pre-K-1 only. Saves $2M but means no more neighborhood schools. They kept saying we don't really have neighborhood schools anymore anyway since half the kids are bused, but parents in Ferry Village and Willard are going to lose their minds over this.

Most of the council landed somewhere between 4-6% for guidance, which is actually higher than I expected given how hard people went at the public comment. Councilor Walker was the only one who straight up said 6%+ because "we need to invest in education." Councilor Matthews made the point that when he was on school board for 12 years they never went above 3%, so this really shows how bad things have gotten. Bottom line - we're looking at cuts no matter what, probably the elementary reconfiguration happening, and your property tax bill going up at least $200-300 even in the "best" case scenario.
