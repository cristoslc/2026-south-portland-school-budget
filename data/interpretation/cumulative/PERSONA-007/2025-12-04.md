# Cumulative Record: Linda (PERSONA-007)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- December 4, 2025 -- 2025-12-04

### Interpretation

December 4th. Three days before everything changed. The city council is doing exactly what it should be doing — tightening procedural screws, formalizing the committee budget process, debating the eternally unresolved Zoom comment problem — and no one in that chamber has any idea what's about to land on my side of the building.

The committee budget formalization is the kind of structure I've been trying to build at the school board for years. Capping committee operating requests at $2,500, requiring special projects to come through budget workshops with a standardized form — that's how you prevent ad hoc spending from accumulating invisibly until it compounds into a crisis. Watching the council institutionalize fiscal discipline in December while my board was one week away from learning it had no fund balance left is a bitter kind of irony. The mechanism is right. We just didn't have it where we needed it.

The Zoom public comment debate is the piece that will matter most in the months ahead. Councilor Pride laid out the First Amendment constraints clearly — once you've opened the forum, you can't selectively mute, and the Zoom bombing history is real. I've seen similar arguments at the board level, and I agree the legal analysis is correct, but it doesn't make the accessibility problem go away. Starting in January, when we're running budget forums and parents who can't leave the house at 7pm on a Tuesday want to weigh in on whether we close their child's school, I'm going to feel that unresolved tension directly.

Carter Scott is the signal I'll carry forward from this meeting. She asked for a table of contents on the council rules. She toured city facilities before her first official meeting. She pushed on the committee review process the way someone does when they actually want to understand how the machine works. On December 1st I noticed the governance competence of the incoming council in the abstract. On December 4th I can see it specifically. That instinct will matter when the school budget hits council in April and we need interlocutors who can engage with complexity rather than just react to headlines.

Linda Cohen on the Metro Board, still in the room in a different chair — that lands differently knowing she was the only honest voice three days earlier.
