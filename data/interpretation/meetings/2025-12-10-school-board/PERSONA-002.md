### Reactions

Honestly, that was a tough meeting to sit through. Three search firms, three totally different price points, and we went with the most expensive one when we literally have zero dollars budgeted for this. I mean, I get why people liked Zeal - they had the most polished presentation and this whole consensus-building angle that sounded appealing. But when you're looking at $8K versus $30K+ and you're simultaneously cutting 78 positions, the math just doesn't work for me.

The data quality issues with their Thought Exchange really bothered me too. George Rich was spot-on when he pointed out they only had 32 participants in their sample survey. That's not statistically meaningful, and if we're paying premium prices, I want premium data. NSEC felt like the sweet spot - more structured than MSMA but not breaking the bank like Zeal. Their screening committee approach was solid and Dave seemed to really know the process inside and out.

What really gets me is that we added reference checks as a contingency after the vote, which tells you we didn't do enough due diligence upfront. If we're going to spend this kind of money, we should have had those conversations before committing. I hope the reference checks uncover something that justifies this expense, but right now it feels like we let the appeal of fancy services override basic fiscal responsibility.
