# Cumulative Record: Amira (PERSONA-013)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- January 20, 2026 -- 2026-01-20

### Interpretation

The Red Bank car wash and the laundromat near Fatima's house. Those aren't abstract places on a map — they're places I walk past. ICE was there on January 20th, and kids from my school didn't come to school because their parents were too scared to put them on a bus. I knew immigration enforcement was a thing happening somewhere in January — back at the January 12th school board meeting I learned kids had missed school over it. But "somewhere" was a different distance than "the car wash."

The police chief told people not to follow ICE agents in unmarked cars, which means someone already had. He said call 911, don't get in the middle of it yourself. I keep trying to figure out how that advice is supposed to feel reassuring. You call 911 and then what? The police said they don't work with ICE. The police chief said he's glad South Portland doesn't cooperate with ICE. But the car wash is still the car wash.

And then — right there at the same meeting — someone announced the school board had cancelled the January 22nd budget forum. The exact forum I'd watched get announced on January 6th, the one being offered as the way families could participate in deciding what got cut from our schools. It was postponed to February 4th. Because families were too afraid to leave their houses. So the community got invited to participate, and then the thing that was supposed to make them afraid to leave their houses arrived, and the invitation had to be rescinded. The district said: come tell us what matters. The world said: stay inside. Nobody could be in two places at once.

The rest of the meeting was about housing — new state rules that would let more apartments get built in neighborhoods like mine, including near Memorial. Adults arguing for an hour about whether duplexes should be required to look like single-family homes. I understand that housing matters, and I know more apartments might mean more tax money might mean — maybe — some pressure off the school budget. But I couldn't really follow the design standards debate while thinking about families who didn't send their kids to school that morning.

What I keep returning to is the sequence. On December 1st, immigrant families were welcomed in their native languages at city council. On December 4th, someone stood up to defend Somali Americans' right to feel invested in this community. On January 20th, ICE was at the laundromat.
