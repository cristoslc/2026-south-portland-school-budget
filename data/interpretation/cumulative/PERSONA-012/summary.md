# Cumulative Summary: Jaylen (PERSONA-012)

### Current Understanding

As of March 23, Jaylen understands the South Portland school budget crisis as a compound institutional failure, not a single fiscal event. The structural gap has grown from $7.2M to $8.4M; 72 pink slips were issued March 18, converting the 78-position/42-teacher proposal from projection to accomplished fact. The crisis has multiple overlapping causes: state underfunding (20% coverage vs. the mandated 55%), declining elementary enrollment, rising health insurance costs (12%), a new $99K athletic complex debt payment, and documented internal financial mismanagement — 34 audit journal entries, illegal overspending, and six finance directors in five years.

At SPHS specifically, 7 teaching positions are being reduced (4 from vacancies, 3 real cuts) across unnamed "multiple content areas." All courses are committed to remain within section size limits, but science averages jump from 18.8 to 21.8, and the evening shuttle is consolidated into a single after-4 PM route — an explicit decrease in service threatening after-school programs. The percussion ed tech position serving IEP and 504 students across grades 5–12 is proposed for elimination for the second consecutive year. Theater and arts programs are currently active and expanding, but the 3 actual teaching positions being cut remain unidentified by department.

The city council's 4-6% budget ceiling was solicited by the school board chair, not imposed unilaterally, and the council's austerity posture was constructed in closed sessions before the school board made its case. The budget crisis and ICE enforcement crisis affect the same families and have been formally weaponized against each other — Matthews used the school deficit as a public argument against immigrant rental assistance on March 19. Jaylen is 16, cannot vote in any referendum, and the budget calendar is locked: April 7 public hearing, May 5 vote, June 9 referendum.
