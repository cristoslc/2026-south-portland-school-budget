Seriously? They just voted to spend $24,000 on a search firm while we're cutting 42 teaching positions? That's literally half a teacher's salary going to consultants so the board can avoid having teachers involved in picking our next superintendent.

The whole thing was a sham. None of these firms even mentioned working with the union - not once in nearly four hours. And the board loved that Zeal would keep community members out of the selection process. Richardson actually said it removes "pain points" of deciding who gets a voice. Pain points? We're talking about the people who work with kids every day, who will have to implement whatever vision this new superintendent brings.

What really gets me is DeAngelo saying board consensus is "worth every penny" - like this is about making the seven of them feel good rather than finding the best leader for our schools. They're spending more on search consultants than some districts spend on entire departments, all so they don't have to deal with hearing from teachers, support staff, or parents about what kind of leader we actually need. Meanwhile, we're supposed to accept that 42 of us are getting pink slips because there's no money. The priorities are completely backwards.
