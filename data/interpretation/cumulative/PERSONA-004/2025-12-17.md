# Cumulative Record: Marcus (PERSONA-004)
## Meeting: School Board Workshop -- December 17, 2025 -- 2025-12-17

### Interpretation

The numbers are finally on the table and they're worse than anything I imagined. Forty-two teachers. That's not an abstraction -- that's entire departments disappearing, colleagues I eat lunch with every day getting pink slips. When you do the math, 42 out of roughly 300 teachers is 14% of our teaching force gone in one budget cycle. My classes are already at 28-30 kids; if we lose that many positions, we're looking at 35 students crammed into rooms built for 20. This isn't education anymore -- it's warehousing.

What's making my blood boil is the ratio. Forty-two teachers, sixteen ed techs, and two administrators. Two. The people who made the decisions that got us here -- adding 82 positions while enrollment dropped 300 students without any organizational adjustment -- they're barely touched. Meanwhile the ed techs they're cutting are the ones who make inclusion work, who sit with the kid having a panic attack so I can keep teaching the other 29 students. Without them, I'm simultaneously teacher, counselor, behaviorist, and crowd control.

The state funding revelation does reframe part of this. When your state is supposed to cover 55% of costs but actually covers 20%, every local district is going to hit a wall eventually. That's a systemic failure, not just a South Portland problem. But understanding the cause doesn't make the effect any less devastating. The 12% health insurance increase on top of everything else feels like the system is designed to break us. I need to talk to my colleagues across departments to figure out the full scope of what's being proposed, because the aggregate numbers hide which specific programs and positions are actually on the block.
