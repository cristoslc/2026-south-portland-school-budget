# Cumulative Record: Amira (PERSONA-013)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- March 9, 2026 -- 2026-03-09

### Interpretation

Everything keeps piling up and somehow also keeps feeling normal at the same time, which is the weirdest part. The 42 teacher cuts are still on the table, and now two more special education teachers (Abigail Mingle and Sherry Beland) are resigning on their own before they can even get cut. If teachers are leaving voluntarily, things behind the scenes must be even worse than what I'm hearing about. Special ed teachers leaving means kids who already need the most help are going to have even less.

And now I'm worried about specific things I care about. Music trips need special board approval now - the All-State Music Conference in May that kids have been working toward all year might just not happen. Robotics trips too. These used to be automatic and now they're question marks. Band is the reason some of my friends come to school. Robotics is the reason some kids feel like they belong somewhere. Cutting the trips is cutting the reason to show up.

The elementary school situation is still terrifying - nine closure scenarios, angry parents everywhere - and I keep thinking about my cousin Layla at Brown. What if her school closes? She'd have to go somewhere new and not know anyone. If they're doing this to elementary schools, what stops them from doing something to the middle school? Could they make all 6th graders go to the high school? Nobody's ruling anything out.

But then I read about all these fun events still happening at the elementary schools - Read-a-Thons, literacy nights, kindness themes, celebration of learning nights. And Memorial got a $1,000 donation. School still feels like school during the day. We're still doing projects and having assemblies. The contrast is surreal: normal school life on top, adult chaos underneath. I just wish someone would tell us straight up which teachers are staying and which programs are safe, instead of making us wonder if everything we love about school is going to disappear over the summer.
