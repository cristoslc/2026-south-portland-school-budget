# Cumulative Summary: Tom (PERSONA-006)

### Current Understanding

Tom's understanding as of March 30, 2026 is that South Portland faces a fully stacked, multi-track fiscal crisis in which every piece compounds on every other piece and no governing body has produced a complete cost picture for homeowners. The school district's structural deficit has grown from a $1.9 million reveal in December to $8.4 million — driven by COVID-era over-hiring (82 positions added while 300 students left), the absence of any minimum fund balance threshold policy, seven business managers in six years, two consecutive years of budget non-compliance, and a $1.1 million annual nutrition fund deficit hidden from main budget tracking. The FY27 superintendent's budget, representing a 6% local levy increase ($257/year on the average home), failed 5-2 on March 30 with a board majority actively pushing to exceed the 6% ceiling before the June referendum. Even if 6% holds for FY27, labor contracts structurally increase personnel costs beyond 6% annually, making the ceiling mathematically unsustainable for FY28.

Simultaneously: sewer rates are rising ~22% annually for three years (average monthly bill ~$56 to ~$90, no referendum required); an $87 million police and fire bond is targeting November 2026; and a stripped-down $70-76 million Mahoney City Center bond is targeting 2027. No public body has produced a combined per-household cost figure. Tom now sees the crisis as systemic — the city itself ran with no capital planning or preventive maintenance until five years ago, the same defer-and-bond logic that produced the school staffing mismatch. The city council has shown inconsistent fiscal discipline: it defeated the eviction moratorium on cost-shifting grounds but drew down the reserve fund twice in consecutive weeks while 72 school employees received layoff notices. Per-pupil spending at $26,651 is the highest among comparable districts, and the board's failure to pass its own submitted budget has extended Tom's view of the district's dysfunction from financial mismanagement into basic governance incapacity.
