# Cumulative Record: Maria (PERSONA-001)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- December 8, 2025

### Interpretation

This meeting hits Maria like a gut punch, revealing that the budget crisis she's been tracking for months has already arrived in the current school year. The district faces a $1.9M deficit right now and has implemented an immediate spending freeze on supplies, books, and classroom materials. Her daughter's teacher probably learned today that she can't order the books she was planning for her classroom. This is exactly the kind of blindsiding Maria has feared throughout this budget season -- decisions made behind closed doors that directly impact her child's daily experience.

The scale of proposed cuts for next year is staggering: 78 positions including 42 teachers, with elementary schools bearing the brunt due to enrollment decline. After months of wrestling with reconfiguration options and grade-band proposals, Maria now faces the possibility that her daughter's beloved teacher might simply lose her job. The abstract budget numbers she's been tracking have crystallized into immediate threats to the people and programs her family depends on.

What frustrates Maria most is the disconnect between community willingness to help and the district's inability to harness that support. Parent advocate Melinda Hut offered "talent, money, resources, connections, and energy" from committed parents, but received only vague promises about a future education foundation. Meanwhile, PTAs are sitting on funds ready to help with the supply crisis, but there's no clear mechanism for schools to request that support. Maria recognizes this as a leadership failure -- the board can't effectively mobilize the resources parents are eager to provide.

The $46,000 in donations this month, including specific support for elementary schools, shows the community cares deeply. But Maria sees these donations shifting from "nice extras" to essential lifelines, which feels fundamentally wrong. She's already planning to call her school's principal tomorrow to coordinate PTA support, because waiting for district leadership clearly isn't an option.
