### Reactions

I genuinely didn't expect to leave that meeting feeling anything other than dread, but Holman said the word. Out loud. "Redistricting." Called it by name, said we need to pick up where the Boundaries and Configurations Committee left off, and the chair backed it up — said that was exactly the issue she had in mind when she talked about Jimenez Paolo joining the policy committee. I've been watching this board dance around that word for years while I drive between three buildings every day watching the same kids wait for services at one school that are available same-day at another. So I'm cautiously — very cautiously — sitting with that.

But here's what I keep turning over: earlier in the same meeting, the chair described equity work as "not moving kids around to make things look even." And I genuinely don't know how to read that. Does she mean redistricting isn't the answer? Or does she mean it's necessary but not sufficient? Those are two completely different policy directions, and she seemed to be saying both in the same meeting. I've heard that framing before — "it's really about instructional quality" — and sometimes it means the speaker is ready to do the hard structural work AND build capacity, and sometimes it means they've found a way to avoid the structural work entirely. I need to watch what happens next with that policy committee very carefully.

The thing that actually made my stomach drop — and I know this is the part nobody else was tracking — was the PTA conversation during the finance report. A board member asked if PTAs could fill the gap from the spending freeze, and the superintendent basically said sure, have the principals reach out. And I'm sitting there thinking: I work at three schools. I know what each PTA can do. One of those schools can raise thousands of dollars with a book fair. Another one runs a food drive and considers it a win. If we make "ask your PTA" the official district response to a spending freeze, we are turning the inequities I see every single week into policy. The kids on my caseload at the building with the smaller PTA are the same kids who need the most support — the multilingual learners, the students waiting for MTSS. Nobody said any of this out loud in that room, which means somebody has to. I'm drafting something tonight.
