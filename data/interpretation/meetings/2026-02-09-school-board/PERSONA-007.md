### Reactions

God, what a night. We're walking into that city council meeting tomorrow with numbers that are worse than what we shared just a month ago - Chen basically admitted we've been carrying this nutrition fund deficit as a "footnote" when it's over a million dollars. That's not a footnote, that's a major budget driver we should have been tracking all along. And now all our scenarios are showing deficits for this year, not just next year. 

The elementary reconfiguration vote bothers me. Not because I'm necessarily against it - I get that we have to look at every option when you're facing a $7M hole - but because we're pushing forward when even Feller admits most people who contact him oppose it. We've got 51 data requests from the public about this, mostly focused on reconfiguration details, and we haven't even produced the formal proposal yet. That's backwards. And then Feller breaks ranks on the 6% tax target right there in public? We agreed to that guidance, and now we look divided going into the council meeting.

The one thing that went right tonight was the community school expansion. That model is actually working at the middle school - real data on attendance, family engagement, academic improvement - and we're leveraging outside funding instead of just cutting everything. Chen staying put and committing to a multi-year cleanup gives me some hope we can actually dig out of this financial mess, but we need the political will to make hard choices and stick to them.
