### Reactions

Well that was the budget meeting I've been dreading since the preliminary numbers started circulating in finance committee. George laid it out pretty starkly - Option A at 19% is obviously DOA, but even Option B at 6% still leaves a $3.5M hole that somehow needs to get filled. The enrollment versus staffing chart tells the whole story, and frankly it's exactly what I suspected when I kept asking for those historical staffing comparisons that apparently are still sitting in some queue somewhere.

What really got to me was Ellen's report on fund balance. Zero. We're essentially operating without a safety net on the school side, and if they run a deficit this year - which seems likely given Abigail's projections - that's coming out of city fund balance temporarily. That's taxpayer money covering what amounts to operational mismanagement, and while I understand the legal mechanics, it puts us in an impossible position politically.

Rosemary was absolutely right to call us out on the guidance issue. We can't keep saying "do your best" when they're facing 50+ staff cuts and community uproar. The 3-6% range we settled on gives them parameters to work with, though I worry even 6% may not be sustainable long-term given the structural problems. The elementary reconfiguration makes sense operationally - I've seen similar consolidations work in other districts - but the community reaction is going to be intense. At least we have concrete data now to defend the decisions that have to be made.
