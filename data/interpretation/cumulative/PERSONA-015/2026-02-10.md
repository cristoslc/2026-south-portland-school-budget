# Cumulative Record: Sheanna (PERSONA-015)
## Meeting: City Council & School Board Joint Budget Guidance Workshop -- February 10, 2026 -- 2026-02-10

### Interpretation

The meeting she has been building toward since January 6 finally happened — and it gave her the external validation she has needed for years while making the personal threat specific enough to name.

Walker said it out loud. A city councilor, not a district employee, not a fellow traveler at a board meeting — a person who votes on the bottom line — named de facto segregation in the official record, attached numbers to it, and connected reconfiguration to equity rather than cost savings. Fifty percent of Skillings kids are students of color and new Mainers. Twenty-seven percent at another school. Sheanna has been driving between those buildings carrying this argument in letters and public comment and professional data that she wasn't sure anyone was reading. Tonight it got said, with numbers, by someone who will vote on the guidance that shapes every cut on the list.

But then Johanna Prince did the math nobody else would do. At 3% guidance — where most of the council landed, even after Walker spoke — the district faces approximately fifty reductions, and they will *not all come from one unit.* That phrase is the one she is taking home. Not all from one unit means traveling positions. It means specialists. It means her. She has been the person carrying the cross-building equity case because she is the person who crosses buildings. And now the guidance that may eliminate that position has been set in the same meeting where a councilor finally said what she has been saying from her car.

What makes it worse is the Lika comment. The weaponizable narrative she flagged in January arrived in exactly the form she feared — eighty-two positions as waste, not as a response to complexity — and Prince provided the counter on the record: the majority are ed techs and classroom teachers serving multilingual learners, IEP students, housing-unstable kids. The counter-narrative is in the official record now. The council still landed at three to four percent. Hearing the truth and lowering guidance are not the same thing.

The sequencing is the part she cannot resolve tonight. The board commissioned the reconfiguration proposal last night. There is no draft. The council is setting the number that determines how many people get cut before the plan that is supposed to save two million dollars has been written. She needs to get her cross-building data into that March 2 document. The window is already closing.
