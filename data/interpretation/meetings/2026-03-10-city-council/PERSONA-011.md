### Reactions

OK here's the recap from tonight's city council workshop (reminder: workshop = no final votes, but the signals are pretty clear on what's coming):

**The story that matters: ICE + rent relief.** City manager confirmed that right now, 80 South Portland families are on Project HOME's waiting list for rent help — all connected to income loss from January's ICE enforcement. The total cost to help all 80 would be about $168,000. A resident named Margot Kralik came to the podium and read written statements from three of these families. One person was held in a freezing detention cell, fell because the shoes they were given were too big, got no medical care, lost their job, got an eviction notice. A parent was too scared to send their kids to school after a neighbor reported seeing ICE agents near the school driveway — stayed home to be with them, lost income. A woman's husband was detained on his way to work; she spent the rent money on a lawyer. Heavy meeting.

The council debated how much to give. Councilor Matthews said he didn't support it at all — he cited the school board chair from last night's meeting who said the board needs to be "very cautious of every dime we spend." Councilor Walker pushed hard for the full $168K. Most others landed somewhere in the middle. The city manager said the average of everyone's positions came out to about $94K, and he'll bring an order to the next council meeting at approximately **$100,000** — structured as a reimbursement to Project HOME, not an upfront check. **That vote hasn't happened yet.** Watch for it at the next meeting.

Also worth noting: school board member Rosemary DeAngelis spoke at the podium and flagged that she's dealing with the school budget right now and wants the city to start with $50K and evaluate before giving more. She's not the only one connecting tonight's spending to whatever's coming with schools. That part of the conversation felt ominous and nobody explained what's actually in the school budget. I'll be watching.

**Good transit news:** High school students are still riding Metro for free on their school ID — there was apparently some confusion after the merger but it got sorted out in a meeting at the school with the principal and Metro. They're also actively working to align bus schedules with SPHS bell times, which would be a real improvement for families who rely on it. Separately, a new bus route is launching this summer along Route 1 and Main Street — serving Cash Corner, Thornton Heights, and Sunset Park directly into Portland, bypassing the long Mill Creek loop. 45-minute frequency, fully funded for two years. Launches as early as July.

**Shorter items:** City council voted against the bike share program — concerns about road safety, city finances, and a local bike shop owner who made a compelling case against it. E-bikes on the Greenbelt are getting the green light — ordinance coming to mirror state law (Class 1 and 2 allowed), with some councilors open to Class 3 as well. Park ranger data presented showed e-bikes and regular bikes ride at basically the same speed. No reported accidents.
