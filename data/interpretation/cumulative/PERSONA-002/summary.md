# Cumulative Summary: David (PERSONA-002)

### Current Understanding

As of March 19, 2026, David understands the school district as facing an $8–9 million structural gap — tightened from the original $7.2 million by the city council's February 10 joint guidance of 3–6% (most gravitating toward 4–6%). Three compounding forces drive this crisis: a 23% elementary enrollment drop (1,401 to 1,080 students) while staffing grew by 82 positions; systematic financial control failures documented in two consecutive material weakness audit findings, six finance directors in five years, and a $1.1 million nutrition fund deficit that ran untracked for years; and a state funding structure covering only ~20% of actual costs against a 55% formula target, pushing 61% of total property tax burden onto local homeowners. No official presentation has integrated these obligations into a single household impact analysis. Councilor Matthews named this gap in ninety seconds on March 19 and was outvoted 6–1.
