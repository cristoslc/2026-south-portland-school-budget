### Reactions

The audit results really crystallized what I've been sensing about our financial management - the city side is running a tight ship while the school department is still struggling with basic reconciliation. Six finance directors in five years tells you everything you need to know about why we keep seeing these material weaknesses. At least the new person seems to understand the accrual timing issues with teacher contracts spanning fiscal years, which has been a recurring problem.

The TI TIF extension actually makes a lot of sense from the numbers. Getting an extra $400K per year for 20 years, even with the new restrictions on how we can spend it, beats letting it expire and losing the tax shift benefits. The fact that they're only asking for a 12.5% effective incentive rate instead of the 50% they used to get shows this is a more balanced deal for taxpayers.

What really concerns me is Chair DeAngelo's warning about the budget crisis coming. With the school taking 61% of our property taxes and clearly having ongoing financial control issues, we need to get serious about oversight. The joint meeting on February 10th better include some hard conversations about why we're still seeing compliance violations two years running.
