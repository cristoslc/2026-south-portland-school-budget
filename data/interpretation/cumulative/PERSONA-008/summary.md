# Cumulative Summary: Rachel (PERSONA-008)

### Current Understanding

Rachel has spent sixteen weeks tracking the South Portland school budget crisis across two governing bodies and 24 meetings. She arrived in December as a worried parent with diffuse anxiety about a $7.2 million deficit she had only begun to understand. She exits April 2nd as a seasoned, exhausted accountability activist who has watched every assurance she was given get quietly reversed.

The final picture: Kaler Elementary is closing. Every other elementary student in the district will be reassigned by September 2026 — PreK through first grade to Dyer and Small, grades 2 through 4 to Brown and Skillin. The board voted 5-1 on closure and 4-2 on full reconfiguration. The deficit is now confirmed at $8.4 million, not the $7.2 million she calculated against all winter. Seventy-two pink slips went out on March 18th, with more expected. The budget itself failed 2-5 and heads to a June referendum — but the board chair has confirmed that a failed budget vote does not reverse the reconfiguration decision. Those are legally separate.

Six months from September, no family has been told which building their child will attend. No transportation routes have been modeled. No sibling or special education policies have been published. The thirteen listening sessions launched after the vote are post-decision implementation theater, not input mechanisms — the same pattern Rachel identified on January 15th and has watched repeat itself in every phase of this process.

The prevention phase is over. Rachel is now fighting for accountable implementation on behalf of families who still cannot answer their first graders' questions about where school will be in the fall.
