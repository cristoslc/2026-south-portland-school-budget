### Reactions

Okay so that was basically a waste of two hours. I went thinking SOMEBODY would at least acknowledge that we're facing massive school cuts and maybe reassure parents that they're going to handle transitions carefully. Instead it was all ceremonies and feel-good speeches while they completely ignored the elephant in the room. The new mayor talked about housing and development projects like we're not about to lose 78 school positions! And don't even get me started on the school board - four brand new people and a new chair who spent her entire speech talking about helping immigrant families (which is fine!) but said absolutely nothing about the enrollment crisis or budget cuts that are supposedly happening right now.

What really gets me is that they highlighted the new middle school investment like that proves they care about education, but if they're cutting 42 teachers and consolidating schools, what good does a fancy building do? The new school board chair talked about "routine, kindness and belonging" but how exactly are kids supposed to feel like they belong when you're uprooting them from schools they've attended for years? I'm starting to think these ceremonial meetings are deliberately scheduled to avoid the hard conversations. Meanwhile, the real decisions about which schools stay open are probably being made in closed sessions where parents can't even hear what's happening.
