### Reactions

Honestly, watching this unfold was like seeing every equity meeting I've ever been to play out in real time - lots of talk about "diversity" and "benefits" but when it came down to actual decisions, they were about to bus all the Black and brown kids across town while keeping the white schools intact. At least Tyler Smith said what everyone was thinking - that it's always politically easier to move kids of color than to actually address structural inequities.

What really got me was hearing Dr. Prince talk about "equitable access through reconfiguration" when their top recommendation literally closes the school that 60% of our BIPOC students attend. Like, how is that equity? They're using all the right language - multilingual learners, diverse peers, structural inequities - but their solution is to scatter the kids from the most diverse school to four other buildings instead of asking why we have such stark segregation in the first place.

The fact that they presented zero academic achievement data while claiming this is about educational outcomes tells you everything. If Skillin kids were struggling academically, they would have led with those numbers. But they didn't, because this isn't actually about education - it's about budget cuts dressed up in equity language. At least the board saw through it this time, but I'm watching closely because they could easily pivot to closing Kaler instead and that would be almost as bad.
