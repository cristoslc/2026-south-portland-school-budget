# Cumulative Summary: Maria (PERSONA-001)

### Current Understanding

As of March 23, 2026, Maria understands South Portland schools to be facing an $8.4M structural deficit — grown from the $7.2M confirmed in February — with no financial cushion remaining. Seventy-two employees received pink slips on March 18 before the board has finalized which reconfiguration option to pursue. The decision space has narrowed from nine scenarios to two: Option A and Option B, both requiring closure of Kayler Elementary. The board has not yet voted; the timeline is locked at March 30 (board vote), April 7 (public hearing), April 14 (school budget workshop), May 5 (council vote), and June 9 (referendum).

The crisis is compound: structural state underfunding (20% of actual costs covered against a statutory 55% obligation), multi-year management failure (six finance directors in five years, repeat material-weakness audit findings, $400,000 in improperly spent grant funds), and a 23% elementary enrollment decline in four years compounded by 82 positions added while enrollment fell 300 since 2017. City council guidance of 3–6% (mostly 4–5%) forced cuts deeper than the school board's own floor.

The support infrastructure being eliminated is interlocking: 16 educational technicians, four elementary MTSS positions, and the sole regular education behavioral strategist (leaving "none full time") — precisely as Kayler's students redistribute to remaining schools and class sizes increase in both options. Transportation and before-care logistics will not be modeled until after the board votes.

Families are simultaneously absorbing school tax increases, 22% annual sewer rate hikes for three years, and an $87M capital bond — with no governing body integrating the combined household burden. Immigrant children are already absent from the classrooms being restructured, while the participation infrastructure meant to amplify their families' voices has been persistently broken across every forum this budget season.
