# Cumulative Record: Linda (PERSONA-007)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- February 9, 2026 -- 2026-02-09

### Interpretation

This was a meeting of fractures and bright spots, and I'm still processing both. The most alarming development is that all FY26 budget scenarios now show deficits -- not just next year, but this year. Chen's finance report represented a significant deterioration from just a month ago when some scenarios still showed potential surpluses. And the nutrition fund deficit of roughly $1.1M that she described as having been a "footnote" is now formally integrated into projections. That's not a footnote -- that's a major budget driver we should have been tracking from the start.

The elementary reconfiguration vote passed unanimously, which means we're moving forward with requesting a formal proposal for the March 2 workshop. But the context is troubling: even the chair acknowledged that most people who've reached out oppose reconfiguration. We have 51 data requests from the public, mostly about reconfiguration details, and we haven't even produced the formal proposal yet. That's backwards -- we're generating public anxiety without giving people the information they need to evaluate what we're actually proposing.

Then Feller breaks ranks on the 6% tax target right there in public, saying he'd be "more comfortable with something exceeding 6%" and doesn't see how to cover the shortfall without being "draconian." I understand his frustration -- we all feel it -- but we agreed to that guidance as a board, and now we look divided going into the city council meeting tomorrow. The union president requesting a precise tax target from council is the right ask to the right entity, and I'm relieved someone else is applying that pressure.

The audit findings were grimly illuminating: material weakness in reconciliation procedures, and Chen is the seventh business manager in six years. That turnover explains how we got here -- no continuity in financial management means no accountability for projections that don't hold up. At least Chen is staying and committing to a multi-year cleanup.

The community school expansion was the one genuinely positive vote tonight. Unanimous approval, with real data from the middle school showing attendance and engagement improvements, and it leverages outside funding -- up to $100K annually in grants plus the South Portland Education Foundation launching July 1st. Finally, a strategy that adds value without adding fiscal burden.
