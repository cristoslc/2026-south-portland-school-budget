This isn't just poor judgment; it's a pattern of dysfunction that started with Tyler Smith's opening criticism and now includes budget mismanagement, spending freezes on classroom supplies, and choosing expensive consultants over community input. I've got a multi-part series here about a board that's lost touch with both fiscal reality and public accountability.
