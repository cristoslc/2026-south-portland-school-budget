# Community Forum Notes — October 24, 2023

## Question 1: What do you like about your child's school? What would you change?

**Likes:**
- Neighbors who attend the school; Dyer is very welcoming to all students
- Teachers, building relationships, community building, culture, walkability
- Community neighborhoods and communication
- Kaler teachers, diversity, school activities and communication
- Community school model
- Follow-up on children
- Organization at Skillin
- Bus drivers are great
- Neighborhood connections and class sizes
- Supportive services and Special Ed programs
- Walking to school; how you build community
- Teachers and school staff

**Desired Changes:**
- Improving English proficiency
- More opportunity for small classes to meet other kids
- More meetings; include parents with children not yet in school
- Skillin has lots of students of color; there should be more diversity in the other schools
- Put only 2 grades in each school to avoid overcrowding
- More homework
- Children learning English would benefit from classes with students who already speak the language
- Children dropped off closer to home
- Choice of what school a child attends
- After school language classes for English learners
- More teachers who represent the diverse community
- Small school neighborhood feel but need more diversity
- Brown School feels like there is too much of a white narrative
- Gardening in schools
- More diversity in school nutrition program
- More Spanish speaking teachers
- More support for teachers teaching students who cannot speak English
- Better communication from schools
- Disparity in communication about this committee's work depending on the school
- Tell us what the actual stakes are; flowery language about priorities does not get attention
- Interest in creating a city-wide PTA to equalize resource distribution

## Question 2: Should our elementary schools be made more similar? Why or why not?

- Make them more similar
- Need a professional needs assessment with funding to do this well
- Giving students the education they need should be the priority
- Resources should be shared equally to meet learning expectations
- Make them more similar in diversity and socioeconomic status
- Make them more equitable by socioeconomic status, race and ethnicity
- Need more people of color and different socioeconomic status on the committee
- Schools should focus on equity; need more input from multilingual families
- More diversity in classes would benefit everyone
- Chose a school because of the community; would not want to change
- Other ways to improve exposure to diversity like after school programs
- Would like things to change and become more equitable and diverse
- Sending kids long distances creates other problems for families
- Diversity for the sake of diversity matters less than equal access to experiences like field trips
- This is really an issue of housing equity across the city
- Kids should not be shuffled around just to spread out the diversity

## Question 3: If schools were to change, what concerns or questions do you have?

- As few transitions as possible
- How will busing be impacted?
- Consider the future of housing in the city
- Losing proximity and sense of community
- Giving special needs kids enough attention
- Are there representatives from all over the community on the committee?
- Logistics and length of bus rides
- Concerned that only children of color grades K-2 will be bused to the east side
- School start time changes and bus ride length
- Worried about breaking up the community feeling
- Families and students getting quality support
- Kids learning English being embarrassed to speak in front of peers
- Students and parents need education about bullying and getting along with people who are different
- How will the grades be divided?
- Would current families be split between schools?
- Are all schools equally secure?
- Concerned with emotional effect on kids and parents
