### Reactions

Hey — just got out of that Mahoney workshop, it's almost midnight. The lede writes itself: after ten months of work, multiple consultants, and hundreds of thousands already spent in design fees, the committee unveiled a $194 million price tag Tuesday night — and the architect who produced it said on the record that he's "not confident" in the number. The committee chair's own framing was that it was "unanticipated." A planning committee called their own estimate unanticipated. That's the story.

For readers who haven't been following this, the quick context is six aging city buildings — city hall, the main library, police, central fire, Hamlin school — all need significant work. The plan on the table consolidates most of them onto the Mahoney school site with a new police station in the field and a rebuilt fire station across Broadway. That's the $194 million. But here's the number I want to anchor the piece on: $153 million. That's what the design team calculated it would cost to just fix all six buildings where they currently stand — no consolidation, no Mahoney, no vision. The big plan costs $41 million more than fixing what you have. That comparison makes the tradeoff legible for people who've been tuning this out.

The bigger story I want to write — and nobody connected these dots Tuesday night — is the cumulative tax hit. South Portland residents are looking at a potential 18–19% school tax increase AND this bond on the same property tax bill. The school district and the city are separate entities, but it's the same homeowner writing the check. The finance director mentioned $514,000 as the average assessed home value in the city. I need to get someone on the phone this week — the business manager or Ellen Sanborn — and put the combined annual number in one sentence residents can actually feel. That's the piece that will matter when this goes to referendum.
