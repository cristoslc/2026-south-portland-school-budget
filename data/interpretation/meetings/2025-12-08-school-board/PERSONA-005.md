### Reactions

This meeting was simultaneously encouraging and infuriating. On one hand, you've got Justine Carlisle presenting actual disaggregated data about who's accessing ELO programs - over half are students the state categorizes as disadvantaged, including multilingual students and students with disabilities. That's the kind of transparency I've been pushing for, and it shows the program is actually reaching the kids who need these opportunities most.

But then we get to the budget crisis and it's like equity just vanished from the conversation. They're talking about a potential $1.9 million deficit, massive spending freezes, and not one person asks how these cuts will differentially impact schools with higher concentrations of low-income students or English learners. When Tyler Smith asked about getting reports on how cuts would affect students day-to-day, that was the right question, but it shouldn't have taken a new board member to surface that.

The donations piece really got to me - $46,000 flowing into the district but it's completely ad hoc. Some schools are getting multiple grants while others get nothing, and when asked about coordination, the superintendent basically admits they don't have anyone managing this strategically. That's how you perpetuate inequity - letting resources flow to whoever has the social capital to write grants while schools serving the most vulnerable families get left behind. At least the new board seems to recognize their own demographic limitations and are talking about bringing in different perspectives, but we'll see if that translates to actual policy changes.
