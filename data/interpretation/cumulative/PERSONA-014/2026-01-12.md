### Interpretation

The abstract threat to Dyer has just become concrete. A board member has formally asked the superintendent to produce an "elementary school reconfiguration proposal" for the March meeting. Lila's mom explained that "reconfiguration" is a fancy word for moving kids around to different schools -- and possibly closing some. This is no longer a vague money problem. Someone in charge is now actively requesting a plan to change or close her school.

At the same time, Lila is learning she's not the only scared kid. Other children missed school because their families were afraid -- though for different reasons (immigration enforcement). But it also reinforces the sense that school itself -- the place that's supposed to be safe and predictable -- is becoming a source of anxiety rather than comfort.

A board member asked for an update on how it's affecting teachers and principals day-to-day, which confirms for Lila that her teachers are still unable to get the supplies they need. This thread from the very first meeting remains open and unresolved. Meanwhile, board members want to talk about phone policies -- a priority that seems absurdly disconnected from the crisis Lila is living through.

The bright spot -- a letter praising the track coaches as "truly inspirational" -- reminds Lila that good things are still happening at school. But it's a small light against a growing darkness. The trajectory is clear: from $1.9M shortfall to $7.2M gap to depleted reserves to 78 jobs cut, and now a formal request for a plan to reconfigure elementary schools. Each meeting brings the threat closer to Lila's daily life.
