### Reactions

Well, I'll give the school board credit for one thing - they finally put a lid on the tax increases instead of just rubber-stamping whatever the administration wanted. Keeping it to 6% instead of the 18-19% they were talking about shows they're at least listening to taxpayers for once. But the more I dig into the numbers, the more steamed I get about how we got into this mess in the first place.

How do you lose 300 kids but ADD 82 staff members? That's backwards from any business I've ever seen. And now we find out we're spending more per kid than any other district around here - $26,651 per student! No wonder my tax bill keeps climbing. They've been running this place like money grows on trees, and now the chickens are coming home to roost with the fund balance wiped out.

The state's not helping either - they promised to cover 55% of school costs but they're only kicking in 20%. That leaves us property owners holding the bag for everything else. At least now they're finally cutting 78 positions, but it should have happened years ago when enrollment started dropping. Better late than never, I guess, but this whole thing could have been avoided if someone had been watching the bottom line instead of just hiring more people every year.
