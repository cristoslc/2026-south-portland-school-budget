### Reactions

Okay so I just sat through three and a half hours of this budget nightmare and I'm honestly shaking. They want to blow up our elementary schools - break them apart and shuffle kids around like puzzle pieces - just to save $2 million. My daughter would go from walking to Skillings where she knows all her teachers and has friends in every grade, to being bused somewhere else with only kids her exact age. They're calling it "good for kids" but it feels like they're destroying the community connections that make these schools special.

The worst part? This whole crisis was totally predictable. They added 82 positions while enrollment dropped by 300 kids since 2017. Nobody wants to talk about those positions or what happened in central office, but they'll cut 42 teachers without blinking. Sarah from the teachers union was right - the staff didn't cause this mess, but they're the ones getting blamed and fired. And now they're asking city council for budget guidance like they're just discovering we have a problem. Council gave them a range from 3-6%, which means even more cuts because the school board was already asking for 6% minimum.

What really got me was School Board Chair DeAngelo lecturing the city councilors about doing their job when parents have been asking basic questions for six weeks about central office staffing and getting crickets. Then she says we don't have neighborhood schools anymore anyway, so breaking them up is no big deal. Tell that to the mom running the bike bus who's worried kids won't be able to participate if they get shipped across town. I'm going to every PTA meeting and parent Facebook group tomorrow because this is exactly the kind of thing that gets decided without us if we don't make noise.
