### Reactions

OK so here's what I think I'm writing about: The superintendent walked into Monday night's meeting with a clear recommendation to close Skillin Elementary and reconfigure all the other schools by grade level, but he walked out with at least four board members saying absolutely not. That's the news.

The equity angle is what makes this more than just a budget story. You had Dr. Prince arguing that reconfiguration would create more diverse schools, which sounds good in theory. But then DeAngelo gets up and basically says you're asking the most vulnerable families in the district to put their kids on buses so they can have the privilege of going to school with white kids on the east side. That's a quote I can use. And Tyler Smith made the same point earlier about how politically easy it is to bus black and brown kids. Those are the moments that cut through all the spreadsheet noise.

What I'm struggling with is whether this whole process is actually about equity or whether equity is just the argument they're using because they need to cut $8 million and closing a school saves the most money. The fact that they didn't present any actual achievement data to back up the equity claims is pretty telling. Richardson called that out directly and it's a fair point. If you're going to disrupt 300 families' lives because their kids will learn better in a different configuration, show me the evidence. They didn't.

The other story here is that parents feel completely caught off guard by this, and based on what I heard tonight, that's legitimate. The district keeps saying they've been transparent, but transparency isn't the same thing as communication. Most of these families had no idea their kid might be going to a different school next year until word of mouth got to them. That's a process story worth digging into.
