### Reactions

Jesus, 42 teachers gone? That's like wiping out entire departments. I'm sitting here trying to figure out which of my colleagues won't be coming back next year, and it's making me sick. The math is simple - if they cut 42 positions and we've got maybe 300 teachers total, that's 14% of our workforce. My class sizes are already pushing 28-30 kids, and now they want to jam even more into each section? Meanwhile they're only cutting 2 administrators. Two. Out of how many in that central office building?

What really gets me is finding out they added 82 positions while enrollment was dropping by 300 kids. Where was the union voice in those decisions? Where was the board oversight? Now when the bill comes due, it's teachers and ed techs who get the axe. Those 16 ed techs they're cutting? Those are the people who help me manage the kids with IEPs, who work one-on-one with struggling students, who make it possible for me to actually teach instead of just manage behavior. Without them, my job becomes impossible.

The state funding thing explains a lot though - 20% when it should be 55%? No wonder every district in Maine is having these conversations. But that doesn't make it easier to look at my class rosters next year and see 35 kids crammed into a room built for 20. This isn't about education anymore, it's about warehousing kids, and we're the ones who have to make it work somehow.
