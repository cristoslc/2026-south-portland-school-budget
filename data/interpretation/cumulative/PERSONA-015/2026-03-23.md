# Cumulative Record: Kira (PERSONA-015)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop -- March 23, 2026 -- 2026-03-23

### Interpretation

This is the meeting where the institutional language finally caught up to what she has been living — and then cut it anyway.

The district put MTSS efficiency in their own Option A slide. Principal Connolly said the quiet part out loud: in a K-4 building, you are fighting the schedule to coordinate intervention staff across grade levels; in a grade-span building, that constraint disappears entirely. She has been making that argument from parking lots between three buildings for years. Tonight it is in the official district analysis, attached to the primary cost-saving strategy, in a document that will be in the public record. The equity case she has been carrying alone finally has an institutional author. And in the same packet: four MTSS positions eliminated. The sole full-time regular-ed behavior strategist in the district cut to zero. Eight MTSS roles remaining for the whole district after a one-third reduction, beginning in a year when class sizes go up and Kaler's students redistribute into buildings already running at capacity for intervention services. The district is building the structural case for Option A with one hand and cutting the infrastructure that makes the transition survivable with the other.

Blair Bacon is the thing she cannot put down. A National Board Certified literacy interventionist — someone doing her work, the same work, at Skillen — stood at that podium holding a pink slip and said exactly what Kira has said in planning periods, in letters, in public comment she wasn't sure anyone ever read: *I was a Band-Aid.* That is the whole argument. The entire case for reconfiguration over itinerant specialists spread thin across inequitably bounded buildings. It is in the official record now, from a peer, and it is devastating and vindicating in the same breath.

What she cannot make add up on the drive home is this: South Portland is already at 210 students per MTSS strategist — second-worst in the region — before the FY27 cuts take effect. Four positions gone. Class sizes rising. Option B's own slide admits it cannot achieve the equity goals. The board goes home without voting. March 30 is next. April 7 is the council deadline. The window for making this matter is not wide.
