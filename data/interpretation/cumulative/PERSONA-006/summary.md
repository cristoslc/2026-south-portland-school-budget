# Cumulative Summary: Tom (PERSONA-006)

### Current Understanding

Tom has tracked the South Portland school budget crisis across eighteen meetings since December 2025 and holds a detailed, hardened picture of compounding fiscal failure. The district faces a $7.2 million structural gap requiring either an 18-19% property tax increase or the elimination of 78 positions — 42 teachers, 16 ed techs, 14 facilities/food/transport workers, 2 administrators, and 4 non-bargaining staff. Elementary enrollment fell 23% (from 1,401 to 1,080 students) while the district added 82 positions, producing a per-pupil cost of $26,651 — highest among comparable districts. The fund balance, $5.9 million in FY23, is now essentially zero. The nutrition fund ran a hidden $715K deficit in FY25 and projects $1.1M in FY26. Two consecutive years of budget non-compliance — with $400K in misapplied grant funds and seven finance directors in six years — have shifted Tom's framing from structural bad luck to operational dysfunction. State funding covers only 20% of costs against a 55% statutory obligation.

The city council's 3-6% guidance at the February 10 joint workshop was the one moment of real fiscal enforcement Tom trusts. But the board's veto of Option 1.7 (Skillin closure, $19.6M in five-year savings) on equity grounds, Feller's open advocacy for exceeding 6%, and the administration's still-undelivered breakdown of those 82 positions have eroded confidence that the ceiling will hold.

Simultaneously, Tom is tracking compounding city-level pressures: a Mahoney City Center whose "bare bones" floor is $57M–$105M with no binding council ceiling set; a Pearl Street pump station at ~$50M bypassing voter approval through sewer rates; the 170 Ocean Street TIF returning half its new tax revenue to the developer for thirty years; and LD 1829 mandating citywide residential densification without planning board review. The mayor's spending-forward inauguration agenda, which Tom can now read as the institutional baseline, makes the council's occasional fiscal backbone feel exceptional rather than reliable.
