# Cumulative Record: Dana (PERSONA-009)
## Meeting: City Council Workshop -- January 13, 2026 -- 2026-01-13

### Interpretation

Dana has been sitting on Mayor Tipton's inaugural promise about the Mahoney city center since December 1 — the "game changer" that was going to transform the oil tank land and free up city hall for housing. Six weeks later, the price tag is $194 million, the architect is saying "I am not confident" on the record, and the mayor herself is calling it a non-starter. That is not just a story. That is a cold open, a middle, and a close, all from one four-hour meeting.

The two clips write the segment. Craig Piper at one hour forty-five minutes answering Councilor Matthews's direct question with "I am not confident, Dickie" — that is the kind of moment where you stop the tape and start building backward. Cut to Mayor Tipton at two forty-three: "$194 million is simply a non-starter for me." The anchor intro writes itself. The architect can't stand behind his own number; the mayor won't take it to voters. How did a project get this far without anyone knowing what it cost?

But Dana is already thinking past the stand-alone segment. This $194 million is landing on the same property tax bill as the 18-to-19 percent school budget increase she has been covering since February 4 — and nobody in that chamber said both numbers out loud in the same sentence. That combinatorial story — a homeowner absorbing the school referendum and the Mahoney bond simultaneously — is the story she is holding for later in the season, when both crises arrive at the ballot together. She does not have that homeowner yet. She is looking.

The human material from public comment is unusually good. Ed Cobb has been on Romano Road since 1993, taxes were $1,600 then, now looking at over $6,000 — and he said he'd have to leave, and you could hear it in his voice. Henry Silve just bought his first house in December and showed up to say this makes young people putting down roots feel impossible. Old-timer priced out, new arrival already precarious — those are the two walk-and-talks.

The Olivia Montgomery clip on police proximity deterring immigrants from city services is the more complicated angle. It connects directly to the ICE fears thread Dana has been tracking since January 12 and the DeAngelos immigrant-inclusion arc that goes all the way back to December 1. She is not leading with it, but it is in the can.

Tipton's inaugural promise is now officially her first broken commitment. Dana has been tracking that thread since December 1 — tonight it closed.
