# Cumulative Record: Maria (PERSONA-001)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- March 9, 2026

### Interpretation

After the marathon March 2 workshop -- three and a half hours of nine reconfiguration scenarios, principal presentations, and 24-plus parents speaking in opposition -- this regular meeting feels anticlimactic but carries an undercurrent of dread. The board is in a procedural holding pattern, approving field trips and reviewing minutes while three more budget workshops loom in March. Maria attends because she has committed to being present for every meeting, but the lack of substantive budget action tonight leaves her in an uncomfortable limbo.

The March 2 workshop validated Maria's instincts in two important ways. First, the massive parent turnout -- at least 24 speakers, nearly all opposing reconfiguration -- confirmed she is not alone. The parent network she has been building since December, texting from pickup lines and posting in Facebook groups, has turned into a real community of advocates. Second, the nine different scenarios the administration presented revealed that this has been in development for longer than the board let on. Nine options do not materialize in a few weeks; this planning was happening while parents were still being told the budget was "difficult" without specifics.

The school-level reports provide a bittersweet moment. All five elementary schools are running reading celebrations, field trips are being approved, teachers are maintaining normalcy for children. Maria sees her daughter's school functioning beautifully right now -- and that is precisely what makes the reconfiguration threat so painful. She is being asked to accept the dismantling of something that is working for children in service of a budget problem that was years in the making.

The finance report showing 92% of the budget spent with only 67% of the year elapsed reinforces the current-year crisis she first learned about in December. The spending freeze is still in effect. The district is simultaneously rationing current-year resources and planning to restructure the entire elementary system for next year. Maria keeps returning to the unanswered question: how many of the 42 teacher eliminations will come from elementary schools? The board has never specified this, and she suspects the answer depends on which reconfiguration option is chosen -- meaning the scale of the impact on her child's school remains unknowable until the board makes its choice.
