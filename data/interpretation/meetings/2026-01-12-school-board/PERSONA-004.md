### Reactions

Man, this meeting was a gut punch. Forty-two teaching positions on the chopping block - that's like losing an entire elementary school's worth of teachers. And they wonder why our class sizes are going to be insane next year. The thing that really gets me is they'll cut teachers without blinking, but when it comes to actually closing a building that could save millions? Unanimous vote to just "study it more." Come on.

At least Sarah Gay nailed it when she told them we don't want to just fill out more surveys. We want to see their actual proposals so we can tell them what it really means in the classroom. Like, when they say "eliminate 2.5 FTE in high school English," we're the ones who know that means 30 more kids per section and no more creative writing electives. Tyler Smith asking about the spending freeze was good too - he gets that this isn't just about spreadsheets, it's about whether I can get printer paper or replace broken lab equipment.

The whole thing just feels backwards. They're treating teacher salaries like the problem instead of recognizing we're the ones actually delivering the education. And hearing that they're not automatically backfilling retirements? That's code for "let's just pile more work on whoever's left." Honestly, after 15 years in this district, I've never seen morale this low. Half my colleagues are already looking at other districts.
