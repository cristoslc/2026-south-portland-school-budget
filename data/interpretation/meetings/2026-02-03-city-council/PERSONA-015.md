### Reactions

The Title I piece is going to stay with me. The auditor said $400K in grant funds — including Title I — had to be swept into the general fund because it was overspent or ineligible. Title I. That's the money that's supposed to be funding interventions for our highest-need kids. The same kids I'm serving across three buildings. The same kids who are going to lose services when we cut 42 teachers. And this isn't a surprise to me — I've watched our grants get mismanaged for years because we've had six people in the finance director role in five years. Joe Prince explained the three-year calendar problem and I actually thought it was the most honest thing said all night. He's right that it's structural and complicated. But it's been repeat findings for at least two years now. At some point "it's complicated" has to become "we fixed it." Rosemary DeAngelo asking the council to accept accountability for the school budget — that was the sharpest moment of the whole audit section for me.

I'll be honest, the Texas Instruments TIF workshop went on for over an hour and I was on my phone for most of it. That's real economic development stuff for the city side, I get it. What snapped me back was the public comment on immigration. I work in those buildings. I have kids on my MTSS caseload right now who I haven't seen in over a week. The person who testified about their friend sitting in the dark every night with the lights off so nobody knows they're home — I knew exactly which neighborhood they meant. The council passing the resolution with the moratorium amendment matters. It's not enough, and I know it. But having it on the record, having the council formally ask the governor to act, is something to point to.

I'm going to that budget forum tomorrow night. Rosemary is right that we need every taxpayer in the room, but I'm going as someone who can tell the board what these cuts actually mean at the ground level — not just from one school, from five. I want to know who's running for superintendent and whether they understand that you cannot close a school for cost savings without redistricting and think you've solved the equity problem. You've made it worse. I've been watching committees study this for years. The Boundaries and Configurations work never went anywhere. The new superintendent is either going to be the person who finally acts on the data we've had for a decade, or the person who uses the budget crisis as cover to cut and call it reform. I need to know which before they get the job.
