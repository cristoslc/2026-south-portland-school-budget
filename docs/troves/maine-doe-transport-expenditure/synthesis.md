## Key Findings

### South Portland Transport Spending in Context

South Portland spends **$1,065.44 per pupil** on transportation (including buses) — slightly below the state average of $1,092.53. This is **the highest transport spend among the peer group** except for Westbrook, which is close at $986.46.

### Analysis

1. **South Portland's transport cost is already high relative to peers.** At $1,065/pupil, South Portland spends 70% more than Portland ($626) and 54% more than Scarborough ($691) on transport. Only Gorham ($946) and Westbrook ($986) are close among comparable districts.

2. **Transport as a share of total spending is moderate.** At 4.3% of total per-pupil expenditure, South Portland's transport share is below the state average (5.5%) — but this is because South Portland's *total* spending is very high ($25,047 vs. state average $19,804). In absolute dollars, the transport spend is substantial.

3. **Total transport budget estimate.** $1,065 × 2,810 pupils = approximately **$2.99M annually** on transportation. Any route expansion from reconfiguration adds to this base.

4. **Portland comparison is notable.** Portland, which South Portland has a bus service agreement with (referenced in evidence pools), spends only $626/pupil on transport — 41% less. This may reflect Portland's greater density, but it also suggests the cross-billing arrangement is worth examining.

5. **Gorham is the closest peer by geography and size** (2,815 pupils vs. South Portland's 2,810) and spends $946/pupil — $119 less per pupil on transport. This difference represents roughly $334K annually.

### Implications for Reconfiguration Cost Modeling

- **Marginal cost estimation:** If reconfiguration adds 10% to route-miles (a conservative assumption for going from 5 schools to 4), and route costs scale roughly linearly, the annual transport cost increase would be approximately $299K.
- **If transport costs rise to Gorham's share of total (5.2%):** That would be $1,302/pupil × 2,810 = $3.66M, an increase of $670K over current spending.
- **These are rough estimates.** Actual costs depend on route structure, fleet utilization, contract terms, and bell schedule tiers. But they provide order-of-magnitude context for the "not significant" claim the administration made without modeling (TC-005).

### Data Limitations

- This is FY25 data (2024-25 school year). Transport costs for reconfigured FY27 would be two years later.
- The "Transportation (includes buses)" category may include field trips, athletics transport, and SPED transport — not just regular route costs. The composition matters.
- Per-pupil figures use resident pupil count (2,810), which may differ from the October 1 enrollment count used elsewhere (2,744 or 2,748).
- Only one year of data was extracted. Multi-year trend data would strengthen the analysis.

## Gaps Requiring Additional Data

1. **Multi-year transport expenditure** (FY23, FY24) to see if costs are trending up
2. **Breakdown of transport spending** by category (regular routes vs. SPED vs. field trips)
3. **Portland bus agreement details** — terms, billing structure, marginal cost implications
4. **Fleet ownership vs. contract** — does South Portland own buses or contract service?
