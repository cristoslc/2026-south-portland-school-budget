# Cumulative Summary: Maria (PERSONA-001)

### Current Understanding

As of March 30, 2026, Maria understands the South Portland school budget crisis as a compound failure spanning five months, two governing bodies, and four converging breakdowns: structural underfunding, internal financial mismanagement, demographic decline, and a participation infrastructure incapable of reaching the families most affected.

The school board voted March 30 to close Kayler Elementary (6-1) and implement Option A reconfiguration — PreK–grade 1 primary schools, grades 2–4 intermediate schools — starting September 2026. The FY27 superintendent's budget failed 2-5, requiring an April 2 special meeting before the April 7 council deadline. Seventy-two pink slips were issued March 18, placing 78 positions in active elimination — including 42 teachers, 16 educational technicians, the district's only regular education behavioral strategist, four MTSS specialists, and seven lunch aides — without a passed budget authorizing it.

The structural gap has grown from a vague "difficult year" to $7.2M to $8.4M. The fund balance is gone. The state covers only 20% of costs against a statutory 55% obligation. City council guidance of 3–6% (mostly 4–5%) fell below the board's own requested floor. Years of financial mismanagement — six finance directors in five years, a second consecutive audit material weakness, $400K in improperly spent grants, repeated illegal budget overages — compound the enrollment-driven pressure.

ICE enforcement suppressed participation by the exact immigrant families DeAngelis centered in her inauguration speech, keeping children from classrooms while reconfiguration decisions were made about those classrooms. The same council bloc whose below-floor guidance forces 42 teacher cuts voted down the eviction moratorium that might have kept those families housed.

Maria carries simultaneous compounding household shocks — school tax increase, Mahoney bond debt service (~$1,152/year average), sewer rate increases of 22% annually for three years — that no one in either governing body is integrating.
