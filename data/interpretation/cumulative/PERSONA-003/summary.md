# Cumulative Summary: Jess (PERSONA-003)

The K-12 system is contracting -- elementary enrollment down 23% in four years, 78 positions being cut, fund balance depleted, and an unresolved $7.2M budget gap that even a 6% property tax increase cannot close without deep structural changes. Elementary school reconfiguration is likely, with the neighborhood school model potentially being replaced by grade-band campuses, though the board has shown equity-driven resistance to the most aggressive closure scenarios.

At the same time, early childhood services are expanding under state mandate. Pre-K capacity is growing from 64 to 128 seats, the district must serve 80-90 four-year-olds by September 2026 with three-year-olds following, and school buildings are being preserved specifically because this growth needs physical space. This expansion has proven durable across five consecutive meetings, supported by state mandates, Head Start partnerships, and community demand that exceeds supply through a lottery system.

Jess's daughter will enter the system at the exact intersection of these trends. After three months of monitoring, Jess has moved from panic and active consideration of leaving the district to a position of weary but anchored watchfulness. The pre-K expansion gives her enough reason to stay and evaluate, but she remains deeply uncertain about what elementary education will look like once her daughter ages past early childhood programming. The board's inability to provide redistricting details before the budget vote, combined with nine unresolved reconfiguration scenarios, means the most consequential question for her family -- which school will my daughter attend and what will it look like -- remains unanswered.
