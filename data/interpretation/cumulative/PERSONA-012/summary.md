# Cumulative Summary: Jaylen (PERSONA-012)

### Current Understanding

As of April 2, 2026, Jaylen understands himself to be inside a compound, worsening crisis whose human costs are now arriving as named people. The deficit has grown from the $7.2M figure he first encountered in December to $8.4M. Seventy-two pink slips went out on March 18. At SPHS, he can now name two of them: Hannah and Matt Meyer, both art teachers, both being cut — one for the second time by the same district. Students were already turned away from art class this year when Meyer's position was quietly halved over the summer with no notification to the students who had registered. Theater remains unmentioned in any public budget presentation. AP teachers and coaches are still unnamed in any public record. The FY27 budget itself has not been formally adopted: it failed 5-2 on March 30, was deferred again at April 2, and a subsequent meeting has been scheduled with no specified changes.

The fiscal crisis is structural, managerial, and politically constrained at once. State funding covers 20% of costs against the required 55%. The district committed 34 audit violations and illegally moved grant funds to cover operating gaps. The city council's 4-6% guidance ceiling — which Jaylen once understood as an external imposition — was explicitly solicited by Chair DeAngelis herself. The teachers' union has been fighting the state funding battle outside the rooms he was watching: a trip to Augusta during this cycle extracted approximately $300K in new funding, with a possible additional $750K still uncertain.

The families most affected by the budget cuts are the same families living under ICE enforcement fear. Councilor Scott has formally connected 80 at-risk households to potential enrollment loss on the public record. Councilor Matthews used the school deficit to vote against those same families' rental assistance in the same week. The April 7 public hearing is next, and Jaylen knows the public comment microphone is the only real seat this process has ever offered him.
