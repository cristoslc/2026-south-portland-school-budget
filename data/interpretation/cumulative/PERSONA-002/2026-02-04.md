# Cumulative Record: David (PERSONA-002)
## Meeting: School Board Public Budget Forum -- February 4, 2026

### Interpretation

This was the most useful budget meeting I have attended in years, and it directly answered the thread I opened last month about whether the public forum would produce meaningful input or be performative. The answer is: meaningful. The district put the real numbers on the table -- $7.2 million gap, fund balance gone, 18.95% tax increase if nothing changes. No sugar-coating.

The enrollment-versus-staffing chart was particularly powerful. You can see exactly where the district hired up to meet the asylum seeker enrollment spike and then kept hiring even as those numbers came back down. That visual confirms the structural imbalance the December 17 workshop quantified: 300+ fewer students, 82 more staff positions.

Three "big ideas" were presented: elementary reconfiguration saving roughly $2M, middle/high school efficiency measures saving roughly $1M, and operational changes saving roughly $1M. Even if all three succeed, over $3 million in additional cuts are still needed. The math is sobering but at least it is honest. The class size data was reassuring -- moving from averages of 16 to 18-19 in K-2 and 22-23 in grades 3-4 stays within existing board guidelines. That is a reasonable trade-off.

What concerns me is the health insurance estimate jumping from 8% to 12% in just a few weeks, with the actual figure not known until March 25. If major budget assumptions are shifting this late in the process, other variables could move too. The middle school bell time consolidation getting pulled because of permitting and DOT traffic study timelines was actually encouraging -- it shows realistic timeline assessment rather than promising what cannot be delivered.

The new structured Q&A system where community questions get data-driven responses posted publicly is exactly what this process has been missing. Show us the numbers and let us evaluate rather than relying on emotional appeals.
