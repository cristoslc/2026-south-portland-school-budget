### Reactions

Honestly, after sitting through three and a half hours of that meeting, I'm more pissed off than when I walked in. They came in with all these fancy matrices and spreadsheets, talking about "educational coherence" and "grade-level collaboration," but when you strip away the jargon, what they're really saying is we're cutting 42 teachers and cramming kids into bigger classes. And the way Abigail had to explain that the "elementary cuts" aren't actually elementary cuts because of seniority? That's the kind of transparency we've been begging for -- it shouldn't take a direct question to get the truth.

The one bright spot was seeing four board members basically tell the administration to stuff it on closing Skillin. When Dowling started talking about the history and DeAngelo went off about equity, you could see the superintendent's face. He's not used to getting pushback like that. But don't kid yourself -- he's still pushing this reconfiguration thing hard, and the board seems split on it. 

What really gets me is how they're framing this like it's all about what's best for kids, but then Sarah from the union had to ask the basic questions nobody thought to address: What happens to all our small group instruction spaces? What about the kids who are already getting services in hallways because we don't have enough room? They want to talk about diversity and equity while they're literally making it impossible for us to do our jobs properly. The whole thing feels like they decided what they wanted to do months ago and now they're just trying to sell it to us.
