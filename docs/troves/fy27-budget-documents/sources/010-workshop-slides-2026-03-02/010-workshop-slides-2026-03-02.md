### Multilingual Students

- 18% of our students are multilingual learners (MLs)
- 26% of our students have a primary/home language other than English
- Students and their families speak at least 40 different languages in addition to English
- Top Four: Portuguese, Spanish, French, & Lingala
- MLs receive specialized instruction for English language development
- MLs benefit from being in balanced classrooms with peers who can be role models for language development
- MLs bring rich experiences and knowledge to share with those peers

#### Development of Transition Programming

The State of Maine has passed legislation in 2023 that requires public schools to provide special education and related services to eligible students until they turn 22 or receive a regular high school diploma, whichever comes first. Prior to 2023 we had responsibilities until age 20. Implications have required expanding programming for students with disabilities preparing them for adult life.

#### New Programming for 2026

The State of Maine has passed legislation that requires public schools to be responsible for the special education needs of 3 and 4 year olds:
- Our department will take responsibility for serving 4 year olds starting September 1, 2026
- Additional students that we will be serving is likely to be between 80-90 children for next school year
- Our Department will take responsibility for serving 3 year olds in FY28

### Instructional Coherence

Over the last 5 years we have made system-wide changes to curriculum:
- Math - Adopted IntoMath in Fall 2025
- Literacy - Model of Instruction with all elements of the Science of Reading - including Heggerty and Phonics to Reading
- Science and Social Studies - Integrated units began last year, and unit 2 will be added this year
- ESOL - Adopted Reach for the Stars/Reach Higher Fall 2024

Even with these improvements, more ability for teachers to team and share and support each other will move outcomes along.
