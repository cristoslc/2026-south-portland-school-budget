# Cumulative Record: David (PERSONA-002)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- February 9, 2026

### Interpretation

The financial picture got worse at this meeting but paradoxically my confidence in the board's approach improved. Finance Director Chen revealed what I have suspected: the current fiscal year is heading for a deficit across all scenarios, and there is a nutrition fund bleeding $1.1 million that nobody was properly tracking. That hidden shortfall partially explains why prior deficit projections seemed insufficient relative to the structural problems. Seven business managers in six years -- surfaced by the FY25 audit -- explains the breakdown in financial controls and reconciliation procedures. You cannot run a $100M+ operation with that kind of leadership churn in your finance office.

The budget forum thread continues to validate: 100 participants attended and 51 detailed data requests came through the online system, most focused on elementary reconfiguration specifics. The community is engaging with actual data rather than just showing up to complain.

The most significant development was Member Feller publicly breaking from the 6% tax ceiling. He did the math and acknowledged what anyone with a calculator can see -- you cannot close a $7M gap without either exceeding the ceiling or making cuts that gut the district. His early retirement buyout proposal -- 41 eligible staff, potential $1M savings if 20 take $50K buyouts -- shows creative problem-solving within realistic constraints. This is the kind of honest trade-off discussion I have wanted.

The board unanimously voted to request a formal elementary reconfiguration proposal for the March 2 workshop. DeAngelis was transparent about her personal support but insisted the board must decide based on evidence. The community school expansion approval, bringing eligibility for $100K annually in state grants, shows they are also pursuing revenue opportunities alongside cuts. Implementation timeline concerns remain -- September 2027 for elementary reconfiguration is ambitious.
