# Cumulative Record: Amira (PERSONA-013)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- February 3, 2026 -- 2026-02-03

### Interpretation

This meeting happened the night before the rescheduled budget forum — the one that got cancelled in January when ICE was at the laundromat. I didn't know that when I was reading about February 3rd the first time, but I know it now, and it changes how I hear everything said in that room.

The auditor told the city council that the school department had 34 accounting errors and moved $400,000 from grant money into the general fund because grants were spent wrong or on ineligible things. And then the assistant superintendent explained something I hadn't heard before: the school district has had six different finance directors in five years, three of them temporary.

Every explanation I've gotten since December for why teachers are being cut has been about things nobody could control — enrollment fell, the state underpaid its share, buildings are old and need expensive repairs. Those feel like weather. But 34 accounting errors and $400,000 in grants that went to the wrong places? That's not weather. That's something that happened in specific rooms by specific people who are no longer there. I'm not saying it's the whole reason for the $7.2 million gap. But it's part of the reason, and nobody told me that before. Money that's gone is money that could have stayed in teacher salaries or ed tech positions.

Texas Instruments asked for twenty more years of tax breaks at the same meeting where the school board chair said we're heading into "a very difficult budget year." Since January I've understood that property taxes are a competition — schools against the Mahoney building against homeowners already crying about their bills. Now there's a fourth player: companies that get money back before it even reaches any budget. I don't understand all the TIF rules, but I understand that if TI pays less, something else gets less.

The immigration resolution happened at the very end of the meeting — after three and a half hours. Someone said it out loud: "you make us wait to talk about it." The council passed it unanimously, which sounds good, but no eviction protections, no rent relief. I watched the forum get cancelled in January because families were too afraid to leave their houses. Then on February 3rd those families' advocates finally showed up, and they had to wait until near midnight to speak about people hiding in dark rooms. The council passed a resolution and went home. Families are still in the dark.
