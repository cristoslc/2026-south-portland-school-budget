# Cumulative Record: Meg (PERSONA-011)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- March 19, 2026 -- 2026-03-19

### Interpretation

Four and a half hours for about 45 seconds that mattered. That's city council for you — and it's why I watch the whole thing when half the people in my group chats would have given up during the stormwater permit update.

The moment came buried inside the Project Home vote. Councilor Matthews votes no on the $100,000 immigrant rental assistance — the lone holdout on a 6-1 decision — and his reason is the school budget. "72 people in the school department got their pink slips yesterday. 72. And 72 is just the first wave." On the record. At a city council meeting. During an unrelated agenda item. That's how I found out the layoff notices went out.

I've been tracking this crisis since December 8th when the spending freeze hit and the district confirmed a potential $1.9M deficit. We've been through the $7.2M structural gap, the 78 positions, the nine reconfiguration options, the Skillin fight — and the pink slips have been sitting out there as an abstraction. "78 positions being eliminated" is a budget line. "72 people got their pink slips yesterday" is a Tuesday. The abstraction collapsed tonight at a council meeting that had nothing to do with the school budget.

The number is also wrong, or at least different. I've been reporting $7.2M for months. Matthews said $8.4M. That gap — $1.2 million — is not noise. Either the deficit has grown since the February 4th forum, or Matthews is using a different accounting basis, or someone has a number I don't have. I'm not posting that figure until I can source it. This is exactly the kind of discrepancy that gets me challenged in the thread, and I need to be right.

The budget calendar is now set and that's actually useful information I can hand people tonight without caveats: April 14 workshop, May 5 council vote, June 9 referendum. Those are the dates. Write them down.

The sewer rate news will catch people off guard — 22% per year for three years, starting this fall, roughly $10 more per month in year one. Not school-related but it lands on the same property tax bill, the same household budget, the same people who are already absorbing whatever comes out of May 5.

Chief Ahern's apology happened, it was public and direct, and the $100K for Project Home passed anyway. The families who were still scared to show up to the January 22nd forum — and whose absence pushed that date to February 4th — that money is still going to them. Small thing, real thing.
