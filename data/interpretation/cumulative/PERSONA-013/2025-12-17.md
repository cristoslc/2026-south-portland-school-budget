### Interpretation

This is the one that made everything real. Before, I was worried about supply freezes and broken fields and expensive consultants. Now I'm finding out they want to cut FORTY-TWO teachers. That's not a small thing - that's like firing every teacher at Memorial and then some. My classes are already pretty full and now they're going to be huge. What if Mrs. Rodriguez gets cut? She's the only teacher who explains math in a way I actually understand.

And it's not just teachers - they're cutting 16 ed techs too. Those are the people who help kids like my friend David who need extra support. Without them, those kids just... what? Fall behind? That's so unfair. The whole thing is 78 positions being eliminated, which is a massive number. Meanwhile, my parents' taxes are STILL going up by 6%. So we pay more and get less? How does that work?

The one thing that kind of made me feel less angry at the school board specifically is learning that the state only covers about 20% of what it costs to run schools when it's supposed to cover 55%. So this isn't totally the school board's fault - it's a bigger problem. But that almost makes it worse because it means nobody at any level is looking out for us. The people in charge of schools don't have money, and the people who are supposed to give them money aren't doing it.

I told Mom I wish I could go to one of these meetings and tell them what it's actually like to be a student, but she said they probably wouldn't listen. And honestly? She's probably right.
