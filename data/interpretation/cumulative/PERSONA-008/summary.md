# Cumulative Summary: Rachel (PERSONA-008)

### Current Understanding

Rachel understands the South Portland school budget crisis as a multi-front institutional failure operating on a compressed, largely predetermined timeline. The deficit is $8.4 million — larger than the $7.2 million she first encountered in December — with 72 pink slips issued March 18th as "just the first wave." Kayler Elementary has been formally recommended for closure via a six-domain evaluation run twice by building administrators, producing the same result both times. The administration's stated preference is Option A: close Kayler and dissolve all neighborhood K-4 schools, reassigning every elementary student into grade-band buildings — K-1 primaries (Dyer, Small) and grades 2-4 intermediates (Brown, Skillin). Option B is not an alternative; Superintendent Prince stated explicitly it is "a path to Option A in the following fall." No transportation modeling exists for either option, and the board adjourned March 23rd without a vote.

The formal decision timeline now runs to an April 7th public hearing, April 14th council workshop, May 5th council vote, and June 9th referendum. "Closing schools" is casual vocabulary at the city council level, and the mayor demonstrated fluency with the reconfiguration talking points months ago.

Rachel understands the crisis as institutionally constructed, not merely inherited: six finance directors in five years, repeat audit non-compliance, and $400K transferred from grants to cover overspending contradict the administration's framing of the deficit as external. The same households absorbing school cuts face an $87M public safety bond in November, sewer fees rising ~22% annually, and state structural underfunding (20% actual vs. 55% expected) that makes reconfiguration pressure permanent regardless of local decisions. An 85% grassroots parent opposition survey, 100+ forum participants, and union resistance have not shifted the institutional momentum. Rachel is going to March 30th. She no longer knows if it will change anything.
