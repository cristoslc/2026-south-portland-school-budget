### Reactions

Honestly, I'm feeling pretty frustrated after sitting through that marathon meeting. The good news is they finally locked down where the police station is going - the old Walgreens site - so at least that's one less variable in this whole mess. But then they spent hours debating the fire station and ended up right back where they started, committing to an $87 million bond referendum while the school board is trying to cut $8 million from the budget. The math just doesn't add up.

What really got to me were the speakers talking about housing segregation driving school segregation. As much as I want my kids to stay in their current schools, I can't ignore that some of our elementary schools are struggling with these deep inequalities. The woman who mentioned families choosing Cape Elizabeth's library over ours because it has better children's spaces - that hits home because I've done the same thing. We're asking our kids to succeed in a system where basic resources aren't distributed fairly.

The most maddening part was watching them postpone every single Mahoney decision after months of workshops and committee meetings. My kids need stability, our schools need resources, and our community needs leadership that can actually make decisions. Instead we got another round of "let's talk about this later" while the school budget crisis gets worse and property taxes keep climbing. At some point, all this indecision becomes a decision to let things fall apart.
