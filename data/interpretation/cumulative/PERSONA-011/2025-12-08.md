# Cumulative Record: Meg (PERSONA-011)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- December 8, 2025 -- 2025-12-08

### Interpretation

This meeting completely blindsided me and I'm still processing how to break this news to my group chats. The district is in serious financial trouble - we're looking at up to $1.9 million in the red by year-end, and they've already implemented a spending freeze that went into effect TODAY. Like, while I was watching the meeting, teachers were getting emails saying they can't buy classroom supplies anymore. No books, no laminating paper, no cleaning supplies - nothing that isn't payroll until June.

What's really frustrating is that the numbers keep changing and nobody seems to have a handle on the real picture. The teacher contract they just ratified apparently costs way more than they told the board last month because the estimates "didn't include the full increases." Then they signed the ed tech contract literally minutes before this meeting started and don't even know what that's going to cost yet. Finance Director Ketchum kept saying they need "more analysis" but meanwhile kids are going to be sitting in classrooms where teachers can't buy basic supplies.

The PTA discussion really hit home because I know exactly which schools in my networks have strong parent fundraising and which don't. They're basically asking PTAs to step in and buy what the district can't afford, but there was zero concrete plan for how that would work fairly across all schools. Some of my friends are going to be scrambling to organize emergency fundraisers while others won't have that option.

The scariest part is that Finance Committee Chair Holman confirmed they've burned through all their savings and legally cannot run a deficit. There's no safety net left - they have to make the math work somehow between now and June.
