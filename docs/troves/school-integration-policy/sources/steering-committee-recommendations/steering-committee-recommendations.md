# Summary of Steering Committee Recommendations

**South Portland Schools Elementary Boundaries and Configurations Steering Committee**
**March 22, 2024**

## Major Findings

The school district's elementary schools differ in substantial and significant ways in terms of the number of students:
- Who are students of color
- Who are multilingual learners
- Who are meeting learning expectations
- Who are economically disadvantaged
- Who are affected by mobility
- Who live in close proximity to their schools

As a result, students can have significantly different educational experiences. Students in homogeneous schools miss out on learning from diverse peers. Benefits of integrated schools include more positive racial attitudes later in life, increased tolerance and better intergroup relations, higher levels of social cohesion, and improved learning outcomes.

Currently, two schools have substantially more economically disadvantaged students than the other three.

## Recommendations

### 1. Attendance Zones

**1.1** The district should conduct an analysis of attendance zones and make changes so that as many families as possible can live within one mile of their assigned elementary school and there is greater equity among families who live in close proximity to their schools. This action should be coupled with viable grade-level configuration, as attendance zone changes alone will not fully address the issues. Analysis should be conducted by outside expert(s) with experience in this work, providing the Board with multiple options by January 30, 2025.

**1.2** District administration should immediately initiate the process of reviewing the assignment of Brickhill students to Brown School. That review should begin with securing language-accessible input from Brickhill families. If Brickhill families are rezoned, current Brown School students should be able to complete their elementary education at Brown if their families so choose.

### 2. Policy Change

**2.1** The School Board should approve a new policy requiring the Board to review and approve attendance zones every three years, beginning in 2024. The policy should also require that the administration report by November 30 of every school year about the impact of attendance zones on the demographic composition of elementary schools.

### 3. Supporting Economically-Disadvantaged Students

**3.1** The Board should ensure that schools with disproportionate numbers of economically disadvantaged students have significantly lower teacher-to-student ratios than other schools.

### 4. Exploring Potential Solutions and Their Impacts

**4.1** The Board should identify a consultant to complete an impact study of potential grade-level configuration changes on schools (including needed resources, traffic implications, etc.).

**4.2** District administration should secure family input and review highly-successful magnet programs in other school districts.

**4.3** The Board should identify a consultant to complete a proposal of a controlled choice model (including needed resources, traffic implications, etc.). The findings from the research above should be presented to the Board by January 30, 2025.

### 5. Additional Topics

**5.1** The Board should continue to support Pre-K expansion within the resource constraints of the district.

**5.2** The district should continue the process of discussion about a possible districtwide PTA.

**5.3** As the district embarks on the strategic planning process, the community involvement must reflect the diversity of the district. The district should specifically identify a strategic planning consultant with considerable experience engaging families of color and multilingual families.
