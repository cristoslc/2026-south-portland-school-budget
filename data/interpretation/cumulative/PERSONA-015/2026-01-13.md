# Cumulative Record: Kira (PERSONA-015)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- January 13, 2026 -- 2026-01-13

### Interpretation

Four hours on a Tuesday night, and the thing she cannot put down is not the $194 million. It is that the room never once said the name of who closed Mahoney in the first place.

She opened the January 6 thread expecting Mahoney to eat council bandwidth before the January 22 forum. What she got was worse and more specific: a four-hour meeting where a councilor stood up and mapped three generations of his family through the school building the district vacated because there aren't enough children anymore — and then the room moved on to bond capacity and library square footage without anyone connecting those two facts. The same enrollment collapse that is cutting forty-two teachers and sixteen ed techs is what made Mahoney available to the city. She knows this from her car, driving between three buildings that are simultaneously over-programmed and under-resourced. Nobody in that chamber said it out loud.

Olivia Montgomery said what Kira has been saying since December 4, with clinical credentials and in three public minutes. Placing a police station next to city hall will stop immigrant families from accessing any service in that building — not hypothetically, not eventually, but now, in this political climate, the one her families are already living inside. The same fear that kept children home on January 12 will keep their parents out of city hall if this design goes forward. Montgomery named it. The council spent two more hours talking about whether to include the library in the bond. Not one councilor brought her point back into the room.

That is the pattern she has been documenting since December 4: the participation barrier gets named in the official record by someone credible, and then it vanishes. DeAngelos read the empty December 10 room as attitude. The council today read Montgomery's three minutes as public comment and moved on. The mechanism is the same. Her families disappear from the policy conversation at the exact moment the policy conversation matters most.

And underneath all of it: the stacking. Athletic field bond, already approved. Pump station, probably fifty million, a sewer rate increase that doesn't even need a ballot question. Now potentially a hundred and ninety-four million more. And the school budget's own tax increase on top of that. The finance director showed the Mahoney bond number. Nobody added the rest. These all land on the same households. The same families. Hers.
