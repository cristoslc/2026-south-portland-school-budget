### Reactions

God, this is the budget meeting I've been dreading since we started seeing the enrollment projections two years ago. The $7.2M gap isn't a surprise - I've been running the numbers in finance committee and pushing administration to model different scenarios - but seeing it all laid out like this is still gut-wrenching. We hired 82 people while enrollment dropped by 300 kids. I know every one of those hires seemed necessary at the time, but the aggregate impact is devastating.

The 78 position cuts are going to be brutal, and I'm already anticipating the calls and emails we'll get. People will focus on the 42 teachers without understanding that we literally cannot maintain current staffing levels with a 23% enrollment drop in elementary alone. The 6% tax increase ceiling was the right call - anything higher and we'd lose the validation vote - but it forces us into exactly these impossible choices. What really gets me is that our per-pupil costs are the highest among comparable districts. That gives ammunition to every critic, but it also suggests there might be efficiency gains we haven't found yet.

The state funding situation just adds insult to injury - 20% when it should be 55%. I've testified at the statehouse about EPS formula changes, but we can't wait for Augusta to fix this. We have to deal with the reality we've got, and that reality is making cuts that will hurt kids and families while asking taxpayers to pay more anyway. This is going to be the hardest budget validation campaign I've ever been through.
