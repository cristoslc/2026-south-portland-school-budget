### Reactions

Jesus, 42 teachers? I've been through budget cuts before but nothing like this. They want to eliminate over 10% of our teaching staff while adding 80 more special ed kids we have to serve. Do the math on that - we're talking about 25-30 more students per classroom for those of us who survive the cuts. And they're calling it "right-sizing" when what it really is is a complete gutting of what we've built here.

What really gets me is how they're handling this whole elementary closure thing. Nine different scenarios? They've got spreadsheets and projections but have they talked to a single teacher about what happens when you cram Small and Brown kids into the remaining schools? Sarah Gay asked the right question at that forum - when will we know which specific positions are getting cut? But of course they don't have an answer because they haven't figured out how to actually deliver education with half the staff.

The union needs to get more aggressive here. I'm glad Sarah and Abby are asking tough questions, but we need to be organizing now, not waiting for the axe to fall. When I started teaching here, we had a fund balance, reasonable class sizes, and a community that valued education. Now we're $7.2 million in the hole and they're acting surprised that you can't run a school district like a business. Those of us left standing are going to be doing the work of two people for the same pay.
