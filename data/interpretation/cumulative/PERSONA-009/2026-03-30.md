# Cumulative Record: Dana (PERSONA-009)
## Meeting: School Board Special Budget Meeting -- March 30, 2026 -- 2026-03-30

### Interpretation

Dana walked into March 30 expecting the decisive vote she had been pointing toward since March 9. She got three votes, two breakdowns, one student who landed punches, and a budget that failed at 11 PM after five hours of the most consequential decisions this board has made all season. The story she planned to close tonight has a new deadline on Thursday.

The closure vote is the cleanest lede she has logged since DeAngelo called the council "not sufficient, not adequate, not appropriate, and not acceptable" in February. Six to one. Kaler is closing. That is the lower-third, the anchor toss, the story. But the footage around the vote is better than the vote itself. Richardson admitting on the record she has not slept, she has been yelling at her kids, she barely talked to her husband — that is not a prepared statement, that is a person breaking in public, and it will be the clip that runs. Holman stopping mid-sentence because Kaler was where his son attended his first English language program at five and a half — Dana has been covering Holman as a procedural vote for months, a number on a split decision. That fifteen seconds is the whole season in one unfinished sentence.

The DEI director demotion is the surprise that complicates everything she thought she knew about the equity frame. Dana has been tracking the board's equity argument since DeAngelis's December 1 inauguration, through the "families west of the Dairy Queen" speech, through the midnight Title VI non-answer at March 23. The administration quietly demoted the district's only BIPOC leader to a teacher-level position that saves zero dollars — and DeAngelis made it her condition for voting no on the budget. The equity story is no longer running only in the direction Dana had mapped it. The board was protecting vulnerable families from school closure while the administration was quietly eliminating the one leadership position that represented them. That is a harder, more accurate story.

The budget failing 5-2 is the twist that turns a one-segment story into a series. A board that just voted to close a school and restructure every other elementary building in the city, and then could not get five votes on the money to do it, is the certified institutional failure Dana first assembled in February from the auditor's findings and confirmed in March when Ketchum said the district drove off a cliff it did not see coming. The cliff edge just moved. Kate Murray describing being bit, hit, spit on, urinated on, and vomited on — and saying what keeps her going is her team, the team that is being eliminated — is the human cost of both the closure and the cuts in a single person who may be willing to talk.

Thursday is the meeting to send a crew to. April 7 is the city council deadline that closes the window.
