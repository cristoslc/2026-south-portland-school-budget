### Reactions

Oh my God, I can't believe what I just watched. So the school board - the same board that's been telling us we need to cut everything because we don't have money - just voted to spend THIRTY THOUSAND DOLLARS on a superintendent search. And get this - they literally said they have "zero" budgeted for it. ZERO. But somehow they found the money for the most expensive option?

The whole thing felt so disconnected from what parents actually need. Like, all three firms talked about "community engagement" but then Zeal - the one they picked - basically said the board would make all the decisions themselves. The other firms would have had actual parents and community members screening candidates, but no, our board decided THEY need to do all the interviewing because it will help them "reach consensus" and "unify." I'm sorry, but when did this become about board team-building?? And Rosemary even admitted that nobody from the public showed up to this meeting, which should tell them something about how they're communicating with us.

The worst part is they kept talking about how this is "an investment" and we can't afford to make the wrong choice. But you know what we also can't afford? Cutting classroom aides and art teachers while spending $30,000 on consultants! I just don't understand how they can look parents in the eye and say we need to tighten our belts, then turn around and hire a search firm that costs four times as much as the other options. This whole thing made me feel like they think they know better than the rest of us, and they're going to find their perfect candidate without really listening to what families actually want.
