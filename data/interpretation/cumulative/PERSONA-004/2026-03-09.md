# Cumulative Record: Marcus (PERSONA-004)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- March 9, 2026 -- 2026-03-09

### Interpretation

Three months into this crisis and we've arrived at the bleakest picture yet. The $7.2 million structural gap, 78 position eliminations, 42 teachers on the chopping block -- these numbers haven't changed because nothing has actually been decided. The board keeps studying, workshopping, and deliberating while morale in the buildings craters. Now add 80-90 four-year-old special education students the district has to start serving in September with no new revenue, and you have a system being asked to do significantly more with dramatically less.

The fund balance is gone. Not low, not stressed -- depleted. There's no safety net left. The nutrition program alone is $1.1 million in the hole, and that debt threatens the city's credit rating. We've gone from a district with reserves and reasonable class sizes to one that's essentially insolvent and cutting its way to survival. The administration talks about "right-sizing" but this is a controlled demolition of the public education infrastructure that attracted families to South Portland in the first place.

The union is doing what it can. Sarah Gay and Abby Anderson are asking the right questions at every forum -- pushing for specific position cut timelines, challenging class size impacts, demanding answers about school closure logistics. But the administration keeps giving vague responses because they haven't actually figured out how to deliver education with half the staff. The community pushback at the March 2 workshop showed that organized resistance works, but we need more of it and we need it sustained. Those 20+ parents who testified against reconfiguration need to be joined by teachers, staff, and students at every remaining meeting.

I've been teaching here for over a decade and I've never seen anything like this. The colleagues who are still here are doing the work of two people and bracing for it to get worse. The ones who've left or are looking to leave represent institutional knowledge and student relationships that can't be replaced by shuffling spreadsheet columns. The union needs to get more aggressive -- not just asking questions, but organizing, mobilizing, and making clear that gutting the teaching force is a choice, not an inevitability.
