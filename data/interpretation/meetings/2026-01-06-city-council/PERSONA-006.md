### Reactions

Well, that was actually better than most of these meetings. Don't get me wrong - they're still talking about spending money we don't have, but at least a couple of them seem to get it. The city manager was refreshingly honest when he said asking the council what tax increase is acceptable is "very unfair" when they don't know what they're dealing with yet. No kidding! That's exactly the problem - they always want us to sign the blank check first.

What really caught my attention was the school board actually asking residents for cost-saving ideas on January 22nd. About time! I've been saying for years there's got to be waste in that budget, especially with all those administrative positions. And the mayor acknowledging that property taxes are stressing out seniors and fixed-income folks - well, that's my household she's talking about. Maybe this state task force will actually do something useful instead of just creating more unfunded mandates.

The cemetery donation fund was smart too. Let the people who actually care about those places chip in instead of making everyone pay through their property taxes. That's the kind of thinking we need more of. But then they had to spoil it by teasing this Mahoney Center thing next week with some mystery "dollar tag." I swear, every time they start talking about spending, my tax bill goes up another hundred bucks.
