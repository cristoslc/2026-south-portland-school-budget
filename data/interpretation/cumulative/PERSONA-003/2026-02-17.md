# Cumulative Record: Jess (PERSONA-003)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- February 17, 2026 -- 2026-02-17

### Interpretation

By February 17, I've been living inside this crisis for ten weeks. I watched the January 20 meeting where DeAngelis announced the forum was postponed because immigrant families were afraid to leave their homes, and it shook me. I thought that was the moment the ICE threat became real. This meeting told me it was still happening -- and getting worse, not better -- three weeks later.

Cassie Moon describes driving a mother, who has a work permit, to work on back roads. The woman dove to the floor of the car. She called her kids and told them to lock the door and don't open it for anyone. Julia Edwards talks about her six-year-old son asking why there are still kids missing from his class. That's the age Mia will be when she starts. Those are the seats next to my daughter. The community stability I've been factoring into every "should we stay" calculation is not a fixed thing -- it's fragile, and it's being hollowed out week by week by something the school board can't budget for and the city council apparently won't protect against.

I now have names. That's what February 17 added to my picture of the city council. Not just a pattern of absence -- a roll call. Carly Williams stood up and said Project Home was ten days from running out of money for the families being economically destroyed by all of this. Four councilors voted no on the moratorium anyway. Coleman, Matthews, Pride, Scott. I've watched twelve city council meetings and the inaction has always felt structural, diffuse, hard to pin on any specific person. Now it's on the record, 4-2, and I know who voted which way.

Then -- past ten at night, in the middle of a Mahoney building workshop -- Mayor Tipton drops the clearest public statement I've heard about reconfiguration: two schools for pre-K through first grade, three schools for grades two through four. No names. No neighborhoods. And then back to geothermal heating. I know from the March 2 workshop that this architecture would become nine specific options on a spreadsheet. But on February 17, it was four sentences delivered after most people had gone to bed, with no follow-up from anyone. The school system my daughter is entering is being redesigned, and this is where it showed up in city governance.
