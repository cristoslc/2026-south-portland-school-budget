# Cumulative Record: Amira (PERSONA-013)
## Meeting: City Council Workshop (Goal Setting) -- January 15, 2026 -- 2026-01-15

### Interpretation

The January 15 meeting is almost invisible. There's an agenda. There's the knowledge that it happened. The city council gathered to set goals for the schools — goals that would shape everything that came after — and left no video, no minutes, no record I can actually read to find out what they decided.

By January 15 I already knew the main numbers. Forty-two teachers. Seven-point-two million dollar gap. State supposed to pay 55% but only paying 20%. What I didn't know — and only learned from the fiscal context attached to this meeting — is that the district's emergency savings are gone. Not low. Gone. No cushion, no fallback. The spending freeze from December, the enrollment that dropped 23% while the district kept hiring — it had all been eating through the reserves until there was nothing left. When I imagined the budget crisis before, I assumed somewhere there was a backup plan adults could pull if things got bad enough. There isn't one.

But the thing that stays with me, looking at this meeting from March, is what a city council goal-setting workshop actually means in the sequence of everything. This happened before the January 22 forum. Before the February 10 joint guidance session. Before the March 2 workshop with the nine closure options. The goals that shaped what the school board was even allowed to propose — those were set here, in this room, by city councilors, without any public notes. All the meetings I've been watching since December, all the forums and workshops and budget presentations — they were all downstream of what got decided on January 15th. Parents showed up angry in February and March. But the goals were already set.

I've been trying to understand where students could actually make a difference in this process. Jules at the December council meeting. Alex and Angela on the superintendent committee. "Dozens" of students for Mahoney. The January 22 forum. None of those are this. This is the upstream meeting, the one where the direction gets set. And it left no public record. Nobody told me to show up. I didn't know showing up was even an option. I'm not sure it was.
