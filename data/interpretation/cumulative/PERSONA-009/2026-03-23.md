# Cumulative Record: Dana (PERSONA-009)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop -- March 23, 2026 -- 2026-03-23

### Interpretation

She almost didn't send a crew. "Budget workshop" on the Redbank agenda, and she has been pointing at this meeting since March 9 as the likely inflection point — but part of her was hedging against another five-hour session that produces presentation slides and no votes. She got five hours and no vote. She also got the cold open she did not know she was waiting for.

Board Chair DeAngelis's personal breakdown before a single budget number appears on screen — partner dead of mesothelioma, immigrant parents, union presidency, cancer diagnosis, a public apology to a colleague — is the kind of opening that does not come from a press release and cannot be reconstructed after the fact. You either have it on tape or you don't. Dana has it on tape. The meeting started as a piece of television before it became a budget meeting.

But the cold open is not the story she is carrying out of the building. The story is Kayler. She has been tracking the school closure equity argument since DeAngelo's "families west of the Dairy Queen" speech on March 2 — moral outrage, political positioning, a board that rejected its cheapest option on conscience. March 23 gives that argument a legal address. A former Kayler PTA president stood at the microphone and said that school is 45% BIPOC and 30-35% multilingual learners, then asked directly whether the district had checked the Kayler closure against Title VI of the Civil Rights Act. The board chair said they would need a legal answer. There is no legal answer yet. That is a different kind of equity story than anything the board has aired — not moral, not political, but potentially actionable. The district just adjourned without a vote and without an answer to a civil rights question that was put to them in public. Dana is already calculating whether she can get a civil rights attorney on camera before March 30.

Blair Bacon is the teacher she has been looking for since December 17, when she first wrote in her head: find one of the 42 teachers willing to go on camera. Twenty-five years in the classroom. National board certified twice. A multilingual endorsement. Hire date: July 10, 2023. That is the only number the contract sees. She said it herself, at the podium, and then she endorsed consolidation and told the board to vote. That is not the bitter-teacher story Dana expected. That is harder to categorize and more usable for exactly that reason.

Finance Director Ketchem disclosed at the podium that she is the seventh finance director in six years — one more than the external auditor's count from February 3 — and said on camera that "there's no way our books could have been in order." Dana has been waiting four months for an inside voice to say what the audit put in a report. She has it now. And the union president's 7.6-to-one salary comparison is the kind of graphic that stops a remote control: $20,800 for a lunch aide, $158,000 for the superintendent who left. No framing required.

March 30 is the meeting she cannot miss.
