# South Portland School Department proposes closure of Kaler Elementary School

**WMTW News 8 (Hearst Television)** — Reporter Bonnie Bishop — March 24, 2026 (updated 10:08 AM EDT)

## Summary

WMTW's television news report on the March 23 South Portland school board budget workshop, covering the proposed closure of Kaler Elementary School as part of the FY27 budget proposal. The piece is primarily a broadcast script with some additional web text. Reporter Bonnie Bishop filed live from South Portland.

## Key claims and data points

- The proposed FY27 budget stands at **nearly $76 million**.
- Proposed **12% reduction in staff** — approximately **70 jobs cut**, including **30 teaching positions**, plus middle and high school reductions.
- Keeping all five elementary schools open would require cutting **12-16 additional positions** beyond those already proposed.
- Letters notifying affected employees went out on the **Wednesday prior** (approximately March 18), per Board Chair Rosemarie De Angelis.
- The goal is to keep **property tax increases below 6 percent**.
- The budget has been in development for approximately **four months**.
- The next budget workshop: **March 30, 6 p.m., South Portland High School lecture hall**.
- The budget is scheduled to be presented to **city council on April 7**.

## Notable quotes (under 15 words each)

- "A path back to building sustainability and financial health." — George Entwistle, Interim Superintendent
- "It does come at a cost to those impacted." — George Entwistle
- "We are in an incredibly painful situation." — Abigail Ketchen, Director of Finance
- "As a district, we have reached what could be described as rock bottom." — Shea Cupit, literacy specialist at Kaler

## Key named officials

- **George Entwistle** — Interim Superintendent of Schools
- **Abigail Ketchen** — Director of Finance

## Process notes

- Report describes the workshop as emotional; "dozens of people — teachers and parents — spoke."
- Board must decide between: (a) splitting remaining four schools into pre-K/1 and grades 2-4 buildings, or (b) keeping all buildings as pre-K through grade 4.
