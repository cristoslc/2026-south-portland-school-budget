### Reactions

Well, that was four hours I'll never get back, but at least I learned where my tax money's actually going. The school situation is just maddening - six finance directors in five years, the budget over-spent again, $400,000 in grant money they had to shuffle around because they couldn't follow the rules. And then the school board chair has the nerve to come up there and tell the council they need to give "clear guidance" for next year's budget. How about this for clear guidance: learn to live within your means like the rest of us have to?

The one bright spot was hearing about the Texas Instruments deal. Now, I'm usually skeptical when big companies come asking for tax breaks, but the city manager laid out the numbers pretty clearly - we'd actually get $400,000 more per year by extending it than by letting it expire. That's because of some complicated tax shift thing, but the bottom line is it's more money in our pockets instead of less. Still don't love giving any company a break when I'm paying full price, but at least this one actually benefits the city instead of just the company.

What really gets me is finding out the city's sitting on over $20 million in fund balance while they're always talking about tight budgets. I get that some of it's reserved for emergencies, but there's still millions they could use for roads or other stuff instead of hitting us with another tax increase. Makes you wonder what else they're not telling us at these budget meetings.
