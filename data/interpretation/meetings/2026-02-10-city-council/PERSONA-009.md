### Reactions

This is exactly why I love budget season - three hours of dense policy talk, but when you strip away all the consultant-speak, you get a story that basically writes itself. The superintendent walks in and casually drops that doing nothing costs taxpayers 19% more in taxes. NINETEEN PERCENT. That's your opening line right there.

But here's what makes this actually compelling TV - you've got this perfect storm where enrollment is tanking, they've hired 82 more people anyway, and now they're looking at cutting 50 positions including teachers while asking taxpayers to eat a massive tax hike. The union president is pissed, the school board chair is screaming at the city council, and you've got seniors in the audience literally worried about becoming homeless. That's not policy wonk stuff - that's real drama with real stakes.

The visual storytelling practically shoots itself. You've got that chart showing enrollment going down while staffing goes up - that's your money shot. Then you can cut between the packed room of worried parents and the spreadsheets full of red numbers. Interview the bike bus mom who's worried about kids having to drive instead of walk to school, then cut to the guy talking about his $6,600 tax bill. It's the classic local news tension between "what about the children" and "what about my wallet" - except this time, both sides have legitimate points and nobody has good answers.
