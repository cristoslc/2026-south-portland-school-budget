# Cumulative Record: Jess (PERSONA-003)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- December 8, 2025 -- 2025-12-08

### Interpretation

This is my first real look inside South Portland's school board, and honestly, I'm getting whiplash from the mixed signals. On one hand, I'm thrilled to see Tyler Smith join the board - he actually has a toddler like me and real education experience, so finally there's someone who gets what it's like to be planning for kindergarten entry. But then the financial picture hits like a truck: they're so broke they can't buy classroom supplies for the rest of the year, and elementary enrollment has crashed by 23% in just four years. That's terrifying - are we looking at a district in free fall?

What's really messing with my head is seeing both crisis and opportunity at the same time. Yes, they might run a $1.9 million deficit, but then I watch this presentation about their Extended Learning Opportunities program where high schoolers are getting real job training and certifications. And the community support is incredible - over $46,000 in donations this month alone, plus parents literally standing up offering to help with connections and funding. That tells me people aren't giving up on these schools.

But here's what's keeping me up at night: all these budget decisions feel reactive to current problems, not strategic about what the district will look like when my son starts kindergarten in 2029. If they're cutting supplies now and enrollment keeps dropping, what's left by then? I need to figure out if this is a temporary rough patch that strong community support can weather, or if South Portland schools are actually in permanent decline and I should be looking at other districts now before we're locked in.
