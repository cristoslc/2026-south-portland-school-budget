# Cumulative Record: Linda (PERSONA-007)
## Meeting: School Board Budget Workshop -- March 23, 2026 -- 2026-03-23

### Interpretation

This is the meeting where the most honest public accounting of how we got here finally happened. Abigail stood at the lectern and named it: no minimum fund balance threshold policy, seven finance directors in six years, COVID relief money propping up positions that had no sustainable funding underneath them. I have been in finance committee for two budget cycles pushing for a fund balance floor. We still don't have one. Hearing those root causes said out loud — with attribution, in a public meeting — is both a professional relief and a personal rebuke I will be sitting with for some time.

But the FY28 math is what I cannot put down. Abigail said it plainly: FY27 resets the path; it does not fix the structure. Labor costs grow faster than 6% if staffing stays static. Electricity is increasing 13-14% annually. The athletic field bond principal payment adds at least $300,000 in debt service next year. She called it mathematically impossible not to have a problem the following year. I have been treating FY27 as the year we stop the bleeding. I now have to treat FY28 as the year we stay stopped — and nobody has shown me a plan for that yet.

Daniel asked about fund balance seeding. The answer was this year is too dire. Eleni followed with the right question: what covers the unexpected? City fund balance, repaid through a future tax increase. I identified the political toxicity of that arrangement at the February 10 joint workshop. Now it is not a contingency — it is the confirmed operational plan.

The Title VI question is what I will be on the phone about first thing tomorrow. Jess Elsner asked it precisely: Kayler is 45% BIPOC, 30-35% multilingual learners. What is our standing under the Civil Rights Act if we close it? Rosemarie said she had done some quick research. I said I want a formal legal brief, not a search. That answer should have been sitting at the table weeks ago — we have known this was the administration's recommendation. And then we adjourned at 11:15 without voting on anything. March 30 is one week away. April 7 is the City Council presentation. The calendar does not negotiate.

The one thread that closed cleanly: the administration confirmed both Option A and Option B are achievable for fall 2026. The board has a real choice — including a bridge year if we need more community engagement before full reconfiguration in 2027-28.
