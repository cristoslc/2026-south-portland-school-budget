# Cumulative Record: Jess (PERSONA-003)
## Meeting: School Board Workshop -- December 10, 2025 -- 2025-12-10

### Interpretation

Two days after learning about the district's financial crisis, I'm watching them spend $30,000 they don't have on a superintendent search, and I honestly don't know whether to laugh or cry. The irony is thick - they're so broke they can't buy classroom supplies, but they'll find money for the most expensive search firm because "you get what you pay for." Part of me actually respects that they're not going cheap on finding leadership, especially after seeing how badly the last superintendent situation went. But another part of me is screaming "fiscal responsibility!" 

What's giving me some hope though is seeing how intentional they are about diversity. Every single board member pushed these search firms on reaching candidates of color and understanding that South Portland isn't typical Maine. Board member Richardson was particularly strong on this, and it makes me feel like they actually see our community for what it is. That matters when I think about my son starting school here in a few years - I want leadership that gets our demographics.

But here's what's really crystallizing for me: this board makes decisions reactively, not strategically. They forced out a superintendent without budgeting for replacement costs. They're cutting classroom supplies while spending on searches. The pattern from Sunday's meeting continues - crisis management instead of forward planning. And that's exactly what terrifies me as a parent planning ahead. If they can't think through basic succession planning, how can I trust them to think through what the district needs to look like when my kids are in elementary school?

At least I'll know who's running things by spring, which gives me time to evaluate whether this new leadership can actually turn the ship around before my family is locked into this district.
