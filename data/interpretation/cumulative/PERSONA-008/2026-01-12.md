# Cumulative Record: Rachel (PERSONA-008)
## Meeting: Regular -- January 12, 2026 -- 2026-01-12

### Interpretation

Tonight the other shoe dropped. The board didn't just hint at restructuring or bury it in euphemisms -- they actually voted to have the superintendent produce a formal elementary reconfiguration proposal by March 2nd. That's the concrete step I've been dreading since the Chair first mentioned "boundaries and configurations" back in December. This isn't a study or a conversation anymore. This is a motion, a vote, and a deadline.

Chair DeAngelis's tone was the most alarming part. She wasn't reluctant or measured -- she was enthusiastic, talking about "creative ways to save money" with an energy that tells me she's been waiting for the budget crisis to provide cover for changes she already wanted. And her emphasis on getting input from "non-parent taxpayers"? That's strategic. She's building a coalition of people who won't feel the pain of redistricting because they don't have kids in these schools. It's clever politics and it makes me furious.

The finance committee's report that 92% of funds are already spent or committed at the halfway point of the fiscal year adds genuine urgency I can't dismiss. The money situation is real. But "real" doesn't mean "the only solution is to uproot kids." Member Feller pushed for extensive community input, and that's exactly the foothold parents need. If we can make the process open and transparent, we have a chance to steer decisions away from the most disruptive options.

The silver lining -- and it's thin -- is that some board members clearly understand the weight of what they're considering. Feller's insistence on community input and the general acknowledgment that this is a "big decision" suggest there's still space to influence the outcome. But the March 2nd deadline means we have about seven weeks. I need to be at the January 22nd budget forum with every parent I can rally. That's where we establish that this community will not quietly accept having its schools dismantled.
