# Cumulative Record: Amira (PERSONA-013)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- February 9, 2026 -- 2026-02-09

### Interpretation

This meeting was a weird mix of things that made me proud and things that made me scared. The proud part: high school students created "The Little Kitchen" near the cafeteria to provide food and essentials for families affected by the immigration enforcement stuff from January. Students actually DID something when adults were still just talking about it. That's exactly what I wished could happen at my school - students helping students' families directly.

But then there's the scary stuff. The board actually voted to request a formal proposal on elementary reconfiguration - some schools for PreK-1 and others for grades 2-4. So it's moving forward. And the teachers' union president said staff won't support plans that result in colleagues losing jobs, and everyone's worried about the rushed timeline to implement by September. My teachers are scared. Like, actually scared about their futures. That makes ME scared because if the adults who take care of us don't know what's happening to them, how am I supposed to feel safe?

The school board chair said something that really bugged me. She said kids are "far more flexible than parents" and that reconfiguration would be "educationally beneficial." But she never asked any kids. She's deciding what's good for us without talking to us. At least the student board members got approved to interview superintendent candidates, which is cool, but that's two students out of thousands.

The nutrition fund deficit is $1.1 million, which is the answer to my question from way back in December about what "reducing nutrition fund deficit" meant. It means school lunches cost way more than what the state pays back, and the district is eating (no pun intended) the difference. And the district officially became a "community school district" which means more partnerships with community organizations for things like health care and afterschool programs. That actually sounds good if it happens.
