# Cumulative Summary: Ben (PERSONA-010)

### Current Understanding

Ben understands the South Portland school budget crisis as a systemic institutional failure, not a discrete planning error. The district faces an approximately $8 million gap for FY27, driven by three compounding forces: a 23% elementary enrollment decline between 2021 and 2025, a simultaneous addition of 82 staff positions, and chronic under-budgeting of predictable expenses documented across at least four consecutive fiscal years. The fund balance is at zero against the district's own stated 9–12% reserve floor. Seven finance directors in six years — confirmed by the current director herself — prevented institutional memory from forming across fiscal years, making the December 2025 crisis structurally near-inevitable. A repeat audit finding of "material weakness" and roughly $400,000 in mismanaged grants confirm the dysfunction predates any single individual.

The school crisis does not stand alone. The city's own department requests entered budget season at a 15% levy increase. A 22% annual sewer rate increase runs through FY29. A November 2026 public safety bond of approximately $87 million is advancing. A scaled-down Mahoney city center project ($70–76 million) targets a November 2027 referendum. No public body has yet stated the combined annual household impact of all four variables against the $514,000 median assessed home value — a gap Ben treats as the defining accountability failure of the season.

The board voted to close Kaler and approve Option A reconfiguration on March 30, but the FY27 budget itself failed 2–5. Attendance boundaries, staffing configurations, and community listening sessions for reconfiguration remain unscheduled as of April 2. The September 2026 implementation timeline is broken. Approximately $1 million in potential new state funding surfaced mid-meeting on April 2 and has not been confirmed by the superintendent's office.
