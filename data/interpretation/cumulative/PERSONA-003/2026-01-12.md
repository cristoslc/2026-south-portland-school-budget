# Cumulative Record: Jess (PERSONA-003)
## Meeting: School Board Regular Meeting -- January 12, 2026

### Interpretation

Just when I was ready to write South Portland off, they hit me with something I did not expect: a pre-K expansion. Starting July 2026 they're joining a state program to provide four-year-old pre-K services, and they already have 64 seats in district schools plus 15-16 at the Chickadee partnership. That is directly relevant to my family -- my daughter could potentially benefit from this within the next couple of years. For the first time in weeks I felt like someone in that room was thinking about kids who aren't yet in the system.

But then the other shoe dropped. The board approved a motion to have the superintendent produce a formal "elementary school reconfiguration proposal" for the March 2 budget workshop. Reconfiguration. That word is doing a lot of heavy lifting and nobody is explaining what it actually means for incoming families. Are they closing schools? Combining grades in different buildings? I have no idea, and the insider language is impossible to decode from the outside. Chair DeAngelis was apparently pushing hard for this, which tells me it's serious, not just a study exercise.

The financial news continues to be grim. They're tracking toward a deficit this fiscal year -- 92% of funds spent or committed at the halfway mark. That confirms what I feared after December: the cuts they announced aren't even solving this year's problem, let alone next year's. The chair called it "another difficult budget year" and said the school budget is 61% of property taxes. So we're the majority of the tax bill and we're still broke.

What frustrates me most is the disconnect between their desire for community input and how inaccessible the process is. They're holding a budget forum on January 22 and want broad attendance, but how am I supposed to give meaningful input when I can't decode what "reconfiguration" means, I don't know which schools are affected, and I'm home with a toddler at bedtime? The superintendent search is also moving forward -- they hired Zeal, community forums in late January -- so at least there will be permanent leadership eventually. But right now everything feels like it's in flux and nobody is translating any of it for parents like me.
