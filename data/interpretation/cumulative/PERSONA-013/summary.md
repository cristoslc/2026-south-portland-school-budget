# Cumulative Summary: Amira (PERSONA-013)

### Current Understanding

Amira now understands South Portland's school budget crisis as a multilayered structural failure with both unavoidable and preventable dimensions. The district faces an $8.4 million gap driven by three converging causes: chronic state underfunding (the state covers roughly 20% of costs against a mandated 55%), a decade of staffing growth during enrollment decline (300+ students lost since 2017 while 82 positions were added), and internal financial mismanagement (34 audit errors, $400K in mishandled grants, six finance directors in five years). The fund balance is entirely depleted. Seventy-two layoff notices were issued on March 18 — the 42-teacher cut she first learned about in December is now in pink-slip form.

Schools represent 61% of South Portland's property tax bill, and the school budget competes for the same tax pool as the Mahoney building project, a proposed $87 million police and fire bond, and corporate tax abatements. The real upstream decisions were set by city council in January in a closed session that left no public record, before any public forum opened. The elementary reconfiguration process has narrowed to closing Skillin — a choice driven by HVAC savings, not educational outcomes — and at least four board members oppose it. The June 9 adoption deadline is approaching.

Running parallel throughout, the Somali and immigrant community Amira belongs to has moved from civic welcome in December to ICE enforcement at neighborhood businesses in January, a cancelled participation forum, a failed eviction protection vote (4–2), and a contested $100K rental assistance appropriation in March. Councilor Matthews' no vote on that appropriation formally named the school budget and immigrant community needs as competing claims on the same limited fund — without asking any students which mattered more. Student voice, Amira now understands, is not absent from South Portland civic life — it is unevenly distributed and invisible to those outside the existing networks that carry it.
