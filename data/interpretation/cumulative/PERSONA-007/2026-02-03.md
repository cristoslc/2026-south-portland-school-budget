# Cumulative Record: Linda (PERSONA-007)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- February 3, 2026 -- 2026-02-03

### Interpretation

This is the night before the rescheduled community budget forum — the one that got pushed from January 22nd because families in Red Bank were afraid to leave their homes. And I'm watching the city council receive an audit that makes what I already knew about our governance failures look worse in daylight.

Tim came to the podium and explained, as matter-of-factly as you can explain something this bad, that the school district required 34 journal entries to close the FY25 audit. His benchmark is under 10. Thirty-four. And $400,000 in grant funds moved back to general fund because they were either ineligible or overspent. Then the compliance piece: the school budget exceeded its legally adopted limit — again. Repeat finding. I've been sitting in finance committee trying to build oversight mechanisms for months, and hearing the word "repeat" from an outside auditor in a city council chamber is one of the more professionally humbling moments of this whole process.

Joe did his best to put a forward-looking frame on it — six finance directors in five years, yes, but confidence in the current one, reconciliations already improving. I want to believe him. I'll believe him more when I see it hold through a full budget cycle. Institutional disruption explains how we got here; it doesn't clear the compliance violation.

The number that I'll carry out of this meeting is the city's fund balance: $8.5 million in undesignated funds. I didn't have that figure before tonight. Knowing what came next — the February 10th joint workshop, the school side functionally at zero — that $8.5 million is the cushion that prevented a much worse governance crisis from becoming a public one. I was modeling the worst-case scenario without knowing whether the city had any floor under it. Tonight gave me the floor.

Rosemary appeared at the podium and asked for "clear guidance" in a way that wasn't alarm but definitely wasn't business as usual. She said 61%, she said 80% personnel, she used the phrase "difficult budget year" to a room that needed to hear it from her directly. It's the first time since her inaugural address — where she was, I now think, being appropriately careful rather than evasive — that she has shown publicly she understands what this year is. The council heard it. Whether they were ready to answer it was the February 10th question.

The immigration resolution — unanimous, with the eviction moratorium amendment — gives me one thing to bring to tomorrow's forum. It's not safety. But it's the city government formally on record about what these families are facing, one day before I walk into a community meeting and try to explain school consolidations to the neighborhoods most affected by it.
