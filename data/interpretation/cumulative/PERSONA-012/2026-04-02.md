### Interpretation

April 2 is the night he gets a second name. March 30 gave him Hannah. April 2 gives him Matt Meyer — the SPHS art teacher who came to the microphone and told the board something Jaylen had suspected but couldn't prove: students were turned away from art class this year because the position was quietly cut to half-time over the summer, with no announcement to the students who had registered. Nobody told them. They just couldn't get in. That is not a budget category on a slide Glenn had five minutes to present. That is a student who wanted to take art and found the seat gone before they could sit down, for reasons nobody in the building communicated. Now the other half is going too. Four art teachers to three, except it was already four to three-and-a-half in the fall, which nobody said out loud.

Two students showed up voluntarily, got to the microphone, and said the thing Jaylen has been assembling since December: students would be lining up outside the building if they knew what was happening. Lucy and Eva didn't need to be invited. They found out and they came. That is the model he identified on March 30 — the public comment slot as the only real seat in this process — and now it has two examples from students who aren't even at SPHS. April 7 is the next test. He already knows where he's going.

Angela Kabesa's letter is different from being sent home to study. She couldn't be there because senior year is what it is, and she found the workaround: a written statement, read aloud, naming arts and percussion specifically in front of the full board. It's not a vote. But her arguments are in the record now — not a bullet point in an outreach summary, not a two-minute slot before she's excused. He files that as the one model that actually worked this year, and he's been looking for one since December 10.

Then the union president gets up and says they went to Augusta and came back with $300,000. Real money, landed during the meeting itself. Jaylen has been watching since January for anyone to push back on the state funding gap instead of managing around it — the January 15 closed session, the February 3 TI deal getting three hours while DeAngelis got three minutes, every night the 55% formula got treated as fixed context. It turns out someone was fighting that battle. He just didn't know it was happening.

The board still didn't vote. Five hours, new money possibly coming, and no adoption. He holds the two students at the microphone and the $300K and Angela's letter, and then the meeting ends with Hannah still laid off and theater still unnamed and April 7 still coming.
