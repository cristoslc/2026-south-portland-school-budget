**Last updated:** 2026-04-02

## What This Source Is

This document is a parent/community resource built around the elementary reconfiguration and closure debate. It is not a district or board record. It combines:

- a status summary of the FY27 budget fight
- meeting dates and contact lists
- a running list of questions and unknowns
- links to outside research on school transitions, belonging, transportation, and sibling effects
- draft emails and talking points for engaging state and district officials

The document is explicitly living content and says it will keep changing as events progress.

## Key Themes

### Community mobilization

The strongest thread is organizing parents to respond to the reconfiguration process. The doc says families were surprised by the pace of the proposal, created a survey, and want broader outreach through translation, flyers, email lists, and public comment.

### Operational readiness

The doc argues that the district is not ready to implement a full elementary reconfiguration on the stated timeline. Its main concerns are:

- no attendance zone map
- no transportation plan
- no labor agreement for staff moves
- no clear facilities readiness plan

This is presented as a compliance and safety problem, not just a policy disagreement.

### Student impact

The research section focuses on school transitions, school belonging, mental health, logistics, and sibling separation. The overall claim is that abrupt structural moves can be harder on younger students, vulnerable students, and working families.

### Equity and communication

The document repeatedly asks how the district will protect multilingual families, students with IEPs, and families with limited flexibility around childcare, transportation, and work schedules. It also argues that district communication has not reached enough families.

## Evidence Cited In The Doc

The doc cites a mix of peer-reviewed and policy sources, including studies on:

- school transitions and academic outcomes
- school connectedness and mental health
- bus commute length and student outcomes
- active transportation
- sibling intimacy and peer competence
- Portland's earlier reconfiguration work as a slower comparison point

## Points Of Agreement

The source aligns with the primary budget troves on a few basics:

- the district is under major fiscal pressure
- reconfiguration is being treated as a serious budget lever
- the process is moving quickly
- transportation and enrollment logistics matter

## Points Of Tension

This source pushes harder than the district materials on readiness and process. It frames the proposal as premature and risky, while district documents frame it as a necessary response to a structural budget gap. That makes this trove useful as a record of the parent-side argument, but not as a neutral account.

## Gaps

- It does not provide independent verification for every factual claim.
- It does not include district responses to each concern.
- It does not replace the primary budget documents or meeting transcripts.
- Because it is a living document, some details may change after the fetch date.
