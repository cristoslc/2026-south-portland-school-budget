# Cumulative Summary: Maria (PERSONA-001)

### Current Understanding

As of March 19, 2026, Maria understands the South Portland school district to be facing a structural budget gap now cited at $8.4M — driven by 23% elementary enrollment decline over four years (from 1,401 to 1,080 students), a state funding system covering only 20% of actual costs against its statutory 55% obligation, and years of internal financial mismanagement confirmed by repeat audit findings, illegal budget overages, and six finance directors in five years. The fund balance is fully depleted.

The board's primary response is elementary school reconfiguration: consolidating five neighborhood K–4 schools into two primary campuses (pre-K–1) and three intermediate campuses (grades 2–4), saving an estimated $2M. Nine options were presented at the March 2 workshop; none has been finalized. Chair DeAngelis publicly endorsed reconfiguration before any formal proposal existed and dismissed neighborhood schools as something the city "doesn't really have anymore." The board advanced this plan unanimously despite near-unanimous parent opposition, 51 public data requests, and the teachers' union withholding support. On March 18, 72 employees received layoff notices — the process is in execution even as the specific reconfiguration option remains unresolved.

City council budget guidance came in at 3–6%, mostly 4–5%, below the school board's own floor, forcing cuts deeper than any scenario the board had modeled at its 6% ceiling. Simultaneously, the council voted to advance an $87M capital bond covering only a new police station and fire station — the library component was eliminated entirely — with Councilor Matthews explicitly invoking the school crisis before voting yes anyway.

ICE enforcement has directly suppressed immigrant family participation in budget forums, kept children absent from classrooms, and contributed to the enrollment instability the board is using to justify reconfiguration. The decision calendar is now locked: April 14 school budget workshop, May 5 council vote, June 9 public referendum. Maria is mobilizing her parent network toward April 14 as the last meaningful window for input before the math is finalized.
