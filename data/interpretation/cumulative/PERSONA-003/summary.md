# Cumulative Summary: Jess (PERSONA-003)

### Current Understanding

Jess is a parent with a toddler (Mia) planning to enter the South Portland school system around 2028. She has tracked the district's budget crisis across four months of school board and city council meetings, moving from initial shock through alarm, partial stabilization, and now into a state of disoriented exhaustion.

The core financial picture: the district faces a structural deficit now cited at $8.4 million (up from the $7.2 million reference figure used since December), a completely depleted fund balance, and seven finance directors in six years. Seventy-two layoff notices went out on March 18 before any budget vote. The FY27 budget still has not passed as of April 2.

Structurally, the board has voted Option A reconfiguration (PreK-1 in one set of buildings, grades 2-4 in another), with Kaler Elementary closing at the end of the 2025-26 school year. Mia will switch buildings between first and second grade. No attendance boundaries, transportation plan, or placement timeline exists yet for incoming families.

The pre-K expansion Jess held as her anchor has proven softer than understood: district-operated seats are flat at 64, and the growth depends entirely on external partnerships with eligibility requirements or off-site placements that may not serve her family. The board's only current elementary parent described projected kindergarten class sizes as "terrifying." The behavioral specialist position has been eliminated.

At the city level, the school crisis has been systematically absent from sustained council engagement. A confirmed triple tax stack — school budget increase, Mahoney bond, sewer rate hikes of ~22% annually for three years — has never been presented to residents as a combined number. The Mahoney project's family-serving components (library, early childhood spaces) have been removed with no alternative plan.

Community stability has been destabilized by ICE enforcement that kept immigrant families from school and emptied civic forums. Partial council response came through a $100,000 rental assistance fund approved 6-1, but downstream enrollment effects remain unquantified.

Jess is staying in South Portland but has no clear picture of what Mia's school experience will look like, from which building she will enter to what support structures will exist when she arrives.
