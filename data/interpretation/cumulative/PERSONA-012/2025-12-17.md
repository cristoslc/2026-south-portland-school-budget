# Cumulative Record: Jaylen (PERSONA-012)
## Meeting: School Board Workshop -- December 17, 2025

### Interpretation

This meeting transforms the budget crisis from abstract anxiety into a direct threat to Jaylen's senior year. The numbers are now concrete: 42 teachers cut out of 78 total positions eliminated, a $7.2M gap, and an 18-19% property tax increase as the only alternative. The spending freeze on supplies from December 8 was just the beginning. This is a wholesale restructuring of the school system that will determine whether the classes Jaylen is planning for senior year even exist.

The enrollment data creates a confusing contradiction. Elementary enrollment dropped 23% but the district somehow added 82 staff positions. Jaylen's lived experience at the high school doesn't match this picture -- his AP Lit class has 28 students and chemistry lab groups are overcrowded. The money went somewhere, but not to the classes he sits in every day. Now the district's solution is to slash positions everywhere instead of examining where those extra hires went.

The state funding revelation shifts some of Jaylen's anger from the local board to the broader system. The state covers only 20% of school costs when it should cover 55%, forcing the entire burden onto local property taxes. But this systemic context doesn't help Jaylen plan his senior year. He needs to pick classes in roughly two months and has no information about which programs survive the cuts. The district talks in budget categories (object codes, per-pupil costs, fund balances) that have zero connection to the things Jaylen experiences -- theater rehearsals, AP course offerings, cross-country coaching.

The fund balance is depleted, meaning there is no cushion for emergencies. Combined with the $30,000 superintendent search spend from the previous meeting, Jaylen sees a district that spent down its reserves and is now cutting teachers while hiring consultants. His determination to attend the next board meeting reflects a shift from passive frustration to active engagement, driven by the realization that his senior year is being decided right now without him.
