# Cumulative Record: Priya (PERSONA-005)
## Meeting: City Council Regular Meeting -- December 1, 2025 -- 2025-12-01

### Interpretation

Reading this meeting back through everything I have watched unfold over the following three months, the December 1 inauguration feels like a prologue — and a deeply ironic one. Rosemary DeAngelos stood at that podium and gave what I can only describe as the most equity-centered speech I had heard from any elected official in South Portland. She centered immigrant students by name, she talked about power inequities directly, she said the district needs to learn from its newest families. I was moved. And within weeks, her administration would recommend closing the most diverse school in the district and call it equity.

That gap between inauguration rhetoric and operational reality is the through-line of this entire budget season, and it was already visible on December 1 — I just did not know it yet. The multilingual community members who spoke at the ceremony, the stories of Iranians and Somalis and Cambodians contributing as educators and accountants — that was real. That representation mattered. But not one word was spoken about the $7.2 million budget hole that was already forming, or the 78 positions that would soon be on the chopping block, or which students would absorb the consequences. DeAngelos celebrated these families and then, weeks later, presided over a process that would restructure the schools serving their children without equity guardrails.

Mayor Tipton's Cushing's Point moment is the other thing I keep returning to. Framing the 1942 forced displacement of working-class families as a stirring example of community sacrifice — with nine days' notice, no choice, no recourse — is exactly the rhetorical move I have seen deployed throughout this budget season: reframing the costs borne by the least powerful as evidence of community virtue. The people who get steamrolled are always invited to feel good about their sacrifice. The housing discussion had the same quality: plenty of language about needing more affordable units, zero specifics about anti-displacement protections or income targeting.

What this meeting confirms, retroactively, is that South Portland's equity commitments were always clearer in ceremony than in structure. The will to celebrate diversity was there from day one. The will to protect it in the budget was not.
